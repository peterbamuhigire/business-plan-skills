# Balanced Scorecard KPI Design

Use this reference when building scorecards, KPI dashboards, or reporting calendars.

## Four-perspective logic

The Balanced Scorecard should show cause and effect:

1. Learning and capability
2. Internal process excellence
3. Customer outcomes
4. Financial outcomes

If the scorecard is just a KPI bucket, it is incomplete.

## Strategy map discipline

For each major objective, state:

- the capability to build
- the process to improve
- the customer result expected
- the financial result expected

Example:

- train field sales team
- improve lead qualification and conversion process
- raise close rate and retention
- improve revenue growth and gross margin

## KPI design rules

- combine lagging outcomes with leading drivers
- define every KPI with formula, source, owner, and threshold
- choose only metrics that trigger management action
- review the scorecard at the cadence the team can realistically maintain

## Practical KPI mix

Financial:

- revenue growth
- gross margin
- DSCR or interest cover
- cash runway or operating cash conversion

Customer:

- acquisition rate
- conversion rate
- churn / retention
- on-time fulfilment or service quality

Internal process:

- cycle time
- defect / rejection rate
- inventory turns or utilisation
- cost per transaction

Learning and capability:

- critical-role fill rate
- training completion
- system adoption
- process-compliance rate

## Common failures

- too many KPIs
- no threshold or action rule
- lagging measures only
- no owner
- no causal link to the strategy
