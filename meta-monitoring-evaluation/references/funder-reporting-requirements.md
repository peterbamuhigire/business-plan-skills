# Funder Reporting Requirements

*This reference file covers reporting obligations by funder type for Ugandan businesses. Loan covenant monitoring, grant quarterly reports, and impact investor metrics are distinct obligations that must be planned and budgeted for from Day 1.*

---

## 1. Commercial Bank Loan Covenants

When a Ugandan commercial bank approves a business loan, the loan agreement attaches performance covenants. Failure to meet covenants can trigger a review, increased interest rate, or demand for early repayment.

### Typical Financial Covenants (Ugandan Commercial Banks)

| Covenant | Typical Threshold | Reporting Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)** | Maintain ≥ 1.25x at all times | Quarterly (from management accounts) |
| **Current Ratio** | Maintain ≥ 1.0 (some banks ≥ 1.5) | Quarterly |
| **Debt-to-Equity Ratio** | Not exceed [X]x (varies by bank and sector) | Annually |
| **Net Worth** | Not fall below UGX [X] (agreed at loan origination) | Annually |
| **Insurance on collateral** | Maintain valid cover at all times | Annually (policy renewal evidence) |
| **No additional borrowing** | Must not take additional unsecured debt above [UGX threshold] without bank consent | Notify bank before borrowing |

### Mandatory Reporting to Bank

| Report | Frequency | Deadline | Format |
|---|---|---|---|
| Management accounts (P&L + balance sheet) | Quarterly | 30 days after quarter-end | Any standard format |
| Bank statements reconciliation | Monthly | 15th of following month | Bank statement + reconciliation |
| Annual audited/reviewed accounts | Annual | 6 months after financial year-end | ICPAU-certified accountant |
| Insurance renewal certificate | Annual | Before policy expiry | Copy of policy schedule |
| Trading licence renewal | Annual | Before licence expiry | Copy of renewed licence |
| NSSF compliance certificate | Annual | On request | NSSF portal printout |
| URA Tax Compliance Certificate (TCC) | Annual / on request | On request | URA e-Tax portal printout |

### Covenant Monitoring — M&E Integration

The business's internal M&E dashboard (from `meta-monitoring-evaluation/SKILL.md`) must include covenant monitoring as a permanent item:

```
MONTHLY COVENANT CHECK

DSCR this month:
  Net Operating Income: UGX [X]
  Loan repayment (principal + interest): UGX [X]
  DSCR = [X / X] = [X.Xx]
  Status: [✓ Above 1.25 / ⚠ Below 1.25 — action required]

Current Ratio:
  Current Assets: UGX [X]
  Current Liabilities: UGX [X]
  Ratio = [X.Xx]
  Status: [✓ / ⚠]

Insurance Status:
  Policy expiry: [Date]
  Days to expiry: [X]
  Status: [✓ Valid / ⚠ Renew within 30 days]

Tax Compliance:
  Last PAYE remittance: [Date]
  Last VAT return filed: [Date]
  TCC valid until: [Date]
  Status: [✓ / ⚠]
```

**Early warning protocol:** If DSCR falls below 1.35 (above covenant minimum of 1.25), the business should proactively contact the bank relationship manager before the covenant is breached. Banks respond better to early communication than to covenant breach notifications.

## 2. Uganda Development Bank (UDB) Reporting

UDB loans carry more extensive reporting requirements than commercial banks, reflecting the DFI's development mandate.

### UDB Mandatory Reports

| Report | Frequency | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Utilisation of proceeds report | Within 60 days of each disbursement tranche | UDB template — receipts + photos |
| Quarterly financial statements | Quarterly | Unaudited P&L + balance sheet + cash flow |
| Annual audited financial statements | Annual | ICPAU-certified; within 6 months of year-end |
| Environmental and Social monitoring report | Semi-annual | Against ESMP indicators; photographic evidence |
| Employment and beneficiary report | Semi-annual | Number of jobs created, gender disaggregation |
| Insurance renewals | Annual | All collateral assets must remain insured |
| Site inspection access | On request | Allow UDB officers access to business premises |

### UDB Environmental and Social Reporting

For any business with an Environmental and Social Management Plan (ESMP), UDB requires:

```
SEMI-ANNUAL ESMP MONITORING REPORT

Period: [Month X] to [Month X], [Year]
Business: [Name], UDB Loan Ref: [X]

1. Environmental indicators:
   - Waste generated this period: [X] kg/month
   - Waste disposal method: [Describe — licensed waste collector, composting, etc.]
   - Water consumption: [X] litres/month
   - Effluent management: [Describe]
   - Any NEMA inspections this period: [Yes/No — outcome]

2. Social indicators:
   - Total employees: [X] (Women: [X]%, Youth: [X]%)
   - New jobs created this period: [X]
   - Workplace accidents/incidents: [X] (describe any)
   - Community complaints received: [X] (describe and resolution)
   - Training conducted: [X] sessions, [X] participants

3. Compliance:
   - NEMA permit status: [Valid / Pending renewal — date]
   - Any regulatory violations: [None / Describe]

4. Corrective actions from previous report: [Describe progress]
```

## 3. Agricultural Credit Facility (ACF) Reporting

ACF loans are on-lent through commercial banks but carry Bank of Uganda reporting requirements:

- Quarterly utilisation reports showing agricultural production outputs
- Evidence that funds were used for agricultural purposes (receipts, photos, buyer confirmations)
- Annual reporting on employment in agriculture
- Repayment performance (tracked by on-lending bank)

## 4. Development Partner Grant Reporting

International donor grants (USAID, EU, GIZ, UN agencies) require the most detailed reporting. Budgeting for M&E and reporting costs (5–10% of grant budget) is standard.

### Quarterly Narrative Report Structure

```
QUARTERLY PROGRESS REPORT
[Organisation Name] | [Project Title] | [Funder Reference]
Period: [Quarter X, Year X]
Submitted by: [Name, Title] | [Date]

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (max 1 page)
   Key achievements this quarter, major challenges, headline numbers.

2. PROGRESS AGAINST RESULTS FRAMEWORK
   [For each Output / Outcome in the LogFrame:]

   Output 1: [Description]
   Target (cumulative): [X by this date]
   Achieved (this quarter): [X]
   Achieved (cumulative): [X]
   Progress: [X]% of target
   Narrative: [What was done, what worked, what challenges]
   Evidence: [Attached: training registers, receipts, photos]

3. FINANCIAL REPORT
   [Actual spend vs. budget — see quarterly financial report format below]

4. CHALLENGES AND RISKS
   [What problems arose? How were they addressed?]
   [Any new risks identified? Mitigation measures?]

5. PLANNED ACTIVITIES NEXT QUARTER
   [What will be done in the next 3 months?]

6. SUPPORT REQUIRED FROM FUNDER
   [Any approvals, no-objections, or guidance needed?]
```

### Annual Financial Report and Audit

Most grants above UGX 100M require an independent annual audit:
- Auditor must be ICPAU-registered
- Some funders require auditors from their approved list
- Audit report must include management letter
- Audit cost should be budgeted in the grant proposal (typically UGX 3–8M)

## 5. Impact Investor Reporting (Acumen, Pearl Capital, BII, Novastar)

Impact investors report on both financial and social/environmental performance (the "double bottom line").

### Typical Impact Investor Reporting Package

| Report | Frequency | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Management accounts | Monthly or quarterly | P&L, balance sheet, cash flow, KPI dashboard |
| Impact metrics report | Quarterly | Jobs created (gender-disaggregated), incomes improved, smallholders reached, CO2 avoided (if applicable) |
| Board report | Quarterly | Strategic update, risks, opportunities, key decisions required |
| Annual audited accounts | Annual | ICPAU-certified |
| Annual impact report | Annual | Detailed impact measurement against theory of change |

### Impact KPI Examples (by sector)

| Sector | Key Impact KPIs |
|---|---|
| Agriculture / agribusiness | Number of smallholder farmers reached; % income increase; % women farmers; tonnes of produce processed |
| Manufacturing | Jobs created; % women employed; local raw material sourcing %; safety incidents |
| Education | Students enrolled; completion rate; learning outcomes improvement; % girls enrolled |
| Healthcare | Patients served; cost per consultation; % uninsured patients served |
| Clean energy | Households connected; CO2 avoided (tonnes); cost of energy vs. kerosene baseline |
| Financial services | Customers served; % first-time borrowers; NPL ratio; % women customers |

## 6. Government of Uganda — Annual Business Reporting

All registered companies must file with URSB and URA annually regardless of whether they have funder obligations:

| Filing | Authority | Deadline | Penalty for Late Filing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Annual return (company) | URSB | Within 60 days of AGM (typically within 6 months of year-end) | UGX 200,000 + UGX 20,000/month late fee |
| Income tax return | URA | Within 6 months of financial year-end | 2% of tax due per month + interest |
| VAT return | URA | By 15th of each month | UGX 200,000 or 2% of tax, whichever higher |
| PAYE return | URA | By 15th of each month | UGX 200,000 or 10% of tax, whichever higher |
| NSSF contributions | NSSF | By 15th of each month | Interest + penalties on outstanding amount |
| Withholding tax return | URA | By 15th of each month | Penalties per TPC |
