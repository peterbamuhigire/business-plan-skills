# Phase 05 - Financial, Valuation, and Funding Excellence

## Objective

Make the engine numerically credible enough for lenders, investors, DFIs, and sophisticated clients by upgrading financial modelling, valuation logic, funding structure, and downside analysis.

## Why This Phase Is Decisive

Many "good" plans fail here. Narrative strength is not enough. To compete with elite advisory work, the financial case must visibly answer:

- how the business makes money
- how cash behaves in reality
- how much capital is required
- what returns or repayment capacity look like
- what breaks under downside scenarios

## Core Workstreams

### 1. Deepen working-capital and cash logic

Upgrade `10-financial-projections` with:

- DSO, DIO, and DPO logic
- cash conversion cycle modelling
- receivables and inventory assumptions
- liquidity ratios
- covenant commentary
- sector-specific modelling notes

### 2. Strengthen scenario and sensitivity analysis

Expand `meta-financial-stress-test` so it can produce:

- standard scenario bands
- tornado-style driver ranking
- two-way sensitivity tables
- DSCR breach corridors
- minimum viable revenue thresholds

### 3. Upgrade valuation and capital-structure logic

Refine `meta-valuation` and `11-funding-request` so they cover:

- method selection by company stage
- scenario valuation ranges
- dilution logic
- capital-stack logic
- debt versus equity suitability
- collateral and covenant framing
- use-of-funds precision

### 4. Add quality-of-earnings and historical normalisation

For established businesses, include:

- EBITDA normalisation
- non-recurring adjustments
- owner-benefit adjustments
- off-balance-sheet risk review
- working-capital normalisation

## Repository Changes to Make

- enhance `10-financial-projections`
- enhance `11-funding-request`
- enhance `meta-financial-stress-test`
- refine `meta-valuation`
- expand reference files for funding logic and valuation practice

## Deliverables

- stronger working-capital modelling guidance
- better sensitivity and downside protocols
- upgraded funding-request standards
- refined valuation workflows
- historical-financial normalisation protocol

## Exit Criteria

Phase 05 is complete only when:

- the narrative and model reconcile cleanly
- lenders can see repayment logic
- investors can see return logic
- downside cases are explicit, not implied
- the funding ask is precise, justified, and sequenced

## Risks if Skipped

- documents that look impressive but fail serious credit or investment scrutiny
- over-optimistic cash assumptions
- weak use-of-funds logic
- valuation claims that undermine credibility

## Suggested Reading Material

- *Financial Intelligence for Entrepreneurs* by Karen Berman, Joe Knight, and John Case
- *Investment Valuation* by Aswath Damodaran
- *Venture Deals* by Brad Feld and Jason Mendelson
- *Corporate and Project Finance Modeling* by Edward Bodmer

Buy and provide these here because this phase is where the engine must become genuinely defensible in front of finance-minded decision-makers.
