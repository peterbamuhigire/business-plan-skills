## What "Top Consulting Firm Proud" Actually Means

McKinsey, BCG, Bain, and Deloitte produce business plans and investment memoranda in three contexts:

1. **Pre-deal advisory** (PE/VC) — Commercial Due Diligence, market sizing, competitive landscape
2. **Strategy consulting** — Growth strategy, market entry, business model transformation
3. **Transaction support** — Vendor Due Diligence, financial modelling, management presentations

In all three contexts, the key differentiators are:
- **Hypothesis-driven structure** — SCQA, MECE, governing thought stated first
- **Quantitative rigour** — driver-based models, not assumptions-free projections
- **Primary research** — proprietary data that competitors do not have
- **Narrative power** — a clear, memorable argument, not just a collection of slides
- **Execution credibility** — evidence the team can actually do this

## Suite Assessment Against Each Differentiator

### Hypothesis-Driven Structure: ✅ ACHIEVED

The Pyramid Principle (Minto) and MECE (Rasiel/McKinsey) are fully implemented. The `01-executive-summary/SKILL.md` implements SCQA with the 30-second test, blank-assertion correction, and governing-thought discipline. A plan produced by this suite, if properly used, will have the same logical architecture as a McKinsey memo.

**This is the most important structural element and it is fully present.**

### Quantitative Rigour: ✅ ACHIEVED (with caveats)

Driver-based modelling is in the financial projections skill. Damodaran's DCF/WACC is in the valuation reference. DSCR, sensitivity analysis, and 4-scenario stress testing are all present.

**Caveat:** The suite cannot generate the numbers. It can structure how to present them. If the user's assumptions are weak, the structure will be strong but the substance will be thin. A McKinsey team would challenge assumptions relentlessly — the `meta-market-validation/SKILL.md` and `meta-bankability-scoring/SKILL.md` serve this role, but only if the user invokes them.

### Primary Research: ⚠️ PARTIAL

This is the area where the suite most falls short of consulting firm standards. McKinsey conducts surveys, interviews C-suite executives, mystery-shops competitors, and accesses proprietary databases. The suite:
- Provides a framework for presenting primary research results
- Guides customer discovery through `meta-market-validation/SKILL.md`
- Offers OSINT methodology for competitor intelligence (Hetherington framework)
- References quality secondary data sources (UBOS, IFC, World Bank, UNDP)

**For the East Africa SME context**, this is actually acceptable. Most Uganda/Kenya business plans are expected to rely on secondary data — few SMEs have the budget for primary market research. The suite's use of World Bank, UBOS, IFC, and UNDP data provides legitimacy and currency that most locally-prepared plans lack entirely.

**For international PE/VC/DFI contexts**, primary research remains a gap. The suite can structure how to present results; it cannot conduct the research.

### Narrative Power: ✅ ACHIEVED (best-in-class for EA)

The combination of `meta-pitch-preparation` and `meta-presentation-design` is the strongest system in the suite. The Sparkline, One Thing, STAR moment, assertion-evidence slide format, audience resistance mapping, and 6-session coaching programme together produce a pitch narrative that would be competitive in any context — Nairobi, London, or New York.

The Klaff STRONG method adds a psychological sophistication (neurofinance, frame control, prizing mechanics) that is not commonly taught even in top-tier business schools.

**For narrative power, the suite is best-in-class for the market.**

### Execution Credibility: ✅ ACHIEVED

The Pre-Phase 0 regulatory gate, 90-day game plans, M&E funder reporting requirements, and quarterly gameplan meta-skill together give execution planning more rigor than most consulting deliverables. Consulting firms are often better at strategy than at operational planning — this suite is strong on both.

## Honest Limitations

### Limitation 1: Quality Depends on the Practitioner

The suite is a tool, not a practitioner. A skilled consultant using these skills will produce world-class output. An inexperienced user following the skills mechanically will produce a better-than-average plan, but not a McKinsey-quality plan.

The value proposition is: **the suite dramatically raises the floor** (eliminates the worst plan outputs) and **significantly accelerates work** for skilled practitioners (reduces time to produce a high-quality plan by 40–60%).

### Limitation 2: Data Fabrication Risk

The biggest risk in any AI-assisted plan generation is fabricated numbers. The suite explicitly addresses this:
- "Financial projections need explicit assumptions — never fabricate numbers" (CLAUDE.md)
- "Market data must be sourced or clearly flagged as estimates" (CLAUDE.md)
- The consistency audit checks that numbers are internally consistent

But the discipline to follow these rules depends on the user. A plan with invented market data, regardless of how well structured it is, is not bankable and is not what top consulting firms would produce.

### Limitation 3: Time Sensitivity of Uganda Data

The Uganda data embedded in the suite (tax rates, macro data, regulatory requirements) is current as of Q1 2025/26. Uganda's regulatory environment changes annually — the 2025 tax amendments are already embedded. But the suite will need annual data refreshes to remain accurate.

**Recommendation:** Add a "Last updated" date to every data-intensive reference file (uganda-tax-framework.md, uganda-macro-context-2025.md, etc.) and establish a quarterly review process.
