# Recommendations — Prioritised Action Plan

Gaps are organised into three phases based on funding impact. Phase 1 fixes the items that cause outright rejection. Phase 2 strengthens the plan's persuasiveness. Phase 3 adds completeness and polish.

---

## Phase 1 — Critical (Fix Before Using for Real Applications)

These seven actions address gaps that will cause a bank or DFI to reject or return the plan. They should be completed before the suite is used to generate any plan intended for actual submission.

### Action 1.1 — Uganda Funding Landscape Reference
**File:** `11-funding-request/references/ea-funding-landscape.md`
**Content to cover:**
- Commercial bank lending (Stanbic, Centenary, dfcu, Equity, ABSA): typical rates (18–24% p.a.), terms (3–7 years), collateral requirements (125–150% coverage), documentation checklist
- Uganda Development Bank: eligibility, minimum amounts, sectors supported, interest rates (~12–15%), required feasibility study
- Agricultural Credit Facility (ACF): Bank of Uganda facility, agribusiness focus, ≤12% on-lending rate, up to UGX 2.1B
- SACCOs and microfinance: amounts, rates (30–36% p.a.), suitability for early-stage
- Development partner grants: USAID, EU, GIZ, UN funds — eligibility, format requirements
- Impact investors: Acumen, Pearl Capital, Novastar — criteria for East Africa
- Youth/women funds: YLP, WEP, GROW — eligibility and application process
- Decision tree: given business stage, sector, and amount needed, route to the right funding source

### Action 1.2 — Credit Assessment Framework Reference
**File:** `11-funding-request/references/credit-assessment-frameworks.md`
**Content to cover:**
- 5 Cs of Credit — definition, how each C maps to plan sections, what evidence satisfies each C
- CAMPARI — same mapping exercise
- DSCR — formula, calculation example, minimum thresholds by lender type, sensitivity analysis
- How to write a "credit narrative" — the 3–4 paragraph summary that front-loads the banker's key concerns
- Credit committee readiness checklist — self-test before submission

### Action 1.3 — Collateral Analysis Reference
**File:** `11-funding-request/references/collateral-analysis.md`
**Content to cover:**
- Accepted collateral types in Uganda: land title (Mailo/Freehold/Leasehold), motor vehicle, machinery, fixed deposit, receivables
- Valuation discounts: banks apply 50–80% on land, 40–60% on equipment, 50–70% on vehicles
- Uganda Credit Guarantee Scheme — how to access for businesses with insufficient collateral
- Group guarantee structures for SACCOs and cooperatives
- Personal net worth statement format (for directors' personal guarantee)
- How to present limited collateral with compensating factors (strong DSCR, experienced management, existing customer base)

### Action 1.4 — Uganda Legal Structures Reference
**File:** `02-company-overview/references/uganda-legal-structures.md`
**Content to cover:**
- All legal structures: sole trader, partnership, private limited company, cooperative, NGO
- URSB registration process: steps, costs, timeline, documents required
- Why banks prefer Private Limited Companies for lending above UGX 50M
- Directors and shareholders register requirements
- Sector compliance matrix: by industry, list required licences and issuing authority (UNBS, NEMA, NDA, UTB, BOU, UMRA)
- Annual compliance obligations: URSB annual return, URA tax returns, NSSF, KCCA/district trading licence renewal
- TIN registration at URA — mandatory for any business seeking a bank account or formal contract

### Action 1.5 — Uganda Tax Framework Reference
**File:** `10-financial-projections/references/uganda-tax-framework.md`
**Content to cover:**
- Corporate income tax: 30% rate, taxable income computation (revenue minus allowable deductions)
- VAT: 18% rate, registration threshold (UGX 150M/year turnover), input vs. output VAT, monthly filing with URA
- PAYE: graduated rates, employer obligation, monthly URA remittance, PAYE computation table
- NSSF: 10% employer + 5% employee, mandatory for ≥5 employees
- WHT: 6% on resident service payments; when to apply, how to remit
- Presumptive tax: for informal businesses below UGX 150M
- How to present taxes in projected P&L (separate line items, not buried in "other costs")
- Common tax optimisation approaches (investment deductions, startup losses carried forward)

### Action 1.6 — Financial Model Templates Reference
**File:** `10-financial-projections/references/financial-model-templates.md`
**Content to cover:**
- Month-by-month Income Statement template (rows explicitly labelled: Revenue by stream, COGS, Gross Profit, operating expenses, EBITDA, depreciation, EBIT, interest, PBT, tax, NPAT)
- Monthly Cash Flow Statement template (separate from P&L: collections, payments, loan drawdown, repayments, opening/closing cash)
- Year-end Balance Sheet template (assets, liabilities, equity)
- Key ratio summary table (DSCR, current ratio, debt-equity, gross margin, NPAT margin, ROE)
- Breakeven analysis template with formula and worked example
- DSCR calculation table linked to the loan repayment schedule
- Instructions for formatting financial statements for bank submission

### Action 1.7 — Rewrite Section 11 (Funding Request) SKILL.md
**File:** `11-funding-request/SKILL.md`
**Required changes:**
- Add a funding-type selector at the top: ask user to choose bank loan / DFI facility / grant / equity — and route to the appropriate format
- Replace "capitalisation table" prompt with a loan application summary format for debt funding
- Add collateral section as a required element for bank loans
- Add DSCR calculation step
- Add ESMP requirement note for DFI applications
- Remove IPO from exit strategies for SME context (or qualify heavily)
- Add covering letter as a required deliverable

**File:** `15-appendices/references/submission-guide.md`

**File:** `meta-financial-stress-test/references/stress-test-methodology.md`
**Content:** Historical shock data (COVID, exchange rate, fuel prices); sector-specific shock factors; base/optimistic/pessimistic/extreme scenario framework; sensitivity table template; Monte Carlo introduction.

### Action 2.4 — Bankability Scoring — Dual Mode
**File:** `meta-bankability-scoring/SKILL.md` update
**Required changes:** Add a "Bank Loan Readiness" scoring track (DSCR, collateral coverage, legal compliance, management experience, market validation) alongside the existing "Investor Readiness" track. Map scoring criteria explicitly to 5 Cs / CAMPARI.

### Action 2.5 — Industry Guide Integration Matrix
**Files:** `04-market-analysis/SKILL.md`, `08-operations-plan/SKILL.md`, `10-financial-projections/SKILL.md`

### Action 2.6 — Operations Plan Uganda Context
**File:** `08-operations-plan/references/uganda-operations-context.md`
**Content:** Power infrastructure (load-shedding mitigation, generator sizing), water supply, internet connectivity (MTN/Airtel fibre, Liquid Telecom, NITA backbone), Uganda Labour Act basics (Employment Act 2006, working hours, minimum wage, probation, termination), supply chain Uganda context (EAC single customs territory, bonded warehousing, NSSF for contractors).

### Action 2.7 — Uganda Insurance Framework
**File:** `12-risk-analysis/references/uganda-insurance.md`
**Content:** Required insurance for bank-collateralised assets; public liability for hospitality/construction; key-person life assurance; business interruption; major Ugandan insurers (UAP, Jubilee, AIG, Liberty, CIC, ICEA Lion); approximate premiums; how to present insurance in the risk mitigation section.

### Action 2.8 — M&E Funder Reporting Integration
**File:** `meta-monitoring-evaluation/references/funder-reporting.md`
**Content:** Bank loan covenants and reporting obligations (quarterly management accounts, DSCR monitoring); grant reporting format (narrative + financial vs. budget); impact investor metrics (jobs, gender, smallholders reached); template for multi-funder reporting schedule.

## Book Recommendations for Remaining Gaps

The following books would provide the reference material needed for Phases 1 and 2. These are gaps the suite's current book collection does not cover:

| Gap | Recommended Book |
|---|---|
| Uganda/EA banking and lending | BOU Supervision Annual Report, Ugandan commercial bank lending guidelines (internal docs) |
| SME finance Africa | Ayyagari, Beck & Demirguc-Kunt (2007) — academic; or World Bank IFC SME Finance reports |
| Financial statement preparation | ICPAU Small Entity Financial Reporting Framework |
| Uganda tax | URA Tax Education department publications (free online) |
| Development finance (DFI process) | UDB Annual Report; AfDB project appraisal guidance |
| Grant writing | USAID ADS (Automated Directives System) — free online |
| East African regulatory environment | EAC Customs Management Act, Uganda Tax Procedures Code Act 2014 |

Note: Most of the critical Phase 1 content (tax rates, URSB fees, CAMPARI criteria, DSCR thresholds) is factual and does not require a commercial book — it can be written from regulatory sources, bank guidelines, and established financial standards.
