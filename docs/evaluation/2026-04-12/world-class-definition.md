# World-Class Definition

## Core Components

A world-class, bankable business plan must include:

1. **Executive summary**
   - conclusion first
   - clear ask
   - SCQA or equivalent logical structure
   - crisp investment case in under two pages

2. **Company and governance foundation**
   - legal structure, ownership, registration, licences
   - governance logic, decision rights, advisory or board structure
   - management capability and compensating measures for founder gaps

3. **Products / services and business model**
   - clear value proposition
   - monetisation logic
   - delivery model
   - differentiation linked to customer value and operational capability

4. **Market analysis**
   - TAM / SAM / SOM
   - market type and stage
   - real demand signals
   - industry structure, growth drivers, barriers, and regulatory context

5. **Target market and customer proof**
   - segmentation
   - buyer behaviour
   - evidence of customer pain and willingness to pay
   - primary research or defensible proxy evidence

6. **Competitive analysis**
   - Five Forces / strategic groups / rival response logic
   - competitor economics where possible
   - real moat, not marketing language

7. **Marketing and sales strategy**
   - acquisition, conversion, onboarding, retention, expansion
   - channel logic
   - pricing
   - ownership model
   - measurable KPIs

8. **Operations plan**
   - process design
   - supply chain
   - capacity
   - quality controls
   - scale path

9. **Management team**
   - capability fit
   - gaps
   - hiring / advisory plan
   - execution accountability

10. **Financials**
    - three statements
    - assumptions
    - unit economics
    - break-even
    - working capital logic
    - cash flow realism
    - DSCR at or above lender minimum where debt is sought

11. **Funding request**
    - exact amount
    - exact use of funds
    - structure and terms
    - repayment or return logic
    - collateral or valuation support

12. **Risk analysis**
    - MECE risk register
    - quantified sensitivity where possible
    - mitigations, owners, triggers

13. **Implementation plan**
    - phases
    - milestones
    - dependencies
    - first 90 days

14. **Appendices**
    - every major claim supported
    - lender and investor documentary expectations met

## Advanced Components

Differentiators for top-tier output:

- Pyramid Principle / bottom-line-up-front writing
- hypothesis-driven analysis
- scenario and sensitivity analysis
- AI integration and workflow redesign
- explicit consistency audit across all sections
- due diligence readiness
- sustainability / ESMP readiness where relevant
- investor-ready narrative, not just plan completeness

## Quality Expectations

Against McKinsey, Bain, EY, and IFC / World Bank standards, world-class output should be:

- **clear:** easy to follow, conclusion first
- **credible:** supported by evidence, no fabricated certainty
- **analytical:** structured, MECE, and logically defended
- **financially rigorous:** assumptions visible; cash, working capital, and debt service explicit
- **persuasive:** tailored to lender, investor, donor, or client psychology
- **consistent:** no contradictions between sections
- **locally grounded:** taxes, labour, regulatory, financing, and market realities fit the country
- **actionable:** implementation path is believable, sequenced, and owned
