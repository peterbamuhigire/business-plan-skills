# Suggested Reading

## Highest-Priority Additions

### Barbara Minto - *The Pyramid Principle*
Why:
- the single best source for conclusion-first, consulting-quality writing
- would improve executive summary, market analysis, risk analysis, and proposal logic

### Ethan Rasiel - *The McKinsey Way* and *The McKinsey Mind*
Why:
- issue trees
- MECE discipline
- hypothesis-driven problem solving
- exactly the missing synthesis layer for a consulting-grade engine

### Aswath Damodaran - *Investment Valuation* or *The Little Book of Valuation*
Why:
- strengthens equity readiness
- provides defensible valuation logic
- helps move the suite from debt-strong to investor-complete

### Stephen Penman / Fridson & Alvarez - financial statement analysis texts
Why:
- raises lender-style and investor-style ratio analysis
- supports more sophisticated financial scrutiny

### Pignataro - *Financial Modeling and Valuation*
Why:
- practical bridge between statements, assumptions, valuation, and sensitivity work

## Strong Secondary Additions

### IFC - SME governance and family-business governance materials
Why:
- directly relevant to East African founder-led businesses
- closes a real investor and DFI credibility gap

### Kim & Mauborgne - *Blue Ocean Strategy*
Why:
- useful for differentiation and strategic positioning
- already partly present conceptually, but worth deeper extraction if not fully covered

### Christensen - *The Innovator's Dilemma* and Jobs-to-be-Done material
Why:
- sharpens product positioning, disruption logic, and customer problem framing

### Kaplan & Norton - *The Balanced Scorecard*
Why:
- improves implementation, monitoring, and investor confidence in execution tracking

### Gene Zelazny - *Say It with Charts*
Why:
- classic McKinsey visual communication discipline
- essential for turning strong analysis into top-tier presentation output

### Cole Nussbaumer Knaflic - *Storytelling with Data*
Why:
- improves visual argument quality
- useful for decks, executive summaries, and appendix evidence presentation

## Standards And Practice Sources

### IFC Performance Standards and World Bank / IFC private-sector diagnostics
Why:
- essential for DFI and development-finance credibility
- already partly used well; should continue to anchor ESG and market context

### IFRS for SMEs guidance
Why:
- lifts financial statement credibility
- important for larger and more formalised businesses

### Bank credit manuals / SME lending standards for East Africa
Why:
- extend the already strong bankability layer with more institution-specific realism
