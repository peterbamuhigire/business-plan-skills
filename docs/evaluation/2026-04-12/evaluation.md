# Business Plan Skills Suite Evaluation
**Date:** 2026-04-12  
**Evaluator:** Codex  
**Scope:** Current repository state after April 2026 updates  
**Benchmark:** McKinsey, Bain, EY, IFC / World Bank advisory and bankability standards

## 1. Executive Summary

The Business Plan Skills Suite is already a serious system, not a prompt dump. It has moved well beyond generic business-plan generation into a modular methodology engine with strong Uganda localisation, explicit bankability logic, and a credible validation layer. In its current form, it is capable of producing plans that can clear many SME bank, DFI, grant, and advisory use cases if operated by a competent consultant with real source data.

Its strongest qualities are:
- deep framework coverage across strategy, finance, marketing, risk, and pitch preparation
- unusually strong Uganda bankability scaffolding: DSCR, CAMPARI, collateral logic, tax, permits, and appendices
- useful meta-skills for stress testing, due diligence, market validation, and bankability scoring
- growing East Africa readiness through Kenya and Tanzania country-context files

Its limiting weaknesses are now narrower and more important:
- consulting-quality synthesis is still inconsistent: the system has many frameworks, but not a fully enforced hypothesis-first, MECE, pyramid-driven operating standard across all analytical sections
- corporate governance, ownership structure, and founder/investor readiness are still thinner than they need to be for top-tier investor scrutiny
- valuation logic exists in references, but is not yet elevated into a first-class integrated workflow
- primary research protocols and evidence discipline are not yet strong enough to guarantee investor-grade market proof without a skilled operator
- workflow orchestration across sections remains partly manual, which creates risk of inconsistency in live use

**Overall score:** **8.2 / 10**  
**Readiness level:** **Near world-class**

### Bottom-line verdict

This repository can already generate plans that are:
- **bankable** for many Uganda and East Africa SME lending contexts
- **credible** for DFI, grant, and impact-investor preparation
- **strong** enough to materially accelerate consulting work

It is **not yet fully world-class** in the McKinsey/Bain/EY sense because the final 15-20% gap is not about having more frameworks. It is about enforcing:
- top-down structured thinking
- harder evidence standards
- sharper cross-section coherence
- stronger governance and valuation depth
- a more opinionated end-to-end operating flow

If those gaps are closed, this can become a genuinely elite business-plan engine.

## 2. Definition of World-Class Standard

## Core Components

A world-class, bankable business plan must include:

1. **Executive summary**
   - conclusion first
   - clear ask
   - SCQA or equivalent logical structure
   - crisp investment case in under two pages

2. **Company and governance foundation**
   - legal structure, ownership, registration, licences
   - governance logic, decision rights, advisory or board structure
   - management capability and compensating measures for founder gaps

3. **Products / services and business model**
   - clear value proposition
   - monetisation logic
   - delivery model
   - differentiation linked to customer value and operational capability

4. **Market analysis**
   - TAM / SAM / SOM
   - market type and stage
   - real demand signals
   - industry structure, growth drivers, barriers, and regulatory context

5. **Target market and customer proof**
   - segmentation
   - buyer behaviour
   - evidence of customer pain and willingness to pay
   - primary research or defensible proxy evidence

6. **Competitive analysis**
   - Five Forces / strategic groups / rival response logic
   - competitor economics where possible
   - real moat, not marketing language

7. **Marketing and sales strategy**
   - acquisition, conversion, onboarding, retention, expansion
   - channel logic
   - pricing
   - ownership model
   - measurable KPIs

8. **Operations plan**
   - process design
   - supply chain
   - capacity
   - quality controls
   - scale path

9. **Management team**
   - capability fit
   - gaps
   - hiring / advisory plan
   - execution accountability

10. **Financials**
    - three statements
    - assumptions
    - unit economics
    - break-even
    - working capital logic
    - cash flow realism
    - DSCR at or above lender minimum where debt is sought

11. **Funding request**
    - exact amount
    - exact use of funds
    - structure and terms
    - repayment or return logic
    - collateral or valuation support

12. **Risk analysis**
    - MECE risk register
    - quantified sensitivity where possible
    - mitigations, owners, triggers

13. **Implementation plan**
    - phases
    - milestones
    - dependencies
    - first 90 days

14. **Appendices**
    - every major claim supported
    - lender and investor documentary expectations met

## Advanced Components

Differentiators for top-tier output:

- Pyramid Principle / bottom-line-up-front writing
- hypothesis-driven analysis
- scenario and sensitivity analysis
- AI integration and workflow redesign
- explicit consistency audit across all sections
- due diligence readiness
- sustainability / ESMP readiness where relevant
- investor-ready narrative, not just plan completeness

## Quality Expectations

Against McKinsey, Bain, EY, and IFC / World Bank standards, world-class output should be:

- **clear:** easy to follow, conclusion first
- **credible:** supported by evidence, no fabricated certainty
- **analytical:** structured, MECE, and logically defended
- **financially rigorous:** assumptions visible; cash, working capital, and debt service explicit
- **persuasive:** tailored to lender, investor, donor, or client psychology
- **consistent:** no contradictions between sections
- **locally grounded:** taxes, labour, regulatory, financing, and market realities fit the country
- **actionable:** implementation path is believable, sequenced, and owned

## 3. Detailed Evaluation

## A. Coverage
**Score:** **8.8 / 10**

Coverage is broad and unusually complete. The repository has:
- the full core plan stack
- strong meta-skills
- proposal and pitch workflows
- industry guides
- country-context architecture
- sustainability, AI, due diligence, and stress testing

This is already beyond most business-plan systems.

Why it is not a 9.5+:
- Section 02 is still thinner than the rest of the suite on governance depth
- Section 15 is serviceable but remains one of the weakest core sections
- some important adjacent capabilities are still missing or underdeveloped: deeper valuation workflow, stronger primary research toolkit, sector coverage for some high-value industries, and more explicit consulting-grade synthesis tools

## B. Instruction Quality
**Score:** **8.1 / 10**

The best skills are strong. `07-marketing-sales-strategy`, `10-financial-projections`, `11-funding-request`, `12-risk-analysis`, `meta-market-validation`, `meta-bankability-scoring`, and the pitch skills are explicit, structured, and operationally useful.

Strengths:
- instructions are generally actionable
- skills often specify required elements, generation steps, quality criteria, and reference routing
- reference wiring is strong and increasingly disciplined

Weaknesses:
- instruction quality is uneven across the repo
- not every analytical skill enforces top-down communication or hypothesis-first reasoning
- some skills still describe what to include more than they force how to think
- the suite depends on operator judgment to combine frameworks well

## C. Financial Rigor
**Score:** **8.7 / 10**

Financial rigor is a flagship strength of the repository.

Strengths:
- explicit assumption discipline
- DSCR logic is wired into the core debt pathway
- funding request, bankability scoring, and stress testing reinforce one another well
- Uganda tax and compliance context is unusually strong
- informal-record reconstruction is a practical differentiator for East African SME reality

Why not higher:
- working capital management is still not elevated enough in Section 10
- valuation is present in references but not yet a first-class integrated workflow
- consulting-grade modelling expectations such as explicit DCF/IRR/NPV decision framing, sector benchmarking, and two-way sensitivity tables are not yet pervasive

## D. Consistency & Integration
**Score:** **7.8 / 10**

The repo understands that consistency matters and has real mechanisms for it:
- cross-references in `CLAUDE.md`
- section references within skills
- `meta-bankability-scoring`

This is stronger than most comparable systems.

But the integration model is still partly manual:
- the user or operator must still invoke the right meta-skills in the right order
- the system does not yet guarantee that all sections are rebuilt around one governing investment thesis
- there is still risk of section quality varying by invocation path

This is one of the main blockers to a true world-class score.

## E. Localisation & Realism

## 4. Gap Analysis

## Critical Gaps

### 1. Governance and ownership depth are still not strong enough
`02-company-overview` has improved, but this remains one of the thinnest critical sections relative to investor scrutiny. The suite still lacks enough structure on:
- founder agreements
- vesting
- board design
- decision rights
- family-business governance
- governance for larger debt and DFI applications

This weakens investor confidence and raises execution-risk concerns.

### 2. Valuation is under-integrated
Valuation references exist, so the old “no valuation” gap is partly closed. The remaining problem is integration:
- no dedicated valuation workflow
- no mandatory valuation path for equity cases
- no consistent bridge from business model and projections to valuation conclusion

This keeps the system strong for debt, but less mature for equity.

### 3. Consulting-grade synthesis is not yet enforced
The repo contains many excellent frameworks, but the operating standard is not yet consistently:
- BLUF / pyramid-first
- hypothesis-driven
- MECE across all analytical sections
- chart-and-footnote disciplined

This is the biggest gap between “very strong business-plan engine” and “top-tier consulting engine”.

## High Gaps

### 4. Primary market research protocols are still not deep enough
`meta-market-validation` is good, but the suite still lacks a more explicit standard toolkit for:
- sample interview guides by sector
- survey instruments
- observation protocols
- field validation evidence formatting for appendices

Without this, investor-grade market proof still depends too much on the operator improvising.

### 5. Workflow orchestration remains too manual
The system is modular, which is a strength, but it still lacks a stronger “opinionated path” for:
- complete plan generation
- sequencing validation, drafting, stress testing, bankability scoring, appendices, and pitch
- locking a governing thesis and key assumptions across all sections

This creates real consistency risk in practice.

### 6. Appendix and evidence architecture are still weaker than the analytical core
`15-appendices` is functional, but it is not yet as strong as the rest of the suite in:
- DD-grade evidence mapping
- appendix architecture for investor decks vs banks vs DFIs
- documentary standards for higher-ticket funding

## Medium Gaps

### 7. Some sector coverage remains missing
The suite is strong on many sectors, but important categories remain thin or absent:
- healthcare / clinics
- real estate / property
- transport / logistics
- ICT / software / startup archetypes
- renewable energy

### 8. Working capital and ratio analysis could be stronger in Section 10
This is now a refinement issue, not a foundational weakness. The missing lift is:
- receivables, payables, inventory, cash conversion cycle
- current ratio / quick ratio / interest coverage benchmarking
- lender-style ratio interpretation

### 9. Multi-country readiness is real but not yet broad
Kenya and Tanzania now exist. That materially improves the suite. But it still needs:
- Rwanda
- country-file maturity checks
- better systematic fallback logic across every section

## 5. Recommendations

## Skill-Level Improvements

### `02-company-overview`
Add:
- shareholder agreement basics
- founder vesting
- governance maturity by stage
- board / advisory architecture
- family-business governance
- beneficial ownership and conflicts-of-interest logic

This is the highest-leverage core-section upgrade.

### `10-financial-projections`
Add:
- working capital mechanics
- receivables / payables / inventory days
- current ratio, quick ratio, interest cover, debt-to-equity
- explicit lender benchmarking table
- simple DCF / IRR / NPV hooks for equity and larger projects

### `11-funding-request`
Promote valuation from reference status to workflow status:
- debt mode
- equity mode
- blended / DFI mode

Require a valuation pathway for any equity request.

### `15-appendices`
Expand to include:
- DD room map
- appendix-by-funder-type architecture
- evidence matrix linking each major claim to appendix support
- board / shareholder / legal pack requirements for larger transactions

### `04-market-analysis`, `05-target-market`, `06-competitive-analysis`
Enforce:
- hypothesis statement at the top
- MECE decomposition
- conclusion-first section structure
- stronger evidence standards for customer proof and competitor economics

### `meta-quarterly-gameplan`
Continue expansion. It is better now, but still not at flagship depth. This skill should become the execution bridge between strategy and funded performance.

## New Skills to Create

### 1. `meta-investor-narrative`
Purpose:
- unify governing thesis across the whole plan
- define the one- to three-line investment case
- enforce BLUF and section hierarchy

This would close a major consulting-quality gap.

### 2. `meta-valuation`
Purpose:
- DCF
- multiples
- pre-revenue methods
- valuation sanity checks
- bridge from projections to investment terms

This is needed to raise equity-readiness materially.

### 3. `meta-consulting-synthesis`
Purpose:
- Pyramid Principle
- MECE issue trees
- hypothesis-driven storylines
- chart logic
- evidence and footnote discipline

This would move the suite much closer to McKinsey/Bain output style.

### 4. `meta-primary-research`
Purpose:
- sector-specific interview guides
- survey templates
- focus group protocols
- field observation templates
- evidence packaging for appendices

### 5. `meta-deal-structuring`
Purpose:
- debt vs equity vs grant vs blended finance selection
- indicative term logic
- concessional vs commercial capital comparisons
- sequencing of funding asks

## System-Level Improvements

### 1. Add a mandatory orchestration path
Create or strengthen an end-to-end skill that enforces:
1. intake
2. country context
3. idea validation / market validation
4. section drafting
5. financial build
6. stress testing
7. bankability scoring
8. appendices
9. pitch preparation

### 2. Enforce a governing thesis
Every full plan should begin with a central investment thesis and all sections should answer it. This is currently possible, but not guaranteed.

### 3. Standardise output patterns
Require, across major sections:
- opening conclusion sentence
- 3-5 key messages
- evidence / source note expectation
- cross-reference line where needed

### 4. Strengthen consistency automation
The repo already has consistency tooling in principle. Extend it so that:
- key numbers
- legal structure
- funding ask
- team claims
- market sizing assumptions

are explicitly checked across sections.

### 5. Add maturity tiers by plan type
Different outputs need different standards:
- SME bank loan plan
- equity investor plan
- DFI / impact-investor plan
- grant proposal
- strategic partner proposal

The suite should become more explicit about which standards are mandatory in each mode.

## Final Assessment

The Business Plan Skills Suite is already strong enough to produce serious, fundable work. It is not early-stage. It is not merely functional. It is a **near world-class advisory engine** with a particular strength in Uganda and East Africa bankability.

To become genuinely world-class, the next phase should not focus on adding many more frameworks. It should focus on:
- synthesis quality
- governance depth
- valuation integration
- stronger evidence standards
- more opinionated end-to-end orchestration

If those are addressed, this repository can plausibly become one of the best specialised business-plan generation systems available.
