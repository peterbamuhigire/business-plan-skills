## Critical Missing Industry Guides

### 1. Healthcare / Medical Clinics `CRITICAL`
**Why critical:** Healthcare is one of Uganda's fastest-growing investment sectors. Private clinics, diagnostic centres, pharmacies, and dental practices are among the most-requested business plans. The services guide has `chemist-retail.md` (one reference file) but NO dedicated healthcare industry guide.

**What's needed:**
- Private clinic setup and licensing (NDA, Allied Health Professionals Council)
- Healthcare financial benchmarks (revenue per consultation, bed occupancy rate, pharmacy margins)
- Medical equipment financing (leasing vs. purchase)
- Insurance panel billing and NHIS integration
- Infection control compliance costs
- Healthcare staffing (Uganda Medical and Dental Practitioners Act)
- Telemedicine business models
- Reference to WHO/MoH Uganda Health Financing Strategy

**Books to find:** Healthcare management for developing countries; health facility business planning guide.

---

### 2. Real Estate & Property Development `CRITICAL`
**Why critical:** Real estate is Uganda's largest informal investment category. Residential rental, commercial development, and land banking are extremely common. Yet there is NO real estate industry guide.

**What's needed:**
- Uganda land tenure types (freehold, leasehold, Mailo, customary) and their investment implications
- Real estate financial model (yield, cap rate, IRR for property investment)
- Construction cost benchmarks (UGX per square metre, Kampala vs. upcountry)
- Rental yield benchmarks by location (Kampala, Entebbe, Jinja, Gulu)
- Property management operations and vacancy rate management
- KCCA building permits and subdivision approval process
- Mortgage products (HFB, Stanbic, dfcu) and LTV ratios
- Real estate developer financing (pre-sales, construction loans)
- Property valuation methodologies (income approach, comparable sales, replacement cost)
- NLC (National Land Commission) and Ministry of Lands processes

**Books to find:** Real estate investment/development in Africa or emerging markets.

---

### 3. Transport & Logistics (Formal Sector) `HIGH PRIORITY`
**Why important:** Beyond boda-boda (already in services), there is a large formal transport sector: trucking/haulage, courier services, passenger transport (buses, taxis), cold chain logistics, and freight forwarding. These have distinct financial profiles.

**Note:** `courier-service.md` exists in services/references. `boda-boda.md` exists. But there is no trucking/haulage guide, no freight forwarding guide, and no cold chain logistics guide.

**What's needed:**
- Trucking and haulage business model (per-km rates, fuel efficiency benchmarks, driver NSSF/payroll)
- Freight forwarding operations (EACAA, clearance costs, Mombasa corridor data)
- Cold chain investment (Uganda's lack of cold storage = market opportunity)
- Fleet management (maintenance schedules, spare parts, CASCO insurance)
- UNRA regulations (vehicle licensing, axle load limits, road fitness certificates)
- EAC transit corridors and border crossing costs
- Fuel cost modelling (UGX fuel price sensitivity)

**Books to find:** Logistics and supply chain management for Africa/East Africa.

---

### 4. ICT / Software / Tech Startups `HIGH PRIORITY`
**Why important:** Uganda's tech ecosystem (Kampala tech hub, fintech, agritech, healthtech) is growing rapidly. Plans for software companies, tech startups, and digital platforms are requested and the existing skills don't cover the tech-startup business model well.

**What's needed:**
- SaaS financial model (MRR, ARR, churn, CAC, LTV — different from product business models)
- Tech startup funding landscape (Antler, Launch Africa, LoftyInc, Google for Startups)
- Uganda technology regulatory environment (UCC licensing, fintech sandbox, NDA telemedicine)
- App development and hosting cost benchmarks (AWS/GCP in Africa)
- Open source vs. proprietary software strategy (relevant for African context)
- Data protection compliance (Uganda Data Protection and Privacy Act 2019)
- Fintech-specific compliance (BoU mobile money regulations, PSP licensing)
- IP protection for software (copyright, patent limitations, trade secret)
- Tech team structure and equity compensation (ESOP for early hires)

**Note:** `14-ai-integration/SKILL.md` covers AI automation but not the ICT startup business model itself.

**Books to find:** SaaS business model books; African tech startup ecosystem reports.

---

### 5. Renewable Energy / Clean Energy `HIGH PRIORITY`
**Why important:** Uganda has significant energy access gaps (only 42% electricity access) and a booming off-grid solar market. Mini-grids, solar home systems, solar water pumping, and biomass businesses are active investment areas.

**What's needed:**
- Solar home system (SHS) business model (PAYGO vs. outright sale)
- Mini-grid project finance (community energy company structure, tariff setting, UEDCL licensing)
- Solar installation business operations and technician certification (UETCL, UNBS)
- Biomass/briquette production business model
- Biogas for institutional cooking (schools, hospitals)
- Carbon credit revenue (REDD+, Gold Standard) as additional income stream
- Energy access impact metrics (for DFI/grant applications)
- ERA (Electricity Regulatory Authority) licensing requirements
- PAYGO MFI partnerships (SunFunder, Oikocredit, responsAbility)

**Books/reports to find:** GOGLA off-grid solar industry reports, IFC/ESMAP energy access reports, mini-grid project finance guides.

---

### 6. Financial Services (MFI, SACCO, Insurance Agency) `MODERATE PRIORITY`
**Why important:** Savings cooperatives (SACCOs), microfinance institutions (MFIs), and insurance agencies are extremely common in Uganda's financial inclusion sector. Their business models (interest spread, loan portfolio management) are completely different from ordinary SMEs.

**What's needed:**
- SACCO regulatory framework (MSCOPE/MDI Act, BoU oversight)
- MFI financial model (loan portfolio yield, PAR, provisioning)
- Insurance agency business model (commission income, premium volumes)
- Mobile money agency margins and regulatory requirements (BoU PSP guidelines)
- Digital banking / neobank considerations (fintech sandbox)
