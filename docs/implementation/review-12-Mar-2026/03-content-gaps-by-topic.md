# Content Gaps by Topic

Gaps identified across all skills, grouped by theme. Each gap includes severity, which skill it affects, and what type of source material would fix it.

## A. Corporate Governance & Company Structure `CRITICAL`

**Affects:** 02-company-overview (primary), 09-management-team, 11-funding-request, 15-appendices

**What's missing:**
- Mission/vision methodology (Sinek's Golden Circle: Why → How → What)
- Shareholders' agreement guidance: what founders must agree before registering a company (equity splits, decision rights, vesting, IP assignment, drag-along, tag-along, non-compete)
- Founder vesting: standard cliff/vesting structures; what happens when a co-founder leaves
- Board composition: when to form a board, role of independent directors, audit committee
- Family business governance: separation of family and business finances; spouse/sibling involvement; succession planning before it becomes a crisis
- Corporate Secretary obligations (annual returns, beneficial owners register, minutes)
- Conflicts of interest: related-party transactions, disclosure obligations under the Companies Act

**Why it matters for Uganda SMEs:** The majority of Uganda business failures involve governance failures — founders who disagreed, families who fought, single-signatory accounts that paralysed operations when the owner travelled. A McKinsey-quality plan addresses governance from the outset.

**Sources to find:**
- Simon Sinek — *Start with Why* (purpose/mission framework)
- IFC — *Corporate Governance for Family-Owned Businesses* or *SME Governance Toolkit*
- A family business management book (Ward, Jaffe, or Gersick's work)
- Brad Feld — *Venture Deals* (shareholder agreement terms, for equity businesses)

## B. Business Valuation `CRITICAL`

**Affects:** 11-funding-request (equity section), 02-company-overview (ownership structure), 15-appendices

**What's missing:**
- No valuation methodology in the suite. When an equity investor asks "what is your company worth?", the plan cannot answer.
- DCF (Discounted Cash Flow) — the gold standard for established businesses with cash flows
- Revenue/earnings multiples — how sector comparables determine valuation
- Pre-revenue valuation methods (Berkus Method, Risk Factor Summation, Scorecard Method, VC Method)
- Cap table construction — pre-money, post-money, dilution modelling
- Liquidation preference and anti-dilution provisions (basic literacy)
- Valuation for Uganda DFI funding (UDB and aBi use grant-equivalent value, not VC methods)
- Asset-based valuation for collateral-rich businesses (land, equipment)

**Sources to find:**
- Damodaran — *Investment Valuation* (comprehensive but heavy) or *The Little Book of Valuation* (accessible)
- Brad Feld & Jason Mendelson — *Venture Deals* (term sheets and cap tables)
- A pre-revenue startup valuation methodology guide

## C. Sustainability / ESG / Environmental Compliance `HIGH PRIORITY`

**Affects:** 11b-grant-proposal, 12-risk-analysis, 11-funding-request (DFI), 08-operations-plan, NEW: ESMP template needed

**What's missing:**
- ESMP (Environmental and Social Management Plan) template — required for UDB, IFC, and EU-funded projects
- Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) process and NEMA requirements in Uganda
- ESG reporting framework (GRI Standards, SASB, or IFC Performance Standards — which applies to Uganda SMEs?)
- Climate risk for agriculture (drought, flooding, crop failure) — different from financial risk
- Carbon credit opportunities as revenue stream (Gold Standard, VERRA/VCS)
- Circular economy business model guidance
- EUDR compliance (already in risk section but no operational guide for coffee/cocoa businesses)
- Waste management compliance (NEMA guidelines on industrial waste)

**Why it matters:** DFI lending (>UGX 500M) increasingly requires an ESMP. EU grants require ESMP. Without this, the suite cannot serve the upper SME tier effectively.

**Sources to find:**
- IFC — *Performance Standards on Environmental and Social Sustainability* (free online)
- A climate-smart agriculture/business guide
- NEMA Uganda environmental compliance guide

## D. AI for Business Strategy `HIGH PRIORITY`

**Affects:** 14-ai-integration (primary)

**What's missing:**
- AI business strategy framework (not just automation — strategic AI positioning)
- AI unit economics (cost per token, API pricing, AI vs. human cost crossover points)
- AI adoption in low-infrastructure contexts (offline-first AI, USSD-based AI, limited power/internet)
- Large Language Model (LLM) applications for Uganda business contexts (Luganda NLP, customer service)
- AI governance for Uganda (Uganda Data Protection and Privacy Act 2019 implications; EU AI Act extraterritorial reach)
- AI risk framework (hallucination risk, model drift, adversarial inputs)
- Competitive AI strategy: when to be an AI leader vs. AI fast-follower
- AI transformation roadmap (beyond a simple adoption list)

**Sources to find:**
- Iansiti & Lakhani — *Competing in the Age of AI* (HBR Press, 2020)
- Agrawal, Gans & Goldfarb — *Prediction Machines* (2018) or *Power and Prediction* (2022)
- A practical AI implementation/transformation book

## E. Supply Chain & Procurement `MODERATE PRIORITY`

**Affects:** 08-operations-plan (primary), 03-products-services (supplier dependencies)

**What's missing:**
- Supply chain design framework (direct vs. distribution, push vs. pull inventory)
- Inventory management models (EOQ — Economic Order Quantity; JIT — Just in Time; safety stock)
- Supplier evaluation and selection frameworks (multi-criteria decision analysis)
- Procurement process: RFQ, tender evaluation, purchase orders, contract management
- Uganda-specific supply chain challenges: Mombasa corridor delays, port congestion, counterfeit goods risk
- Cold chain design for perishables (agricultural/food/pharmaceutical businesses)
- Supplier concentration risk mitigation (over-reliance on single supplier)
- Supplier development for local sourcing (EAC Rules of Origin compliance)

**Sources to find:**
- Chopra & Meindl — *Supply Chain Management: Strategy, Planning, and Operation*
- A procurement management book (CIPS-aligned or equivalent)

## F. Financial Reporting Standards `MODERATE PRIORITY`

**Affects:** 10-financial-projections, 15-appendices

**What's missing:**
- IFRS for SMEs — the internationally recognised accounting standard. Uganda companies above a certain threshold must comply; bank auditors reference it
- Financial statement presentation format (which line items to include, how to classify items)
- Management accounts vs. statutory accounts distinction
- Audit requirements by company size (when audited accounts are mandatory in Uganda)
- Ratio analysis as used by bank credit departments (not just DSCR — current ratio, quick ratio, debt-to-equity, interest coverage, inventory turnover, receivables days)
- Altman Z-Score (bankruptcy prediction — banks use it silently)

**Sources to find:**
- An IFRS for SMEs guide (IASB publishes a free version; a companion book by Epstein or Wiley)
- Fridson & Alvarez — *Financial Statement Analysis: A Practitioner's Guide*

## G. Consulting-Quality Analytical Frameworks `CRITICAL FOR McKINSEY/BAIN/DELOITTE STANDARD`

**This is a newly identified gap raised by user (March 2026). Full detail in `04-consulting-quality-gap.md`.**

The suite currently produces bankable, credible plans. It does NOT yet produce the structured, hypothesis-driven, MECE-compliant analysis expected from management consulting deliverables.

Key gaps:
- Minto's Pyramid Principle (structured communication for analytical documents)
- MECE thinking and issue tree construction
- McKinsey/BCG strategic portfolio tools (BCG matrix, GE-McKinsey, McKinsey 7-S)
- Rigorous financial modelling standards (sensitivity tables, scenario analysis as McKinsey does it)
- Consulting document standards (storyboarding, chart selection, data visualisation)
- Structured problem-solving methodology (hypothesis-first vs. data-first)

## H. Insurance `LOW PRIORITY`

**Affects:** 12-risk-analysis

**What's missing:** The risk section recommends insurance as a mitigation strategy but gives no guidance on:
- Types of business insurance available in Uganda (property, public liability, professional indemnity, group life, workmen's compensation, fire, burglary)
- Insurance costs as a proportion of revenue or asset value
- Major Uganda insurers (UAP-Old Mutual, Jubilee, Sanlam, CIC) and products

## I. Family Business Management `LOW-MODERATE PRIORITY`

**Affects:** 02-company-overview, 09-management-team

**What's missing:** Most Uganda SMEs are family businesses. The skills reference this but provide no framework for:
- Three-circle model (family / ownership / business overlap)
- Family council vs. board of directors
- Employment of family members (nepotism risk, professionalisation)
- Succession planning before the crisis happens
- Buy-sell agreements among family shareholders

**Sources to find:** John Ward — *Keeping the Family Business Healthy*; Gersick et al. — *Generation to Generation*.
