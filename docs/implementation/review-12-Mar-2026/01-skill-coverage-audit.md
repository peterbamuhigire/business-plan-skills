# Skill Coverage Audit — Status by Section

**Classification:**
- **Flagship** — 5+ book citations, rich reference files, Uganda-specific, actionable
- **Solid** — 3+ book citations, adequate references, covers the topic well
- **Moderate gap** — functional but thin references; would benefit from 1–2 more books
- **Critical gap** — fewer than 2 book citations or fundamentally incomplete

---

## Core Business Plan Skills (01–15)

### 01 — Executive Summary `Solid`
**Lines:** 178 | **Book citations:** Jewinski, Bradbury/NIBM, Blank & Dorf, Brito, Nager et al. (5)
**Reference files:** presentation-structure.md, writing-quality.md, proposal-patterns.md

**What's good:** Four-step formula (Jewinski), brand story test (Brito), venture type framing (Blank & Dorf), elevator pitch templates.
**What's missing:** No dedicated bankability summary format for loan applications (vs. investor pitches). The skill treats all executive summaries the same but a bank officer reads differently from a VC.
**Book to find:** A dedicated business plan writing book that differentiates bank-submission formats from investor decks.

### 02 — Company Overview `CRITICAL GAP`
**Lines:** 60 | **Book citations:** RSM/Baker Tilly 2025 (regulatory only) — no business books
**Reference files:** doing-business-uganda.md, regulatory-compliance-matrix.md

**What's good:** Uganda legal structures (Ltd, SMC, sole trader), NIN/BRN 2025 requirement, sector licences list.
**What's critically missing:**
- Mission/vision writing methodology (no Sinek, Collins, or equivalent)
- Shareholders' agreement guidance — what founders need to agree before incorporation
- Founder vesting schedules — standard 4-year/1-year cliff structure
- Governance frameworks for growing companies (board composition, audit, compliance)
- Family business dynamics — most Uganda SMEs are family-owned; succession tension at Year 3–5 is real
- Corporate valuation at formation — what determines initial equity splits?
- Pre-revenue company description for startups vs. established business narrative

**Books to find:**
- Simon Sinek — *Start with Why* (mission/vision/purpose framework)
- A corporate governance for SMEs book (IFC toolkit adaptation, or similar)
- A family business management book

### 10 — Financial Projections `Solid–Strong`
**Lines:** 136 | **Book citations:** ILM/Morlidge, Page, Dennis et al., Blank & Dorf, Kagan, UBOS (6+)
**Reference files:** 7 files (tax framework, financial model templates, Uganda wage benchmarks, informal records, IP framework, startup metrics, budgeting/forecasting, process costing)

**What's good:** Comprehensive Uganda tax coverage, financial model templates, informal business records reconstruction.
**What's missing:**
- **IFRS for SMEs** — No accounting standards reference. Financial statements should follow a recognised framework and the suite doesn't specify which one.
- **Ratio analysis** — The DSCR is covered but no comprehensive financial ratio analysis guide (Altman Z-score, liquidity, profitability, activity ratios as used by bank credit departments).
- **Project finance / infrastructure finance** — For capital-intensive businesses (mini-grids, real estate, manufacturing), project finance techniques (DSCR modelling, waterfall structures) are needed.
**Books to find:**
- An IFRS for SMEs guide or non-accountants financial reporting book
- A financial ratio analysis book (possibly Fridson & Alvarez — *Financial Statement Analysis*)

### 11 — Funding Request `Solid`

**Reference files:** credit-assessment-frameworks.md, women-financing-uganda.md

**What's good:** Uganda bank loan mode (DSCR, CAMPARI, collateral), funder type table, use-of-funds format.

- A startup fundraising / VC term sheet book (Brad Feld — *Venture Deals*)
- A business valuation book

### 14 — AI Integration `CRITICAL GAP`
**Lines:** 124 | **Book citations:** Dumas et al. (BPM), Dennis et al. (build/buy/outsource) — **zero AI-specific books**
**Reference files:** process-automation-readiness.md, uganda-ict-ip-guidelines.md

- Iansiti & Lakhani — *Competing in the Age of AI* (HBR Press, 2020)
