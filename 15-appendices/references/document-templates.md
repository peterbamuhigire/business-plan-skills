*Ready-to-use templates for the documents most commonly required by Ugandan banks, DFIs, and development partner funders. Every template is calibrated to Uganda's legal and regulatory context.*

## Template 3: Loan Repayment Schedule

Including a pre-calculated repayment schedule shows the bank you understand exactly what you're committing to. Use a reducing-balance method.

```
LOAN REPAYMENT SCHEDULE

Loan Amount:        UGX ___________
Interest Rate:      ___% per annum  (= ___% per month)
Loan Term:          ___ months
Monthly Repayment:  UGX ___________ (equal instalments)
[Or: state if repayment is structured — e.g., grace period first 3 months, then reducing balance]

Month | Opening Balance | Interest | Principal | Total Payment | Closing Balance
  1   |  UGX [X]       | UGX [X]  | UGX [X]   |   UGX [X]     |   UGX [X]
  2   |  UGX [X]       | UGX [X]  | UGX [X]   |   UGX [X]     |   UGX [X]
  3   |  UGX [X]       | UGX [X]  | UGX [X]   |   UGX [X]     |   UGX [X]
  ... |  ...           | ...      | ...       |   ...         |   ...
  [N] |  UGX [X]       | UGX [X]  | UGX [X]   |   UGX [X]     |   0

TOTALS:
  Total Principal:   UGX ___________  (= original loan amount)
  Total Interest:    UGX ___________
  Total Cost:        UGX ___________
  Effective APR:     ___________%

Note: Bank will calculate its own schedule. This schedule is prepared at
[X]% per annum for illustration. Actual schedule per bank terms.

Formula used: Monthly payment = P × r(1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n − 1)
where P = principal, r = monthly rate, n = term in months
```

## Template 5: Character Reference Letter

For CAMPARI Character assessment, a reference letter from a respected community member, existing banker, or professional contact strengthens the application.

```

[Date]

The Branch Manager / Credit Manager
[Bank Name]
[Branch]

Dear Sir/Madam,

RE: CHARACTER REFERENCE FOR [APPLICANT NAME], NIN [XXXXXXXX]
    [COMPANY NAME] — LOAN APPLICATION

I write in support of the above-named individual's application for credit
facilities from your institution. I have known [Applicant Name] for [X] years
in my capacity as [relationship — e.g., "pastor of [Church Name]" / "LC1
Chairperson of [Village]" / "Managing Director of [Company]"].

During this period, I have observed [him/her] to be:
- Honest and of good character, with no known involvement in criminal
  activity or financial disputes
- Hardworking and committed to [his/her] business interests in [sector]
- Responsive to community and financial obligations, meeting [his/her]
  commitments on time

confidence in [his/her] ability and willingness to repay any facility granted.

I make this reference freely and without inducement.

Yours faithfully,

[Full Name]
[Title / Occupation]
[Organisation — if applicable]
[Contact Number]
[NIN — if bank requires it]
[Date]
```

## Template 7: Tax Compliance Certificate (TCC) — How to Obtain

The URA Tax Compliance Certificate confirms that a business has no outstanding tax obligations. Most banks require it.

**How to obtain:**
1. Log into URA e-Tax portal at efris.ura.go.ug or tax.ura.go.ug
2. Navigate to: Taxpayer Services → Tax Compliance Certificate
3. All returns (PAYE, VAT, Income Tax) must be filed and all taxes paid (or payment plan in place)
4. Certificate generates automatically if compliant; valid for 6 months
5. Print or download PDF; submit with loan application

**If not compliant:** File any outstanding returns and pay outstanding taxes (or arrange a payment plan with URA) before applying for the TCC. A business with URA arrears will not receive a TCC and will not qualify for most formal loans.

## Template 8: NSSF Compliance Certificate

Required for businesses with 5+ employees.

**How to obtain:**
1. Log into NSSF e-portal at member.nssfug.org
2. Navigate to compliance certificate section
3. All monthly contributions must be filed and paid
4. Certificate confirms compliance status
5. Download and submit with loan application
