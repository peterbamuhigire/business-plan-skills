# Sustainable Product Design

## 1. Sustainable Product Design Principles (Kumar 2025)

**Definition**: designing products and services that meet customer needs while minimising negative environmental and social impact across the full lifecycle.

**4 design-stage decisions that determine 70–80% of a product's lifetime environmental impact:**

1. **Material choice**: renewable, recycled, or low-impact materials vs virgin or toxic materials
2. **Production process**: energy-efficient, low-waste manufacturing vs high-energy, high-waste
3. **Use phase**: durable, energy-efficient, repairable vs disposable, energy-intensive
4. **End of life**: reusable, recyclable, compostable vs landfill-bound

**Key principle**: sustainability must be designed in, not added later. Post-hoc sustainability fixes cost 3–10× more than design-stage decisions.

## 2. Life Cycle Thinking for Uganda SMEs

Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) is complex for SMEs — use simplified life cycle thinking instead.

**Simple 4-stage lifecycle scan:**

| Stage | Questions to Ask | Common Uganda Issues |
|---|---|---|
| Raw materials | Where do inputs come from? Are they sustainably sourced? | Deforestation (timber, charcoal); unsustainable fishing; chemical fertilisers |
| Production | What waste, water, energy, chemicals are used? | High water use; electricity from diesel generators; food processing waste |
| Use | How does the customer use/consume the product? Any disposal issues? | Single-use packaging; food safety; short product lifespan |
| End of life | Where does the product go after use? Can it be recycled/composted? | Plastic waste; e-waste; organic decomposition |

**Minimum standard**: scan all 4 stages and identify the top 2 risks; document mitigation in §16.

## 3. Circular Product Design (Leleux 2019 + Ellen MacArthur)

Linear product design (take → make → dispose) is increasingly commercially and regulatorily unacceptable.

**Circular product design principles:**

- **Design for durability**: products last longer; reduce replacement rate; warranty as competitive advantage
- **Design for repairability**: modular components; spare parts available; repair manuals published
- **Design for disassembly**: products can be separated into material streams at end of life
- **Design for reuse**: packaging, containers, or components can be returned and reused
- **Design for biological cycle**: organic materials can be safely composted or biodegraded

**Circular product business models for Uganda SMEs:**

| Model | Description | Uganda Application |
|---|---|---|
| Product-as-a-service | Sell outcome, not product; retain ownership; recover at end of use | Solar-as-a-service (M-KOPA model); equipment leasing |
| Refurbishment/remanufacturing | Collect used products; refurbish; resell | Electronics repair; tyre retreading; furniture restoration |
| By-product exchange | Sell waste from one process as input to another | Dairy manure → fertiliser; brewery waste → animal feed |
| Deposit-refund system | Charge deposit on packaging; recover on return | Glass bottles; refillable containers for cooking oil, honey |
| Sharing platform | Increase utilisation of underused assets | Equipment hire; shared cold storage; shared transport |

## 4. Technology-Enabled Sustainable Products (Kumar 2025 — Technopreneurship)

Technopreneurs create value by applying technology to sustainability problems.

**High-opportunity sustainable product categories for Uganda/East Africa:**

| Product/Service Category | Technology Enabler | Market Opportunity |
|---|---|---|
| Clean cookstoves/biogas | Biomass gasification; biogas digester design | 95% of Uganda households use biomass — health and climate co-benefit |
| Solar water pumping | PV + pump controller | Reduces diesel pump costs; water security for agriculture |
| Cold chain / solar refrigeration | Solar-powered cold storage | 30–40% post-harvest loss reduction |
| Digital soil testing | IoT sensors + mobile app | Precision fertiliser use; soil health monitoring |
| Organic waste-to-compost | Process design + quality control | Premium pricing; import substitution for synthetic fertiliser |
| Water purification | UV, filtration, SODIS | Safe drinking water in underserved areas |
| Digital financial services | Mobile money integration | Smallholder farmer payment systems; savings groups |

**Technopreneurship principle**: sustainable products often have a larger total addressable market in low-income contexts because they solve genuine problems (water, energy, food safety) rather than creating new consumption.

## 5. Product Sustainability Assessment Tool — 10-Question Checklist

Score each item: 0 (no), 1 (partially), 2 (fully).

1. Are raw materials sourced from verified sustainable suppliers?
2. Does the production process minimise waste to <5% of inputs?
3. Is energy use per unit of output measured and being reduced?
4. Is the product free from hazardous chemicals/materials?
5. Is the product designed to last ≥3 years (or equivalent lifecycle)?
6. Can the product be repaired? Are spare parts available?
7. Is packaging minimal, recyclable, or biodegradable?
8. Does end-of-life disposal have a clear pathway (compost, recycle, return)?
9. Does the product address a real need for low-income or underserved customers?
10. Is there a plan to reduce the product's environmental impact by 20% over 3 years?

**Scoring:**
- **≥14 / 20**: sustainability-positive product
- **10–13 / 20**: adequate but improvement needed
- **< 10 / 20**: redesign required

## 6. Uganda/East Africa Application

- Uganda's most sustainable product opportunities align with solving real problems: food safety, water security, clean energy, post-harvest preservation, sanitation.

- **Packaging**: KCCA has banned polythene bags <30 microns since 2009; enforcement is increasing. Any Uganda SME dealing in packaged goods should use woven polypropylene, paper, glass, or biodegradable alternatives.

- **Organic certification**: Premium organic markets are growing in Kampala (Nakasero Market, Village Mall organic stalls, hotel supply chains). NOGAMU (National Organic Agriculture Movement of Uganda) provides certification. Premium: 20–40% above conventional.

- **Digital products**: zero marginal environmental cost per additional user — inherently scalable sustainability. Software, digital services, and data products have minimal environmental footprint if powered by renewable energy.

- The ESMP template (`11-funding-request/references/esmp-template.md`) includes product-specific screening for Category A/B/C compliance.
