# Product Development Lifecycle

> Reference: *Valuepreneurs* by Bates (2024); *Managing Innovation* 5th ed. by Tidd & Bessant (Wiley, 2013)

> Figures adapted to Ugandan context (UGX). Percentages and ratios from the sources are universal.

---

## 1. Value-Driven Product Development (VDPD) Process

Seven-stage framework from idea to market, with customer validation at every gate:

```
Idea Stage → Concept Development → Prototype → IP Protection → MVP → Launch → Growth
     ↕              ↕                  ↕                              ↕        ↕
  Customer      Customer           Customer                       Customer  Market
  Discovery     Validation         Testing                        Feedback  Data
```

Each stage includes integration with the Business Model Canvas — update canvas blocks as assumptions are validated or invalidated.

## 2. Stage 1: Idea Generation and Validation

### Where Ideas Come From

| Source | Description | Example (Uganda) |
|---|---|---|
| **Gap in market** | Unmet need observed in an industry | No affordable cold-chain logistics for rural farmers |
| **Personal frustration** | Founder solves own problem | Difficulty finding reliable artisans leads to marketplace app |
| **Technology transfer** | Applying existing tech to new context | Solar-powered irrigation adapted from Asian models |
| **Customer conversations** | Direct feedback reveals pain points | Retailers requesting mobile money integration for stock orders |
| **Industry trends** | Macro shifts creating new demand | Urbanisation driving demand for ready-to-eat food products |

### Painkiller vs. Vitamin Test

| Type | Description | Investor Preference |
|---|---|---|
| **Painkiller** | Solves an urgent, existing problem | Strongly preferred — customers will pay now |
| **Vitamin** | Nice-to-have improvement | Harder sell — must prove habit formation |

**Business plan implication:** Frame every product as a painkiller. Articulate the specific pain point, who feels it, and how severely.

### Idea Validation Steps

1. **Identify the pain point** — What specific problem does this solve?
2. **Customer discovery interviews** (minimum 20-30 conversations)
3. **Test willingness to pay** — Would they buy this? At what price?
4. **Map to Business Model Canvas** — Customer segments, value propositions, channels
5. **Build/buy decision** — Can this be built with available resources?

### Customer Discovery Interview Guide

- Who has this problem? (Demographics, role, context)
- How do they currently solve it? (Existing alternatives)
- What do they dislike about current solutions? (Unmet needs)
- How much do they spend on current solutions? (Willingness to pay)
- How would they describe the ideal solution? (Feature priorities)

**Rule:** Never ask "Would you buy this?" — instead ask about current behaviour and spending.

## 3. Stage 2: Concept Development

### Building Customer Personas

For each target customer segment, document:

```
Name: [Fictional but realistic]
Demographics: Age, gender, location, income level
Role: Job title or social role
Goals: What they are trying to achieve
Frustrations: What blocks them
Current solution: How they solve the problem today
Decision factors: Price, quality, convenience, trust
Quote: One sentence capturing their mindset
```

**Uganda example — Agribusiness product:**
```
Name: Sarah Nambi
Demographics: 35, female, Mbale district, UGX 800,000/month income
Role: Smallholder coffee farmer, 2 acres
Goals: Get better prices for her coffee, reduce post-harvest losses
Frustrations: Middlemen pay 40% below market price, no quality grading
Current solution: Sells wet cherry to nearest buyer, no bargaining power
Decision factors: Trust > Price > Convenience
Quote: "I know my coffee is good but I cannot prove it to buyers"
```

### Proof of Concept (PoC)

| PoC Type | When to Use | Cost (Uganda) |
|---|---|---|
| **Paper prototype** | Service businesses, apps | UGX 0 — sketches and flowcharts |
| **Landing page test** | Digital products | UGX 200,000-500,000 (domain + hosting) |
| **Manual MVP** | Marketplace/logistics | UGX 500,000-2M (run process manually) |
| **Physical mockup** | Consumer products | UGX 1-5M (3D printing or fabrication) |
| **Pilot programme** | B2B services | UGX 2-10M (serve 5-10 customers manually) |

### Customer Validation Checklist

- [ ] Minimum 10 customers have seen the concept
- [ ] At least 50% expressed strong interest (not just politeness)
- [ ] At least 3 customers offered to pay or pre-order
- [ ] Key objections documented and addressed
- [ ] Business Model Canvas updated with validated assumptions

## 4. Stage 3: Prototype Development

### Make vs. License Decision

| Factor | Make It Yourself | License It |
|---|---|---|
| **Control** | Full control over quality and brand | Limited — licensee decides |
| **Capital required** | High — manufacturing, equipment | Low — royalty-based income |
| **Speed to market** | Slower — must build capacity | Faster — licensee has infrastructure |
| **Risk** | Higher — you bear all costs | Lower — licensee bears production risk |
| **Margin** | Higher per unit | Lower per unit (royalties typically 3-10%) |
| **Best for** | Core product with strong differentiation | Non-core products, geographic expansion |

### Prototype Development Process

1. **Design specifications** — Functional requirements, materials, dimensions
2. **First prototype** — Rough functional model (does it work?)
3. **Customer testing** — 10-20 users interact with prototype
4. **Iteration** — Refine based on feedback (may repeat 2-5 times)
5. **Final design** — Production-ready specifications
6. **IP protection** — File patents/trademarks before sharing widely

### Customer Testing Protocol

1. Define testing goals (usability? desirability? willingness to pay?)
2. Recruit 10-20 participants matching target persona
3. Observe — do not lead or explain how it "should" work
4. Record feedback systematically (not just what confirms your hopes)
5. Identify patterns — what 3+ users mention independently matters most

## 5. Stage 4: Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

### MVP vs. Prototype

| Aspect | Prototype | MVP |
|---|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Test if it works | Test if customers will buy |
| **Audience** | Internal team + test users | Real market |
| **Revenue** | None | Yes — charges real money |
| **Quality** | Rough, functional | Good enough to deliver value |
| **Feedback** | "Does this solve the problem?" | "Will you pay for this?" |

### MVP Cost Benchmarks (Uganda)

| Business Type | Typical MVP Cost | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Mobile app | UGX 5-20M | 2-4 months |
| Consumer product (physical) | UGX 10-50M | 3-6 months |
| Service business | UGX 2-10M | 1-3 months |
| Food/beverage product | UGX 5-30M | 2-4 months |
| SaaS platform | UGX 15-60M | 3-6 months |

### Manufacturing Considerations

- **Local manufacturing** — Lower transport costs, supports local economy, but limited capacity for specialised products
- **Import components, assemble locally** — Common model for electronics, solar products
- **Full import** — Higher cost but faster for complex products

## 6. Stage 5: Launch and Market Introduction

### Pre-Launch Checklist

- [ ] Regulatory approvals obtained (UNBS, URA, sector-specific licences)
- [ ] Supply chain tested with at least one full production run
- [ ] Pricing validated with real customer transactions
- [ ] Distribution channels established (at least 2)
- [ ] Marketing materials ready (minimum: website, brochures, social media)
- [ ] Customer support process defined

### Marketing Quadfecta (Bates)

Four pillars for product launch:

1. **Content marketing** — Educate the market about the problem you solve
2. **Social media** — Build community and social proof
3. **Paid advertising** — Targeted reach to ideal customer profile
4. **PR and partnerships** — Credibility through third-party endorsement

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Benchmarks

| Channel | Typical CAC (Uganda) | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Referrals | UGX 5,000-20,000 | Service businesses, B2B |
| Social media organic | UGX 10,000-50,000 | Consumer products, apps |
| Paid digital ads | UGX 30,000-150,000 | E-commerce, SaaS |
| Agent network | UGX 50,000-200,000 | Financial services, agritech |
| Events/activations | UGX 100,000-500,000 | Premium products, B2B |

## 7. Innovation Strategy (Tidd & Bessant)

### Types of Innovation

| Type | Description | Example (Uganda) |
|---|---|---|
| **Product innovation** | New or improved offering | Solar-powered milk cooler |
| **Process innovation** | New way of making/delivering | Mobile money collection for utilities |
| **Position innovation** | New market or customer segment | Repurposing commercial software for SMEs |
| **Paradigm innovation** | New business model | Pay-as-you-go solar replacing one-time purchase |

### Innovation Strategy Approaches

| Approach | Description | Risk | Return |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Incremental** | Small improvements to existing products | Low | Steady |
| **Modular** | New components within existing architecture | Medium | Moderate |
| **Architectural** | New ways of linking existing components | Medium-High | High if successful |
| **Radical** | Completely new product/market | High | Very high if successful |

### Dynamic Capabilities Framework (Teece/Pisano)

Three elements of innovation strategy for a business plan:

1. **Competitive position** — Where does the firm sit relative to competitors? What assets and competencies does it have?
2. **Technological paths** — What technological opportunities are available? Where is the industry heading?
3. **Organisational processes** — Can the firm learn, adapt, and reconfigure its resources?

**Business plan application:** The R&D pipeline section should address all three — current position, future direction, and organisational readiness to innovate.

## 8. Funding Product Development

### Funding Sources by Stage

| Stage | Appropriate Funding | Typical Amount (Uganda) |
|---|---|---|
| Idea validation | Bootstrapping, personal savings | UGX 1-5M |
| Prototype | Friends & family, angel investors | UGX 5-30M |
| MVP | Angel investors, grants, competitions | UGX 20-100M |
| Launch | Seed funding, bank loans, crowdfunding | UGX 50-500M |
| Growth | Series A, venture capital, DFIs | UGX 500M+ |

### Business Model Canvas Integration

Update the canvas at each stage gate:

| Canvas Block | Idea Stage | Concept | Prototype | MVP | Launch |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Customer Segments | Hypothesis | Validated personas | Confirmed | Paying customers | Segmented |
| Value Propositions | Problem statement | Solution concept | Tested | Priced | Positioned |
| Channels | Assumed | Explored | Tested | Active | Optimised |
| Revenue Streams | Model chosen | Willingness tested | Pricing set | First revenue | Scaled |
| Key Resources | Identified | Costed | Procured | Operational | Scaled |
