# Startup Financial Metrics & Quick Feasibility

> Based on Blank, S. & Dorf, B. (2012). *The Startup Owner's Manual*; Kagan, N. (2024). *Million Dollar Weekend*.

## Metrics That Matter

Traditional P&L statements are insufficient for early-stage ventures. Use these startup-specific metrics instead (Blank & Dorf, 2012).

### Core Metrics for All Startups

| Metric | What It Measures | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Cash burn rate** | Monthly cash outflow | How fast you're spending |
| **Months of cash remaining** | Cash balance ÷ burn rate | When you run out of money |
| **Customer acquisition cost (CAC)** | Total acquisition spend ÷ new customers | Cost to get each customer |
| **Customer lifetime value (LTV)** | Revenue per customer over their lifetime | Total value of each customer |
| **LTV:CAC ratio** | LTV ÷ CAC (target >3:1) | Whether customers are worth acquiring |
| **Conversion rates** | % moving through each funnel stage | Where prospects drop off |
| **Average selling price (ASP)** | Total revenue ÷ units sold | Revenue per transaction |
| **Revenue growth rate** | Month-over-month or quarter-over-quarter | Trajectory of the business |
| **Churn rate** | % of customers lost per period | Retention health |
| **Payback period** | CAC ÷ monthly revenue per customer | Months to recover acquisition cost |

### Metrics by Channel Type

**Physical product / physical channel:**
Revenue, COGS, gross margin, CAC, LTV, burn rate, months of cash, units sold, ASP, inventory turns, accounts receivable days

**Web/mobile product / web channel:**
Registered users, monthly active users (MAU), daily active users (DAU), activation rate, conversion to paid, ARPU, CAC, LTV, churn rate

**Multi-sided market (e.g., marketplace):**
Supply-side metrics (sellers, listings, fill rate) + demand-side metrics (buyers, conversion, AOV) + platform metrics (take rate, GMV, liquidity)

## One-Minute Business Model

Quick feasibility calculation before building detailed projections (Kagan, 2024):

```
Revenue per unit - Cost per unit = Profit per unit
Target annual revenue ÷ Profit per unit = Units needed per year
Units needed ÷ 12 = Units needed per month
```

**Example — Chapati business in Kampala:**
```
Revenue per chapati: UGX 500
Cost per chapati: UGX 200
Profit per chapati: UGX 300
Target annual profit: UGX 36,000,000 (UGX 3M/month)
Units needed/year: 120,000
Units needed/month: 10,000
Units needed/day: ~333 chapatis
```

Is 333 chapatis per day achievable? If yes, the model works. If not, adjust the dials.

## Six Revenue Dials

When the One-Minute Business Model doesn't pencil out, adjust these levers (Kagan, 2024):

| Dial | Strategy | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Average order value** | Increase purchase amount per transaction | Bundle chapatis with tea; add egg option |
| **2. Frequency** | Increase how often each customer buys | Subscription/standing order for offices |
| **3. Price point** | Increase or decrease price | Test premium chapatis at UGX 800 |
| **4. Customer type** | Target higher-value segments | Hotels and restaurants instead of street buyers |
| **5. Product line** | Add complementary products | Add mandazi, samosas, rolex |
| **6. Add-on services** | Bundle services with products | Catering service, delivery, bulk orders |

**Application in business plans:** When presenting financial projections, show which dials are being used and how they affect the revenue model. Investors want to see that you understand the levers available.

## Freedom Number

Calculate minimum monthly revenue needed for personal financial sustainability (Kagan, 2024):

```
Housing (rent/mortgage) + Food & Transport + Savings/Investment = Freedom Number
```

**Purpose in business plans:** The Freedom Number grounds financial projections in reality. If the founder needs UGX 3,000,000/month to survive, the business must reach that level before external funding runs out. Include this as a milestone in financial projections.

## Burn Rate Guidelines by Stage

Approximate monthly burn rates by development stage (Blank & Dorf, 2012, via Fred Wilson):

| Stage | Focus | Approximate Burn Rate (USD equivalent) |
|---|---|---|
| Customer Discovery | Finding problem-solution fit | $50-75K/month |
| Customer Validation | Proving repeatable sales | $100K/month |
| Customer Creation | Building demand | $250K/month |
| Company Building | Scaling operations | $500K+/month |

**Uganda localisation:** These Silicon Valley figures should be scaled down significantly. A Ugandan startup might operate at 10-30% of these levels due to lower labour, rent, and operating costs. The important principle is that burn rate should **increase only as validation milestones are achieved**, not before.

## Revenue Curves by Market Type

Match financial projections to the market type (Blank & Dorf, 2012):

| Market Type | Year 1 Revenue | Year 3 Revenue | Year 5 Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Existing market** | Modest (taking share) | Growing predictably | Significant if execution is strong |
| **New market** | Minimal (educating market) | Still modest (adoption is slow) | Inflection point possible |
| **Re-segmented market** | Modest (finding the niche) | Growing as niche is validated | Strong if segment expands |
| **Clone market** | Depends on local adaptation | Growth mirrors original model | Mature if execution succeeds |

**Anti-pattern:** Don't project "hockey stick" growth curves for new markets. Investors will immediately flag this as unrealistic (Blank & Dorf, 2012). Show the honest curve and explain the traction milestones that will accelerate it.

## Practical Application in Business Plans

When writing section 10 (Financial Projections):

1. **Start with the One-Minute Business Model** — prove basic unit economics work before building spreadsheets
2. **Show which Revenue Dials are active** — demonstrate understanding of the levers
3. **Include startup-specific metrics** — not just income statements; show CAC, LTV, burn rate, months of cash
4. **Match revenue curves to market type** — existing vs. new vs. re-segmented markets have different trajectories
5. **Set the Freedom Number** — ground projections in founder sustainability
6. **Stage the burn rate** — show spending increases only after validation milestones
7. **Cross-reference validation** — link revenue assumptions to Customer Development evidence from meta-market-validation
