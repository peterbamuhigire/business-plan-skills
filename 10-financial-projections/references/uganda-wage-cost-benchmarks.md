*Sources: UBOS National Labour Force Survey 2021, UBOS UNHS 2023/24, UBOS CPI February 2026, UBOS KEI Q1 2025/26. Cite UBOS when using these figures.*

## 1. Labour Cost Benchmarks

### Median Earnings (NLFS 2021 / UNHS 2023/24)

| Category | Monthly (UGX) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Median cash earnings (all paid workers) | **200,000** | NLFS 2021 & UNHS 2023/24 |
| Median cash + in-kind earnings | **260,000** | NLFS 2021 |
| Average daily hours of work | 7.6 hours | NLFS 2021 |
| Implied daily rate (200,000 ÷ 26 days) | ~7,700 | Rough estimate |

> **Business plan guidance:** UGX 200,000/month is the national median for paid employees. Unskilled workers in Kampala typically earn UGX 150,000–300,000/month depending on sector. Skilled trades (mechanics, welders, carpenters) command UGX 400,000–800,000/month. Professionals (accountants, engineers) UGX 1.2M–3M+.

**42%** of employed persons earn below two-thirds of the national median (below ~UGX 133,000/month) — indicates deep informal economy.

Only **27%** of employees are entitled to any workplace benefits (pension, leave, sick pay) — NSSF coverage is limited.

### Education/Skills Wage Premium
- Professionals: significantly above median
- Technical/associate professionals: above median
- Service/sales workers: around median
- Agricultural workers: below median
- Elementary occupations: below median

## 2. Inflation Planning Rates

*Source: UBOS Consumer Price Index February 2026*

### Annual Inflation by Category (for cost escalation assumptions)

| Category | Annual Rate (Feb 2026) | Use in projections |
|---|---|---|
| **Headline inflation** | **2.9%** | General cost escalation |
| Core goods | 2.1% | Manufactured inputs |
| Services (e.g. rent, professional fees) | **4.1%** | Service costs, salaries |
| Food commodities | 1.8% | Food/restaurant COGS |
| Energy, fuel & utilities | **2.7%** | Fuel, electricity, charcoal |
| Education services | 4.1% | School/training costs |
| Health services | 2.8% | Healthcare costs |
| Insurance & financial services | **12.3%** | Financial charges |
| Housing/water/electricity/gas | 3.9% | Rent, utility bills |
| Clothing & footwear | 1.9% | Uniforms, workwear |
| Restaurants & accommodation | 4.0% | Hospitality COGS |

> **Planning rule:** Use 3.0–4.0% as the general annual cost escalation rate for 3-year projections. Apply 4.1% for salary/wage growth. Apply 10–11% for maize-related inputs. Apply 6–10% for firewood/charcoal in energy-intensive businesses.

**Historical average:** 3.3% CY2024; 3.5% FY2024/25. Long-term planning rate: **4.0% per annum**.

## 4. Household Consumer Spending Patterns

*Source: UNHS 2023/24 — useful for market sizing and pricing*

| Category | Share of Budget |
|---|---|
| Food & non-alcoholic beverages | **44.2%** |
| Housing, water, electricity, gas | **15.9%** |
| Clothing, footwear | ~7.0% (estimated) |
| Health (out-of-pocket) | Median UGX 32,000/month |
| Secondary education | Average UGX 2,255,000/year/learner |

**Implication for market sizing:**
- A Ugandan household earning median UGX 200,000/month spends ~UGX 88,400 on food
- Healthcare spending median UGX 32,000/month = UGX 384,000/year per household
- Kampala households spend up to UGX 3,753,000/year on secondary education

## 5. Poverty Lines & Consumer Segmentation

*Source: UNHS 2023/24*

| Segment | Threshold | % Population |
|---|---|---|
| Below poverty line ($1/day) | <~UGX 90,000/month | **16.1%** |
| Low income (rural) | Mean UGX 140,120/month per capita | 63.2% rural |
| Middle income (urban) | Mean UGX 214,698/month per capita | 36.8% urban |
| Kampala affluent | Mean UGX 302,179/month per capita | ~4% |

> **Market segmentation guidance:** The Ugandan mass market has limited disposable income. Products priced above UGX 5,000/unit face significant resistance at rural/low-income level. Urban middle-class consumers (Kampala, Mbarara, Jinja, Gulu) can support prices comparable to EA regional norms.

**Gini coefficient:** 0.382 (2023/24) — moderate inequality, improving. Bottom 40% key target for volume-based business models.

## 6. Financial Projections — General Assumptions Template

When building Uganda financial projections with no sector-specific data, use these defaults:

| Assumption | Default Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Annual revenue growth (Year 2–5) | 15–25% | Conservative for SME market entry |
| General cost escalation | 4.0% p.a. | CPI trend 2024–26 |
| Salary/wage increase | 4.0–5.0% p.a. | Above core CPI for retention |
| Food input cost increase | 5.0–10.0% | Commodity volatility |
| Energy cost increase | 5.0–10.0% | Fuel/biomass inflation |
| Exchange rate (USD conversions) | UGX 3,700 | Conservative planning rate |
| Commercial lending rate (BOU) | 18–24% p.a. | Bank of Uganda repo rate plus spread |
| Corporate tax rate (URA) | 30% | Income Tax Act |
| VAT rate | 18% | Uganda Revenue Authority |
| NSSF employer contribution | 10% of gross salary | National Social Security Fund Act |
| PAYE (graduated) | 10–30% | Uganda Revenue Authority |
