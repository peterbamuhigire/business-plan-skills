# Informal Business Records — Financial History Reconstruction

*Many Ugandan businesses applying for loans have 2–5 years of operation but no formal financial records. They have bank statements, mobile money transaction records, receipts, and customer order books — but no audited accounts. This reference shows how to reconstruct a credible financial history and present it to lenders.*

---

## 1. The Informal Business Reality in Uganda

The majority of Ugandan SMEs operate without formal financial records. This does not mean they are not creditworthy — it means they have not yet invested in an accountant. A business with 3 years of consistent M-Pesa inflows is more creditworthy than a registered company with fabricated accounts.

### What Informal Businesses Typically Have

| Record Type | Coverage | Credibility with Lenders |
|---|---|---|
| Mobile money statements (MTN/Airtel) | 3–12 months downloadable | HIGH — timestamped, tamper-evident |
| Bank statements (any bank account) | 3–24 months | HIGH — bank-certified |
| Supplier invoices and receipts | Variable | MEDIUM — corroborates COGS |
| Customer order books / delivery notes | Variable | MEDIUM — corroborates revenue |
| Stock records / inventory counts | Variable | MEDIUM — supports asset claims |
| Social media sales records | WhatsApp order history, Facebook Marketplace | LOW-MEDIUM — supplementary |
| Utility bills (power, water) | Monthly | LOW-MEDIUM — confirms operating status |
| Land/rent payments (receipts) | Monthly | MEDIUM — confirms tenure |
| NSSF payment receipts | Monthly (if registered) | HIGH — government-issued |
| URA presumptive tax receipts | Annual | MEDIUM — implies turnover band |

## 2. Reconstructing Revenue from Available Records

### Method 1: Mobile Money Statement Analysis (Most Reliable)

MTN Mobile Money and Airtel Money statements are downloadable from the app and are timestamped. They cannot be altered.

**Step 1:** Download all available statements (usually 12 months from app; request full history from telco for longer periods).

**Step 2:** Sort transactions into:
- Revenue receipts (incoming from customers)
- Business expense payments (outgoing to suppliers, rent, staff wages)
- Personal withdrawals (owner drawings)
- Business-to-business transfers (cannot count as revenue if subsequently sent to suppliers)

**Step 3:** Calculate monthly averages:
```
Monthly Revenue (average):
  Total incoming receipts ÷ Number of months = Average monthly revenue

Monthly Expenses (average):
  Total outgoing payments (business-related) ÷ Number of months = Average monthly expenses

Estimated Monthly Net Income = Average Revenue − Average Expenses
```

**Step 4:** Identify seasonal patterns (coffee harvest season, school fees month, rainy season) and note them.

**Presentation format for bank:**
```
MOBILE MONEY INCOME RECONSTRUCTION
[Business Name]
Period: [Month/Year] to [Month/Year]

Month | Incoming (UGX) | Outgoing — Business (UGX) | Net
[Jan] | [X]            | [X]                        | [X]
[Feb] | [X]            | [X]                        | [X]
...
Total | [X]            | [X]                        | [X]
Monthly average | [X]  | [X]                        | [X]

Source: MTN Mobile Money statements (attached), verified by [accountant name, ICPAU no.]
```

---

### Method 2: Bank Statement Analysis

Bank statements from any commercial bank, microfinance institution, or SACCO are useful. Centenary Bank, Equity Bank, and most microfinance institutions provide certified statements on request.

**Process:**
1. Obtain certified bank statements for all available periods (minimum 6 months; 12–24 preferred)
2. Separate business deposits from personal deposits (use transaction descriptions)
3. Identify regular payments that represent business expenses (rent, supplier payments, utilities)
4. Compute the same monthly summary as mobile money method

**Combined approach:** Where a business uses both mobile money and a bank account, produce a combined statement showing both sources.

---

### Method 3: Customer Order Book / Sales Ledger Reconstruction

Many informal traders maintain a handwritten sales book, WhatsApp order history, or delivery note copies.

**Reconstruction process:**
1. Compile all available records chronologically
2. Group by month
3. Sum sales values per month
4. Cross-check against mobile money/bank deposits (sales should roughly equal deposits + cash retained)

**Limitation:** This method is harder to verify independently and banks give it less weight than bank/mobile money statements. Use as supplementary evidence only.

---

### Method 4: Supplier Evidence (COGS Verification)

If the business cannot document revenue directly, supplier purchase records can help establish COGS — and from COGS, impute revenue using industry gross margin benchmarks.

**Example (retail business):**
```
Supplier invoices (purchases) for 12 months: UGX 48,000,000
Typical retail gross margin for this sector: 30%
Implied revenue = Purchases ÷ (1 − Gross Margin) = 48,000,000 ÷ 0.70 = UGX 68,571,000
Implied annual revenue: ~UGX 68.6 million
Monthly average: ~UGX 5.7 million
```

**This method is accepted by** microfinance institutions and some banks as a secondary verification. Reference the industry margin benchmarks from the relevant industry guide in this skills suite.

## 4. Presenting Reconstructed Records to Lenders

### What Each Lender Will Accept

| Lender Type | Minimum Record Requirement | Preferred |
|---|---|---|
| Commercial banks (large: Stanbic, ABSA) | 2 years audited accounts OR 3 years bank statements | 2+ years audited |
| Commercial banks (SME-focused: Centenary, Equity, dfcu) | 12 months bank + mobile money statements + management accounts | 12 months + management accounts |
| Uganda Development Bank | 2 years audited OR detailed management accounts + site visit | 2 years audited |
| Microfinance (FINCA, Pride, UGAFODE) | 6 months mobile money + bank statements | Bank statements + character reference |
| SACCOs | Savings record + character reference from SACCO officials | Internal savings history |
| Post Bank Uganda | 6 months records | Bank statements |
| Equity Bank | 12 months statements (bank or mobile money) | Mobile money + bank combined |

### Strengthening a Weak Records Application

1. **Get a management accounts certification:** An ICPAU-registered accountant can prepare and certify management accounts from available records. Cost: UGX 200,000–500,000. This significantly increases credibility.

2. **Bank account migration:** If the business has been running purely on mobile money, open a business bank account 6–12 months before applying for a loan. Start routing all business transactions through it.

3. **URA presumptive tax registration:** Register for income tax under the presumptive tax regime and pay the annual flat tax. The URA payment receipt implies a turnover bracket and demonstrates tax compliance.

4. **NSSF registration:** Even for small businesses with fewer than 5 employees, voluntary NSSF registration demonstrates compliance and provides a government record of wage payments.

5. **Reference letters:** Strong character references from community leaders, religious leaders, or existing bank customers known to the institution carry weight for CAMPARI Character assessment.

6. **Site visit invitation:** Invite the bank loan officer for a site visit. Physical evidence of a going concern — stock, customers, equipment, staff — compensates for limited paper records.

## 5. Cash-Flow Based Lending Narrative

When formal accounts are absent, frame the entire loan application around cash flow:

```
CASH FLOW LENDING NARRATIVE

"[Business Name] has operated for [X] years as a [type] business. While formal
audited accounts are not yet available, the attached mobile money and bank
statements for the period [dates] demonstrate consistent monthly cash inflows
averaging UGX [X], with operating outflows of approximately UGX [X], yielding
average monthly net operating cash flow of UGX [X].

The proposed loan repayment of UGX [X] per month represents [X]% of average
monthly net cash flow, leaving a comfortable surplus of UGX [X] per month.
This implies a DSCR of [X.Xx] based on actual operating cash flows.

The business has maintained a consistent balance in the [MTN Mobile Money /
Bank] account, with no instances of negative balance. [If applicable: The
business has successfully repaid a previous [SACCO / microfinance] loan of
UGX [X] over [period], as evidenced by the attached repayment history.]"
```

## 6. Transition Plan: Informal to Formal Records

For any informal business applying for credit, include a records formalisation plan:

```
RECORDS FORMALISATION PLAN

Current state: Management estimates from mobile money and bank records
Target state: Audited annual accounts from [Year]

Year 1 (immediately upon loan approval):
  - Engage ICPAU-registered accountant (monthly retainer UGX [X])
  - Open dedicated business bank account (separate from personal)
  - Install simple accounting software (QuickBooks, Wave, or Xero) — budget UGX [X]
  - Route ALL business transactions through bank account
  - Issue receipts for all sales (EFRIS-compliant if VAT-registered)

Year 2:
  - First formal management accounts prepared quarterly
  - URA income tax return filed for Year 1

Year 3:
  - First audited accounts for Year 2 prepared by ICPAU firm
  - Graduation to commercial bank lending terms
```

## 7. SACCO and Microfinance Pathway

For businesses that cannot qualify for commercial bank lending due to insufficient records or collateral, the SACCO and microfinance pathway provides a track record:

**SACCO pathway:**
1. Join a reputable SACCO relevant to sector (agri-SACCO, women's SACCO, boda-boda SACCO)
2. Make regular savings deposits for 6–12 months (minimum 3× the desired loan amount in accumulated savings)
3. Apply for SACCO loan: typically 2–3× savings balance
4. Repay SACCO loan on time — this creates a repayment track record
5. After 12–24 months of SACCO history, approach Centenary Bank or Equity Bank for individual loan using SACCO records as reference

**Microfinance pathway:**
- FINCA Uganda: group lending (groups of 10–30); individual loans after group track record
- Pride Microfinance: individual and group; maximum ~UGX 50M
- UGAFODE: cooperative-based; strong in western Uganda
- BRAC Uganda: group lending; focus on women

**The key:** A history of on-time microfinance repayments is highly persuasive evidence of creditworthiness, even without formal accounts.
