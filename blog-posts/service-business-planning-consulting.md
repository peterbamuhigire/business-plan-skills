# Business Planning Consulting Service

## The Complete Package: Plan. Deck. Delivery.

You have a business idea — or a growing business that needs capital, a new partner, or a strategic investor. You know what you want to achieve. What stands between you and that outcome is a document that speaks fluently to the people who hold the keys: bank credit officers, DFI programme managers, angel investors, and procurement managers.

A business plan is not a form to fill in. In the hands of a skilled preparer, it is the most powerful tool an entrepreneur owns.

We build it for you. Then we build the presentation deck that goes with it. Then we train you — personally — to stand in front of any room and defend it with confidence.

---

### What Is Included

#### 1. The Business Plan

A complete, professionally structured business plan covering all fifteen sections — from your company overview and market analysis to your five-year financial projections, risk register, and implementation timeline.

Every plan we deliver is:

**Bankable.** Structured around the criteria that commercial banks, Development Finance Institutions (DFIs), and impact investors use to evaluate funding applications. We apply the CAMPARI and DSCR frameworks used by Ugandan and East African lenders. Every number is internally consistent, every assumption is documented, and the debt service coverage ratio is calculated and prominently stated.

**Evidence-based.** Every market size claim is sourced. Every competitive advantage is substantiated — not asserted. Every management credential is accurate and verifiable. We apply a due diligence audit to every section before delivery, so you are not surprised when an investor's team investigates.

**Strategically sound.** Your business model, competitive positioning, and growth strategy are tested against consulting-grade frameworks — the same analytical tools used by McKinsey, Bain, and Deloitte. The market analysis is MECE. The financial model is stress-tested against four scenarios. The risk register has real mitigations with named owners and dates.

**Executable.** A plan that looks good on paper but cannot be carried out is worse than no plan — it creates false confidence. Every milestone in the implementation timeline maps to a specific action, a responsible person, a regulatory requirement, and a realistic lead time. The plan describes a business that can actually be run, not a business that sounds good in a document.

#### 2. The Pitch Deck

A professional slide presentation — designed to accompany and support the business plan in every high-stakes meeting.

Not a condensed version of the plan. A visual argument. Twelve to fifteen slides, each built around a single assertion, supported by one piece of visual evidence, designed to persuade the specific audience you are presenting to — whether that is a bank credit committee, an angel investor, a DFI programme officer, or a major client.

Every deck is built on a narrative structure that alternates between the current reality and the future your business creates — drawing the audience toward your ask before the ask is even made. Financial slides are headlined with conclusions, not labels. The request is specific, confident, and supported by everything that came before it.

The result is a deck that an investor could read in five minutes and understand exactly what you are asking for and why it makes sense — or that you could walk through in twenty minutes and leave no doubt in the room.

#### 3. Presentation and Pitch Training

The plan and the deck are only as powerful as the person presenting them.

We work with you personally over six structured sessions to ensure that when you walk into that bank meeting, investor presentation, or client pitch, you are not performing — you are commanding the room.

**What the training covers:**

- **Your narrative.** The single most important thing you need the audience to walk out of the room knowing. We find it, refine it, and make it second nature.
- **The pitch frame.** How to position yourself as the prize — not the supplicant. The psychological dynamics of a funding conversation, and how to manage them from the moment you enter the room.
- **Content mastery.** You will know every number on every slide cold. You will know where each figure comes from, how it was calculated, and how to defend it if challenged. There will be no surprises.
- **Delivery.** Voice pace, eye contact, pausing at the right moments, managing nerves — the physical and vocal skills that separate a confident presenter from a nervous one. Recorded, reviewed, and improved.
- **Q&A preparation.** The ten hardest questions your specific audience will ask — and the exact bridging technique to acknowledge the question, reframe it on your terms, and answer from a position of strength.
- **The close.** The exact words for the closing request. Practised until it feels natural, not rehearsed.

By the end of training, you will be able to present your business plan to any audience — including walking through the key points without the slides if the projector fails.

### Who This Service Is For

**Entrepreneurs applying for bank loans or DFI financing.** If you are applying to Centenary Bank, Stanbic, ABSA, dfcu, Equity Bank, the Uganda Development Bank, or any DFI, your application competes with dozens of others. A professionally prepared plan, properly structured for the lender's decision framework, significantly increases your probability of approval — and may determine the terms you receive.

**Business owners seeking equity investment.** If you are raising angel funding, impact investment, or a venture capital round, your business plan and pitch deck are the primary documents that determine whether you get to a term sheet. Investors receive hundreds of plans. Most are rejected in the first five pages. The difference between those that advance and those that don't is almost always the quality of the argument — not the quality of the business.

**Established businesses entering new markets or expanding.** An expansion that requires capital, a new joint venture, a government tender, or a strategic partnership almost always requires a formal plan. The rigour of the plan signals the rigour of the management team.

**Entrepreneurs who have been declined.** If you have applied for funding before and been declined — or received feedback that your plan was weak — we diagnose exactly what the lender found unconvincing and rebuild from there.

**Business plan consultants serving clients.** If you run a consulting practice and need a skills upgrade or a structured methodology for the plans you produce, this service can be adapted for professional development and licensing.

### Our Process

**Discovery (Week 1)**
We interview you, study your business, and gather everything we need: existing financial records, customer data, supplier relationships, market knowledge, and your goals. We ask hard questions — the same questions an investor or bank will ask — and identify every gap that needs to be filled before the plan is submitted.

**Research and Structuring (Weeks 1–2)**
We conduct independent market research, validate your financial assumptions against benchmarks, and build the analytical foundation of the plan. We do not fabricate numbers. We do not copy templates. Every figure in the plan has a source.

**Plan Development (Weeks 2–4)**
We write all fifteen sections, build the full financial model (five-year projections, monthly cash flow, DSCR calculation, break-even analysis, sensitivity tables), and produce the risk register and implementation timeline. You review each section and we incorporate your feedback.

**Deck Design (Week 4)**
We design the pitch deck, structuring the narrative to match the specific audience, designing each slide to the assertion-evidence standard, and ensuring every financial slide is headlined with a conclusion.

**Presentation Training (Weeks 4–6)**
Six sessions — story and frame, content mastery, delivery fundamentals, Q&A simulation, dress rehearsal, and day-of briefing. Sessions are conducted in person or by video call.

**Final Delivery**
You receive: the bound business plan document, the pitch deck file, a speaker notes version of the deck, your personalised Q&A preparation sheet, and a day-of checklist.

### What Makes This Different

Most business plans are written from templates. The sections are present, the language is professional, the numbers are there — but a trained eye sees immediately that the argument has not been made. The market data is top-down and unsourced. The competitive advantages are assertions. The financial projections have no assumptions page. The risk register is generic. The implementation timeline ignores regulatory lead times.

These plans do not get funded. They get polite rejections.

We build plans the way consultants build investment memoranda — from the evidence outward, not from the template inward. The market analysis is MECE: every segment is distinct and the segments together cover the whole market. The financial model is built on assumptions that can be defended, not just figures that look good. The risk register identifies risks that are real — and mitigation actions that can actually be taken.

And then we make sure you can present it.

Because a plan that lives in a folder achieves nothing. The plan that gets funded is the one where the entrepreneur walks into the room, holds the frame, states the ask with precision, handles the hard questions without flinching, and closes with confidence.

We build the plan. We build the deck. We build the presenter.
