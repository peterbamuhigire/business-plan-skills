## 1. Generational Buying Patterns (Kotler 6.0)

### Generation Overview

| Generation | Born | Key Traits | Discovery Channel | Purchase Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Baby Boomers | 1946-1964 | Brand loyal, quality-focused | TV, print, word-of-mouth | Trust, reputation, value |
| Gen X | 1965-1980 | Sceptical, research-driven | Search engines, email, reviews | Comparative analysis, deals |
| Millennials | 1981-1996 | Experience-seeking, social proof | Social media, influencers, blogs | Peer recommendations, values |
| Gen Z | 1997-2012 | Phygital native, pragmatic | TikTok, Instagram, YouTube | Authenticity, personalisation |
| Gen Alpha | 2013+ | AI-native, immersive-first | Voice, AR/VR, AI assistants | Gamification, interactive |

### Phygital Natives (Gen Z and Alpha)

Gen Z and Alpha are "phygital natives" who do not distinguish between physical and digital experiences:

- **Accelerated maturity** — Adopt adult purchasing behaviours earlier
- **Highly pragmatic** — Value function and authenticity over brand prestige
- **Self-expression** — Seek personalisation in every interaction
- **Digital-first discovery** — Find brands through social media algorithms, not advertising
- **Values-driven** — Prioritise sustainability, inclusivity, and ethical business practices
- **Low brand loyalty** — Switch easily if a better option appears

**Persona implication:** If targeting Gen Z/Alpha, every persona must include social media habits, values alignment, and personalisation expectations.

## 2. Digital Maturity Assessment

Rate the target customer base on digital maturity to calibrate channel strategy:

### Customer Digital Maturity Stages

| Stage | Behaviour | Marketing Approach |
|---|---|---|
| **Analogue** | Prefers in-person, phone, print | Traditional media, sales reps, events |
| **Digitising** | Uses email, basic web, some mobile | Email marketing, basic website, search |
| **Digital-first** | Online primary, mobile comfortable | Full digital marketing, e-commerce, apps |
| **AI-augmented** | Uses AI tools, expects personalisation | AI-powered recommendations, chatbots |
| **Immersive** | Expects AR/VR, phygital experiences | Immersive marketing, metaverse presence |

### Assessment Questions

For each target segment, answer:
- What % of customers have smartphones?
- What % shop online regularly?
- What % use social media for product discovery?
- What % are comfortable with AI-powered interactions?
- What % have used AR/VR for shopping or experiences?

Score each 0-100% and map to the maturity stage.

## 3. The AARRR Customer Acquisition Funnel

Growth marketing framework for mapping customer journey metrics:

```
ACQUISITION  → How do users find us?
ACTIVATION   → Do they have a great first experience?
RETENTION    → Do they come back?
REFERRAL     → Do they tell others?
REVENUE      → Can we monetise them?
```

### Metrics at Each Stage

| Stage | Key Metrics | Benchmark Range |
|---|---|---|
| **Acquisition** | Traffic sources, CAC by channel, signup rate | Varies by channel |
| **Activation** | Onboarding completion %, time to first value | 20-60% activation |
| **Retention** | Day 1/7/30 retention, churn rate, NRR | D30: 10-30% typical |
| **Referral** | Referral rate, viral coefficient, NPS | NPS > 50 = strong |
| **Revenue** | ARPU, CLV, conversion rate, expansion revenue | CLV:CAC > 3:1 |

### Persona-to-Funnel Mapping

For each persona, document:
- **Acquisition:** Where does this persona first encounter us?
- **Activation:** What is their "aha moment" — when do they first experience value?
- **Retention:** What brings them back? What would make them leave?
- **Referral:** Are they likely to refer? Through what channel?
- **Revenue:** What is their expected spend pattern over time?

## 4. Education-Based Marketing Triggers

### The Education-Based Buying Process

Customers who are educated about a problem buy more confidently and pay more:

1. **Problem awareness** — Customer recognises the problem exists
2. **Solution education** — Customer learns about available solutions
3. **Differentiation** — Customer understands why one solution is better
4. **Risk reduction** — Customer's objections are addressed with proof
5. **Purchase confidence** — Customer feels informed enough to buy

### Purchase Trigger Events

Common events that shift a prospect from "considering" to "buying":

- **Pain amplification** — Problem becomes urgent (deadline, cost increase, competitor threat)
- **Budget availability** — New fiscal year, funding round, bonus season
- **Peer adoption** — Seeing competitors or peers adopt the solution
- **Regulatory change** — New compliance requirement forces action
- **Life event** — Personal or business milestone (expansion, hiring, relocation)
- **Free trial expiry** — Time-limited access creates urgency
- **Price increase announcement** — Fear of paying more later

### Objection Categories

For each persona, map objections to one of four categories:

| Category | Example Objections | Resolution Approach |
|---|---|---|
| **Price** | "Too expensive," "Can't afford it" | ROI demonstration, payment plans, value comparison |
| **Trust** | "Never heard of you," "Too new" | Social proof, guarantees, case studies, free trial |
| **Timing** | "Not now," "Maybe next quarter" | Urgency triggers, cost of delay, limited offers |
| **Fit** | "Not for us," "Too complex" | Customisation, onboarding support, similar customer stories |

## 5. B2B Buyer Behaviour

### The B2B Buying Committee

B2B purchases typically involve multiple stakeholders:

| Role | Concern | Content Needed |
|---|---|---|
| **Champion** | "Will this make me look good?" | Case studies, ROI data |
| **Decision maker** | "Is this worth the investment?" | Executive summary, financial impact |
| **Technical evaluator** | "Does it actually work?" | Technical specs, demos, integration docs |
| **End user** | "Will this make my job easier?" | Product tours, training materials |
| **Procurement** | "Is this the best deal?" | Pricing comparison, contract terms |
| **Legal/Compliance** | "Is this safe and compliant?" | Security docs, compliance certifications |

### B2B Purchase Timeline

| Deal Size | Typical Sales Cycle | Touchpoints Needed |
|---|---|---|
| < $5,000 | 1-4 weeks | 3-5 touchpoints |
| $5,000-$50,000 | 1-3 months | 7-12 touchpoints |
| $50,000-$500,000 | 3-9 months | 15-25 touchpoints |
| > $500,000 | 6-18 months | 25+ touchpoints |

Map persona buying timelines to these benchmarks when estimating CAC and conversion rates.
