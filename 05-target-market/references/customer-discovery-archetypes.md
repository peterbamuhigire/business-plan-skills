## Customer Archetype Framework

A customer archetype goes beyond a basic persona to include behavioural detail that drives marketing and sales strategy (Blank & Dorf, 2012).

## Problem Recognition Scale for Segmentation

Segment target customers by pain level (Blank & Dorf, 2012):

| Level | State | Segment Value | Validation Approach |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Latent** | Have the problem, don't know it | Low (must educate first) | Awareness campaigns, demonstrations |
| **Passive** | Know the problem, not motivated | Medium (need trigger event) | Quantify cost of inaction |
| **Active** | Searching for a solution | High (ready to evaluate) | Present solution, test willingness to pay |
| **Vision** | Built a workaround, will pay for better | Highest (earlyvangelists) | Show superiority over their workaround |

**For business plans:** Focus initial targeting on Active and Vision-level customers. They convert fastest and validate the business model. Expand to Passive and Latent as the business scales.

## Six B2B Customer Types

In business-to-business sales, identify all six types within the buying organisation (Blank & Dorf, 2012):

| Type | Role | What They Care About | How to Engage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **End Users** | Will actually use the product | Ease of use, time savings, daily workflow | Demonstrate hands-on |
| **Influencers** | Shape the decision through expertise | Technical merit, compatibility, risk | Technical presentations, pilots |
| **Recommenders** | Formally evaluate and recommend | Compliance, standards, best practices | Reference cases, certifications |
| **Economic Buyers** | Control the budget | ROI, total cost, payback period | Financial justification, business case |
| **Decision-Makers** | Have final authority | Strategic alignment, risk, reputation | Executive summary, vision alignment |
| **Saboteurs** | Actively or passively resist | Threat to their role, change aversion | Identify early, neutralise concerns |

**For business plans:** Map which customer types exist in the target organisation. Show the sales strategy addresses each type, especially Saboteurs.

## Three-Tier Stakeholder Map

Three concentric rings for identifying who matters beyond the direct customer (Alam):

### Ring 1: Target Stakeholders (1-3 primary)
- Who directly experiences the problem?
- Who will use the product/service?
- Who pays? (May differ from the user)

### Ring 2: Connected Stakeholders
- Who directly influences the purchase decision? (family, advisors, supervisors)
- Who implements or delivers the solution? (partners, suppliers, staff)
- Who gatekeeps access? (regulators, distributors, landlords)

### Ring 3: Influenced Stakeholders
- Who is indirectly affected? (community, neighbours, industry peers)
- Who might advocate or oppose? (activists, media, traditional leaders)
- Who regulates the space? (URA, KCCA, UNBS, NEMA)

## Empathy-Based Interview Methodology

### 8-Category Interview Guide (Alam)

1. **Introduction** — build rapport, explain purpose ("I'm researching how people handle [X]. I'm not selling anything.")
2. **Jobs to Be Done** — "What are you trying to accomplish when you [do X]?"
3. **Customers** — "Who else is involved in this process? Who decides?"
4. **Challenges** — "What makes [X] difficult? What goes wrong?"
5. **Aspirations** — "What would the ideal [X] look like for you?"
6. **Stories** — "Tell me about the last time you [experienced the problem]."
7. **Emotions** — "How did that make you feel? What was most frustrating?"
8. **Conclusion** — "Anything else I should know? Who else should I talk to?"

### Key Interview Rules

- Ask "why?" at least 3 times to dig beneath surface answers
- Look for inconsistencies between what people say and what they do
- Never suggest solutions during the interview
- Embrace silence — let interviewees think
- Don't interview only friends and family (they tell you what you want to hear)
- Use the "George technique" — attribute the idea to a fictitious colleague to get honest feedback (Cooper & Vlaskovits, 2010)

### Empathy Map Synthesis

After interviews, synthesise using three columns (Alam):

| Observations | Interpretations | Insights |
|---|---|---|
| What they're doing and saying | What they might be thinking/feeling | What they need (unmet needs) |
| Their environment and context | Hidden motivations | Challenges to address |

Colour-code by interviewee. Look for patterns across 5-8 interviews.

## Persona Template

Create 2-3 distinct personas representing key customer segments (Alam):

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Realistic fictional name |
| **Demographics** | Age, gender, education, location, income |
| **Goals & Motivation** | What drives them; what success looks like |
| **Challenges** | What frustrates them; barriers they face |
| **Primary Responsibilities** | Daily role and duties |
| **Needs** | What they require from a solution |
| **Current Workaround** | How they handle the problem today |
| **Decision Triggers** | What would make them switch/buy |
| **Tagline** | One-sentence essence of this persona |

## Current Journey Map

Map the customer's current experience step by step (Alam):

| Row | Content |
|---|---|
| **Steps** | Sequential actions the customer takes to accomplish their goal |
| **Touch Points** | Systems, resources, people involved at each step |
| **Emotions** | How the customer feels (draw an emotional line across steps) |
| **Pain Points** | Mark with red dots where frustration/failure occurs |
| **Moments of Truth** | Mark with green dots where the customer is emotionally open to a new solution |

**For business plans:** Include a simplified journey map in section 05. Show where your product/service intervenes at the pain points and moments of truth.
