# TikTok Advertising: Strategy, Content, and Campaign Frameworks

**Source:** Marshall, P. and Yu, D. — The Definitive Guide to TikTok Advertising: How to Access 1 Billion People in 10 Minutes (Perry Marshall & Associates, 2022) | **Application:** TikTok ad strategy, video content creation, campaign structure, targeting, metrics, early-mover advantage

> TikTok is not a social media hobby — it is the world's most powerful content distribution algorithm applied to short-form video. For businesses, the question is not whether to like TikTok, but whether TikTok will give you more customers. If the answer is yes, that is reason enough to invest.

## Why TikTok Now: The Early-Mover Advantage

Marshall's central argument is historical: Google Ads (2002–2007) and Facebook Ads (2011–2016) each created a generation of businesses that were built on the early-mover advantage of a new medium — cheap traffic before the world caught on. TikTok is that medium now.

**The predictable cycle of a new ad platform:**
1. Wild west phase — unfamiliar, underpriced, high variance (TikTok: 2020–2024)
2. Platform matures — advertisers learn the rules, CPMs rise, ROI normalises
3. Established players dominate — late entrants face full-price competition

**EA application:** TikTok adoption in Uganda and East Africa is accelerating among the 18–35 demographic. Businesses that build TikTok competency in 2025–2026 gain the compounding advantage of audience familiarity, content libraries, and algorithmic learning before the platform becomes crowded.

**Critical distinction (Marshall and Yu):** This is not about becoming an influencer. TikTok advertising requires 30 minutes of content creation per week, not 6 hours per day. The model is: advertiser spending $20/day to acquire customers — not influencer seeking viral fame.

## The Power Triangle: Traffic, Conversion, Economics

Every marketing channel — TikTok, Facebook, Google, WhatsApp — operates on the same three-element model (Marshall, citing Jack Born):

```
        TRAFFIC
       /        \
      /  80/20   \
ECONOMICS ——— CONVERSION
```

- **Traffic:** People who see your content or ad
- **Conversion:** Your content or landing page convincing them to act
- **Economics:** The exchange of value — customer pays, business profits

**80/20 applied to the Power Triangle:**
- 20% of your content drives 80% of your results — identify and amplify winners
- 4% of your customers (20% of 20%) will spend 16× the average — VIP economics
- Design backwards: define the economics first (what must a customer be worth?), then reverse-engineer conversion and traffic requirements

**The "Make $1 Model" (Marshall):** Before scaling, achieve break-even on customer acquisition. Spend $10, make $11. The goal is not profit on the first sale — it is proving the machine works. Once COCA ≤ first sale value, scale aggressively.

**Magic Gumball Machine Question:** "If you had a machine where every time you put money in, a new customer came out — how much would you put in?" The answer is constrained only by TLV (Total Lifetime Value). A business with high TLV can spend more per customer acquisition than competitors, making it nearly impossible to compete with.

## The 80/20 Content Strategy

**Core principle:** Repurpose what is already working on other platforms. Do not create new content for TikTok first — extract winners from Facebook, YouTube, and WhatsApp and reformat for TikTok.

**The 80/20 content workflow:**
1. Identify the best-performing content from the last 30 and 90 days across all platforms (highest engagement, most leads, most shares)
2. Cut those pieces to 15–23 seconds for TikTok
3. Run as TikTok In-Feed ads at $10–20/day
4. Test until 1 in 10 videos wins; pause the rest; scale the winners

**The Content Factory (Yu):** A systematic approach to content abundance through repurposing:
- One long interview or demo video → 5 TikTok clips + YouTube short + blog excerpt + WhatsApp tip
- One customer testimonial → TikTok testimonial + Facebook post + Google review request
- One behind-the-scenes walkthrough → series of 15-second daily clips

**EA Content Factory application:** For a restaurant, one 10-minute kitchen tour video yields: 4 TikTok clips of food preparation, 2 clips of staff and ambiance, 1 "how we make X" educational, 1 customer reaction — 8 pieces of content from one filming session.

## Why/How/What Video Framework

The highest-converting TikTok content structure for business (Yu):

| Video Type | Purpose | Content Examples | Funnel Stage |
|------------|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Why** | Awareness — create emotional connection | Testimonials, your origin story, the problem you solve, your mission | Top of funnel |
| **How** | Consideration — provide value, build trust | Tips, techniques, how-to tutorials, demonstrations, interviews | Middle of funnel |
| **What** | Conversion — present your offer | Product showcase, pricing, call-to-action, what makes you unique | Bottom of funnel |

**Practical application:** Maintain a 3×3 grid — at least 3 Why, 3 How, and 3 What videos running simultaneously. Replace the lowest-performing video in each row with a new test. This creates a self-renewing content library that never goes stale.

**Sequencing principle:** "You don't propose on the first date." Show Why videos to cold audiences, How videos to warm audiences, and What videos to hot audiences (retargeting).

## 15-Second Video Structure

The anatomy of a high-converting 15–23 second TikTok ad (Yu):

```
Seconds 0–5:   HOOK — Stop the scroll; on-screen text + voiceover
Seconds 0–5:   PAIN/PLEASURE — Identify with the problem or opportunity
Seconds 5–10:  SOLUTION — What you offer; product or service description
Seconds 10–15: CALL TO ACTION — One clear instruction: "Message us / Link in bio / Visit X"
```

**Hook formulas that work:**
- "One reason why [target audience] [struggle/fail/succeed]..."
- "5 things that [target audience] need to know about..."
- "Ever wonder how [desirable outcome]..."
- "When I was [relatable story opening]..."
- "How to [specific result] without [common obstacle]..."

**The XYZ template (Yu):**
> "Improve your [outcome] with these three [tips/steps/secrets]. Tip 1 / Tip 2 / Tip 3. For a personalised [service/consultation/offer], [call to action]."

**Video production standards (Yu):**
- Smartphone purchased within the last 3 years is sufficient
- Film vertically (9:16 portrait) always for TikTok/Reels/Stories
- Natural light preferred; ring light for indoor shooting
- Sound quality matters more than video quality — use a clip-on microphone
- Film in 4K where possible; add text overlays and captions
- Closed captions ON — essential for silent-scroll viewers
- Movement (walking, changing scenes, punch-ins) sustains attention better than static shots

**"Flawsome" principle (Yu):** Raw, authentic videos outperform polished productions on TikTok. A phone-recorded customer testimonial walking and talking converts better than a studio-shot testimonial. Authenticity is the signal of trust on TikTok.

## TikTok Ad Formats

| Ad Format | Description | Best Use | Minimum Spend |
|-----------|-------------|---------|--------------|
| **In-Feed Ads** | Appear in the "For You" feed between organic videos; most like a native post | Awareness, traffic, leads, conversions for all businesses | $20/day at ad group level |
| **Spark Ads** | Boosts an existing organic TikTok post (yours or a creator's) as a paid ad | Amplifying high-performing organic content; influencer collaborations | Same as In-Feed |
| **TopView** | Full-screen video shown at app open; premium placement | Large brand awareness campaigns; not for SMEs | High minimum |
| **Branded Hashtag Challenges** | Invites users to participate in a branded challenge | UGC generation; mass brand awareness | High minimum |

**For EA SMEs: use only In-Feed Ads and Spark Ads.** These require the lowest budget, are the most authentic, and give full creative control.

**Spark Ads advantage (Yu):** When you run a Spark Ad on an organic post, paid and organic engagement metrics are combined on that single video — creating the appearance of massive social proof. A video with 50 organic likes + 500 paid engagements shows 550 total, not separated. This dramatically increases perceived credibility.

## TikTok Metrics: What to Measure

| Funnel Stage | Key Metric | Definition | Target Benchmark |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Awareness** | CPM (Cost per 1,000 impressions) | Cost to reach 1,000 users | $2–10 depending on targeting |
| **Awareness** | Video View Rate | Impressions ÷ video views | 50%+ is good on TikTok |
| **Engagement** | Engagement Rate | (Likes + comments + shares) ÷ impressions | 10%+ is solid; 35%+ is exceptional |
| **Consideration** | CPV (Cost per view) | Total spend ÷ video views | Varies; lower is better |
| **Consideration** | CPC (Cost per click) | Total spend ÷ clicks | Benchmark against Facebook CPC |
| **Conversion** | CPA (Cost per Acquisition) | Total spend ÷ conversions | Must be ≤ 30% of first sale value |
| **Conversion** | ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) | Revenue ÷ ad spend | Target: 3× minimum |

**Video completion rate is more important than engagement rate (Yu).** TikTok's algorithm rewards videos that people watch to the end. Short videos (15 seconds) achieve higher completion rates than long videos. Never pad video length.

**Remarketing has the highest ROI.** Always start campaign spend on retargeting (website visitors, email list, social engagers) before cold audiences. Only expand to cold audiences using Lookalike Audiences built from your customer list.

## Campaign Structure

TikTok Ads Manager mirrors Facebook's three-level structure:

```
CAMPAIGN (objective + total budget)
  └── AD GROUP (targeting + placement + daily budget: $20 minimum)
        └── ADS (individual video creatives + copy + CTA)
```

**Campaign objectives used most (Yu):**
- **Video Views** — maximise content watch time; top-of-funnel brand building
- **Traffic** — drive clicks to website or WhatsApp link
- **Conversions** — optimise for a specific action (purchase, form submission, WhatsApp message)
- **Lead Generation** — native TikTok lead form (no website required)

**Budget discipline:**
- Set campaign budget to "no limit" (control spend at ad group level)
- Run ad groups for one week at a time; do not leave campaigns open-ended without review
- Minimum: $20/day per ad group; plan for $140/week per active ad group

**Targeting options (TikTok has fewer than Facebook; use broadly):**
- Demographics: age, gender, location, language
- Interests and behaviours: categories TikTok assigns based on viewing history
- Custom Audiences: website visitors (TikTok Pixel), customer email list upload
- Lookalike Audiences: built from your best customers — highest-quality cold audience

**Algorithm-first targeting:** TikTok's algorithm is powerful enough that broad targeting often outperforms narrow interest targeting. Use broad demographics + strong creative, and let the algorithm find the converting audience. This is the same principle as Meta Advantage+.

## TikTok vs. Other Channels: When to Use Each

| Scenario | Recommended Platform | Reason |
|---------|---------------------|--------|
| Established business with proven offer | TikTok + Facebook | Repurpose winning content; diversify traffic |
| New business, unproven offer | Facebook first | Cheaper testing; faster feedback loop |
| B2C, young audience (18–35) | TikTok primary | Highest concentration of target audience |
| B2B, professional services | LinkedIn + Facebook | TikTok has lower B2B intent; higher CPL for B2B |
| Local geographic targeting | TikTok + Facebook | Both have strong geo-targeting; TikTok cheaper per impression |
| Immediate lead generation | Facebook Ads/WhatsApp | More established conversion infrastructure |
| Brand building over 6–12 months | TikTok organic + Spark Ads | Compounding audience and algorithm learning |

**Key caution (Yu):** TikTok works best as an amplifier of an existing, proven funnel. It is not for testing new offers or unproven products. Start with Facebook, prove the funnel, then add TikTok to diversify and scale.

## EA-Specific Application Notes (2026)

**Platform penetration:** TikTok is the fastest-growing platform in the 18–30 age group across Uganda, Kenya, Tanzania, and Rwanda. Facebook remains dominant for 30+ demographics. Both platforms are mobile-first, which aligns with EA's smartphone-dominant internet access.

**Content localisation:**
- Film in your local language or a combination of English + local language for broader reach
- Local landmarks, markets, and familiar settings increase relatability
- Customer testimonials from recognisable community members are the highest-trust content type

**EA video production setup (approximate costs):**
- Smartphone (Tecno Camon / Samsung A series / iPhone SE): existing device
- Ring light from local electronics shop or Jumia: UGX 80,000–150,000
- Clip-on microphone: UGX 50,000–100,000
- CapCut (free) for editing with captions
- Total setup investment: UGX 130,000–250,000

**Budget starting point for EA TikTok ads:**
- Minimum viable: UGX 75,000/day ($20) per ad group
- Recommended starting budget: UGX 375,000/week ($100) — one ad group, 5 days
- Test for 2 weeks before scaling; expect 2–3 weeks for algorithm to optimise
