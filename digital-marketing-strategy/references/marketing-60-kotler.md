# Marketing 6.0: The Future Is Immersive

**Source:** Kotler, P., Kartajaya, H., and Setiawan, I. — Marketing 6.0: The Future Is Immersive (Wiley, 2023) | **Application:** Immersive marketing strategy, phygital customer experience, next-generation digital channels, generational marketing

> Marketing 6.0, or metamarketing, is the convergence of physical and digital customer experience into a seamless, immersive whole. It does not replace digital marketing — it evolves it. The question is no longer "digital vs. physical" but "how do we make the two indistinguishable to the customer?"

## The Marketing Evolution: From 1.0 to 6.0

| Era | Name | Focus | Key Technology | EA Relevance |
|-----|------|--------|---------------|-------------|
| Marketing 1.0 | Product-driven | Features and specifications | Mass media | Historical baseline |
| Marketing 2.0 | Customer-oriented | Customer satisfaction, CRM | Internet | CRM tools (WhatsApp, etc.) |
| Marketing 3.0 | Human-centric | Values, mission, sustainability | Social media | SDG alignment, community trust |
| Marketing 4.0 | Digital | Content marketing + omnichannel | Mobile + AI | Content + WhatsApp omnichannel |
| Marketing 5.0 | Technology for humanity | AI-powered personalisation | AI, big data | ChatGPT, Meta Advantage+ |
| Marketing 6.0 | Metamarketing (immersive) | Phygital convergence | IoT, AR/VR, Spatial, Blockchain | Practical from 2026+ for EA |

**Business plan implication:** Most EA businesses in 2026 are executing Marketing 4.0 (content + omnichannel). Marketing 5.0 (AI-assisted) is immediately accessible. Marketing 6.0 elements (AR try-on, metaverse) are early-adopter territory for 2026–2030 planning.

## Metamarketing: The Core Concept

**Metamarketing** = marketing that transcends the boundaries between physical and digital, creating an immersive customer experience where the distinction between the two disappears.

**Progression:**
- **Multichannel:** Multiple separate channels for different customer types (online vs. offline)
- **Omnichannel:** Integrated channels creating a seamless journey (research online → buy in-store)
- **Metamarketing:** Physical and digital fully merged — digital enhances physical, physical authenticates digital

**EA metamarketing examples (2026):**
- Customer sees product on TikTok → scans QR code in-store for more info → pays via MTN MoMo → shares unboxing on WhatsApp Status (full phygital loop)
- Restaurant advertises on Facebook → customer walks in and scans QR menu → reviews on Google → generates owned media (photos on Instagram)
- Salon uses Instagram to showcase portfolio → client visits → WhatsApp follow-up with care tips → client shares referral → repeat cycle

## The Building Blocks of Marketing 6.0

Three layers enable metamarketing:

**Layer 1 — Enablers (Technology Foundation):**
Five technologies power Marketing 6.0:
1. **IoT (Internet of Things)** — sensors capturing real-time customer data in physical spaces (e.g. in-store movement tracking, smart fitting rooms)
2. **Artificial Intelligence (AI)** — replicates human cognitive skills; enables one-to-one personalisation at scale from IoT data streams
3. **Spatial Computing** — digital interactions in physical space (smart fitting rooms, interactive displays, gesture-controlled interfaces)
4. **AR and VR** — Augmented Reality overlays digital onto physical (virtual try-on, furniture placement); Virtual Reality creates fully immersive digital environments (virtual showrooms, training simulations)
5. **Blockchain** — decentralised internet ownership; powers community-governed metaverses and NFT-based brand loyalty programmes

**EA application (2026):** AI and AR are immediately accessible (available on smartphones). IoT, Spatial Computing, and Blockchain require significant infrastructure investment — plan for 3–5 year horizon.

**Layer 2 — Environments:**
- **Extended Realities (XR):** Digitally augmented physical spaces — QR codes, AR overlays, interactive in-store displays, shoppable pop-ups
- **Metaverses:** Virtual worlds where avatars interact, commerce occurs, and experiences replace physical visits — primarily relevant for Gen Z and Gen Alpha entertainment, gaming, and socialising

**Layer 3 — Experiences:**
- **Multisensory marketing:** Engaging all five senses (sight, sound, touch, smell, taste) — retail ambiance, music playlists, signature scents, tactile packaging
- **Spatial marketing:** Digital content that responds to physical movement — proximity-triggered notifications, location-based offers, in-store AR displays
- **Metaverse marketing:** Brand activations in virtual worlds — virtual product launches, digital collectibles, avatar-brand interactions

## Phygital Natives: The New Customer Baseline

**Generation Z (born mid-1990s to early 2010s) and Generation Alpha (born after 2010)** are phygital natives — they see no distinction between physical and digital because they have never experienced a world where the two were separate.

**Key characteristics of phygital natives (implications for EA marketing):**

| Characteristic | What It Means | EA Marketing Implication |
|---------------|--------------|--------------------------|
| **Pragmatic** | Value-focused, research-driven, price-conscious | Show value clearly; include price; avoid hype |
| **Authenticity-seeking** | Reject staged perfection; prefer raw, real content | Behind-the-scenes, unfiltered UGC, founder-led content |
| **Short-form video preference** | TikTok/Reels native; selective attention for non-engaging content | Lead with video; first 3 seconds must hook |
| **AI-native** | Comfortable with personalisation, chatbots, AI recommendations | WhatsApp bot, personalised broadcast lists, AI content |
| **Community-oriented** | Trust niche communities over mass media | WhatsApp groups, Facebook Groups, Discord — peer referral |
| **Experiential over material** | Spend on experiences (events, gaming, travel) over possessions | Market the experience of using your product, not just the product |
| **Sustainability-aware** | Support brands aligned with values (environment, DEI) | Highlight ethical sourcing, community impact, local employment |

**KGOY (Kids Getting Older Younger):** Younger generations adopt adult purchasing behaviours earlier. Gen Z entering the workforce are financially pragmatic, digitally capable, and experience-oriented. Marketing to them in 2026 must treat them as sophisticated adults, not teenagers.

## Five Micro-Trends Leading to Metamarketing

Kotler et al. identify five micro-trends within Marketing 6.0:

**1. Short-Form Video Content**
- Micro-moments (Google/BCG): brief, intent-driven moments when customers pull out phones to learn, discover, or buy
- Short-form video (TikTok, Reels, YouTube Shorts) is optimal for micro-moments — captures attention during idle time, commutes, queues
- Shoppable content (video with embedded purchase links) converts immediately at the moment of discovery
- **EA application:** WhatsApp Status videos, TikTok product demos, Facebook Reels. 60–90 second format. Always include a visible call-to-action.

**2. Community-Based Social Media**
- Shift from mass feed-based platforms (Facebook, Instagram) to niche community platforms (Reddit, Discord, WhatsApp Groups, Telegram)
- Users trust community recommendations more than brand posts
- Community engagement drives higher conversion than broad advertising
- **EA application:** Join and contribute to relevant WhatsApp groups and Facebook Groups. Create a branded customer WhatsApp community. Engage in LinkedIn groups for B2B. The referral from a trusted community member converts better than any paid ad.

**3. Conversational Commerce** (inferred from the book's themes)
- Chat-based purchasing: customers discover, enquire, negotiate, and buy through messaging apps
- WhatsApp Business catalogues + mobile money = complete conversational commerce loop in EA
- AI-powered chat automation handles initial enquiries; human agents close complex deals
- **EA application:** Set up WhatsApp Business with product catalogue, auto-reply, and broadcast list. This is the highest-ROI owned channel in EA.

**4. Social Commerce / Shoppable Content**
- E-commerce embedded within social media (Instagram Shopping, TikTok Shop, Facebook Marketplace)
- Reduces friction: customer discovers, clicks, and buys without leaving the app
- **EA application:** Facebook Marketplace is widely used in EA. Instagram Shopping requires a website. TikTok Shop is emerging. For now, "link in bio" + WhatsApp conversion is the practical shoppable workflow for EA SMEs.

**5. AI-Driven Personalisation**
- AI algorithms (Meta, Google, TikTok) already deliver hyper-personalised content without the marketer needing to build the personalisation engine
- Using broad targeting + strong creative allows algorithms to find the right audience
- **EA application:** Use Meta Advantage+ campaign settings. Allow the algorithm to optimise rather than over-targeting manually. The algorithm learns from your existing customer data.

## Multichannel → Omnichannel → Metamarketing Roadmap

For EA business plan purposes, map the journey in three horizons:

**Horizon 1 (Year 1–2): Omnichannel Foundation**
- Consistent presence across online (Facebook, WhatsApp, Google) and offline (physical signage, events)
- Unified brand voice and value proposition across all touchpoints
- Mobile money as the universal payment bridge between digital and physical

**Horizon 2 (Year 2–4): Phygital Integration**
- QR codes linking physical products/spaces to digital content (menus, product info, tutorials)
- Location-based WhatsApp notifications for walk-in customers
- In-store digital displays and shoppable social media content
- AI chatbot handling first-line customer service 24/7

**Horizon 3 (Year 4+): Metamarketing Experiments**
- AR try-before-you-buy (fashion, furniture, cosmetics)
- Virtual showroom/tour (real estate, hospitality, automotive)
- Digital loyalty NFTs / blockchain-based community membership
- Metaverse presence for Gen Z/Alpha engagement

## Sustainability as Marketing Strategy

Marketing 6.0 emphasises sustainability as a non-negotiable element of brand positioning for Gen Z and Gen Alpha audiences. UN SDG alignment is not just ethical — it is commercially strategic.

**EA business applications:**
- Highlight local sourcing and supply chain transparency
- Community employment: how many local jobs does the business create?
- Environmental practices: waste management, energy, packaging
- Women's empowerment (high resonance in EA development context)
- Partner with local NGOs or community programmes

**Caution:** Authenticity over greenwashing. Gen Z detects performative sustainability claims. Show proof, not just claims.
