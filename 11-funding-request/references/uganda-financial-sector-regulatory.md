# Uganda Financial Sector Regulatory Framework

## 1. Sector Overview

Uganda's financial sector is divided into four regulatory domains. Every entity that lends money, accepts deposits, or deals in securities must hold a licence from the appropriate regulator.

| Tier | Institution Types | Regulator | Primary Law |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier I | Commercial Banks (incl. Islamic banking) | Bank of Uganda | Financial Institutions Act, 2004 (as amended 2016, 2023) |
| Tier II | Credit Institutions | Bank of Uganda | Financial Institutions Act, 2004 |
| Tier III | Microfinance Deposit-Taking Institutions (MDIs) | Bank of Uganda | Microfinance Deposit Taking Institutions Act, 2003 |
| Tier IV | Non-Deposit-Taking MFIs, SACCOs, Self-Help Groups, Money Lenders | Uganda Microfinance Regulatory Authority (UMRA) | Tier 4 Microfinance Institutions and Money Lenders Act, 2016 |
| Capital Markets | USE, Brokers, Fund Managers, CIS, VCs | Capital Markets Authority (CMA) | Capital Markets Authority Act, Cap 84 |
| Other | Insurance, Hire Purchase, Real Estate Finance, DFIs | IRA, Commissioner (Internal Trade), Ministry of Lands, UDB Act | Various |

## 2. Bank of Uganda — Regulated Institutions (Tiers I–III)

### 2.1 Tier I: Commercial Banks

A commercial bank is a company licensed to accept call, demand, savings and time deposits; provide overdrafts and short-to-medium term loans; trade foreign exchange; participate in interbank clearing; and issue guarantees.

**Licensing requirements:**
- Constituted as a company limited by shares
- Submit application with details of shareholders, directors, business plan, risk management policies
- Minimum paid-up capital: **UGX 120 billion** (by 31 December 2022)

**Current landscape:** 26 licensed commercial banks as at September 2023. Includes Stanbic Bank, DFCU Bank, Centenary Bank, and Salaam Bank Uganda (first Islamic banking licence, issued 8 September 2023).

**Authorised activities:** Checking/savings/time accounts (local and foreign currency); loans; foreign exchange; letters of credit; guarantees.

### 2.2 Tier II: Credit Institutions

Credit institutions accept call and time deposits (not demand/cheque accounts) and deploy those deposits by lending.

**Licensing requirements:**
- Private company limited by shares or guarantee
- Minimum paid-up capital: **UGX 20 billion** (by 31 December 2022)

**Current landscape:** 4 licensed credit institutions (as at 30 June 2022): BRAC Uganda Bank, Mercantile Credit Bank, Yako Bank, Top Finance Bank.

**Not authorised to:** offer cheque accounts; trade in foreign currency.

### 2.3 Tier III: Microfinance Deposit-Taking Institutions (MDIs)

MDIs provide microfinance business: small loans to micro-enterprises and low-income households, using collateral substitutes (group guarantees, compulsory savings). Islamic microfinance is permitted.

**Licensing requirements:**
- Company limited by shares or guarantee
- Minimum paid-up capital: **UGX 20 billion** (by 31 December 2022)

**Current landscape (as at 19 January 2024):** FINCA Uganda Limited (MDI), PRIDE Microfinance Limited (MDI), UGAFODE Microfinance Limited (MDI).

**Not authorised to:** offer cheque accounts; trade in foreign currency.

### 2.4 Capital Requirements Summary

| Tier | Minimum Capital (UGX) | Structure |
|---|---|---|
| I — Commercial Banks | 120,000,000,000 | Company limited by shares |
| II — Credit Institutions | 20,000,000,000 | Private company (shares or guarantee) |
| III — MDIs | 20,000,000,000 | Private company (shares or guarantee) |

### 2.5 Key Legislation (BOU-Supervised Institutions)

**Primary:**
- Financial Institutions Act, 2004
- Financial Institutions (Amendment) Act, 2016
- Financial Institutions (Amendment) Act, 2023
- Microfinance Deposit Taking Institutions Act, 2003
- Microfinance Deposit Taking Institutions Act (Amendment) Act, 2023

**Secondary (selected):**

- Financial Institutions (Liquidity) Regulations, 2023

## 3. Uganda Microfinance Regulatory Authority (UMRA) — Tier IV

UMRA was established by the Tier 4 Microfinance Institutions and Money Lenders Act, 2016 as an autonomous government body. Its mandate is to promote a sound and sustainable non-banking financial sector, enhance financial inclusion, and protect lower-income consumers.

### 3.1 Tier IV Institution Types

| Type | Description | Licence from |
|---|---|---|
| Non-Deposit Taking MFI (NDTMI) | Company or NGO; provides micro loans only | UMRA |
| SACCO | Savings and Credit Cooperative; pools member savings and lends to members | UMRA |
| Self-Help Group | Time-bound; mobilises own savings; lends to members; shares equity annually | District CDO |
| Commodity-Based MFI | Provides goods/services (not cash) for income generation | UMRA |
| Money Lender | Company lending own funds (not deposits) at agreed rates | UMRA |

### 3.2 Non-Deposit Taking MFIs (NDTMIs)

**Micro loan limits** (statutory definition):
- Individual borrower: ≤ 1% of the institution's core capital
- Group borrower: ≤ 5% of total core capital

**Licensing requirements:**
- Constituted as a company or NGO
- Certificate of incorporation (URSB) or NGO registration certificate
- MEMARTS or NGO Constitution
- Management structure
- Application fee: UGX 50,000
- Annual renewal: UGX 500,000

**No minimum capital** has been prescribed by UMRA (as at 2024), though UMRA has the mandate to do so.

### 3.3 SACCOs

SACCOs are voluntary cooperative societies where members pool savings and access credit.

**Licensing requirements** (SACCO Regulations, 2020):
- Certificate of registration under Cooperative Societies Act
- Minimum equity: not less than **10% of total assets**
- Evidence of membership and shareholding
- Business plan and credit policy
- Organisational structure

**SACCO minimum equity:** 10% of total assets (statutory).

**Stability mechanisms:**
- SACCO Stabilisation Fund (established under the Act)
- SACCO Savings Protection Fund (protects member savings in event of failure)
- Central Financing Facility (SACCO-to-SACCO liquidity)

### 3.4 Money Lenders

Money lenders are companies that lend their own funds (not deposits). No restriction on loan size or borrower type. No maximum interest rate has been prescribed by UMRA or the Ministry of Finance (as at 2024).

**Licensing requirements:**
- Must be constituted as a **company limited by shares** (even single-person lenders)
- Certificate of incorporation
- Director/secretary particulars and National IDs
- Application fee: UGX 50,000; annual renewal: UGX 500,000

**Key statutory protections for borrowers (Tier 4 Act, 2016):**
- **No compound interest** (Section 86): money lenders prohibited from compounding interest
- **No charge for loan costs** (Section 96): lenders cannot charge expenses related to loan negotiation (e.g., administration fees labelled as costs)
- Court may reopen any money lending transaction deemed harsh or unconscionable (Section 89)
- Lender must issue receipts and maintain records (Section 87)
- Interest rate control provisions: Section 90 (Minister may prescribe maximum rates)

**Distinction: NDTMI vs Money Lender**

| Feature | NDTMI | Money Lender |
|---|---|---|
| Loan size | Capped (1%/5% of core capital) | No cap |
| Borrower type | Micro/SME focus | Any |
| Structure | Company or NGO | Company only (limited by shares) |
| Compound interest | Not explicitly prohibited (no deposit risk) | Prohibited by statute |

### 3.5 Digital Lending (2024 Update)

Following a February 2024 Ministerial directive, UMRA issued **Tier 4 Digital Lending Guidelines** (available at umra.go.ug):
- All digital credit providers must hold a UMRA licence
- Digital lenders forbidden from collecting deposits
- Additional application requirements: ICT system description, AML/CFT policies, data protection certificate (Data Protection Act, 2019), pricing model, credit policy
- Existing unlicensed digital lenders required to apply within 3 months of publication

### 3.6 Islamic Microfinance (Tier IV)

Tier IV institutions may offer Islamic microfinance with UMRA approval. Requirements: Shari'ah compliance proof, nominated Shari'ah advisor, proposed finance modes and product structures, segregation mechanism between Islamic and conventional microfinance business.

## 4. Capital Markets Authority (CMA) — Securities and Investment

### 4.1 Overview

The CMA is a semi-autonomous government body established by the Capital Markets Authority Act, Cap 84 (1996). Its mandate is to promote, develop, and regulate a fair, efficient, and orderly capital market in Uganda.

**CMA regulates:**
- Uganda Securities Exchange (USE)
- Licensed brokers/dealers (8 brokers, 2 dealers as at 2024)
- Investment and transaction advisors (7 investment advisors)
- Venture capital and private equity funds
- Fund managers (7 licensed)
- Collective Investment Schemes — CIS (5 CIS managers, 2 trustees)
- 17 listed companies (subject to change)

Uganda is a signatory to the IOSCO Multilateral MOU on information sharing (Appendix A signatory).

### 4.2 Uganda Securities Exchange (USE)

The USE was authorised in June 1997 and operates a securities central depository (since 2009). It is a company limited by guarantee, owned by its members. Trading operates on two days per week, two hours per day — limiting price discovery.

**USE Market Segments:**

| Segment | Eligibility | Minimum Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| Main Investment Market Segment (MIMS) | Public company limited by shares | Paid-up capital ≥ UGX 1 billion; net assets ≥ UGX 2 billion; 5 years audited accounts |
| Growth Enterprise Market Segment (GEMS) | Recently incorporated public company (≤ 1 year); does not meet MIMS | Statement of assets and liabilities; does not need to meet all MIMS criteria |
| Fixed Income Securities Segment (FISMS) | Government bonds, corporate bonds, commercial paper, preference shares, debentures | Net assets ≥ 100,000 currency points; OR bank guarantee; 3 years audited accounts |

### 4.3 Disclosure Requirements for Listed Companies

Companies on the USE must:
- Publish annual financial statements within **4 months** after financial year end (Rule 45)
- Disclose dividends to the Exchange within **24 hours** of board resolution
- Give 21 days' notice of AGMs
- Disclose director changes, material litigation, and substantial changes in shareholding
- Adopt corporate governance code in Table F (Companies Act, 2012); file annual compliance statement with URSB and CMA
- Non-compliance penalty: UGX 1 million (50 currency points); listing may be suspended (Uchumi precedent, July 2021)

### 4.4 Board Governance Requirements (CMA Corporate Governance Guidelines)

- Independent and non-executive directors must form at least **one-third** of the board
- Separate roles: Chairperson and CEO must be different individuals
- Chairperson must be independent or non-executive
- No individual may hold more than **5 directorships** in listed companies or chair more than 2
- Audit committee mandatory: majority independent/non-executive directors
- Nominating committee and remuneration committee required

### 4.5 Capital Market Constraints in Uganda

| Constraint | Effect |
|---|---|
| Low investor awareness | Few domestic retail investors; low trading volumes |
| Government dominance | NSSF (≈USD 500M) earning ≈7% return; BoU prime rate 11% — government institutions crowd out private capital |
| Limited trading hours | 2 days/week, 2 hours/day — large price discovery lag |
| Lack of product diversity | Limited asset classes reduce investor ability to diversify |
| Governance/transparency deficits | Economic rent-seeking reduces IPO appetite |
| External financing preference | International USD borrowing available at LIBOR+3% (≈8%); local UGX borrowing at 26% — rational preference for offshore finance |
| FDI without local market activity | FDI portfolio ≈USD 1.1B (Q1 2023) but not translating into USE activity |

## 6. Available Financing Channels Across the Spectrum

Business plan authors should match financing need to the appropriate channel. Regulatory context determines eligibility, cost, and obligations.

| Need | Best Channel | Regulator | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Large-scale business/infrastructure loan | Tier I commercial bank or UDB | BOU | Requires collateral, audited accounts, DSCR ≥ 1.25× |
| SME working capital / term loan | Tier I or Tier II credit institution | BOU | Standard CAMPARI appraisal |
| Micro-enterprise credit | MDI (Tier III) or NDTMI/SACCO (Tier IV) | BOU / UMRA | Group collateral accepted; smaller loans; higher rates |
| Emergency or informal credit | Money lender (Tier IV) | UMRA | No compound interest; no expense surcharges; no max rate |
| Member savings and credit | SACCO | UMRA | Must be a member; equity ≥ 10% of assets |
| Growth equity / IPO | USE (GEMS or MIMS) | CMA | Requires incorporation; audited accounts; disclosure obligations |
| Bond / fixed income | FISMS (USE) | CMA | Net assets ≥ 100,000 currency points OR bank guarantee |
| Collective investment | CIS (fund manager) | CMA | Pooled investor fund; not direct company finance |
| Venture / private equity | PE/VC fund | CMA (draft regulations) | Typically >UGX 500M deal size; high governance requirements |
| Insurance-linked credit | Insurance company | IRA | Collateral assignment via insurance policy |
| Asset / equipment finance | Hire purchase company | Commissioner (Internal Trade) | No deposit taking; asset serves as security |

## 7. Borrower Protections — Key Rights Under Ugandan Law

| Protection | Law / Source |
|---|---|
| No compound interest by money lenders | Tier 4 Act, Section 86 |
| No expense charges on loan negotiation | Tier 4 Act, Section 96 |
| Court may reopen unconscionable transactions | Tier 4 Act, Section 89 |
| Lender must issue receipts and maintain records | Tier 4 Act, Section 87 |
| Consumer financial protection guidelines | BOU Financial Consumer Protection Guidelines, 2011 |
| Credit reference data rights | Borrower may challenge CRB data; right to dispute lodged with CRB |
| CIS investor protection | Collective Investment Schemes Act, 2003 |
| Insurance choice (no coercion) | Stanbic T&Cs, Clause 1.12(a); borrower may choose own insurer |
| Right to early repayment | FIA; Stanbic T&Cs, Clause 1.5 (fees may apply) |
| KYC transparency | FIA and related BOU guidelines; borrower identity protected |
