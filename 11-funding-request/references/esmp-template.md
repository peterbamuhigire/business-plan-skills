# Environmental and Social Management Plan (ESMP) — Template

An ESMP is required by Development Finance Institutions (DFIs) including Uganda Development Bank (UDB), IFC, AFDB, and most impact investors for any business seeking funding above a risk-based threshold. It demonstrates that the business has identified environmental and social risks, committed to mitigation measures, and has a monitoring system to track compliance.

**When an ESMP is required:**

| Funder | Threshold | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Uganda Development Bank (UDB) | All loans above UGX 100M | Standard requirement for all non-micro lending |
| IFC / World Bank Group | All projects in IFC financing | IFC Performance Standards apply |
| AfDB / EADB | Project finance and DFI loans | Environmental and Social Assessment required |
| Impact investors | Varies — typically any investment | ESG due diligence is standard; ESMP expected |
| Commercial banks (major) | Large project finance (>UGX 500M) | Some banks require ESMP for sector-specific risks |

**Risk categorisation:** IFC and UDB use a tiered system:
- **Category A** — significant adverse environmental or social impacts: full Environmental and Social Impact Assessment (ESIA) required. Examples: mining, large-scale manufacturing, major construction.
- **Category B** — moderate, manageable impacts: ESMP sufficient. Examples: agri-processing, hospitality, healthcare, logistics, SME manufacturing.
- **Category C** — minimal or negligible impacts: ESMS Lite (environmental and social checklist). Examples: software, consulting, retail trade, professional services.

Most Uganda SME business plans seeking DFI funding fall into **Category B** or **Category C**. This template covers both.

**Note on NEMA EIA:** A full EIA is required for projects listed in the EIA Regulations Schedule 3 (SI 13/1998). These include: manufacturing plants, agro-industrial facilities, hotels above 50 rooms, hospitals, slaughterhouses, large-scale crop production, and any project within 200m of a water body. For most SMEs, a Certificate of Approval from the District Environment Officer is sufficient.

**Note on land tenure:** Uganda has four main tenure types — freehold, leasehold, Mailo, and customary. Approximately 84% of rural land is held under unregistered customary tenure (World Bank, 2022). Any project on customary land must document community consent and the tenure arrangement. For DFI loans, a formal lease or agreement is strongly preferred over informal occupation.

### Common Mitigations by Business Type (Uganda)

| Business Type | Typical Risks | Standard Mitigations |
|---|---|---|
| **Agri-processing** (milling, dairy, juice) | Wastewater, solid waste (husks/whey), dust, noise | Settling pond or biodigester for liquid waste; compost/animal feed for solid waste; dust suppression; scheduled hours for noisy equipment |
| **Poultry / livestock** | Manure, odour, disease risk, flies | Manure pit or biogas digester; 200m setback from neighbours; biosecurity protocol; MAAIF compliance |
| **Retail / supermarket** | Solid waste, supplier labour standards | Waste segregation (recyclable / organic / general); supplier code of conduct |
| **Construction / real estate** | Dust, noise, worker safety, community disruption | PPE (helmets, boots, gloves); site hoarding; work hours 7am–6pm; community notice board |
| **Hospitality / hotel** | Wastewater, solid waste, water use, food safety | Grease trap; food waste management plan; NWSC-compliant water management; NDA food handler certificates |
| **Healthcare / pharmacy** | Medical waste, chemical handling, infection control | Colour-coded waste segregation; sharps containers; autoclaving or incineration; biohazard training |
| **Transport / logistics** | Vehicle emissions, road safety, driver fatigue | Fleet maintenance log; rest-hour policy; third-party liability insurance; vehicle inspection certificate |
| **Manufacturing (light)** | Chemical storage, worker safety, waste generation | MSDS (material safety data sheets) on file; PPE schedule; waste manifest |
| **Software / consulting** | Minimal — Category C | ESMS Lite (checklist only); data protection compliance (PDPA 2019) |

### 5.2 Reporting Obligations

**To UDB / DFI:**
- Annual Environmental and Social Monitoring Report submitted with financial statements
- Incident notification within 48 hours for any serious environmental or social incident
- Material changes to the project (new activities, new sites, new products) require prior notification

**To NEMA:**
- Annual Environmental Audit (for projects with NEMA Certificate of Approval)
- Reporting of any accidental spill, emission breach, or environmental incident within 24 hours

**To employees:**
- Annual review of employment contracts and NSSF contributions
- Workplace health and safety inspection (annual minimum)

### 5.3 Grievance Mechanism

A grievance mechanism allows employees, community members, and other affected parties to raise concerns without fear of retaliation.

**Minimum requirements:**
- A named person responsible for receiving and investigating grievances
- A written log of all grievances received and how they were resolved
- A response timeline commitment (recommended: 14 days for acknowledgement, 30 days for resolution)
- Anonymous reporting option (suggestion box, WhatsApp number, or email)

## Appendix A — ESMS Lite (Category C Checklist)

For software, consulting, trading, and professional services businesses, a full ESMP is not required. Complete this checklist instead:

- [ ] Business registered and compliant with NIN/BRN/TIN requirements
- [ ] All employees have signed employment contracts (Employment Act 2006)
- [ ] NSSF registered and contributions current
- [ ] PAYE registered and remitting to URA
- [ ] Data protection compliance (if handling personal data) — PDPA 2019
- [ ] Office/premises has fire safety equipment (extinguisher, exit signs)
- [ ] No hazardous materials stored on premises
- [ ] No child labour (all employees ≥18 years, or 16+ in non-hazardous light work)
- [ ] Waste disposed through licensed collector or municipal service
- [ ] No outstanding NEMA or environmental violations
- [ ] Grievance mechanism in place for employees
