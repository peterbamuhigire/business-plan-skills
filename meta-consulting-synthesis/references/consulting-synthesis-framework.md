# Consulting Synthesis Framework

Use this file when a plan has enough content but still does not read like one integrated advisory product.

## 1. Structure the problem before writing

From McKinsey-style problem solving:

- Define the decision clearly
- Break it into MECE branches
- Form a hypothesis for each branch
- Gather only the evidence needed to confirm, refine, or reject that hypothesis

Do not start from section order. Start from the decision.

## 2. Build a key line, not a topic list

A strong investment case is not:

- market
- product
- team
- financials

It is:

- the market gap is real and timely
- this business has a differentiated right to win
- the economics support repayment or return
- the execution plan is realistic
- the remaining risks are known and mitigated

That is the key line. The sections exist to prove it.

## 3. Use assertion-evidence, not label-content

Bad heading:

- Market Opportunity

Better heading:

- Urban poultry demand is growing faster than formal supply in the target corridor

Each section should follow the same pattern:

1. State the conclusion
2. Show the evidence
3. Explain the implication

## 4. Write for the client decision

From strategic storytelling and executive-presentation practice:

- establish the current reality
- show the gap or tension
- show the better future
- explain why this business is the credible bridge
- close with a clear requested decision

This is a decision narrative, not a chronology.

## 5. Keep evidence hierarchy explicit

Prefer evidence in this order:

1. audited or official internal data
2. signed contracts, LOIs, customer commitments
3. primary interviews or field validation
4. reputable third-party market data
5. informed estimates with transparent assumptions

Any claim using level 4 or 5 evidence should be labelled accordingly.

## 6. Force synthesis at section boundaries

At the end of every major section, answer:

- what have we now proved?
- what decision concern does this resolve?
- what does the next section need to prove?

This stops section drift.

## 7. Audience-specific emphasis

### Bank

- DSCR
- cash conversion
- collateral quality
- downside resilience
- management discipline

### Equity

- growth drivers
- scalability
- return path
- valuation logic
- exit route

### DFI

- development impact
- governance
- implementation readiness
- safeguards
- additionality

## 8. Red flags

- multiple competing theses
- section headings that are only labels
- claims with no proof burden
- market optimism that is not reflected in operations or hiring
- financial ambition that is not reflected in execution sequencing
- appendices that do not support the core claims
