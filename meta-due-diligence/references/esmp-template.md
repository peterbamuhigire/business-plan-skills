## 1. When an ESMP is Required

| Funder | Threshold | Instrument Required |
|---|---|---|
| **AfDB** | Category 1 or 2 projects; all FI-funded sub-projects | Full ESMP + Annex A–I |
| **World Bank / IDA** | All investment project financing | ESMP or ESMF (programme level) |
| **UNDP / GEF** | Moderate or High risk (SESP result) | ESMP; Ethnic Minorities Plan if applicable |
| **IFC** | Performance Standards 1–8 applicable | ESMP or Environmental and Social Action Plan (ESAP) |
| **Uganda (NEMA)** | EIA required for projects in Schedule 3 of NEMA (SI 13/1998) | National EIA + ESMP |

**Rule for business plans:** Any plan requesting >UGX 500M from a DFI, multilateral, or development bank must include an ESMP or commit to preparing one. Include the ESMP as Appendix E or reference it in Section 12 (Risk Analysis) and Section 11 (Funding Request).

## 2. Standard ESMP Document Structure

A complete DFI-standard ESMP (30–50 pages) contains these sections in order:

| # | Section | Typical Pages | Key Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Executive Summary | 1–2 | Project overview, risk rating, key safeguard instruments, summary of mitigations |
| 2 | Introduction and Scope | 2–3 | Legal framework (national + DFI standards), project objectives, purpose of ESMP |
| 3 | Project Description and Locations | 2–4 | Activity-by-activity breakdown, site maps, beneficiary profile |
| 4 | Screening and Categorisation | 1–2 | Risk rating result (SESP/EIA), triggers, justification |
| 5 | Baseline Data | 2–4 | Environmental/social baseline: air quality, water, ecology, demographics, vulnerable groups |
| 6 | Impact Assessment and Mitigation | 5–8 | Core impact/mitigation matrix (see §4 below) |
| 7 | Stakeholder Engagement Plan (SEP) | 2–3 | Consultation log, vulnerable group strategy, FPIC if applicable |
| 8 | Grievance Redress Mechanism (GRM) | 1–2 | Channels, procedure, SEAH protocols, worker routes |
| 9 | Labour and OHS | 1–2 | Worker screening, PPE, incident reporting, health checks |
| 10 | Specific Management Plans | 2–4 | Sub-plans: C-ESMP, CHSMP, WMP, EPPR, TMP, Asbestos/Hazmat (if needed) |
| 11 | Implementation Arrangements | 2–3 | PIU roles, contractor responsibilities, capacity building |
| 12 | Environmental Monitoring Plan | 2–3 | Indicators, frequency, responsible parties, reporting tools |
| 13 | ESMP Budget | 0.5–1 | Cost breakdown — contractor, E&S specialist, monitoring |
| 14 | Annexes (A–I) | Varies | Screening forms, ESAP log, E&S clauses, GRM form, SEP template, FPIC protocol |

## 3. Risk Categorisation Framework

| Risk Level | Definition | Typical Triggers | DFI Instruments Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Low** | Minor, localised, reversible impacts | Office-based training; no construction; no land acquisition; non-sensitive sites | Generic ESMP provisions only; SESP checklist |
| **Moderate** | Manageable impacts with standard mitigation | Small construction (<500m²); equipment installation; mangrove/tree planting; temporary land use | Full ESMP; SEP; GRM; monitoring plan; EMP if ethnic minorities affected |
| **High / Category 1** | Significant adverse impacts; potential irreversible harm | Major construction; permanent land acquisition; displacement; Indigenous Peoples; hazardous materials; protected areas | Full ESIA + ESMP; RAP/LRP; C-ESMP; CHSMP; EPPR; third-party monitoring |
| **Category 2** | Moderate adverse impacts; standard mitigation adequate | Medium infrastructure; minor resettlement; limited environmental modification | ESMP; concurrent DFI review; SEP; GRM |
| **Category 3** | Minimal impacts | Small grants; training; technical assistance | ESMF applies; no project-level ESMP required |

## 4. Impact/Mitigation Matrix — Template

Use this column structure for the core impact table in Section 6:

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| **Project Phase** | Pre-construction / Construction / Operation / Decommissioning |
| **Activity** | Specific project activity (e.g., "Site clearance and excavation") |
| **Potential Impact/Risk** | Specific environmental or social consequence |
| **Likelihood** | Low / Medium / High (before mitigation) |
| **Severity** | Low / Medium / High |
| **Risk Rating** | L × S (composite score or Low/Moderate/High) |
| **Mitigation Measures** | Specific, actionable measures numbered (M1, M2...) |
| **Residual Risk** | Risk level after mitigation |
| **Responsibility** | Named party (e.g., "Contractor's HSE officer") |
| **Cost** | "Embedded in BoQ" / specific budget line |
| **Verification / KPI** | How compliance is checked |

## 5. Representative Mitigation Measures by Impact Type

Based on Yemen FAO/World Bank ESMP (Al-Nasser University Rehabilitation, 2025):

### Air Quality / Dust
- Spray greywater on earthworks and material storage areas during dry weather
- Schedule dusty activities during daytime only; avoid windy conditions
- Cover material trucks with tarpaulins; maintain roads to minimise vehicle dust
- Maintain equipment and vehicles to reduce exhaust emissions; turn off when idle

### Noise
- Restrict noisy activities to daytime (7am–6pm); notify neighbours of schedule
- Use noise barriers/screens around stationary equipment (generators, compressors)
- Regular equipment maintenance to reduce mechanical noise
- Position generators in areas away from residential zones

### Waste Management
- Classify waste on site: general solid, construction debris, hazardous (chemicals, asbestos)
- Store each category in clearly labelled, covered, wind-shielded areas
- Dispose of solid waste at designated approved landfill (obtain disposal receipt)
- **Asbestos:** Double-bag in sealed, puncture-resistant containers; record weight; dispose via encapsulation with receipt from local authority Cleaning Fund; full PPE with EN 140 respiratory filters mandatory; medically examine workers 2 months post-handling
- No burning of waste on site

### Water Quality
- Store fuels, oils, and cement on concrete-lined secondary containment pads
- Spill kit and fire extinguisher at all fuel/chemical storage points
- Inspect soil around generators weekly for leak signs
- All wastewater from site activities collected and removed — no discharge to storm drains or watercourses

### Worker Occupational Health and Safety (OHS)
- Daily safety induction briefing before work commences
- Provide PPE: hard hats, safety boots, high-vis vests, gloves, respiratory protection
- Prohibit underage workers (<18 years); verify age at employment
- Medical insurance mandatory for all workers and subcontractors
- First aid kit, fire extinguisher, and emergency contact list at site office
- Implement signed Job Safety Analysis (JSA) for each high-risk task
- Report all incidents within 24 hours with written report (description, time, location, names, actions taken, medical report)

### Community Safety
- Install perimeter fencing/barriers; separate pedestrian and vehicle routes
- Post warning signs at all site entrances and along perimeter
- Appoint site guard during non-working hours
- All drivers hold valid licences; enforce speed limits near residential/school zones
- Distribute GRM cards to nearby residents and businesses
- Develop site-specific Traffic Management Plan (TMP) if traffic diversion required

### Asbestos (where present)
- Survey and map asbestos materials before work commences
- Wet-method removal only (suppress fibre release); no dry sanding or breaking
- Air monitoring at site boundary during removal operations
- All asbestos waste in sealed, labelled double-bags; medical surveillance programme for workers
- Attach Asbestos Waste Management Plan as annex

## 6. Environmental Monitoring Plan

Standard monitoring table structure (from UNDP Vietnam ESMP, 2025):

| Indicator | Monitoring Method | Frequency | Responsible Party | Report Format |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dust/noise levels at site boundary | Spot measurement; resident complaints log | Weekly during construction | Contractor's HSE officer | Weekly site report |
| Worker OHS incidents | Incident register; HSE inspection | Daily; reported monthly | Contractor + PIU | Monthly incident log |
| Waste disposal receipts | Record-keeping; landfill receipts | Per disposal event | Contractor | Attached to progress report |
| GRM cases received, resolved, pending | GRM register | Bi-weekly | PIU / GRM focal point | Bi-weekly summary |
| Stakeholder consultation records | Attendance sheets; meeting minutes | Per consultation | PIU | Appended to E&S report |
| E&S compliance status — overall | Field visits; spot checks | Quarterly (missions) | PIU E&S specialist | Back-to-office report (BTOR) |
| GRM/SEAH statistics | GM register audit | Quarterly | PIU | Quarterly E&S report to funder |
| Annual ESMS/monitoring report | Full review | Annual | PIU | Annual E&S Monitoring Report |

**Reporting timelines (AfDB standard):**
- Quarterly E&S progress report: within **45 business days** after end of reporting period
- Annual ESMS/E&S Monitoring Report: within **90 days** after end of reporting year

## 7. Grievance Redress Mechanism (GRM)

### 5-Step Standard Procedure

**Step 1 — Receipt and Registration (Immediate)**
- Access channels: toll-free hotline; SMS/WhatsApp; email; project website; office drop-box; field staff verbally
- Anonymous complaints accepted
- Register: date, complainant details (or "anonymous"), complaint category, channel used

**Step 2 — Classification and Assignment (Within 1 business day)**
- Category A (Low) — Inquiries/requests → Phone/email response; same day
- Category B (Moderate) — Operational issues (late delivery, quality, selection disputes) → Programme team; reviewed bi-weekly
- Category C (Critical) — Misconduct, corruption, SEAH, abuse of authority → FAOR/Project Coordinator + SEAH Focal Point; immediate escalation

**Step 3 — Investigation and Assessment (Within 5 working days)**
- Assign case owner; acknowledge receipt to complainant
- For SEAH: strictly need-to-know access; no sharing beyond SEAH Focal Point and designated support staff

**Step 4 — Resolution and Response (Within 7 working days of receipt)**
- Inform complainant of outcome; document agreement
- Escalate unresolved cases to DFI-level GRM or independent arbitration

**Step 5 — Closure and Record-Keeping**
- Mark closed in GM register; document lessons learned
- Cases recorded: date, channel, category, case owner, status, resolution date

**GRM Register columns:** Date received | Channel | Complainant (or "Anon") | Category (A/B/C) | Case owner | Status (open/investigating/resolved/closed) | Resolution date | Notes

**SEAH Special Protocols:**
- Dedicated confidential channel (separate phone/email from general GRM)
- Survivor-centred approach: no retaliation; restricted information access; referral to medical/psychosocial/legal support
- Annual aggregate reporting to DFI (number received, resolved, pending — no personal details)
- Target resolution rate: 80–90% of cases within defined timeframe annually

**Worker-Specific Routes:**
- Site complaint box (opened weekly, contents logged)
- Contractor-level GRM disclosed at site briefings and posted at canteen/notice boards
- Contractor GRM integrated into project-level GRM log

## 8. Stakeholder Engagement Requirements

### Timeline

**Pre-Project (Before Key Decision Points):**
- Develop and disclose Stakeholder Engagement Plan (SEP)
- Initial public consultation before: screening, ESIA/ESMP finalisation, RAP approval, works commencement
- Specific outreach to: women, youth, ethnic minorities, disabled, low-literacy groups (use visual aids)
- For Indigenous Peoples: FPIC documentation prepared and consultation completed

**During Implementation:**
- Ongoing engagement via community meetings, GRM, site notices
- Women/vulnerable group participation targets documented (e.g., ≥60% women in project committees)
- Contractors briefed on community relations; project information shared in local languages

**Post-Project (Operation/Closure):**
- Final stakeholder meeting; community feedback; handover to local management bodies
- Community-led monitoring set up where applicable

**Documentation (every consultation):**
- Date, venue, number of attendees by category (women / men / youth / community leaders)
- Issues raised and project response
- Action items and responsible party
- Sign-in sheet (or headcount if literacy barrier)

## 9. AfDB 14 Material Actions

Summary of binding requirements for AfDB-financed projects (from Zambia Grid Resilience ESMP, 2025):

| # | Action | OS Basis | Key KPI | Deadline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Periodic E&S reports to AfDB | OS1, ESP | Quarterly progress + annual ESMS report | Quarterly (45 days); Annual (90 days) |
| 2 | Recruit E&S Specialists | OS1 | Qualified E&S Manager appointed with JD and CV | Within 3 months of Effective Date |
| 3 | Establish GRM | OS1, OS10 | GRM disclosed (website, email, phone); operational register; SEAH channels | Before first disbursement |
| 4 | Compensation for displaced people | OS5 | RAP/LRP cleared by AfDB and fully implemented | Before any physical displacement |
| 5 | E&S clauses in all RFPs | OS1 | Standard E&S clauses in 100% of financing agreements | Before first call for proposals |
| 6 | Contractor ESMP (C-ESMP) clearance | OS1, ESP | C-ESMP cleared for all Category 1/high-risk works | Before contractor mobilisation |
| 7 | Contractor GRM and worker information | OS1, OS2, OS10 | Site-level GMs posted at worksite; worker grievance routes | Before contractor mobilisation |
| 8 | National licences before works | OS1, OS2, national laws | All ZEMA/NEMA permits obtained prior to first disbursement | Before first disbursement |
| 9 | E&S documentation for all sub-projects | OS1, national | ESIA/ESMP/RAP prepared per risk category; disclosed | Before financial close |
| 10 | Stakeholder engagement | OS1, OS10 | SEP implemented; consultation records maintained | Throughout project |
| 11 | EPPR Plans | OS1, OS4 | EPPR plans tested annually; high-risk plans adopted before works | Within 6 months of Effective Date |
| 12 | Riparian notification | OS3 | Not applicable to most SME projects; confirm annually | Annual |
| 13 | Capacity building | OS1 | Annual training plan; training records; ≥1 cycle in 9 months | Within 9 months of Effective Date |
| 14 | ESMS/ESAP implementation | OS1, OS9 | ESMS Manual approved; ESAP tracked; annual review | Within 1 month of Effective Date |

**OS reference guide:** OS1 = E&S Assessment; OS2 = Labour; OS3 = Resource Efficiency/Pollution; OS4 = Community Health/Safety; OS5 = Involuntary Resettlement; OS6 = Biodiversity; OS7 = Cultural Heritage; OS8 = Indigenous Peoples; OS9 = Gender; OS10 = Stakeholder Engagement/Information Disclosure.

## 10. ESMP Budget Structure

| Line Item | Who Pays | Typical Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Mitigation measures (dust suppression, waste disposal, PPE, safety equipment) | Contractor | Embedded in Bill of Quantities (BoQ); stated as "Mandatory" |
| E&S Specialist / Supervising Engineer | Project budget | USD 50,000–80,000 for medium projects |
| Environmental monitoring (3–5 years) | Project budget | USD 20,000–40,000 (UNDP Vietnam example: USD 30,000) |
| GRM operation (hotline, registration, response) | Project administration | % of project admin budget |
| Asbestos/Hazmat specialist (if required) | Project or contractor | USD 10,000–20,000 per site |
| Incident remediation contingency | Project contingency | 5–10% of mitigation budget |
| Training and capacity building | Project budget | USD 10,000–30,000 per year |
| **Total ESMP implementation estimate** | **Project + Contractor** | **Typically 1–3% of total project cost** |

**Cost reporting:** All ESMP costs reported in quarterly ESMS implementation reports. Contractor costs verified against BoQ line items during site supervision.

## 11. Uganda Regulatory Context

Uganda-specific ESMP requirements (supplement to generic DFI standards above):

| Requirement | Regulatory Body | Key Detail | ESMP Application |
|---|---|---|---|
| **EIA Certificate** | NEMA | Required for projects listed in Schedule 3 of NEMA (SI No. 13/1998); lead agency NEMA; process 20–90 days | Section 2 (Legal Framework) + Annex (EIA certificate copy) |
| **EIA Screening** | NEMA / District Environment Officer | Projects below Schedule 3 threshold need DEO screening and ESMP review | Section 4 (Categorisation) |
| **Construction Permit** | KCCA (Kampala) or District Local Government | Physical planning permit and building permit required before construction | Action 8 (Licences) |
| **Occupational Safety** | DOSH (Directorate of Occupational Safety and Health) | Factories Act Cap 220; must register factory/workshop; report accidents to DOSH within 24 hours | Section 9 (OHS) |
| **Waste Management** | NEMA / Kampala Capital City Authority | Solid waste management regulations; hazardous waste licence required for disposal | Section 6 (Waste row) |
| **Worker Registration** | NSSF + URA + NIN/BRN gate (2025) | All workers require NIN; employer must deduct NSSF (15%), PAYE; register with URA | Section 9 (Labour) |
| **Water Discharge** | DWRM (Directorate of Water Resources Management) | Abstraction licence and effluent discharge permit for significant water use/discharge | Section 6 (Water quality row) |
| **Environmental Levy** | NEMA | Annual levy for registered facilities with environmental impact | Section 13 (Budget) |
| **EUDR Compliance** | MAAIF / EU Regulation | From 31 Dec 2025: coffee, cocoa, timber products must prove deforestation-free supply chain | Section 6 (Land use/forest row) — for agribusiness projects |

**NEMA EIA categories:**
- **Schedule 1** (mandatory EIA): Dams/reservoirs, roads, irrigation (>500 ha), mining, large buildings (>5,000m²), waste treatment plants
- **Schedule 2** (EIA at NEMA discretion): Small roads, workshops, hostels, service stations, animal farms >50 ha
- **Schedule 3** (exempt): Small buildings, minor agriculture, maintenance works

**Typical EIA timeline in Uganda:** Application → Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) — 14 days; NEMA review — 30–60 days; Certificate issued — 90 days total for standard projects; complex projects up to 12 months.

## 12. Key Terminology Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **ESMS** | Environmental and Social Management System — organisation-wide framework covering policies, procedures, training, and monitoring |
| **ESIA** | Environmental and Social Impact Assessment — detailed study quantifying project impacts on environment and communities |
| **ESMP** | Environmental and Social Management Plan — action plan with measures, timelines, responsibilities, and budgets for managing impacts |
| **ESMF** | Environmental and Social Management Framework — programme-level instrument for projects/subprojects not yet identified |
| **C-ESMP** | Contractor Environmental and Social Management Plan — site-specific contractor plan; cleared by DFI before mobilisation |
| **GRM** | Grievance Redress Mechanism — formal system for receiving, investigating, and resolving project-affected complaints |
| **SEAH** | Sexual Exploitation, Abuse, and Harassment — requires dedicated confidential GRM channels and PSEA Focal Point |
| **FPIC** | Free, Prior and Informed Consent — mandatory consultation process for projects affecting Indigenous Peoples |
| **RAP** | Resettlement Action Plan — compensation/rehabilitation plan for physically or economically displaced people |
| **LRP** | Livelihood Restoration Plan — sub-component of RAP addressing economic displacement/income loss |
| **OHS** | Occupational Health and Safety — worker protection measures: PPE, training, incident reporting, health checks |
| **SEP** | Stakeholder Engagement Plan — consultation strategy for affected communities, authorities, and vulnerable groups |
| **EPPR** | Emergency Preparedness and Response Plan — fire, spill, flood, and crisis response procedures |
| **PIU** | Project Implementation Unit — day-to-day project management team responsible for E&S compliance |
| **ESAP** | Environmental and Social Action Plan — agreed DFI action log tracking closure of E&S gaps |
| **OS1–OS9** | AfDB Operational Safeguards 1–9 (Assessment; Labour; Pollution; Community Safety; Resettlement; Biodiversity; Heritage; Indigenous Peoples; Gender) |
| **IFC PS** | IFC Performance Standards 1–8 — World Bank Group sustainability standards; referenced by AfDB, UNDP, EIB |
| **BoQ** | Bill of Quantities — detailed cost schedule in construction contracts; ESMP costs embedded here |
| **BTOR** | Back-to-Office Report — field visit report submitted by DFI or PIU specialist after monitoring mission |
