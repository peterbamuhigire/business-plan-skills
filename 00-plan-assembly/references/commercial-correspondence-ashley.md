## 1. Professional Letter Structure

**Block style** (most widely used internationally): every line starts at the left margin; no indentation.

### Standard Components (in order)

| Component | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Sender's address** | Top right (or top left if no letterhead); no sender name — only address |
| **Date** | Below address, separated by a space; write month in full (e.g. 15 March 2026) — never figures only (3.15.26 is ambiguous) |
| **Inside address** | Recipient name/title → building → street → town + postcode → country |
| **Attention line** | *For the attention of the Sales Manager* — used when letter goes to a department |
| **Salutation** | See table below |
| **Subject line** | Bold or underlined; states the topic; placed between salutation and body |
| **Body** | Blocked paragraphs; blank line between paragraphs |
| **Complimentary close** | See table below |
| **Signature block** | Handwritten signature + typed name + job title below |
| **Enc. / Ref.** | Enclosures listed below signature; reference numbers above salutation |

### Salutation and Complimentary Close Rules

| Situation | Salutation | Close |
|---|---|---|
| Name unknown, gender unknown | Dear Sir/Madam | Yours faithfully |
| Name unknown, addressing company | Dear Sirs | Yours faithfully |
| Surname known (any gender) | Dear Mr/Mrs/Ms/Dr [Surname] | Yours sincerely |
| Close colleague | Dear [First name] | Best wishes |
| American formal | Gentlemen: | Yours truly |

**Key rules:**
- Use *Ms* when marital status unknown (avoids offence)
- *Sir* (courtesy title for a knight) ≠ *Dear Sir* (salutation for unknown male)
- *Messrs* (plural of Mr) is old-fashioned except as part of a company name
- Comma after salutation and close is optional — be consistent

### Courtesy Titles Quick Reference

| Title | Use |
|---|---|
| Mr | Any man |
| Mrs | Married woman |
| Miss | Unmarried woman |
| Ms | Woman — marital status unknown or unspecified |
| Dr / Prof. / Capt. | Academic, medical, military — do not abbreviate salutation (use full title) |
| Esq. | Seldom used; old-fashioned |

## 2. Email and Fax Standards

**Email:**
- Salutation and close optional for internal; include for external formal email
- Acceptable to omit *Dear Sir/Madam* and close with *Thank you* (less formal)
- Attachments (invoices, contracts) should be separate — do not paste documents into email body
- Signature block: name, title, company, contact details

**Fax:**
- Include: To, From, Date, Subject, Number of pages, Ref.
- Mark *URGENT* or *CONFIDENTIAL* on cover sheet when needed
- Keep style professional — same standards as a letter

## 3. Content and Style Principles

**Length:** Long enough to cover all necessary points; short enough to respect the reader's time. Plan before writing — note all required points, check for completeness, then sequence logically.

**Sequence:** First paragraph → acknowledge / purpose. Middle paragraphs → key points in logical order (each paragraph one idea). Final paragraph → next steps, encourage response.

**Planning template (standard reply):**
1. Acknowledge enquiry/order/complaint + date and reference
2. Main content — answer each point raised
3. Draw attention to key items (products, terms, conditions)
4. State guarantees / delivery / payment terms
5. Close — invite further contact

**Tone:** Polite but direct. Avoid archaic phrases (*We beg to remain…*, *Respectfully yours*). Prefer passive/impersonal where avoiding blame (*There appears to be an error* vs *You made a mistake*).

## 4. Enquiries, Replies and Quotations

### Enquiry Openings
- *Please would you send me your catalogue and price list, quoting CIF prices, [destination]?*
- *Could you let us know if you allow cash discounts?*
- *We would appreciate a prompt reply quoting trade prices.*

### Quotation — Incoterms 2000 Quick Reference

| Abbreviation | Full Name | Seller Covers |
|---|---|---|
| **EXW** | Ex-Works | Nothing — buyer collects from factory |
| **FCA** | Free Carrier | Delivery to buyer's carrier; export cleared |
| **FAS** | Free Alongside Ship | Delivery to port quayside |
| **FOB** | Free On Board | Loaded on ship; export cleared |
| **CFR** | Cost and Freight | Freight to named destination; no insurance |
| **CIF** | Cost, Insurance and Freight | Freight + insurance to named destination |
| **CPT** | Carriage Paid To | Freight to named destination (air/road/rail) |
| **CIP** | Carriage and Insurance Paid | Freight + insurance (non-ship modes) |
| **DAF** | Delivered at Frontier | All costs to buyer's frontier; no import duty |
| **DDP** | Delivered Duty Paid | All costs including import duty to buyer |
| **DDU** | Delivered Duty Unpaid | As DDP but buyer pays import duty |

*CIF [port name] is the standard quotation term for sea cargo. Always specify port and Incoterms year (e.g. CIF Mombasa Incoterms 2000).*

### Types of Discount

| Type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Trade discount | For buyers in the same trade/sector |
| Quantity discount | For orders above a threshold |
| Cash discount | For early payment (e.g. 3/10 net 30 = 3% if paid in 10 days) |
| Loyalty discount | For long-term customer relationships |

### Quotation Reply Structure
1. Thank for enquiry + reference
2. Enclose catalogue / price list (CIF price + discount terms)
3. Draw attention to relevant products
4. State guarantee period
5. Give delivery dates
6. Invite further contact

## 5. Orders and Dispatch

### Covering Letter for an Order
- State order number and enclose official order form
- Confirm which discount and payment terms are accepted
- Specify any substitution policy (*If out of stock, do not send alternatives*)
- Request target delivery date
- Provide banking details for shipping documents

### Packing Instructions
- Specify packing material (straw, bubble wrap, crates)
- Mark crates with agreed symbol/number
- State requirements for fragile/moving parts (*secure before loading*)

### Advice of Dispatch (from seller to buyer)
- State order number, vessel/flight, departure date, arrival date
- Confirm where shipping documents have been sent (usually buyer's bank)
- Typical wording: *Your order No. [X] is aboard SS [Name], sailing [port] on [date], arriving [port] on [date]. Shipping documents have been forwarded to your bank.*

## 6. Payment Methods and Documents

### Payment Methods Summary

| Method | Typical Use | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Cheque** | Domestic; small amounts | Slow for international — 3-week clearing |
| **Bank draft** | Domestic; large amounts | Requires two director signatures; bank fee applies |
| **Bank transfer / SWIFT** | International preferred | Fastest; bank notifies both parties |
| **Bill of exchange (B/E)** | International trade | See below |
| **Letter of credit (L/C)** | International; high-value | Safest for both parties |
| **Cash on delivery (COD)** | Domestic; postal goods | Post office collects on delivery |
| **Open account / monthly statement** | Trusted domestic buyers | Credit facility — see Section 8 |

### Bill of Exchange (B/E) — Key Concepts
- **Drawer** = seller / exporter (writes the bill)
- **Drawee** = buyer / importer (accepts and pays)
- **Sight draft**: payable immediately on presentation
- **Term draft**: payable X days after sight (e.g. *30 days after sight*)
- **Accepting a bill**: drawee signs the face = legally bound to pay
- **Discounting a bill**: seller sells accepted bill to bank at a discount to receive cash immediately
- **Endorsing**: drawer signs back of bill to transfer it to a third party
- **Inland B/E**: no shipping documents attached
- **Documentary B/E**: accompanied by shipping documents (invoice, bill of lading, insurance certificate)

### Letter of Credit (L/C)
- Issued by buyer's bank to seller; guarantees payment if conditions met
- **Documentary credit**: payment triggered by presenting shipping documents
- Seller must present: commercial invoice, bill of lading, insurance certificate
- Revocable L/C: can be cancelled before payment (rare)
- Irrevocable L/C: cannot be cancelled without seller's consent (standard)

### Invoice Components
- Invoice number + date
- Buyer's order number
- Description, quantity, unit price, total
- Incoterm price (e.g. CIF Mombasa)
- Discount deducted
- VAT (if applicable) — separate line
- *E. & O.E.* (Errors and Omissions Excepted) — seller's right to correct mistakes

### Statement of Account
- Monthly summary of all transactions: invoices, debit notes, credit notes, payments received
- Opening balance → running balance → closing balance
- *E. & O.E.* — same correction right as invoice

## 7. Complaints and Adjustments

### Principles for Making Complaints
- **Do not delay** — complain as soon as a mistake is discovered
- **Do not apologise** for writing (*I am sorry to trouble you*)
- **Be specific**: quote order number, date, exact fault
- **Suggest a solution** (credit note, replacement, collection of goods)
- **Use passive/impersonal** language (*There appears to be an error* not *You made a mistake*)
- **Never use fault/blame** language (*It is your fault*, *You are to blame*)

**Openings:**
- *I am writing to complain about Order No. [X] received [date].*
- *We would like to inform you that the consignment received on [date] was damaged.*
- *There appears to be an error on Invoice No. [X].*

### Replying to Complaints
1. **Acknowledge** immediately — thank for writing
2. **Apologise** (if justified): *We apologise for the inconvenience caused.*
3. **Explain** what went wrong (without blaming staff — you employed them)
4. **Resolve**: replacement, credit note, debit note, or cash refund
5. **Close**: confirm this is exceptional — it rarely/never happens
6. **Unjustified complaint**: politely state your case, provide evidence, decline adjustment if warranted

### Debit Note
- Sent by seller to *increase* the buyer's balance (e.g. under-invoiced, return of credit)

### Credit Note
- Sent by seller to *reduce* the buyer's balance (e.g. returned goods, overcharge, compensation)

## 8. Credit and Open Account Facilities

### Requesting Open Account (Monthly Statement Settlement)
- Make request while placing an order (not instead of one)
- Reference length of trading relationship
- State preferred terms (*settle by monthly statement*)

### Granting Credit
- Check customer's trading history before granting
- State terms clearly: *Net 30 days* / *3% cash discount for payment within 7 days*
- Consider cash discount as an alternative to open account (may suit small customers better)
- Long-standing customers: no references needed
- New customers: request two trade references + bank reference

### Credit Status Enquiries
- Sent to a company's bank or trade contacts asking about creditworthiness
- Must be marked *STRICTLY PRIVATE AND CONFIDENTIAL*
- Reply should be tactful — state facts without committing to strong opinions

## 9. Banking Correspondence

### Requesting an Overdraft or Loan
Structure:
1. State purpose (expansion, equipment, working capital)
2. Amount required
3. Evidence of need (orders received, growth data)
4. Proposed security/collateral
5. Repayment timeline
6. Request meeting to discuss

*Wording example: "I would like to make an appointment to discuss a loan of UGX [X] to enable me to expand my business. I have received [X] in confirmed orders that I am unable to fulfil with current capacity."*

### Bank's Loan Approval Letter Includes
- Amount approved
- Form (overdraft vs term loan)
- Interest rate (calculated on half-yearly balances or reducing balance)
- Repayment date
- Security held
- Guarantor requirement
- Next steps (sign enclosed agreement; return by [date])

### Declining a Loan
- State regret clearly and early
- Give reason briefly (credit climate, existing overdraft, sector risk)
- Suggest alternative (finance corporation, development bank, equity)
- Close on positive note

### Standing Order Request
- Account number, amount, recipient, recipient's bank, first payment date, frequency

### Cancelling a Cheque
- Cheque number, amount, payee, reason (lost in post; replacement issued)

## 10. Agency Correspondence

### Offering to Act as Agent (from potential agent to manufacturer)
- Describe your company, market coverage, and existing contacts
- Explain why your market is suitable for their products
- Suggest commission terms
- State type of agency sought (sole, del credere, general)

### Del Credere Agent
- Guarantees customers' debts to the principal
- Earns *del credere commission* (additional %) as compensation for the guarantee risk
- *Wording: "We would be willing to offer an additional 2.5% del credere commission if you agree to guarantee all customers' accounts."*

### Principal's Response to Agency Offer — Key Questions to Settle
1. Payment: does the customer pay the principal direct, or pay the agent who remits?
2. Stock: does the agent hold stock or does the principal ship direct?
3. Advertising: who funds local promotion?
4. Dispute resolution: which jurisdiction / arbitration body?
5. Contract length (3 years is standard for a new market)

### Agent's Quarterly Report to Principal
Structure: Market overview → Sales performance → Advertising spend → Pricing/competitive issues → Recommendations

## 11. Memos and Reports

### Memo Format
```
MEMO
To:      [Recipient/s or All Staff]
From:    [Sender + title]
Date:    [Full date]
Subject: [Clear, specific subject line]

[Body — concise; one topic per memo; impersonal and formal]
```

**When to use:** Internal communication; policy changes; meeting requests; announcements

### Report Structure

**Regular report** (monthly/quarterly): Title → Introduction → Main Findings → Conclusions → Recommendations

**Ad hoc / special report** (investigation, feasibility, research): Title → Summary (≤ 1 page for long reports) → Introduction → Methodology → Findings (with headings and subheadings) → Conclusions → Recommendations

**Numbered heading system for long reports:**
```
1. Introduction
2. Data Collection
   2.1 Questionnaires
   2.2 Interviews
3. Main Findings
   3.1 Market Segment A
      3.1.1 Age group 18–30
```

**Summary rule:** Busy readers may only read the summary — make it self-contained. State why the report was written, main findings, conclusions, and recommendations (max one page).

**Sources:**
- Primary: interviews, surveys, questionnaires, direct observation
- Secondary: books, industry publications, newspapers, databases

## 12. Personnel Correspondence

### Job Advertisement — Full vs Abbreviated
Abbreviated (*small ads*): *Wntd PA, f.t., sml mnfg co. Gd slry. 5-day wk.*
Full: Wanted, personal assistant for full-time employment in small manufacturing company. Good salary. Five days per week.

### Application Letter — Structure
1. **Opening:** State how you heard of the vacancy; reference the job title
2. **Background:** Relevant education and experience (be specific; quantify where possible)
3. **Why this employer:** Show you know the company; why you want to join them
4. **Closing:** Offer to provide more information; availability for interview

**Unsolicited application (no vacancy advertised):** Name who recommended you contact them; ask about possible vacancies in a specific department.

**Leaving current employer — acceptable explanations:**
- *Offered an opportunity to use my [skills] in a new context*
- *Sought a role with more responsibility in [area]*
- *Company was restructuring and offered voluntary redundancy*

*Never criticise a former employer, colleagues, or products.*

### CV (Curriculum Vitae / Résumé)
Sections: Personal details → Education (most recent first) → Work experience (most recent first) → Skills → Referees (two: one professional, one academic)

### Covering Letter for CV
- Para 1: Job applied for; how you heard of it
- Para 2: Why you are suitable — link CV to job requirements
- Para 3: Availability; invite for interview

### Replying to an Application
**Invitation to interview:**
*We are pleased to invite you to attend an interview on [date] at [time] at [address]. Please confirm your attendance by [date].*

**Declining an applicant:**
- Thank for applying; state appointment has been made; wish them well
- No obligation to state reasons

### Offering a Post
1. State the post and start date
2. Salary + increments
3. Terms and conditions (hours, holidays, probation period)
4. Request written acceptance by [date]
5. Attach contract or state it will follow

**Accepting a post:**
*I am pleased to accept your offer of the post of [title] at a starting salary of [amount], to commence on [date].*

## 14. Uganda/East Africa Application Notes

**Letter style:** Block style with no punctuation in addresses is correct — matches current UK and international practice. Uganda banks and DFIs expect professional block-style letters; avoid old-fashioned indented style.

**Salutation for Ugandan banks:** *Dear Sir/Madam* is correct when writing to the Credit Manager or Branch Manager by title. If addressing a named contact, use *Dear Mr/Ms [Surname]* → *Yours sincerely*.

**Payment terms common in Uganda:**
- Cash on delivery or prepayment (most retail)
- 30–60 day open account (established supplier relationships)
- SWIFT bank transfer (for imports)
- Mobile money (MTN, Airtel) for small domestic transactions — functionally equivalent to bank transfer
- Letters of credit: used for imports >USD 10,000; facilitated by Uganda's tier-1 banks (Stanbic, ABSA, dfcu, Centenary)

**Bills of exchange in Uganda:** Less common for domestic trade; used for larger export/import contracts. Bank of Uganda governs foreign exchange; BOU approval required for B/E transactions above USD 50,000.

**Incoterms for Uganda imports:**
- *CIF Mombasa* or *CIF Dar es Salaam* → buyer arranges inland transport from port
- *DDP [Kampala]* → seller bears all costs including Uganda customs duty (uncommon; used for sensitive imports)
- *EXW [factory]* → buyer arranges all freight (common for regional East African trade)

**Complaints:** Ugandan business culture values relationship maintenance — complaints should be even more diplomatically worded than the Ashley standard. Lead with the business relationship: *"Given our long trading partnership, we are confident this was an oversight…"*

**Credit terms:** SACCO and microfinance lenders rarely grant open account. Use of post-dated cheques is common in Uganda as an informal credit mechanism — acknowledge in payment terms section of operations plan.

**Memo format for Ugandan businesses:** Same block format; memos are standard in formally registered businesses; WhatsApp group messages often replace memos for micro-businesses — note this distinction in the operations plan.
