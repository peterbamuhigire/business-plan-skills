## 1. Trend Identification Framework

### PESTEL Analysis for Trend Mapping

| Factor | What to Analyse | Impact on Market |
|---|---|---|
| **Political** | Government policy, trade agreements, stability | Regulatory enablers/barriers |
| **Economic** | GDP growth, inflation, disposable income, currency | Purchasing power, investment climate |
| **Social** | Demographics, cultural shifts, health consciousness | Demand patterns, preferences |
| **Technological** | AI adoption, digital infrastructure, innovation rate | Disruption potential, efficiency |
| **Environmental** | Climate policy, sustainability demand, resource scarcity | Compliance costs, green premiums |
| **Legal** | Data privacy, employment law, IP protection | Operational constraints |


## 2. Digital Transformation Indicators

Key indicators that signal digital maturity of an industry:

- **E-commerce penetration** — % of sales occurring online
- **Digital ad spend share** — % of marketing budget in digital channels
- **SaaS adoption rate** — % of businesses using cloud solutions
- **AI integration level** — % of companies using AI in operations
- **Mobile transaction share** — % of purchases via mobile devices
- **Data-driven decision making** — % of companies using analytics for strategy

### Industry Digital Maturity Stages

| Stage | Characteristics | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Analogue** | Paper-based, face-to-face, manual | Massive disruption opportunity |
| **Digitising** | Moving processes online, early adoption | Growing but still fragmented |
| **Digital-first** | Online primary, offline secondary | Competitive, requires tech edge |
| **AI-augmented** | Automated decisions, predictive models | High barrier, high efficiency |
| **Immersive** | AR/VR, metaverse, phygital experiences | Frontier — first-mover advantage |

## 3. Kotler's Five Components of Digital Marketing

Every industry's digital marketing landscape has five components (Kotler 6.0):

1. **Content** — Information created and shared across digital media (articles, videos, infographics, whitepapers)
2. **Social media** — Primary channel for distribution and amplification
3. **E-commerce** — Digital transaction infrastructure
4. **Data analytics** — Measurement, attribution, and optimisation
5. **AI/Automation** — Personalisation, prediction, and workflow automation

When analysing a market, assess the maturity of each component within the industry.

## 4. Phygital Marketing Evolution

Kotler's framework for understanding how physical and digital experiences merge:

**Phygital = Physical + Digital**

Industries are moving toward blended experiences:
- Retail: Physical stores + AR try-on + online ordering
- Healthcare: In-person visits + telemedicine + wearable data
- Education: Classrooms + online courses + VR labs
- Financial services: Branches + mobile banking + AI advisors

### Immersive Marketing Readiness Assessment

For the target market, evaluate:
- What % of target customers use AR/VR technology?
- What competitors offer immersive experiences?
- What is the cost-benefit of phygital vs. purely digital approaches?
- Is the industry's customer base Gen Z/Alpha dominant?

## 5. Generational Market Shifts

### Generation Z and Alpha as Market Drivers (Kotler 6.0)

Gen Z (born 1997-2012) and Gen Alpha (born 2013+) are "phygital natives":

- **Accelerated maturity** — Adopt mature mindsets faster than previous generations
- **Highly pragmatic** — Value authenticity over brand prestige
- **Self-expression** — Seek personalisation in products and experiences
- **Digital-first discovery** — Find brands through social media, not traditional advertising
- **Values-driven purchasing** — Prioritise sustainability, inclusivity, ethics

**Business plan implication:** If these generations are in the target market, the plan must address:
- Social media as a primary marketing channel
- Content authenticity over polished corporate messaging
- Personalisation capabilities
- Values alignment (sustainability, diversity)
- Mobile-first and potentially immersive experiences
