## 8. Financial Access & Household Enterprises

*Source: UNHS 2023/24*

- **31%** of households operate a non-crop farming household enterprise
- Enterprise types: Trade 50%, Manufacturing 17%
- 35% of enterprises located at home
- **77%** of enterprises funded by own savings; 13% required no capital
- Most enterprises have no formal registration

**Bank credit (Q1 2025/26):**
- Commercial bank loans to private sector: **UGX 72.1 trillion**
- Personal/household loans: 25.4% of total (UGX 18.4 trillion)
- Building/mortgage/construction/real estate: UGX 13.4 trillion

## 11. Agriculture Sector Context

*Source: UHIS 2021/22 (UBOS), NPHC 2024, World Bank 2023*

- **33%** of households are in the subsistence economy (NPHC 2024)
- Of subsistence households, **75% rely on subsistence agriculture**
- At end of NDP II: 68.9% of households were still in the subsistence economy
- Agricultural sector: ~6 in 10 of total workers are in agriculture

|---|---|
| 2014/15 | 926 |
| 2015/16 | 808 |
| 2016/17 | 824 |
| 2017/18 | 855 |
| 2018/19 | 889 |
| 2019/20 | 916 |
| 2020/21 | 957 |
| 2021/22 | 1,042 |
| 2022/23 | 1,081 |
| 2023/24 | 1,159 |
| 2024/25 | **1,306** |

> Uganda's GDP per capita crossed USD 1,000 in 2021/22 and reached USD 1,306 by 2024/25. This trajectory reflects consistent growth; however, at UGX ~3,550/USD, this equates to approximately UGX 389,000/month per capita at national average — below the UGX 167,573/month actual household consumption (reflecting distribution inequality and measurement differences).
