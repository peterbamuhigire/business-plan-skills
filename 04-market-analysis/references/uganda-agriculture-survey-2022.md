# Uganda Agriculture Survey 2021/22
**Source:** Uganda Bureau of Statistics (UBOS). (2023). *Annual Agricultural Survey 2021/22*. Kampala: UBOS.
**Relevance:** Crop production benchmarks, livestock data, input adoption rates, farm size planning, agricultural market sizing, agribusiness business plans

---

## 1. Agricultural Household Sector

- **Total agricultural households:** 6.95 million (national)
- **Share of all households:** ~62% (NPHC 2024)
- **Gender of household head:** 68.7% male | 31.3% female (Karamoja highest female: 62.6%)
- **Literacy:** 68% of ag HH heads literate (78% male, 48% female)
- **Activity participation:**
  - Crop cultivation: **95%** of ag HHs
  - Livestock keeping: **70%**
  - Both crop and livestock: **71%**

**Regional distribution (top 5 subregions):**
- Buganda South: 16.3% (1.13M HHs) | Buganda North: 12.8% | Ankole: 9.8% | Busoga: 8.3% | Lango: 7.9%

## 4. Livestock

|---|---|
| Small ruminants (goats/sheep) | 68.3% |
| Poultry | 64.7% |

**Cattle detail:**
- 74.5% of cattle-keeping HHs have at least one cow
- 41.4% have calves; 41.2% have bulls; 25.5% have oxen
- Regional variation: Ankole 64.1% (exotic cattle dominant), Teso 56.5%, Lango 52.6% (cattle-keeping highest rates)

**Poultry regional variation:** Bukedi 82%, Elgon 75%, Lango 73.6% (highest); Kigezi 36.5% (lowest)

## 5. Agricultural Inputs

### Fertiliser Use (Critical — Very Low Adoption)
| Measure | Season 2, 2021 | Season 1, 2022 |
|---|---|---|
| HHs applying any fertiliser | 22.5% | 22.6% |
| Crop area under fertiliser | 17.0% | 13.6% |
| **Inorganic fertiliser only** | **7.4%** | **5.2%** |
| Organic (animal dung, compost) | 17.8% | 19.6% |

- Highest inorganic use: Kigezi 52–71%, Ankole 43–45%; Lowest: Acholi 1.5–2.1%
- *Uganda applies 3–8 kg/ha vs SSA average 60 kg/ha, Kenya 55 kg/ha (WB Dec 2025)*

### Seeds
- Improved seed adoption: **4.0%** (season 2); **3.9%** (season 1) — very low
- Traditional seeds dominant: 95.7%
- Exception: Karamoja 26.3% improved seed (drought-resistant variety push)
- Purchased seed varies by crop: Irish potatoes 52%, rice 29–52%, beans 37–45%, maize 32–35%, sweet potatoes 8–12% (lowest)

### Agro-Chemicals / Pesticides
- 14.6% (season 2), 10.8% (season 1) of crop-growing HHs use agro-chemicals
- Highest: Buganda North 29%, Buganda South 24%; Lowest: Karamoja 1.6%

### Irrigation
- **0.7%** of crop-growing HHs have irrigation system (national)
- Highest: Buganda South 6%; Methods: flooding/surface 8.1%, manual irrigation
- *Uganda: <1% irrigated vs Kenya 16%, Tanzania 10%, Rwanda 10% (WB Dec 2025)*

## 9. Agro-Processing Sector Context (WB Dec 2025 Update)

- Agro-processing contributes **~65% of manufacturing output**
- Generates **USD 13.24 billion** in value; employs ~400,000 people
- Geographic concentration: Buganda, Busoga, Tooro host 57% of 10,669 agribusinesses
- **SME processing multiplier:** USD 2.86 GDP per USD 1 invested; 1.2 jobs per USD 1,000 spent
- NDP IV Ten-Fold Growth Strategy target: agro-industrialisation to contribute **USD 20 billion/year by 2040**

## 11. Key Constraints for Business Plans

| Constraint | Data Point |
|---|---|
| Fertiliser use | 5–8 kg/ha (SSA avg 60 kg/ha) |
| Improved seed adoption | 4% |
| Irrigation coverage | 0.7% of HHs |
| Mechanisation | <2% of landholdings (WB 2025) |
| Extension access | 24% of farmers (WB 2025; Kenya 75%) |
| Counterfeit agro-inputs | 30% of market (WB 2025) |
| Soil degradation | 41% of Uganda's land degraded (WB 2025) |
