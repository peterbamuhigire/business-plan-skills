Comprehensive market measurement formulas from Farris's "Marketing Metrics."

## 1. Market Size Metrics

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)

```
TAM = Total number of potential buyers x Average annual spend per buyer
```

**Bottom-up validation:**
```
TAM = (Population in geography)
    x (% matching demographic criteria)
    x (% with relevant need/problem)
    x (Average spend on solutions)
```

### Serviceable Available Market (SAM)

```
SAM = TAM x (% reachable with current business model)

Filtering factors:
- Geographic reach
- Distribution channel coverage
- Language/cultural fit
- Regulatory access
- Price point alignment
```

### Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)

```
SOM = SAM x (Realistic capture rate)

Capture rate influenced by:
- Competitive intensity
- Brand awareness timeline
- Sales capacity
- Marketing budget relative to market
```

## 2. Market Share Metrics

### Unit Share vs. Revenue Share (Farris)

```
Unit Market Share (%) = Unit sales of brand / Total unit sales in market x 100

Revenue Market Share (%) = Revenue of brand / Total market revenue x 100
```

**When they diverge:** If revenue share > unit share, the brand commands premium pricing. If unit share > revenue share, the brand competes on volume/price.

### Relative Market Share

```
Relative Market Share = Brand's market share / Largest competitor's share
```

- Value > 1.0 = market leader
- Value = 0.5 = half the size of the leader
- Used in BCG matrix positioning

### Brand Development Index (BDI)

```
BDI = (% of brand sales in segment / % of population in segment) x 100
```

- BDI > 100 = brand over-indexes in segment (strength)
- BDI < 100 = brand under-indexes (opportunity or weakness)

### Category Development Index (CDI)

```
CDI = (% of category sales in segment / % of population in segment) x 100
```

- High CDI + Low BDI = high opportunity (category exists, brand doesn't)
- Low CDI + Low BDI = low priority (weak category, weak brand)

## 3. Market Growth Metrics

### Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR)

```
CAGR = (Ending value / Beginning value)^(1/years) - 1
```

### Year-over-Year Growth

```
YoY Growth (%) = (Current year - Prior year) / Prior year x 100
```

### Market Growth Rate

```
Market Growth Rate = (Market size end of period - Market size start) / Market size start x 100
```

## 4. Market Penetration Metrics

### Market Penetration Rate

```
Penetration Rate = Customers who purchased / Total addressable customers x 100
```

### Penetration Share

```
Penetration Share = Brand penetration / Category penetration x 100
```

### Share of Requirements (Share of Wallet)

```
Share of Requirements = Purchases of brand by its customers / Total category purchases by those same customers x 100
```

High share of requirements = loyal customer base
Low share of requirements = customers also buying from competitors

### Heavy Usage Index

```
Heavy Usage Index = Average consumption by brand buyers / Average consumption by all category buyers x 100
```

- Index > 100 = brand attracts heavy users (valuable)
- Index < 100 = brand attracts light users

## 5. Industry Concentration Metrics

### Concentration Ratio (CR)

```
CR4 = Combined market share of top 4 firms
CR8 = Combined market share of top 8 firms
```

| CR4 Value | Industry Structure |
|---|---|
| 0-40% | Competitive (many small players) |
| 40-60% | Moderately concentrated |
| 60-80% | Oligopolistic |
| 80-100% | Highly concentrated / monopolistic |

### Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI)

```
HHI = Sum of (market share of each firm)^2
```

| HHI Range | Competition Level |
|---|---|
| < 1,500 | Competitive |
| 1,500 - 2,500 | Moderately concentrated |
| > 2,500 | Highly concentrated |

**Why this matters for business plans:** High concentration means barriers to entry are high but available market may be underserved. Low concentration means easier entry but more competition.
