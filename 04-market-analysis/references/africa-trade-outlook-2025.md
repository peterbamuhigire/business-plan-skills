## 2. Africa Macroeconomic Summary (2024)

| Indicator | 2023 | 2024 | Trend |
|---|---|---|---|
| Africa real GDP growth | 3.3% | **3.4%** | Slight recovery |
| Continental inflation (avg) | 18.2% | **19.3%** | Still elevated |
| Africa fiscal deficit (% GDP) | 4.3% | **5.8%** | Widening |
| Africa debt-to-GDP ratio | 69.6% | **67.2%** (est) | Declining |
| Interest payments (% govt revenue) | — | **27.5%** | High burden |
| Effective borrowing cost | 5.4–6.3% (2008–19) | **8.2%** (2024) | Rising sharply |
| Current account deficit (% GDP) | ~2.0% | **2.4%** | Widening |

**Growth drivers:** Household consumption contributed +2.7 pp to GDP growth (2024). Net exports subtracted −0.8 pp — Africa's structural trade deficit persists.

**Growth inhibitors:** Terrorism (Sahel), conflict (DRC, Sudan), climate shocks (Somalia droughts, Mozambique cyclones).

## 6. Intra-African Trade (AfCFTA Impact)

| Year | Intra-Africa Total Trade | Change |
|---|---|---|
| 2023 | US$193 billion | — |
| 2024 | **US$208 billion** | **+7.7%** |

**By sub-region (intra-Africa exports, 2024):**
- Eastern Africa: **US$23.3B** (grew from US$29.5B → US$35.9B total, +21.7%)
- Western Africa: **US$28.8B**
- Northern Africa: US$20.6B (up from US$16.9B)
- Central Africa: US$10.6B (slowest growth)

**Key intra-Africa players:** South Africa (25% of intra-African exports, 12% of imports), Egypt, Nigeria, Côte d'Ivoire.

**AfCFTA effect:** Aims to reduce trade barriers and enhance intra-African cooperation. Implementation is driving measurable increases, particularly in Eastern and Western Africa.

|---|---|---|---|
| **Uganda** | B2 (Negative) | **B3 (Stable)** | **Downgraded** |
| Tanzania | B2 (Positive) | B1 (Stable) | **Upgraded** |
| Kenya | — | — | Positive outlook revised |

**Uganda downgrade reason:** Fiscal deterioration. Rating: B3 (Moody's) — sub-investment grade.
**Implication for business plans:** Uganda sovereign risk affects borrowing costs, DFI appetite, and investor risk premiums. Use country risk premium of 4–6% in WACC calculations (consistent with Damodaran methodology).

## 9. Africa Inflation Context

- Africa average inflation 2024: **19.3%** (up 1.1 pp)
- **Eastern Africa:** Slight disinflation, 21.2% → **20.9%** (2024) — still elevated
- Non-resource-intensive economies: **7.8%** (down from 12.5% in 2022) — disinflationary
- Southern Africa dropped sharply: 38.9% → 20.1% (Zimbabwe effect)
- Root cause of persistent inflation: currency depreciation vs. USD, agricultural disruptions

---

## 10. Africa Debt and Fiscal Stress

- Africa debt-to-GDP: **67.2%** (2024, down from 69.6%)
- Eastern Africa debt: **52.2%** (2024, down from ~60% in 2023) — excluding Sudan (354% of GDP)
- Africa interest payments: **5% of GDP** / **27.5% of government revenue** — crowding out investment
- Effective borrowing cost: **8.2%** (vs 5.4–6.3% pre-pandemic) — elevated risk premiums

**Policy implication:** Fiscal space is constrained across Africa. Business plans targeting government contracts or public sector funding should account for payment delays.

## 11. Business Plan Application Notes

**For market size framing:**
- Position Uganda/East Africa within the fastest-growing African sub-region (Eastern Africa 4.4%)
- Uganda GDP growth of 6.8% (2025 forecast) outperforms the continental average of 3.4% significantly
- Uganda inflation declining (4.0% in 2024, ~4.9% forecast 2025) — relatively stable cost environment

**For trade-exposed businesses:**
- Intra-Africa trade expanding — AfCFTA reducing barriers for EAC exporters
- East Africa intra-regional exports: US$23.3B — services and manufactured goods exporters benefit
- Uganda exports competitive: coffee and gold price surges (2024) improve foreign exchange availability

**For risk sections:**
- Uganda sovereign rating downgraded to B3 (Moody's) — incorporate country risk premium
- Uganda fiscal deficit improving (forecast −1.0% by 2027) but currently elevated at −4.9%
- High Africa-wide inflation (20.9% Eastern Africa) creates input cost uncertainty despite Uganda's lower domestic rate
