## Section B: Uganda Sectoral Data

### B1. Agriculture

- Share of GDP: **24%**; share of exports: **35%**; share of labour force: **68%**
- Arable land: 80% of Uganda (193,200 km²); only 35% currently cultivated
- **Productivity gaps vs peers:**

| Metric | Uganda | Kenya | Tanzania | Rwanda | SSA Avg |
|--------|--------|-------|----------|--------|---------|
| Fertiliser use (kg/ha) | 3–8 | 55 | 20 | 32 | 60 |
| Irrigation (% potential) | <1% | 16% | 10% | — | — |
| Improved seed use | 4% of crop farmers | — | — | — | — |
| Mechanisation | <2% of landholdings | 30% | — | — | — |
| Extension service access | 24% of farmers | 75% | — | 65% | — |
| Digital advice access | 63% | 92% | — | 78% | — |

- Land degradation: 41% of Uganda's land degraded; 39% prone to soil erosion
- Registered cooperatives: 45,159; 60% SACCOs, 30% agricultural commodity
- Agriculture share of bank lending: 11.3%
- Agricultural insurance penetration: <1%

**Agro-processing:**
- ~65% of manufacturing output
- Generates ~US$13.24 billion annually
- Employs ~400,000 workers
- Animal feed market: growing 5% annually; Uganda exported 219 million kg earning US$72M (2023)
- Livestock disease losses: US$1.1 billion annually

**Key cash crops (FY25 performance):**
- Coffee: nearly doubled (+US$1.1 billion); Uganda became Africa's largest coffee exporter (May 2025, 47,606.7 tons/month; annual revenue >US$2 billion)
- Cocoa: doubled (+US$0.4 billion)
- Coffee export detail (UEU-21): price US$2.4/kg; volume 1.32M bags (Q2 FY22/23); value US$417M (H1 FY22/23)

### B2. Manufacturing and Industry

- Industry: 26.5% of GDP (UEU-21)
- Construction growth: +12.2% (FY25)
- Agro-processing: ~65% of manufacturing output (see B1)

### B3. Services and ICT

- Services: 45% of GDP; grew 11.7% H1 FY22/23
- ICT: fastest-growing sub-sector in services
- Accommodation & food services: +10.6% growth (FY25)

### B4. Tourism

*(See Section H for deep dive)*

- Tourism contribution: 5.9% of GDP (2019); fell to 2.7% (2020); recovered to 4.2% (2021)
- Travel receipts (UEU-26): US$1.6 billion FY25, exceeding pre-pandemic FY19 peak; grew +16%
- Uganda vs peers: Rwanda 11%, Tanzania 10%, Kenya 8% of GDP from tourism

### B5. Oil and Gas

- **Tilenga project:** 190,000 bpd peak production (TotalEnergies-led)
- **Kingfisher project:** 40,000 bpd (CNOOC-led)
- **EACOP (East Africa Crude Oil Pipeline):** 1,443 km to Tanga, Tanzania; 62–75% complete
- **Investment:** >US$11 billion total
- **Timeline:** First oil July 2026; exports commence Q1 FY26/27
- GDP impact: accounts for FY27 growth surge to 9.8%

### B6. Infrastructure and Connectivity

- Road density: 662 km/1,000 km² vs SSA avg 1,370; LIC avg 2,110
- Only 0.28% of district roads paved
- Transport costs = 56% of costs in agriculture
- 60% of national road network vulnerable to flooding

## Section J: Stress Test Reference Data (Historical Shocks)

### J1. Historical Growth Volatility (Uganda)

| Period | Shock | GDP Impact |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| FY19/20–FY20/21 | COVID-19 | Growth fell; tourism from 5.9% to 2.7% GDP |
| FY22/23 | Global inflation shock | Inflation peaked 8.8%, food inflation 22.9% |
| FY22–FY23 | BoU rate cycle | CBR raised 350 bps in 12 months |

### J2. Climate Shock Projections (UEU-26)

By the 2040s, under current trajectories:
- Total crop production: could fall **1.4–2.6%**
- Livestock productivity: could fall **3%**
- Road network: 60% of national roads vulnerable to flooding

**Crop production impacts (Table 7, UEU-26):** effects vary by crop type — heat stress and water deficit projected to reduce yields across major staple crops. Details in original Table 7 of UEU-26.

### J3. External Shock Vulnerabilities

- Oil price shock: current account deficit of 6–8% GDP creates vulnerability; EACOP timeline delays would extend oil-import dependency
- ODA dependency: ODA >US$3.1 billion (2020), 6% of GNI; on-budget 18% of national budget. US accounts for >40% of Africa ODA — high concentration risk
- Commodity price risk: coffee near-doubled in FY25; reversal would significantly affect exports and current account
- Exchange rate: shilling appreciated 3% in FY25 and further 5.6% in Oct FY26 — planning rate of UGX 3,700/USD provides depreciation buffer
- Remittance risk: global remittances to Africa contracted 6.2% in 2023 (AfDB AEO 2025)

### J4. Debt Stress Indicators

- Debt service to exports: 42.3% (FY21/22) → 63% (FY22/23) — UEU-21; warning-level ratio
- Interest payments as % of tax revenue: 28.1% (FY25), up from 24.5% three-year average
- Domestic arrears: UGX 7.55 trillion (2022)
- Public debt trajectory: 46.6% (FY24) → 51.3% (FY25) → 52.8% (FY26) — rising but below Africa median (65.5%)

### J5. Sectoral Stress Reference Points

- Food insecurity peak: 56% (Oct/Nov 2022) → recovered to 38% within 2 months — resilience demonstrated
- Tourism shock: 5.9% GDP → 2.7% GDP in one year (COVID 2020) — sector can lose >50% of value rapidly
- COVID arrivals: 1.5 million (2019) → ~512,270 (2021); recovery to 814,508 by 2022
