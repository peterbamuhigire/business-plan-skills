# Africa Economic Outlook 2025
**Source:** African Development Bank (AfDB). (2025). *African Economic Outlook 2025*. Abidjan: AfDB Group.
**Relevance:** Africa-wide and East Africa GDP projections, inflation trajectories, fiscal/debt dynamics, capital mobilisation, risk factors, and country-level data for Uganda, Kenya, Tanzania, Rwanda, Ethiopia

---

## 1. Africa-Wide Growth Projections

| Metric | 2024 (Actual) | 2025 (Projected) | 2026 (Projected) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Real GDP growth | 3.3% | **3.9%** | **4.0%** |
| Per capita growth | 0.9% | 1.5% | 1.7% |
| Inflation | 18.7% | 13.8% | **9.9%** |
| Current account balance | -1.8% GDP | -2.6% GDP | -2.6% GDP |

**Global context:** Global growth 2.8% (2025); Euro area 1.0%; Latin America 2.2%; Asia (emerging/developing) 4.6%. **Africa outperforms global average and all regions except Asia.**

**Key headline:** 21 African countries will exceed 5% growth in 2025. Four countries (Ethiopia, Niger, Rwanda, Senegal) on track for 7%+. 2026 will be the first time since the pandemic that Africa-wide inflation falls to single digits.

## 3. East Africa Country Profiles (2025–26 Average)

| Country | GDP Growth | Inflation | Current Account | Fiscal Balance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Ethiopia** | **7.5%** | 13.5% | -1.5% GDP | -1.8% GDP |
| **Rwanda** | **7.7%** | 4.8% | -12.1% GDP | -4.7% GDP |
| **Uganda** | **6.8%** | 4.1% | -6.6% GDP | -5.7% GDP |
| **Djibouti** | 6.4% | 2.2% | +20.7% GDP | 0.0% GDP |
| **Tanzania** | 5.9% | 3.3% | -3.0% GDP | -3.0% GDP |
| **Kenya** | 4.9% | 4.2% | -3.2% GDP | -4.5% GDP |

### Uganda (Detail)
- Growth 6.8% — one of 10 African countries with >1.0pp increase from 2023 to 2024
- Supported by public investment in domestic value chains and energy infrastructure
- **Highest current account deficit** in East Africa peer group (-6.6%)
- Moderate inflation (4.1%) relative to Africa-wide average
- Fiscal deficit (-5.7%) improving toward oil revenue impact (2026–27)

### Kenya (Detail)
- Growth 4.9%; shilling appreciated 3.1% in 2024 (reversed 2023 depreciation)
- Improved market sentiment; KRA digitalization success (+12.7% revenue increase, 2016/17)
- Project Mocha: blockchain tokenization of coffee trees for smallholder farmers (pilot innovation)
- Fiscal deficit -4.5%; smallest current account deficit in EA peer group

### Tanzania (Detail)
- Growth 5.9%; **lowest inflation in East Africa at 3.3%** — most stable price environment
- **Best fiscal position in EA peer group** at -3.0% deficit
- Supported by public investment in domestic value chains and energy
- Greatest fiscal space for expanded public investment

### East Africa Resilience Factors
1. Diversified economies with growing manufactured goods share in intra-regional trade
2. 2023 EAC total trade: **USD 12.1 billion** (+13.1% YoY); intra-EAC trade share: 15%
3. Continued public investment in domestic value chains and energy infrastructure

## 4. Inflation Dynamics

| Period | Africa-wide Rate | EA Rate | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2024 (actual) | 18.7% | ~20.9% | Above medium-term targets; 15 countries double-digit |
| 2025 (projected) | **13.8%** | Declining | -0.4pp vs. Feb 2025 AEO |
| 2026 (projected) | **9.9%** | — | First sub-10% since pandemic |

**Primary inflation driver:** Domestic food supply shocks. **Supporting disinflation:** OPEC+ production increase reduces energy prices; improving agricultural supply; drought-resistant seeds adoption.

## 5. Fiscal & Debt Position

**Africa average fiscal deficit:** -5.8% GDP (2024); gradually returning to 3–5% GDP threshold.

**Africa debt-to-GDP:** 69.6% (2023) → 67.2% (2024 projected); above pre-pandemic 50% level.

**Interest burden:** 27.5% of government revenue spent on debt service (5% of GDP) — crowding out social investment.

**Debt distress:** 7–9 countries in distress (stable since 2021); Gambia and Ghana upgraded from distress to high-risk. Systemic risk "broadly contained."

**Domestic debt:** Rising from 32% (2010) to 39% (2023) of total public debt — increasing local currency exposure.

## 6. Capital Flows & External Position

**Total external inflows (2023):** USD 204.6 billion (+7.3% from 2022)
- Portfolio flows: Reversed from -USD 23.1B to +USD 322.9M (full reversal)
- ODA from DAC countries: USD 35.9B (US = >40% of total)
- Remittances: USD 91.1B received (contracted 6.2%); projected USD 100B in 2025

**Net capital position paradox:** Africa is a **net creditor to the world** despite being a capital-importing continent.
- External inflows (2022–23 avg): USD 196.8B/year
- Net resource outflow: **USD 390.2B/year**

**Remittance potential:** Formal market could reach USD 283 billion by 2035 (threefold increase from 2023) if transfer costs fall from 7% to 3% (UN SDG target). Diaspora bonds: USD 30B/year potential.

## 7. Domestic Capital Mobilisation — USD 1.43 Trillion Opportunity

Africa could mobilize **USD 1.43 trillion in additional domestic resources** — exceeding the USD 1.3 trillion annual SDG financing gap.

### Five Forms

| Capital Type | Potential | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **Fiscal** | **USD 469.4B/year** (14.4% of GDP, 2025–29) | Digitalize tax admin; broaden base; enforce existing regulations |
| **Natural** | 30-fold value uplift | Local mineral processing (DRC bauxite→aluminium: USD 65→USD 2,335/tonne) |
| **Financial** | USD 70B (pension funds, 6 countries) + USD 411.9B forex reserves | Ring-fence 1% pension AUM; leverage central bank reserves |
| **Business** | USD 560B AfCFTA export increase | Regional value chains; SME formalisation (USD 125.3B/year if 10pp informality reduction) |
| **Human** | 1–2% GDP growth per 10pp informality reduction | Demographic dividend; youth workforce (60%+ under 25) |

**Revenue leakage:** USD 275B/year in illicit flows + corruption + profit shifting — addressable for development financing.

**Government revenue-to-GDP gap:**
- Africa: 19.8% (only +2.3pp since 2015)
- Europe/Central Asia: 41.0% | Latin America: 28.6% | East Asia: 26.2%

**Kenya precedent:** KRA digitalization of tax procedures (2016) delivered **+12.7% annual revenue increase** — replicable model for other EA countries.

## 8. Downside Risks

### Tier 1 — External Shocks

**US Tariff Shock (April 2025):**
- Average 10% tariff announced; 90-day pause active
- US = 5% of Africa's total merchandise trade
- Growth revisions: -0.2 to -0.4pp across regions
- **Highest exposure:** Lesotho (apparel = 45% of exports to US); Botswana (diamonds)
- **Uganda/Tanzania:** Low direct exposure

**Aid Cuts:**
- DAC ODA to Africa (2023): USD 35.9B; US contribution: >40%
- Anticipated reduction: 7% in 2025 vs. 2023 (US USAID-led)
- Disproportionate impact on low-income, ODA-dependent countries

### Tier 2 — Macroeconomic

**Persistent Inflation:** Domestic supply bottlenecks; monetary policy transmission weakened; exchange rate depreciation pass-through.

**Capital Account Deterioration:** Widening current account deficits (-2.6% GDP 2025–26); trade uncertainty reducing investor appetite.

**Exchange Rate Volatility:** 28 countries recorded depreciation in 2023; 17 reversed in 2024 (Kenya shilling +3.1%, rand +0.7%).

### Tier 3 — Structural

**Debt Distress:** 7–9 countries at risk; high debt service limits fiscal space.

**Regional Conflicts:** DRC, Sahel — lower tax revenue, higher military spending, capital flight.

**Climate & Agricultural Vulnerability:** Food supply shocks are the primary inflation driver; drought/flooding disrupt harvest cycles.

---

## 9. Strategic Opportunities (2025–2030)

**AfCFTA full implementation:** USD 560B export increase (mostly manufacturing); regional value chains; 173M+ EAC market.

**Natural resource beneficiation:** 30-fold value increase via local processing; green minerals (cobalt, lithium, manganese) critical for global energy transition.

**Informal sector formalisation:** 50–65% of African economies informal; each 10pp reduction adds 1–2% GDP growth annually.

**Diaspora engagement:** Reduce remittance transfer costs (7% → 3%); securitize via diaspora bonds (USD 30B/year potential).

## 10. Business Plan Application Notes

**For market projections:**
- Use East Africa 5.9% as regional growth anchor (2025–26)
- Uganda 6.8% growth outperforms Africa 3.9% and global 2.8% significantly
- Tanzania's fiscal discipline (-3.0%) and low inflation (3.3%) position it as most stable EA market

**For risk section:**
- US tariff indirect risk: global slowdown → commodity price softening → Uganda/Tanzania export revenue pressure
- Aid cut risk: development project delays; government contract payment risk
- Climate risk: food supply shocks = persistent inflation upside risk despite monetary tightening

**For financing/funding section:**
- Reference USD 1.43T domestic capital mobilisation potential — supports DFI appetite for EA deals
- Kenya digitalization precedent supports tax compliance assumptions in financial models
- Remittance income (Uganda USD 1.4B/year) = stable consumer income underpinning demand projections
