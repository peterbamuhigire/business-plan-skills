# Risk Sensitivity Analysis

> References: *Strategy Plain and Simple* by Evans (Pearson, 2018); *The Strategist's Toolkit* by Harris & Lenox (Darden, 2013); *The Portable MBA in Strategy* ed. Fahey & Randall (Wiley, 2000)

> Figures adapted to Ugandan context (UGX). Percentages and ratios from the sources are universal.

---

## 1. What Sensitivity Analysis Does

- Identifies the **leverage points** — the assumptions where small changes produce large swings in profitability or cash flow
- Links directly to the financial projections section of the business plan
- Provides investors with transparency about what must go right (and what happens if it does not)

## 2. One-Variable Sensitivity Table

Change one assumption at a time while holding others constant.

**Example: Kampala Solar Installer — Sensitivity of Year 3 Net Profit**

| Variable | -20% | -10% | Base Case | +10% | +20% |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Installation price per unit** | (UGX 45M) loss | UGX 28M | UGX 100M | UGX 173M | UGX 245M |
| **Units sold per month** | (UGX 20M) loss | UGX 40M | UGX 100M | UGX 160M | UGX 220M |
| **Cost of panels (import)** | UGX 180M | UGX 140M | UGX 100M | UGX 60M | UGX 20M |
| **Staff costs** | UGX 130M | UGX 115M | UGX 100M | UGX 85M | UGX 70M |
| **Exchange rate (UGX/USD)** | UGX 160M | UGX 130M | UGX 100M | UGX 70M | UGX 40M |

**Reading:** Installation price and units sold are the highest-leverage variables — a 20% drop in either pushes the business into loss. Panel costs and exchange rate are also significant. Staff costs are less sensitive.

## 4. Break-Even Sensitivity

Identify the threshold value for each key variable at which the business breaks even:

| Variable | Base Case Value | Break-Even Threshold | Margin of Safety |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monthly units sold | 25 units | 18 units | 28% below plan |
| Installation price | UGX 12M per unit | UGX 10.2M | 15% below plan |
| Panel cost | UGX 1,300,000 per unit | UGX 1,550,000 per unit | 20% above plan |
| Exchange rate | UGX 3,800/USD | UGX 4,500/USD | 18% depreciation |

**Investor insight:** The business can tolerate an 18% UGX depreciation before breaking even — reasonable resilience. However, a 15% price decline would eliminate profit — pricing power is a critical assumption to validate.

## 5. Linking Sensitivity to Risk Mitigation

For each high-sensitivity variable, the business plan should include:

1. **Evidence** supporting the base case assumption
2. **Monitoring mechanism** — what data will you track to detect deviation early?
3. **Trigger point** — at what threshold will you activate the contingency plan?
4. **Contingency action** — specific steps to take if the variable moves adversely

| High-Sensitivity Variable | Evidence for Base Case | Monitor | Trigger | Contingency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Installation price (UGX 12M) | Competitor pricing survey; 3 signed contracts at this price | Monthly win/loss analysis; average deal price | Average price drops below UGX 11M for 2 consecutive months | Introduce value-added maintenance package to justify premium; reduce component costs |
| Units sold (25/month) | Pipeline of 40 qualified leads; conversion rate of 60% in pilot | Weekly pipeline report; monthly conversion rate | Pipeline falls below 30 leads or conversion below 50% | Increase marketing spend by 30%; add referral incentive; expand to commercial segment |
