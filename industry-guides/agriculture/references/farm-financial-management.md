# Farm Financial Management

> Reference: *The Organic Farmer's Business Handbook* by Richard Wiswall (Chelsea Green Publishing, 2009)
> Figures adapted to Ugandan context (UGX). Percentages and ratios from the source are universal.

## 1. Core Financial Equation

The fundamental business equation for any farm enterprise:

```
Profit = Income - Expenses
```

A farm's overall annual profit is merely an **average** of all its individual enterprises. Each crop or enterprise has its own profitability equation. Some enterprises are highly profitable, some break even, and some **lose money**. The key to increased farm profitability is identifying which enterprises fall into which category.

### Worked Example: The Problem of Averages

A farm with UGX 150M+ gross sales can yield a net profit of only UGX 1.4M (approximately UGX 3,800/day) when costs are not managed at the enterprise level.

## 2. Planning for Profit

Profit should be **planned proactively**, not treated as whatever is left over at year's end.

### Five-Year Profit Planning

| Year | Gross Sales (UGX) | Net Profit (UGX) |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Current | 15M | 3M |
| Year 1 | 30M | 12M |
| Year 2 | 50M | 22M |
| Year 3 | 75M | 32M |
| Year 4 | 100M | 45M |
| Year 5 | 130M | 55M |

Typical farm expenses mean gross sales need to be **2-3x the desired net profit**.

## 3. Enterprise Budgets and Crop Budgets

### Simple Crop Budget Structure

A simple crop budget (excluding fixed costs) allows **side-by-side comparison** of crops:

**Example: Carrots (5 x 100m beds = 1/4 acre)**

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Labour rate | UGX 8,000/hour (includes NSSF, insurance, unassigned time) |
| Tractor + implement rate | UGX 20,000/hour |
| Net profit (1/4 acre) | UGX 6M |
| **Net profit per acre** | **UGX 24M** |

Compare this with average gross sales of only UGX 8M/acre before enterprise budgeting.

### Comprehensive Crop Budget Components

Before calculating crop budgets, determine:

1. **Average hourly employee labour rate** (base wage + NSSF + insurance + unassigned time)
2. **Farmer's hourly rate** (counted as cost even though no payslip is written)
3. **Total overhead costs** (telephone, insurance, taxes, office, advertising, utilities)
4. **Greenhouse/nursery costs** per unit area
5. **Tractor and implement cost per hour**
6. **Irrigation cost per unit area per use**
7. **Delivery/transport costs**
8. **Market fees and marketing costs**

### Labour Rate Calculation

Base employee wage: UGX 5,000/hour

Additional costs applied:
- NSSF contributions: 10% of payroll (employer portion)
- Workers' compensation/insurance: ~3-5% of payroll
- Unassigned time (meetings, cleanup): variable

**All-in labour rate: UGX 8,000/hour**

The farmer's field hours are also costed at this rate, even though no payslip is written. This ensures:
- All production hours are accounted for
- If the farmer is incapacitated, replacement labour keeps the crop profitable
- The farmer earns both hourly "wages" and any crop net profit

### Tractor and Implement Cost Formula

```
Hourly cost = (Purchase price / Years of useful life + Annual repairs + Annual fuel)
              / Hours used per year
```

A tractor used 50 hours/year costs significantly more per hour than one used 300 hours/year.

Implement costs follow the same formula but without fuel expense.

### Overhead Appropriation

Total overhead is divided across all crop budgets by percentage of acreage or sales. Example split:
- 75% allocated to 2 hectares of field crops
- 25% allocated to nursery/greenhouse operations

**All overhead costs must be assigned somewhere** — otherwise off-farm income subsidises the farm.

### Market Day Cost

Cost for two people to load, set up, staff, and travel for **one** market day: **UGX 180,000**. This is a fixed cost regardless of sales. Gross sales below UGX 180,000 at a single market make that market unprofitable.

## 4. Index of Profitability

Rank all crops by **net return per acre** (not gross sales). This single sheet of paper drives all management decisions:

- Seek new markets for top earners
- Scrutinise sale prices and costs for bottom-rung crops
- Drop unprofitable crops altogether
- Challenge the "loss leader" paradigm

**Key insight:** High sale price does not guarantee high net return. Gross sales and net profit are largely unrelated.

### Profitability Examples

- A single sukuma wiki (kale) plot can yield 10+ harvests per season = potentially UGX 100,000+ per bed
- High-value vegetables (tomatoes, peppers): UGX 20M-40M net profit per acre
- Low-value staples (maize, beans): may be unprofitable on small acreage when all costs are included

### Balance Sheet

Measures assets and liabilities **at a specific moment in time**. Formula:

```
Net Worth = Assets - Liabilities
```

**Sample Balance Sheet structure:**
- **Short-term assets:** Farm bank/mobile money balance, crop inventory, accounts receivable
- **Medium-term assets:** Farm equipment, greenhouses, vehicles (at fair market value)
- **Long-term assets:** Land, buildings, permanent crops (coffee, fruit trees)
- **Short-term liabilities:** Accounts payable, SACCO/microfinance loans
- **Medium-term liabilities:** Equipment loans
- **Long-term liabilities:** Mortgage, UDB/bank land loans

Track net worth year to year to measure whether the farm is gaining or losing financial ground.

**Key insight:** Farm purchases may create a low year-end net profit whilst increasing net worth. The Balance Sheet reveals this.

### Balance Sheet Example: Tractor Purchase Impact

| | No Purchase | UGX 45M Tractor |
|--|-------------|-----------------|
| Net profit | UGX 55M | UGX 10M |
| Taxes (30%) | -UGX 16.5M | -UGX 3M |
| Profit after tax | UGX 38.5M | UGX 7M |
| Tractor value | UGX 0 | +UGX 45M |
| **Net worth** | **UGX 38.5M** | **UGX 52M** |

### Cash Flow Projection

A multi-year road map in numbers, extending the P&L with projected future years. Key points:

- Start with last-known sales and expense figures as a benchmark
- Model proposed changes (new equipment, expanded production, new markets)
- **A profitable farm can be put out of business by lack of cash flow**
- Farm cash flows are "lumpy" — heavy expenses early (land preparation, planting), income later (harvest)
- Line of credit, SACCO savings, or cash reserves are essential to bridge seasonal gaps

## 6. Tax Impact and Business Spending

### Tax Burden for Farm Businesses in Uganda

For registered farm businesses, key taxes include:

| Tax | Rate |
|-----|------|
| Income tax (individual, UGX 120M+ bracket) | 30% |
| PAYE (employees) | 10-30% graduated |
| NSSF (employer contribution) | 10% |
| WHT on supplies | 6% |
| VAT (if registered, turnover > UGX 150M) | 18% |

**Note:** Agriculture has various tax exemptions in Uganda — consult URA for current rules on unprocessed agricultural produce.

### The "Shilling Saved" Rule

```
A shilling saved in expenses is worth approximately UGX 1.40 earned in revenue
```

Because of the tax burden, you must earn UGX 1.40 to spend UGX 1.00 on non-farm purchases. Every unrecorded farm expense costs you in unnecessary taxes.

### Tax Reduction Through Capital Purchases

**Example:** Farm net profit of UGX 55M

| Scenario | No Purchase | UGX 45M Tractor |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Net profit | UGX 55M | UGX 10M |
| Taxes (30%) | UGX 16.5M | UGX 3M |
| After-tax income | UGX 38.5M | UGX 7M |
| Tractor value | UGX 0 | UGX 45M |
| **Net position** | **UGX 38.5M** | **UGX 52M** |

The UGX 45M tractor effectively costs only UGX 31.5M after tax savings.

**Caution:** Things that rust, rot, and depreciate are **expenses, not investments**. Investments must increase in value over time.

## 7. Retirement and Investment Planning

### Investment Growth Comparison (Single UGX 40M Investment)

| Option | Year 1 | Year 10 | Year 20 | Year 30 |
|--------|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| Under mattress (0%) | 40M | 40M | 40M | 40M |
| Unit trust/equity fund (12%) | 40M | 124M | 386M | 1.2B |
| Treasury bills/bonds (10%) | 40M | 104M | 269M | 697M |
| Fixed deposit (8%) | 40M | 86M | 186M | 437M |

**Note:** Uganda's higher interest rate environment means treasury bills and fixed deposits offer stronger returns than in developed markets, but inflation must be factored in.

### Retirement Savings for Farm Businesses

- NSSF contributions for employees are mandatory (employer 10%, employee 5%)
- Voluntary additional contributions to NSSF or private pension funds
- Consider unit trusts (e.g., UAP, Britam, ICEA) for longer-term farm savings
- Reinvesting in farmland remains a strong long-term asset in Uganda

## 8. Preventing Financial Leaks

### Common Sales Leaks

| Leak Type | Impact | Prevention |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| No invoice/receipt written | Sales completely lost to records | Write receipt for every transaction |
| Lost invoice | Cannot chase payment | Use triplicate invoice books |
| Maths errors | Over/undercharging | Double-check; use bookkeeping software or mobile apps |
| Market overgiving | Revenue per unit drops significantly | Monitor actual weight/count vs charged |
| Good weight at market | UGX 500,000 loss on 400 kg tomatoes at UGX 3,000/kg | Use digital scales (UGX 150,000-300,000, pays for itself in 1-2 market days) |

### Scales Example

Selling 400 kg of tomatoes at UGX 3,000/kg:
- Digital scale (exact weight): **UGX 1,200,000 revenue**
- Estimated weight (good measure, 1.1 kg charged as 1 kg): **UGX 1,090,000 revenue**
- **Loss: UGX 110,000 per market day, per item**

### Expense Leaks

- Unrecorded farm expenses cost ~30% of their value in extra taxes
- Vehicle mileage/fuel log: tracking 4,000 km of farm business travel at UGX 500/km = **UGX 2M in deductible expenses**
- Hiring a part-time bookkeeper (UGX 300,000-500,000/month) typically recovers their cost in found errors and tax savings

## 9. Cash vs. Accrual Accounting

| Method | Principle | Best For |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Cash accounting | Record when money changes hands | Simpler; mirrors how a bank/mobile money account works |
| Accrual accounting | Record when transaction occurs; uses inventory | More accurate picture of each year's performance |

**Example:** Pre-buying UGX 3M of fertiliser in December:
- Cash method: Current year's profit drops by UGX 3M; next year's profit inflated by UGX 3M
- Accrual method: Both years reflect actual usage accurately through inventory adjustments
