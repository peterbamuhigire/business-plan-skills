# Farm Financial Analysis Reference

> Source: *Whole Farm Management* by Garry Stephenson (Storey Publishing, 2019)

> Figures adapted to Ugandan context (UGX). Percentages and ratios from the source are universal.

## Financial Statements

### Three Core Financial Statements

| Statement | Purpose | Frequency |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Profit & Loss (Income Statement) | Shows profitability over a period | Monthly/Annually |
| Cash Flow Statement | Tracks actual cash movement | Monthly |
| Balance Sheet | Snapshot of assets, liabilities, equity | Annually (minimum) |

### Cash vs Accrual Accounting

| Method | Revenue Recognition | Expense Recognition | Best For |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|----------|
| Cash basis | When payment received | When payment made | Small farms, simplicity |
| Accrual basis | When earned/invoiced | When incurred | Larger farms, accurate period matching |

> **Key difference**: A farm delivering UGX 7.5M of produce in December but paid in January would show that revenue in December (accrual) or January (cash). Accrual gives a more accurate picture of true profitability per period.

## Budgeting

### Four Budget Types

1. **Income Budget** — Projects revenue by crop/product and market channel
2. **Expense Budget** — Estimates all operating and capital costs
3. **Cash Flow Budget** — Maps timing of inflows and outflows monthly
4. **Assets, Liabilities & Equity Budget** — Projects balance sheet changes

### Cash Flow Statement Example (Monthly Pattern)

| Month | Starting Cash | Inflows | Outflows | Ending Cash |
|-------|-------------|---------|----------|-------------|
| Jan | UGX 15M | UGX 750,000 | UGX 4.8M | UGX 11M |
| Feb | UGX 11M | UGX 750,000 | UGX 6.8M | UGX 4.9M |
| Mar | UGX 4.9M | UGX 1.5M | UGX 10.2M | -UGX 3.8M |
| Apr | -UGX 3.8M | UGX 3M | UGX 10.8M | -UGX 11.6M |
| May | -UGX 11.6M | UGX 7.5M | UGX 9.8M | -UGX 13.9M |
| Jun | -UGX 13.9M | UGX 18M | UGX 9M | -UGX 4.9M |
| Jul | -UGX 4.9M | UGX 22.5M | UGX 9.8M | UGX 7.8M |
| Aug | UGX 7.8M | UGX 27M | UGX 10.5M | UGX 24.3M |

> **Critical insight**: This farm needs a **line of credit or reserve of at least UGX 15M** to cover the cash deficit from March through June. Maximum cash deficit reaches -UGX 13.9M in May.

### Variance Analysis

```
Variance = Actual Value - Budgeted Value
Variance % = (Actual - Budgeted) / Budgeted x 100
```

**Worked example:**
- Budgeted seed cost: UGX 5.5M
- Actual seed cost: UGX 6.3M
- Variance: UGX 6.3M - UGX 5.5M = **UGX 800,000 unfavourable (15% over budget)**
- Action threshold: Investigate any variance exceeding **10%**

## Depreciation

### Straight-Line Depreciation

```
Annual Depreciation = (Purchase Price - Salvage Value) / Useful Life
```

**Worked example — Rototiller:**
- Purchase price: UGX 7.8M
- Salvage value: UGX 400,000
- Useful life: 10 years
- Annual depreciation: (UGX 7.8M - UGX 400,000) / 10 = **UGX 740,000/year**
- Book value after 5 years: UGX 7.8M - (5 x UGX 740,000) = **UGX 4.1M**

### Tax Depreciation Systems

| System | Method | Typical Farm Assets |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| Straight-line | Equal annual deductions | Most farm equipment |
| Reducing balance | Accelerated (higher early deductions) | Vehicles, machinery |
| Initial allowance | Partial first-year deduction | New qualifying assets |
| Full write-off | Immediate full deduction for small items | Items below UGX 1M |

### Operating vs Capital Expenses

| Criteria | Operating Expense | Capital Expense |
|----------|------------------|----------------|
| Useful life | Less than 1 year | More than 1 year |
| Cost threshold | Below capitalisation limit | Above capitalisation limit (commonly UGX 1M+) |
| Tax treatment | Fully deductible in current year | Depreciated over useful life |
| Examples | Seed, fuel, fertiliser, labour | Tractor, building, irrigation system, land |

## Enterprise Analysis

### Enterprise Budgeting

An enterprise budget isolates the costs and returns of a **single crop or product line**.

```
Enterprise Profit = Gross Revenue - (Direct Costs + Allocated Overhead)
```

**Worked example — Tomato enterprise (1 acre):**

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Yield: 25,000 lbs @ 80% marketable | 20,000 lbs |
| Price: UGX 2,500/lb average | |
| **Gross Revenue** | **UGX 50M** |
| Transplants | UGX 1.8M |
| Fertiliser & amendments | UGX 1.5M |
| Pest management | UGX 800,000 |
| Irrigation (variable cost) | UGX 600,000 |
| Harvest labour (500 hrs @ UGX 7,000) | UGX 3.5M |
| Packing & transport | UGX 1.5M |
| **Total Direct Costs** | **UGX 9.7M** |
| Allocated overhead (20% of direct) | UGX 1.9M |
| **Total Costs** | **UGX 11.6M** |
| **Enterprise Profit** | **UGX 38.4M** |
| **Return per acre** | **UGX 38.4M** |

### Pricing Methods

| Method | Approach | Formula |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Cost-based | Cost plus desired margin | Price = Total Cost per Unit x (1 + Markup %) |
| Competition-based | Match or undercut market | Price = Market Price +/- adjustment |
| Value-based | Customer perceived value | Price = what market will bear for quality/brand |

**Cost-based pricing example:**
- Total cost per lb (tomatoes): UGX 11.6M / 20,000 = UGX 580/lb
- Target markup: 200%
- Minimum price: UGX 580 x (1 + 2.0) = **UGX 1,740/lb**
- Actual market price: UGX 2,500/lb — margin of **UGX 1,920/lb** or 71.2%

## Financial Ratios and Benchmarks

### Key Financial Ratios

| Ratio | Formula | Good | Caution | Trouble |
|-------|---------|------|---------|---------|
| Current ratio | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | > 1.5 | 1.0 - 1.5 | < 1.0 |
| Debt-to-asset | Total Debt / Total Assets | < 0.3 | 0.3 - 0.5 | > 0.5 |
| Debt-to-equity | Total Debt / Owner's Equity | < 0.5 | 0.5 - 1.0 | > 1.0 |
| Operating expense ratio | Operating Expenses / Gross Revenue | < 0.65 | 0.65 - 0.80 | > 0.80 |
| Net farm income ratio | Net Farm Income / Gross Revenue | > 0.25 | 0.10 - 0.25 | < 0.10 |
| Asset turnover | Gross Revenue / Total Assets | Higher is better | — | — |
| Return on assets | Net Income / Total Assets | > 5% | 2-5% | < 2% |
| Return on equity | Net Income / Owner's Equity | > 10% | 5-10% | < 5% |

**Worked example — Current ratio:**
- Current assets: UGX 67.5M (cash UGX 15M + receivables UGX 12M + inventory UGX 33M + prepaid UGX 7.5M)
- Current liabilities: UGX 42M (accounts payable UGX 18M + current portion of loans UGX 24M)
- Current ratio: UGX 67.5M / UGX 42M = **1.61 (Good)**

## Capital Budgeting

### Net Present Value (NPV)

```
NPV = -Initial Investment + Sum of [Cash Flow_t / (1 + r)^t] for t = 1 to n
```

**Worked example — High tunnel investment:**
- Cost: UGX 22.5M installed
- Annual additional net income: UGX 6.75M/year
- Useful life: 10 years
- Discount rate: 6%
- Salvage value: UGX 1.5M

```
NPV = -UGX 22.5M + UGX 6.75M/(1.06)^1 + UGX 6.75M/(1.06)^2 + ... + UGX 8.25M/(1.06)^10
NPV = -UGX 22.5M + UGX 6.37M + UGX 6.01M + UGX 5.67M + UGX 5.35M + UGX 5.04M
     + UGX 4.76M + UGX 4.49M + UGX 4.24M + UGX 4.0M + UGX 4.61M
NPV = +UGX 28M
```

**Decision rule**: NPV > 0 means the investment earns more than the discount rate. **Accept.**

### Payback Period

```
Payback Period = Initial Investment / Annual Net Cash Flow
```

**High tunnel example:** UGX 22.5M / UGX 6.75M = **3.3 years**

### Internal Rate of Return (IRR)

The IRR is the discount rate that makes NPV = 0. For the high tunnel example, the IRR is approximately **27%**, well above the 6% cost of capital.

## Credit and Financing

### Financing Sources

| Source | Typical Use | Interest Range | Term |
|--------|-----------|----------------|------|
| Uganda Development Bank | Farm development | 12-15% | 3-10 years |
| Commercial bank (agricultural lending) | Equipment, expansion | 16-22% | 1-7 years |
| Microfinance / SACCOs | Small-scale needs | 18-30% | 6 months-3 years, up to UGX 50M |
| Agricultural credit facility | Land, equipment, operations | 12-15% | 1-10 years |
| Development partners / NGO schemes | Underserved farmers | 8-15% | Varies |
| Vendor financing | Equipment purchase | 0-20% | 1-3 years |

### Five Cs of Credit

1. **Character** — Credit history, reputation, experience
2. **Capacity** — Cash flow to service debt (debt coverage ratio > 1.25)
3. **Capital** — Owner's equity and net worth
4. **Collateral** — Assets pledged to secure the loan
5. **Conditions** — Economic environment, industry outlook, loan purpose

## Break-Even and Sensitivity Analysis

### Break-Even Analysis

```
Break-Even Volume = Fixed Costs / (Price per Unit - Variable Cost per Unit)
Break-Even Revenue = Fixed Costs / (1 - Variable Cost Ratio)
```

**Worked example — CSA farm:**
- Fixed costs: UGX 35M/year (land, equipment, insurance, base labour)
- Variable cost per CSA share: UGX 280,000 (inputs, harvest labour, delivery)
- Price per CSA share: UGX 600,000
- Break-even shares: UGX 35M / (UGX 600,000 - UGX 280,000) = **110 shares**
- At 140 shares sold: Profit = 140 x (UGX 600,000 - UGX 280,000) - UGX 35M = **UGX 9.8M**

### Sensitivity Analysis

Test profitability under varying assumptions:

| Scenario | Price/Share | Shares Sold | Variable Cost | Net Profit |
|----------|-----------|-------------|---------------|------------|
| Base case | UGX 600,000 | 140 | UGX 280,000 | UGX 9.8M |
| Price drop 10% | UGX 540,000 | 140 | UGX 280,000 | UGX 1.4M |
| Volume drop 15% | UGX 600,000 | 119 | UGX 280,000 | UGX 3M |
| Cost increase 20% | UGX 600,000 | 140 | UGX 336,000 | UGX 2M |
| Worst case | UGX 540,000 | 119 | UGX 336,000 | -UGX 10.7M |
| Best case | UGX 660,000 | 160 | UGX 252,000 | UGX 30.3M |

> **Key insight**: This farm is most sensitive to **price changes** — a 10% price drop reduces profit by 86%. Building a strong brand and customer loyalty is the primary financial risk mitigation strategy.

### Tax Considerations

- **Income tax** returns filed with URA for all business forms
- **Presumptive tax**: Applies to small businesses below UGX 150M turnover
- **Rental income tax**: 20% on rental income (relevant for land leasing)
- **Withholding tax**: 6% on supplies to government entities
- **VAT**: Registration required above UGX 150M annual turnover
- **PAYE**: Employer obligation for staff earning above tax-free threshold
