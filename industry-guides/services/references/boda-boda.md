- **Engine capacity:** 100–125 cc is optimal for fuel economy and urban use; 150 cc for hilly terrain or goods carrying
- **Best resale brands in Nairobi:** TVS, Boxer; verify most popular brand in your specific area
- **Financing:** Available from dealers, banks, SACCOs, and microfinance institutions; daily repayment schemes (e.g., Yamaha/Toyota Crux Finance at KES 300/day) lower entry barriers

## Revenue Models

**Model 1 — Owner-Operator:** Owner rides own motorcycle. Keeps all income after fuel and maintenance. Full control; maximum earning potential but high personal time commitment.

**Model 2 — Owner + Hired Rider:** Owner employs a rider on daily remittance or revenue-share. Rider pays agreed daily amount (KES 400–800; $4.80–$9.60) to owner. Owner earns passive income; success depends entirely on rider honesty and reliability.

**Model 3 — Hire Purchase Fleet:** Owner buys 3–10 motorcycles on hire purchase, finds riders, earns net margin between rider daily payment and loan repayment. Scalable; management-intensive.

## Market

Kenya had ~800,000 registered motorcycles by end of 2013 (up from 3,759 in 2005) following zero-rating of sub-250 cc motorcycles in 2008. The sector is now the primary last-mile transport solution across East Africa. 3 in 10 boda boda investors in Kenya exit within year 1 — primarily from dishonest riders, breakdowns, and accidents. Owner-presence or reliable management is critical. Regulation tightening (helmet requirements, SACCO registration, route restrictions) increases compliance costs but has not curtailed market growth.

## East Africa Notes

- **Uganda:** Boda bodas dominate urban Kampala, all upcountry towns, and rural connectivity. KCCA requires boda operators to be registered with a SACCO. National helmet law enforced inconsistently. TVS, Boxer, and Bajaj Boxer most popular. Honda and Yamaha assemblies active locally. Typical daily earnings (Kampala operator, post-fuel): UGX 15,000–30,000 ($4–$8).
- **Tanzania:** Bajaj, TVS, and Hero popular. Dar es Salaam has motorised tricycle (bajaji/tuk-tuk) market competing in the last-mile space. Mainland regulations tightening.
- **Rwanda:** Rwanda's strict traffic enforcement and helmet laws apply to boda bodas. Kigali boda bodas are uniformly registered, wear reflective vests with numbers, and are organised into SACCOs — one of the best-regulated boda boda environments in East Africa. Rates are higher than Kenya/Uganda.
- **Goods boda:** Carrying small goods and deliveries (pharmacies, supermarkets, restaurants) is a growing revenue stream — consider the motorcycle type and carrying capacity carefully.
