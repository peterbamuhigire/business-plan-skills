# Petrol Station (Independent)

**Source:** Crack A Business Kenya (CAB) — Petrol Station (Independent) Guide (2016) | **Sub-sector:** Services — Petroleum Retail
**Conversion:** KES ÷ 106 × 1.27 → March 2026 USD

> An independently owned and operated petrol station (non-franchise/non-OMC branded), selling fuel sourced from brokers or directly from oil marketing companies. Total capital KES 10,533,000 ($126,189) for a full-scale station with service bay, tyre centre, and car wash. Gross margin KES 6–15/litre (plan conservatively at KES 6). Average performing stations sell 100,000–170,000 litres/month. Monthly operating expenses for a mid-size station ~KES 596,000. This is a capital-intensive, complex business requiring experienced management and a 10+ year lease.

## Fuel Supply Chain

```
Importers → Kenya Petroleum Refineries (KPRL)
→ Kenya Pipeline Company (KPC) depots (Nairobi, Nakuru, Eldoret, Kisumu)
→ OMC depots → Brokers/Wholesalers → Independent Petrol Stations
```

**Buying from brokers (recommended for new entrants):**
- Minimum quantities as low as 1,000 litres
- Delivery to your station
- Credit available after relationship established
- Verify quantity (dip stick calibration) and quality (testing kit) every delivery
- Have your trustee present at the depot to escort the tanker to your station

**ERC pump price formula:** Maximum retail = Wholesale price + KES 3 maximum margin + delivery rate from nearest depot

## Key Operational Risks

| Risk | Prevention |
|------|------------|
| Fuel adulteration by broker | Accompany delivery or deploy trusted observer at depot; test on arrival |
| Revenue leakage (attendants) | Underground safe; drop protocol; shift-end reconciliation; pump vs. tank reading cross-check |
| Calibration errors | Monthly calibration jar tests on all dispensers |
| Stock-out | Reorder before stock is depleted; maintain relationships with multiple brokers |
| Security/robbery | Underground safe; guards; CCTV; limit cash on forecourt |
| Electricity failure | Generator; essential for overnight operations |

## Location Principles

- High vehicle traffic corridors; growing towns; major roads
- Easy entry and exit geometry from the road
- Long-term lease minimum 10 years (OMCs require 15 years for franchise conversions)
- Proximity to depots reduces transport costs per litre
- Avoid locations where the break-even volume cannot be achieved within realistic traffic projections

## East Africa Notes

- **Uganda:** The independent petroleum retail sector in Uganda is regulated by the Petroleum Authority of Uganda (PAU), which replaced the defunct Petroleum Supply Department. ERC equivalent licence required from PAU. Fuel pump prices in Uganda are not centrally regulated as in Kenya — stations set their own prices based on market competition. Major OMCs operating in Uganda: Total Uganda, Shell/Vivo Energy Uganda, Stabex International, and Gulf Energy. Independent stations source from licensed wholesale importers. The fuel supply chain flows through Mombasa (80%+) and Port Bell/Jinja (Lake Victoria tanker route). Operating licences: KCCA trade licence for Kampala; district commercial officer permit upcountry. Fire certificate from DCIC required. URA TIN mandatory.
- **Tanzania:** Independent petrol stations are common outside the OMC franchise system. The Energy and Water Utilities Regulatory Authority (EWURA) regulates petroleum retail — an EWURA Petroleum Product Dealer licence is required. Fuel prices in Tanzania are regulated by EWURA monthly. Tanzania imports refined products via Dar es Salaam port; the TPC (Tanzania Petroleum Development Corporation) pipeline runs Dar–Tunduma. BRELA registration and TRA TIN required. TBS product quality standards apply to lubricants.
- **Rwanda:** Rwanda Energy Group (REG) regulates the downstream petroleum sector through RURA. All petrol stations require an RURA fuel retail licence. Rwanda imports all refined petroleum products (it has no refinery). Supply comes via pipeline from Mombasa or road tankers from Uganda/Tanzania. Rwanda's compact geography means transport cost differentials are minimal. Kigali has a competitive petrol station market; margins are similar to Kenya. RDB registration and RRA TIN required.
- **EA general:** The independent petrol station model has the same fundamental economics across all EA markets: thin per-litre margins requiring high volumes, fuel quality risks, and revenue leakage management. The service centre model (lubricants + car wash + tyre service) is the universal margin-improvement strategy. In all markets, a new independent entrant should plan for at least 12 months before achieving target volumes.

## Data Quality Notes

Equipment prices and fuel prices are from July 2016 Kenya data. ERC pump prices and wholesale broker margins change monthly — the KES 6/litre minimum guidance is a structural rule of thumb, not a current figure. Verify current ERC pump prices, broker wholesale rates, and regulatory licence fees before investing. The total capital figure (KES 10,533,000) does not include land purchase (assumes leased site) and represents a full-service station. A minimal 1–2 pump rural station can be established for significantly less — scale down all capital line items proportionally.
