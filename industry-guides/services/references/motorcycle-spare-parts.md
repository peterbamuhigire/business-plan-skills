## Fast-Moving Items

The following items have the highest replacement frequency and should be prioritised in initial stocking:

- Tires and tubes
- Rims
- Side mirrors and side mirror holders
- Brake shoes
- Indicator lamps
- Spark plugs
- Front and rear sprockets
- Engine oil
- Cables
- Return springs
- Head cases
- Dimmer switches

> A bodaboda replaces a tyre approximately every 5 months. With hundreds of new motorcycles joining monthly, this generates consistent daily demand even from the same fleet.

## Stock Strategy

### Identifying the Right Models to Stock For

- **Step 1:** Identify the dominant motorcycle brands in your area before stocking. Chinese models (Skygo, Yamahana, Flyboy, Tiger, Keweseki) and Indian models (Focin, Boxer, Hero, TVS) require different parts.
- **Step 2:** Most parts for universal items (spokes, bulbs, brake fluid, roller chains, tyres, tubes) are cross-compatible. Brand-specific parts (pistons, gaskets, clutch assemblies) require knowing the dominant brand.
- **Step 3:** Identify the trusted spare parts brands in the area (e.g., YOG, MSR, Avon, Railin) — trust is cultural and location-specific, not purely quality-based.

### Counterfeit Risk

- Fake YOG, MTR, Raicillin parts are common in the market; lower quality, shorter lifespan, damage shop reputation.
- Purchase from reputable wholesalers (e.g., YOG Motorcycles directly on Luthuli Avenue) to guarantee genuine products.

| Source | Advantage | Consideration |

| Nairobi wholesalers (Luthuli Ave, River Road, Industrial Area) | Wide variety, reliable | Higher price than Uganda/Dubai |
| Uganda (Kampala) | 10–200% cheaper than Nairobi | Travel costs (KES 5,000 return + KES 1,000+ accommodation); first trip needs guidance |
| Dubai | 60–400% margin potential; become a wholesaler | Requires KES 1,000,000+ capital; flights ~KES 50,000 return; large minimum quantities |
| Local wholesalers (upcountry) | Convenient for urgent restocking | More expensive; reduced margins |

**Uganda sourcing model (after relationship is established):** Pay by M-Pesa/bank transfer; supplier sends parts by bus or courier to Kenya. Saves travel cost and time. Minimum order ~KES 50,000 for remote transfer; KES 20,000 for in-person purchase.

**Retailers from diverse areas joining forces:** Pooling KES 15,000 each for Uganda joint purchase — enjoy wholesale prices without full capital burden.

## Mechanic Relationships

- Mechanics diagnose breakdowns and recommend which part to buy
- Many riders prefer to buy the part themselves (fear of markup by mechanic), but mechanic's recommendation of your shop is a strong referral channel
- Develop trust with local mechanics: offer fair prices, genuine parts, willingness to assist with technical identification questions

## East Africa Notes

- **Uganda:** Uganda's bodaboda market is more mature than Kenya's — established supply chains, more experienced riders, and stronger brand loyalties (Indian models historically dominant, though Chinese brands now common). Kampala's Nakawa Market and Kikuubo area have the highest concentration of motorcycle spare parts wholesalers. Uganda's lower import taxes make parts cheaper — a key reason Kenyan retailers source from Kampala. ERA motorcycle regulations require safety equipment and licensing, but enforcement is inconsistent. MTN MoMo widely used for inter-city payment.
- **Tanzania:** Dar es Salaam and Mwanza have significant bodaboda fleets. Chinese brands (TVS, Boxer, and locally rebranded models) dominate. Kariakoo Market in Dar and Mwanza's commercial centre are the main spare parts wholesale hubs. TBS certification required for commercial sale of safety-critical parts (brakes, tyres). BRELA business registration and TRA TIN required for formal operation.
- **Rwanda:** Rwanda's motorcycle sector operates within a stricter regulatory framework — RURA requires registration of all bodaboda operators, and RTDA (Rwanda Transport Development Agency) enforces equipment standards. This actually benefits formal spare parts retailers as operators need certified, documented parts. Kigali's Nyabugogo transport hub area is the main spare parts hub. RDB registration is fast.
- **EA opportunity:** As motorcycle fleets expand rapidly throughout secondary cities (Mbarara, Mwanza, Huye, Kisumu, Nakuru), first-mover spare parts shops in under-served areas can establish strong customer loyalty before competition intensifies.

## Data Quality Notes

Revenue and price data are from 2013–2015 Kenya sources. Motorcycle fleet size has grown significantly since (Kenya had ~800,000 registered units in December 2013). Wholesale/retail margin structures remain relevant — the 30% average margin is structurally stable across different price levels. Uganda price advantage is real but factor in transport and relationship-building costs for first trips.
