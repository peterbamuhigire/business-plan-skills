# Printing Business

**Source:** Crack A Business Kenya (CAB) — Printing Business Guide (2018) | **Sub-sector:** Services — Commercial Printing
**Conversion:** KES ÷ 106 × 1.27 → March 2026 USD

> Commercial printing covers multiple distinct technologies: offset printing, laser printing, inkjet printing, large format printing, screen printing, and heat transfer/sublimation. Each requires different equipment, serves different customers, and has different capital requirements. Entry ranges from KES 130,000 ($1,558) for a laser printer bureau to KES 3M+ ($35,943+) for a full offset press operation. Most sustainable printing businesses focus on 1–2 printing types and supplement by subcontracting the rest.

> Offset is the most mature printing technology in Kenya. It remains dominant for volumes above ~500 copies where digital cannot compete on cost per unit. The market has high idle capacity at the small/medium level — new entrants need aggressive marketing to survive.

> Laser printing is the default technology for cyber cafes and print bureaus. The most profitable model is maximising the printer's capability — not just photocopies, but business card design and print, certificates, stickers, posters in small quantities. Inkjet cannot compete on cost per copy; laser wins for anything above 20 pages/job.

### Large Format Printing (Outdoor)

Used for: banners, billboards, vehicle branding (stickers/wrapping), posters, signage, backlit displays, floor graphics, wallpapers.

| Equipment | Cost (KES) | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Roland RF640 series (large format outdoor) | 700,000–2,000,000+ | Popular in Kenya; eco-sol ink; max 64" width |
| HP / Epson / Chinese large format alternatives | 400,000–1,500,000 | Price vs. ink cost, support tradeoff |
| Vinyl cutter/plotter (for sticker cutting) | 150,000–400,000 | Servo motor (digital) preferred over stepper |
| Computer for RIP software | 50,000 | |
| **Minimum viable large format setup** | **~600,000–1,200,000** | **~$7,189–$14,377** |

**Large format materials (rolls, 50m):**
- Blackback banner 3.2m wide
- Backlit media 3.2m wide
- Reflective vinyl 1.27m wide
- Satin paper 1m wide
- One-way vision 1.27m wide

**Vehicle branding:** Print vinyl stickers → apply by specialist (charge KES 200+/sq m for application labour).

**Billboard extension:** Some large format printers acquire billboard sites, erect steel structures, and sell advertising space as an integrated service.

> Large format demand is driven by business signage, elections (campaign materials), sports events, and outdoor advertising. Ink quality (eco-sol vs. UV) and dryer time are key differentiators. Printer downtime = revenue loss; spare parts availability and local technician support are critical purchase factors.

**Screen printing process (6 steps):**
1. Artwork design (Corel Draw / Photoshop)
2. Colour separation (CMYK)
3. Film positives (inkjet printer on film)
4. Screen making (emulsion coating, exposure unit, washing)
5. Printing (ink through mesh onto garment)
6. Drying (blow dryer; tunnel dryer for volume)

> Screen printing is most profitable at volumes of 50+ identical items. Below 20 items, heat transfer or laser printing is more economical. Key markets: political campaign T-shirts (election years), school uniforms, sports teams, NGO branded items, corporate workwear.

### Heat Transfer Printing

Used for: custom-printed T-shirts (small quantities), caps, mugs, phone cases, mouse pads, key holders, tiles.

| Equipment | Cost (KES) | 2026 USD |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Heat press (5-in-1 or 8-in-1) | 40,000–100,000 | $479–$1,198 |
| Vinyl cutter/plotter | 100,000–300,000 | $1,198–$3,595 |
| Inkjet printer | 19,000–25,000 | $228–$300 |
| Computer | 40,000–50,000 | $479–$599 |
| **Minimum heat transfer setup** | **~200,000–500,000** | **~$2,396–$5,991** |

> Heat transfer is ideal for 1–10 custom items. Vinyl is limited to simple text and graphics — cannot reproduce photographs. Sublimation produces photo-quality images that become part of the fabric, but requires sublimation paper and inks and is best for polyester garments. The mugs/phone cases/personalised gift market is trend-driven — entry points are low but so is margin per item.

## Choosing What to Invest In

| Consideration | Guidance |
|--------------|---------|
| Market access | What customers do you already have or can reach? Politicians → screen print; business community → offset or large format |
| Capital | Buy what you can afford; subcontract what you cannot; upgrade as revenue grows |
| Location | Rural towns: laser and screen printing for local demand; urban: all types viable |
| Market trends | Signage demand is steady year-round; T-shirts spike during elections; custom gifts are trend-sensitive |
| Subcontracting | Take all orders; subcontract what you lack equipment for; use this to learn market demand before investing |

## East Africa Notes

- **Uganda:** Commercial printing is concentrated along Kampala Road, Nasser Road, and the Ben Kiwanuka Street area in Kampala. Demand for screen printing is strong — campaign T-shirts (Uganda's competitive multi-party politics), school uniforms, church and NGO branded items. Large format printing for signage and banners is a growing sector. KCCA trade licence and URA TIN required. Screen printing machine operators are available in Kampala's jua kali sector. Election years (Uganda: every 5 years; 2026 is an election year) create a large spike in T-shirt and poster printing demand — significant opportunity for well-equipped screen and large format printers.
- **Tanzania:** Dar es Salaam's printing cluster is around Kariakoo and Kisutu. Large format printing for advertising is well developed with Roland and Chinese machines operating across the city. Screen printing is increasingly distributed to district towns as the economy grows. BRELA registration and TRA TIN required. Tanzania's strong NGO sector (health, agriculture, education programmes) creates steady demand for branded T-shirts, banners, and print materials. TBS certification technically applies to printed food packaging.
- **Rwanda:** Kigali's printing sector is concentrated in Nyarugenge and Kacyiru districts. Rwanda's MICE tourism and business conference sector (Kigali Convention Centre) drives demand for high-quality large format prints and branded materials. Offset and large format are the dominant commercial technologies. Screen printing for uniforms is growing. RDB registration required. Rwanda's relatively higher-income urban base supports premium print quality expectations — lower quality output struggles more than in Kenya.
- **EA general:** The most universally applicable printing technologies across EA are: (1) large format printing for business signage and banners, and (2) screen printing for branded garments. Both have steady, growing demand regardless of country. Laser printing is the lowest-capital entry point that can be combined with design services for a sustainable small business model. In all markets, the biggest success factor is marketing — demand exists, but customers choose printers through networks and referrals rather than passive walk-in.

## Data Quality Notes

Equipment prices are from early 2017–2018 Kenya market surveys. Printer technology changes quickly — verify current prices from Roland Kenya, HP Kenya, local Konica Minolta dealers, and Chinese equipment importers on Alibaba/Indiamart before investing. The Roland RF640 remains a popular reference point for large format; newer models (RF Series successors) may offer better value. Screen printing machines at KES 100,000–500,000 reflect the wide quality range — a KES 100,000 machine can produce commercial quality if mechanically sound; test before purchase.
