This model targets a niche market of pupils from middle-class families with the ability to pay for quality education at a premium. Revenue grows significantly as enrolment increases through the phased approach — from $877,800 in year 1 to $3,686,200 by years 4-5.

| **Payback Period** | 5 months |
| **Production Capacity** | 1 tonne of syringes per day; 30 tonnes per month |
| **Key Equipment** | Disposable syringe moulding machine, syringe packaging machine, fixed data equipment |

**Market Context:** High demand for disposable syringes across Uganda's health sector — hospitals, clinics, health centres, and vaccination programmes. Production must comply with FDA specifications and good manufacturing practices (GMP). Local production reduces dependence on imports and benefits from the "Buy Uganda Build Uganda" policy. Quality certification from UNBS and NDA is essential for market access.

## Hygiene Products

### Sanitary Towels Manufacturing (Idea 187)

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| **Capital Investment** | $60,640 |
| **Annual Revenue** | $67,360 |
| **Annual Operating Costs** | N/A |
| **Annual Profit** | N/A |
| **Profit Margin** | N/A |
| **Payback Period** | N/A |
| **Key Equipment** | Hand-driven knitting machine, yarn twister |

**Market Context:** Sanitary towels are essential hygiene products for women and girls. Menstrual hygiene management remains a significant barrier to girls' school attendance in Uganda and across East Africa — studies estimate that girls miss 2-3 school days per month due to lack of affordable sanitary products. Local production reduces cost compared to imports, making products more accessible. This business combines commercial viability with strong social impact, making it attractive to impact investors, NGO partnerships, and government programmes promoting girls' education.
