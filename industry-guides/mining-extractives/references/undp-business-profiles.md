| **Payback Period** | N/A |

**Key Equipment:** Silver extraction machine, testing equipment, tools and equipment for production.

**Market Context:** Silver is a precious and important metal extensively used in photography, X-ray films, jewellery, electrical materials, and medicine. Extraction from waste materials (photographic waste, electronic waste, industrial by-products) represents a circular economy opportunity.

**Note:** This is a niche operation requiring technical knowledge of silver recovery chemistry. The 45 kg/year production capacity is modest. Market price for silver fluctuates internationally (approximately US$ 25-35 per troy ounce as of 2026). Revenue depends heavily on commodity prices and quality of waste feedstock.

| **Payback Period** | N/A |
| **Production Capacity** | 48.1 kg per day |

**Key Equipment:** Oil-fired tilting furnace, weighing balance, ladle and tongs, hand moulding equipment, bench grinder, mixing machine.

**Market Context:** Brass and bronze castings have wide application in engineering, marine, and automobile industries. Also extensively used in manufacturing decorative items for daily use. Products include fittings, valves, bushings, ornamental pieces, and hardware.

**Note:** The capital investment of US$ 400 is clearly equipment cost only and does not include premises, raw materials (copper, zinc, tin), fuel, or working capital. Source data inconsistency — a realistic startup budget for a small foundry would be US$ 10,000-25,000 including raw material inventory and workshop setup. The profit margin of 20% is reasonable for metal casting operations.

## Oil & Gas Support Services

### Catering in the Oil and Gas Sector (Ref #204)

| Item | March 2026 Estimate |
|---|---|
| **Capital Investment** | US$ 31,800 |
| **Annual Revenue** | N/A |
| **Annual Operating Costs** | N/A |
| **Annual Profit** | N/A |
| **Profit Margin** | 36% |
| **Payback Period** | 1 year 3 months |
| **Production Capacity** | 400 clients daily (breakfast, lunch, and supper), serving 2 oil well sites |

**Key Equipment:** Blenders (6), fridges (3), cutlery sets (60), furniture, cooking equipment, music system, TV and computers (3), food warmers (6), mixers and flasks (10), delivery van, bouquet sets (5), gas and water tanks (2), decoration materials, standby generator, plates and other kitchen equipment.

**Market Context:** The oil and gas sector is a growing industry in Uganda with high demand for quality catering services. A typical oil well team consists of 200-400 people located on isolated oil fields away from normal towns. Catering contracts provide steady, high-volume revenue with predictable demand.

**Key Considerations:**
- Oil companies require certified food safety and hygiene standards (HACCP or equivalent)
- Contracts are typically awarded through competitive tender; pre-qualification is essential
- Remote location operations require reliable logistics for food supply
- Menu planning must account for diverse dietary requirements (international crews)
- Revenue depends on securing contracts with oil companies or their tier-1 contractors
- The Albertine Graben development (Tilenga and Kingfisher projects, East African Crude Oil Pipeline) drives demand through the late 2020s

**Note:** Revenue data was not available in the source, but at 400 clients daily with typical oil field catering rates (US$ 30-60 per person per day for 3 meals), annual revenue could range from US$ 3.5M-8.8M. The 36% margin is achievable in oil field catering given premium pricing, though operating costs (food, transport, labour, generator fuel) are also high.
