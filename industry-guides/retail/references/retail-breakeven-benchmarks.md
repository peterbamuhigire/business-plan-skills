# Retail Break-Even Analysis and Financial Benchmarks

> References: *Retail Management: A Strategic Approach* by Berman & Evans (Pearson, 2013); *Analytics for Retail* by Okunev (Apress, 2022); *Retail Management* by Tiwari (Global Media, 2008); *Start and Run a Shop* by Penrith (How To Books, 2009)

> Figures adapted to Ugandan context (UGX). Percentages and ratios from the sources are universal.

---

## 1. Break-Even Analysis

Break-even tells a retailer the sales volume needed to cover all fixed costs (Penrith 2009).

### Formula

```
Break-Even Sales (UGX) = Fixed Costs / Gross Margin %

Or equivalently:

Break-Even Sales = Fixed Costs / [(Selling Price – Variable Cost per Unit) / Selling Price]
```

### Worked Example — Kampala Retail Shop

```
Monthly fixed costs:
  Rent                     3,000,000
  Wages                    7,000,000
  Utilities                1,000,000
  Insurance                  350,000
  Loan interest              400,000
  Advertising                500,000
  Other fixed costs          750,000
  ─────────────────────────────────
  TOTAL FIXED COSTS       13,000,000

Gross margin:              40%

Break-Even Sales = 13,000,000 / 0.40 = UGX 32,500,000 per month

Annual break-even = 32,500,000 x 12 = UGX 390,000,000 per year
```

**Interpretation:** The shop must generate at least UGX 32,500,000 in sales each month before it starts earning a profit. Every shilling of sales above that contributes 40 ngwee to profit (at the 40% gross margin).

### Break-Even in Units

```
Break-Even Units = Fixed Costs / Contribution Margin per Unit

Example:
  Average selling price:    UGX 25,000
  Average variable cost:    UGX 15,000
  Contribution margin:      UGX 10,000

  Break-Even Units = 13,000,000 / 10,000 = 1,300 units per month
```

### Sensitivity

If gross margin drops to 25%, break-even sales must be four times fixed costs (Penrith 2009):
```
Break-Even = 13,000,000 / 0.25 = UGX 52,000,000 per month
```
A 15-percentage-point drop in margin increases the required sales by 60%.

## 2. Financial Benchmarks by Retail Format

The table below synthesises benchmark data from multiple sources (Berman & Evans 2013; Okunev 2022; Tiwari 2008), adapted for Ugandan retail formats.

| Retail Format | Gross Margin % | Net Profit Margin % | Inventory Turnover (times/yr) | Asset Turnover | Typical GMROI |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Grocery / Supermarket** | 18–25% | 1–3% | 12–24 | 2.5–3.5 | 250–350% |
| **Convenience Store** | 20–30% | 2–4% | 15–25 | 2.0–3.0 | 250–350% |
| **General Merchandise** | 30–40% | 3–6% | 3–6 | 1.5–2.5 | 150–250% |
| **Clothing / Fashion** | 40–55% | 4–8% | 2.5–5.0 | 1.2–2.0 | 150–300% |
| **Hardware / Building** | 30–40% | 3–6% | 2.5–5.5 | 1.5–2.0 | 150–250% |
| **Pharmacy / Drugstore** | 25–35% | 2–5% | 8–12 | 2.0–3.0 | 200–300% |
| **Electronics** | 20–30% | 2–4% | 4–7 | 2.0–3.0 | 150–250% |
| **Furniture / Homeware** | 40–50% | 4–8% | 3–4 | 1.0–1.5 | 150–250% |
| **Gift / Specialty** | 40–55% | 5–10% | 2–4 | 0.8–1.5 | 120–200% |
| **Market Stall / Kiosk** | 25–40% | 5–15% | 10–30 | 3.0–5.0 | 250–400% |

**Reading the table:**
- High-turnover, low-margin formats (grocery, convenience) earn profit through volume.
- Low-turnover, high-margin formats (fashion, gifts) earn profit through price per item.
- Both can achieve comparable GMROI and ROA when well managed.
- Market stalls and kiosks often show high net margins because of minimal fixed costs (no rent, low wages).

## 3. Quick-Reference Formula Sheet

```
PROFITABILITY
  Gross Margin %     = (Net Sales – COGS) / Net Sales x 100
  Net Profit Margin  = Net Profit After Tax / Net Sales x 100
  ROA                = Net Profit / Total Assets x 100
  ROE (RONW)         = Net Profit / Net Worth x 100
  EBITDA             = Net Income + Interest + Tax + Depreciation + Amortisation

EFFICIENCY
  Inventory Turnover = COGS / Average Inventory at Cost
  Asset Turnover     = Net Sales / Total Assets
  GMROI              = Gross Margin (UGX) / Average Inventory at Cost
  Sell-Through %     = Units Sold / (Units Sold + Beginning Units) x 100

LIQUIDITY
  Current Ratio      = Current Assets / Current Liabilities
  Quick Ratio        = (Cash + Receivables) / Current Liabilities

LEVERAGE
  Debt Ratio         = Total Liabilities / Total Assets
  Debt-to-Equity     = Total Liabilities / Net Worth

RETAIL MATHS
  Markup % (cost)    = (Selling Price – Cost) / Cost x 100
  Markup % (retail)  = (Selling Price – Cost) / Selling Price x 100
  Markdown %         = (Original Price – Sale Price) / Original Price x 100
  Break-Even Sales   = Fixed Costs / Gross Margin %
  Contribution Margin = Selling Price – Variable Cost per Unit

INVENTORY
  Avg Inventory      = (Beginning Inventory + Ending Inventory) / 2
  Cost Complement    = Total Cost of Goods Available / Total Retail Value Available
  Cash Conversion Cycle = Days Inventory + Days Receivables – Days Payable

BUDGETING
  OTB (retail) = Planned Sales + Reductions + End-of-Month Stock – Beginning Stock
                 – Purchase Commitments
  Required Initial Markup % = (Expenses + Profit + Reductions) / (Net Sales + Reductions)
```
