## What to Stock

### Priority (Fast-Moving) Items

1. **PPR pipes and fittings** — most widely used in domestic plumbing; sockets, elbows, unions, tee, adapters in sizes 20–110mm
2. **GI (galvanised iron) pipes and fittings** — common in older properties; bends, sockets, unions, tee, elbows ½"–4"
3. **Waste pipes** — 1"–4" waste and drainage pipes
4. **CPVC pipes and fittings** — growing in market share
5. **Taps** — common types sell reliably
6. **Basic toilet/bathroom fittings** — flash valves, shower heads, angle valves, stop corks
7. **Gate valves and ball corks**

> Stock both original branded items and cheaper alternatives ("1" vs "2" for Cobra brand). Customers ask for both and many plumbers will buy whichever their client budget allows. There are counterfeits for major brands — experienced buyers know to specify; novices learn over time.

### Secondary Items (Stock After Establishing Core)

- Bathtubs, basins, shower mixers (market-dependent)
- Ceramic cisterns, toilet seats, urinal bowls
- Soap dishes, towel holders, bottle traps
- Kitchen sinks (ASL, double-bowl models)
- Shower heads (Rose, Cobra, Brimix, Eurobath, Tapis)

### Pricing Rule of Thumb

- Fittings: add KES 5–20 per item over wholesale; target minimum KES 10 per piece
- Larger/slower-moving items: higher mark-up to compensate for holding cost
- Average margin: **40%** across the range

> Price differences of even KES 5 per fitting matter when buying in bulk. Shop around at multiple wholesalers — the guide found significant price differences for identical items at Fairman Limited, Jocha Limited, Weskam Hardware (Duruma Road), Ramco, and Pramuh Towers.

## Market Entry Strategies

| Strategy | Description | Risk/Note |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Scale | Large stock, large shop — shock and awe | Higher capital; harder to sustain expectations |
| Economies of location | Open in known hardware cluster | Existing foot traffic but intense competition |
| Market gap | Area with high demand, low supply | Best margins initially; attracts competition fast |
| Specialisation | Focus on fittings, pipes, or sanitary ware | Works if demand justifies depth; don't leave related items absent |
| Variety | Wide range across categories | Avoids dead stock by following actual local demand |

## Location Principles

- Near active construction areas or dense residential areas served by piped water
- "Hardware cluster" areas provide built-in foot traffic (customers already seeking items)
- Visibility, easy access, and proximity to supporting businesses (M-Pesa, bus routes)
- Avoid premises too large for your stock level — empty shelves undermine credibility

## Operations

- Open 8am–6pm (some open earlier to catch plumbers starting morning jobs)
- Stock cards or simple ledger book per category — minimum viable tracking
- Commission-based or salary + bonus for shop assistants; range KES 10,000–25,000/month
- Reconcile stock against sales weekly; monthly full stocktake
- Build relationships with known plumbers and site foremen — they direct client spending

## East Africa Notes

- **Uganda:** Plumbing hardware retail is well established in Kampala along Nakasero Market Road, William Street, Kikuubo, and the Equatorial Shopping Mall hardware clusters. Uganda's construction sector is growing rapidly driven by urban housing. Key brands: the same Chinese and European brands common in Kenya (Cobra, Twyford, ASL, Orient, Peglar) are available but Kampala sourcing goes through Mombasa or Dar es Salaam ports. KCCA trade licence and URA TIN required. PPR pipe is the dominant modern system; GI is being phased out in new construction but still widely used in repairs.
- **Tanzania:** Dar es Salaam hardware districts concentrate in Kariakoo and Kinondoni. Tanzania's construction boom (especially in Dar, Arusha, and Dodoma) drives strong demand. BRELA registration and TRA TIN required. EWURA standards apply to certain plumbing components (water fittings in public projects). Import tariffs under EAC common external tariff apply for items not manufactured regionally. Sanitary ware (Twyford, Orient) is imported — verify latest import duty rates.
- **Rwanda:** Kigali's ongoing urban development (Vision 2020 legacy projects, affordable housing drives) creates strong demand for plumbing hardware. Nyabugogo, Kimironko, and Remera markets have hardware clusters. RDB registration required; Rwanda Bureau of Standards (RBS) enforces product standards more actively than Kenya or Uganda on imported fittings. Premium items (shower systems, bathtubs) have a market in Kigali's higher-income areas (Nyarutarama, Gacuriro).
- **EA general:** The key trend across all EA markets is PPR pipe displacing GI in new construction. A plumbing shop opening today should bias stock heavily toward PPR (pipes + fittings) and only carry GI for the existing repair market. CPVC is growing, particularly for hot-water applications. Stock branded fittings (Cobra, Peglar) alongside unbranded Chinese generics — customers in all price brackets buy both.

## Data Quality Notes

Wholesale and retail prices are from a 2018 Kenya field survey of multiple Nairobi wholesalers. Prices vary with negotiation, supplier, and exchange rate movements — treat the tables as directional rather than definitive. The 40% average margin and KES 5,000–15,000 daily turnover range are from a Githurai, Nairobi sample. Daily turnover in lower-density or rural markets will be lower; higher-traffic urban locations with good variety may exceed these figures.
