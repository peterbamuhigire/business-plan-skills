# Non-Woven Bags Manufacturing

**Source:** Crack A Business Kenya (CAB) — Non Woven Bags Making Guide (2017) | **Sub-sector:** Manufacturing — Packaging / Textile Products
**Conversion:** KES ÷ 106 × 1.27 → March 2026 USD | Equipment prices are in KES from 2017

> Manufacturing of polypropylene (PP) non-woven bags — the primary replacement for polythene bags after Kenya's August 2017 polythene bag ban. Equipment is imported from China; raw material (PP fabric) from China or alternative suppliers. A 5-in-1 machine setup costs KES 7,220,815 ($86,545) in equipment; total starting capital KES 20,338,415 ($243,651). Production capacity: up to 120,000 bags/day at KES 8/bag minimum factory price; monthly revenue (ideal full sell-through) KES 28,800,000 ($345,132). Monthly costs ~KES 8,343,380 ($99,949). High capital, high complexity, high-volume business requiring factory premises and professional management.

## Market Channels

### Key Buyers

1. **Supermarkets and retail chains** — steady volume, branded bag orders; long credit terms (30–90 days); approach mid-tier chains (regional/area supermarkets) before national chains
2. **Mass market consumers** — through distributors and wholesale intermediaries to kiosks, markets, and individual vendors
3. **Corporate customers** — companies requiring branded bags for marketing; shorter credit terms; higher per-unit value

### Distribution Model

Segment the country into regions; appoint regional distributors (existing wholesalers preferred) who supply retailers and market vendors. Distributors receive margins; you supply in bulk. Minimum factory order quantities reduce logistics cost.

### Factory Direct Sales

Accept direct purchase orders above a minimum quantity threshold — reduces distribution complexity and improves cash flow vs. long credit terms with large supermarkets.

## Bag Types and Market Applications

| Bag Type | Primary Market |
|----------|---------------|
| T-shirt bag (loop handle, cut-out) | Mass market consumers, market stalls, kiosks |
| D-cut bag (die-cut handle) | Supermarkets, branded retail |
| Handle/tote bag (stitched handle) | Branded corporate, premium retail |
| Zipper bag | Specialty products, reusable carry |

> 50gsm D-cut bags suit everyday market use. Heavier grammages (70–90gsm) are more durable and command a premium — target supermarkets and corporates.

## East Africa Notes

- **Uganda:** Uganda introduced a partial ban on lightweight polythene bags in 2009 (reinforced subsequently). Demand for non-woven and paper bags is established. Manufacturing operates in Kampala's Ntinda and Namanve industrial zones. PP fabric imports via Mombasa (road). KCCA trade licence and URA TIN required. UNBS product certification applies to packaging materials. Uganda's market is smaller than Kenya's but has less local manufacturing competition — an opportunity for a well-positioned Ugandan producer or cross-border supply from Kenya.
- **Tanzania:** Tanzania banned single-use plastic bags (below 30 microns) in 2019. Non-woven bag demand is growing rapidly in Dar es Salaam's retail sector. Local manufacturing capacity is limited. PP fabric is importable via Dar es Salaam port. BRELA registration, TRA TIN, and TBS product certification required. NEMC (National Environment Management Council) compliance required for manufacturing operations. The Dar market (pop. 6M+) represents a large opportunity, particularly for the mass market channel.
- **Rwanda:** Rwanda was EA's first country to ban polythene bags (2008). Non-woven and paper bag demand is well-established. However, Rwanda is a smaller market (pop. ~13M). RDB investment promotion available for manufacturing; import duty waivers on machinery possible. PP fabric imports via Mombasa/Kampala road are expensive compared to Dar-routed imports. RBS product certification strictly enforced. Opportunity: branded bags for Kigali's hotel and hospitality sector (MICE tourism).
- **EA general:** Across EA, the market for non-woven bags was structurally created by polythene bans — this demand is permanent and growing. The competitive risk is cheap Chinese imported bags undercutting local manufacturers. A local manufacturer's advantage is: faster delivery, no import duty, ability to customise (branding, size, colour) on smaller minimum orders, and local compliance. These advantages are most valuable to supermarkets and corporate customers — target these segments.

## Data Quality Notes

Equipment prices (KES 7,220,815 for 5-in-1 setup) and PP fabric prices (US$1,400–$1,800/tonne) are from December 2017 — both will have changed with exchange rates and commodity price movements. The monthly production projection (120,000 bags/day, 3.6M bags/month) assumes 20 hours of operation; actual production depends on machine efficiency, product complexity, and downtime. Monthly revenue calculation (KES 28,800,000) assumes full sell-through at KES 8/bag — actual revenue will be lower at launch and subject to price competition. Verify current PP fabric prices and Chinese machine costs before investing; both fluctuate with oil prices and exchange rates.
