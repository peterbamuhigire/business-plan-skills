# Wooden Furniture Manufacturing

**Sub-sector:** Furniture and Interior Products
**Sources:** Thankaraju, P. (Startup Manufacturing Business Ideas 200, NestFame 2018)

> Uganda's growing residential construction sector, expanding middle class, and institutional procurement (schools, hospitals, offices) create sustained demand for locally made furniture. Skilled woodworkers can compete against Kenyan and Chinese imports on price, customisation, and delivery time.

---

| Variant | Scale | Capital Range | Primary Market |

| Metric | Estimate |
|--------|----------|
| Capital investment | $15,000–$35,000 |
| Monthly revenue | $4,000–$10,000 |
| Raw material cost (timber, hardware, finish) | 40–55% of revenue |
| Labour | 20–30% |
| Overheads (power, rent, tools) | 10–15% |
| Net margin | 15–25% |
| Payback period | 2–4 years |

**High-value product advantage:** Custom-made hardwood furniture commands 2–4× the price of mass-produced equivalents. A single bedroom set (bed, 2 side tables, wardrobe) in quality hardwood sells for UGX 2,500,000–5,000,000. Material cost: UGX 800,000–1,500,000. Gross margin: 40–60%.

## Wood Types and Sourcing (Uganda)

| Wood Type | Local Name / Source | Properties | Best Use |
|-----------|-------------------|------------|---------|
| African mahogany | Khaya (planted/forest) | Durable, works well, attractive grain | High-end furniture, cabinets |
| Mvule (African teak) | Milicia excelsa | Very hard, very durable, heavy | Outdoor furniture, heavy tables |
| Eucalyptus | Siiti (plantation grown) | Fast-growing, lower quality, light colour | Budget furniture, structural components |
| Pine (plantation) | Imported or Ugandan softwood | Soft, easy to work, knots | Painted furniture, budget items |
| Plywood / MDF | Imported (Kenya, China) | Uniform thickness, stable, paintable | Cabinet backs, flat-pack, painted pieces |
| Engineered hardwood | Imported | Stable, veneer-faced | Modern fitted furniture |

**Timber sourcing:** Uganda Timber Board and NFA licensed dealers (Kampala timber markets: Mengo, Ndeeba, Katwe). Verify legality of timber — National Forestry Authority (NFA) licence required for dealers.

## Key Machinery and Tools

### Basic Workshop (hand-tool dominant)

- Circular saw / table saw (for ripping and cross-cutting)
- Band saw (for curves and resawing)
- Jigsaw (portable, for curved cuts)
- Random orbital sander + belt sander
- Drill press + power drills
- Router + router table (for joints, edges, decorative profiles)
- Measuring and marking tools (tape, square, marking gauge)
- Clamps (pipe clamps, bar clamps, F-clamps) — essential for glue-up
- Workbenches

### Small Factory (machine-assisted)

All basic tools plus:
- Thickness planer (makes boards uniform thickness)
- Jointer (produces flat, square edges for gluing)
- Mortiser (cuts mortise joints for frames)
- Lathe (for turned legs, balusters)
- Spray painting booth + HVLP spray gun

**Power requirement:** Single-phase adequate for home workshop; three-phase preferred for factory with multiple machines running simultaneously.

## Production Process

1. **Design and costing** — draw or sketch design; calculate timber quantities; price to client
2. **Timber selection and purchasing** — select boards for grain, defects, moisture content
3. **Kiln drying or air drying** — moisture content should be 8–12% before machining
4. **Primary machining** — rip to width, cross-cut to length, plane to thickness
5. **Joinery** — cut joints (mortise and tenon, dowels, biscuits, pocket screws)
6. **Assembly and glue-up** — assemble with glue and clamps; check for square
7. **Sanding** — progressive grits (80 → 120 → 180 → 240)
8. **Finishing** — apply stain (if required), sanding sealer, topcoat (lacquer, varnish, wax)
9. **Hardware fitting** — hinges, handles, locks, drawer slides
10. **Quality inspection** — check stability, finish, fit of doors and drawers
11. **Delivery and assembly at client site** (for large pieces)

---

## Uganda Regulatory Requirements

- **Business registration:** URA TIN, KCCA or district trading licence
- **Timber dealer/user licence:** NFA licence if purchasing direct from forest; verify supplier licence
- **NEMA:** Environmental review if using solvent-based finishes (VOC emissions) at scale
- **Factory licence:** Ministry of Trade for operations with 10+ workers

## Market and Distribution

**Key buyer segments:**
- **Households:** Walk-in to workshop, referrals, social media (Facebook Marketplace, WhatsApp catalogue)
- **Interior designers and architects:** Refer clients; willing to pay premium for quality and reliability
- **Property developers:** Furnish multiple units at once — high volume, negotiated price
- **Schools and government:** Desks, benches, chairs via tender; lower price, volume orders
- **Restaurants and hotels:** Dining furniture; repeat orders as items wear; values aesthetics

**Pricing benchmarks (Kampala, 2026):**

| Product | Market Price Range (UGX) |
|---------|--------------------------|
| Single bed frame (pine) | 350,000–700,000 |
| Double bed frame (hardwood) | 700,000–1,800,000 |
| 3-seater sofa (frame only) | 500,000–1,200,000 |
| Dining table, 6-seater (hardwood) | 800,000–2,500,000 |
| Office desk | 400,000–1,200,000 |
| School desk and chair (set) | 120,000–250,000 |
| Kitchen cabinet (per linear metre) | 300,000–700,000 |

**Marketing approach:**
- WhatsApp catalogue with photos of completed work
- Facebook page with before/after photos and testimonials
- Show at trade fairs (Kampala International Trade Fair)
- Partner with interior designers (share referral fee)
- Register with Uganda government procurement portal for school tender access

## Key Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Timber quality variation | Kiln-dry all timber; test moisture content before machining |
| Finish quality issues | Invest in proper finishing room; use quality products |
| Slow payment on institutional orders | Require 50% deposit; progress payment milestones |
| Copy of designs by competitors | Build brand reputation and customer relationships; offer after-sale service |
| Wood dust health hazard | Dust extraction system; PPE (dust masks, goggles) mandatory |
| Fire risk (sawdust, finishing chemicals) | Proper dust collection; no open flames in finishing room |
