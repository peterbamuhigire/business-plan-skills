# Steel Furniture Manufacturing

**Sub-sector:** Furniture and Interior Products
**Sources:** Thankaraju, P. (Startup Manufacturing Business Ideas 200, NestFame 2018)

> Steel furniture — almirahs, filing cabinets, lockers, security doors, shelving — commands consistent institutional demand from schools, hospitals, offices, and banks. Local fabrication competes against Kenyan and Chinese imports on delivery speed, customisation, and price.

## Production Process

1. **Receive order / prepare cutting list** — calculate steel sheet quantities from design
2. **Sheet shearing** — cut sheet to required panel sizes
3. **Bending / pressing** — form panels to required profiles on press brake
4. **Frame assembly** — tack weld angle iron or tube frame
5. **Panel fitting and welding** — weld formed panels to frame; full-penetration welds at stress points
6. **Grinding** — grind all welds smooth; remove spatter
7. **Surface preparation** — grind or sandblast; wipe with degreaser
8. **Primer application** — spray or brush epoxy primer; allow to cure
9. **Topcoat** — spray enamel (typical colours: cream, grey, green, blue for institutional)
10. **Hardware fitting** — fit hinges, locks, handles, drawer slides
11. **Quality check** — test doors/drawers open and close smoothly; check squareness
12. **Delivery and installation** (for heavy items)

**Key infrastructure:** 3-phase electricity (380V) for shearing machine and press brake; overhead crane or lifting equipment if handling heavy steel sheets.

## Uganda Regulatory Requirements

- **Business registration:** URA TIN, KCCA or district trading licence
- **Factory licence:** Ministry of Trade, Industry and Cooperatives (Factories Act compliance for welding and metalwork hazards)
- **NEMA permit:** Required if operating spray painting booth (VOC emissions)
- **UNBS:** Product quality standards may apply to institutional procurement (verify per tender)

---

## Market and Distribution

**Key buyer segments:**
- **Schools and universities:** Lockers, benches, storage cabinets — government tender and private procurement
- **Hospitals and clinics:** Medical trolleys, utility cabinets, ward lockers
- **Banks and financial institutions:** Teller counters, security cabinets
- **Offices:** Filing cabinets, storage, office partitioning (steel framing)
- **Households:** Almirahs (wardrobes), kitchen shelving, storage units
- **Construction sector:** Security doors, gates, steel window frames

**Tender registration:** Register with government procurement portals (PPDA, district procurement offices) for school furniture tenders. Government is a large volume buyer but payment terms are slow (30–90 days); plan cash flow accordingly.

## Key Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Steel price volatility | Maintain 30-day stock; build supplier relationships for advance notice |
| Rust/corrosion complaints | Quality primer application; proper surface prep before painting |
| Slow government payment | Require 30–50% deposit on large orders; diversify to private sector |
| Welding injury (burns, eye damage) | PPE mandatory (welding mask, gloves, apron); safety training |
| Paint fumes (fire and health hazard) | Dedicated spray booth with extraction fan; fire extinguisher |
| Competition from cheap imports | Differentiate on customisation, delivery speed, and after-sale service |
