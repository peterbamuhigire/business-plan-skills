# Liquid Soap and Detergent Powder Manufacturing

**Sub-sector:** Household Cleaning Products
**Sources:** Thankaraju, P. (Startup Manufacturing Business Ideas 200, NestFame 2018) | UNDP Uganda Compendium Vol. 2

> Liquid soap is rapidly replacing bar soap and block detergent in urban East African households, vehicle wash facilities, hotels, and commercial laundries. A locally manufactured brand competes on price, availability, and custom formulations against Kenyan and Asian imports.

## Raw Materials — Liquid Soap

| Ingredient | Role | Approx. Cost Contribution |
|-----------|------|--------------------------|
| LABSA (Linear Alkyl Benzene Sulphonic Acid) | Primary surfactant — creates cleaning action | 30–40% of raw material cost |
| CDEA (Cocamide DEA) | Foam booster and thickener | 10–15% |
| Sodium chloride (salt) | Viscosity builder | <1% |
| Sodium hydroxide (caustic soda) | pH neutralisation of LABSA | 5–10% |
| Preservative (e.g., Kathon CG, DMDM Hydantoin) | Shelf-life extension | 1–3% |
| Fragrance | Consumer appeal | 2–5% |
| Colorant | Product identity | <1% |
| Deionised or clean water | 50–60% of product volume | Minimal cost |
| HDPE bottles + caps + labels | Packaging | 15–25% of final cost |

*All primary chemicals available from Kampala chemical distributors or via Nairobi import.*

## Raw Materials — Detergent Powder

Key ingredients: LABSA, Sodium carbonate (soda ash), Sodium sulphate, Sodium silicate, STTP (Sodium Tripolyphosphate), CMC (Carboxymethyl cellulose), Enzymes (optional), Bleach (optional), Fragrance, LDPE bags/cartons.

## Key Machinery

### Liquid Soap
- Stainless steel mixing tanks (200–1,000L capacity) with agitator paddles
- Heating element or steam jacket for hot-process methods
- Transfer pump
- Semi-automatic or automatic liquid filling machine
- Capping machine (manual or automatic)
- Labelling machine (manual or automatic)
- pH meter and viscosity tester (QC)

### Detergent Powder
- Crutcher (mixing vessel with high-shear agitator)
- Spray drying tower (largest capital item — converts slurry to powder)
- Screening/sieving system
- Packaging machine (auto-weighing and bag-sealing)

**Alternative (dry-mix/slurry-blend process):** Skip spray drying by blending dry ingredients directly — lower capital cost ($5,000–$15,000) but inferior powder quality vs. spray-dried product.

---

## Production Process — Liquid Soap

1. Neutralise LABSA with caustic soda solution in mixing tank → SLES (Sodium Lauryl Ether Sulphate) base
2. Add CDEA and mix until dissolved and uniform
3. Add preservative and fragrance
4. Dissolve colorant separately, add to mixture
5. Adjust viscosity with salt solution
6. Check pH (target: 6.5–7.5) and adjust if necessary
7. Allow to cool and settle
8. Fill into bottles, cap, and label

**Batch time:** 2–4 hours depending on tank size and heating

| Floor space | 500–1,500 sq ft (mixing, filling, storage, office) |
| Water supply | Municipal or borehole (clean water essential; deionised water preferred) |

## Market and Distribution

**Key customer segments:**
- **Households:** Retail through supermarkets, hardware shops, dukas; price-sensitive; need attractive packaging
- **Institutions:** Hospitals, hotels, schools, government offices; buy in bulk (20L jerricans); less packaging-sensitive; need reliability
- **Industrial:** Vehicle wash facilities, laundries, food processing; high volume, specification-driven
- **Resellers:** Wholesalers who repackage into smaller units for rural markets

**EA pricing benchmarks (2026):**
- Liquid hand soap (500ml bottle, retail): UGX 4,500–8,000
- Liquid dish soap (1L): UGX 6,000–10,000
- Washing powder (500g packet, retail): UGX 3,500–6,000
- Bulk liquid soap (20L jerrican, institutional): UGX 55,000–90,000

**Competitive strategy:** Local brands compete on price (15–25% below imported brands) and on custom fragrance/formulation for institutional buyers. Build first with institutional buyers (hotels, hospitals) who buy on contract, then expand to retail.

## Key Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| LABSA supply disruption | Maintain 30-day stock; two approved suppliers |
| Price competition from imports | Focus on service, reliability, and institutional relationships |
| UNBS non-compliance | Budget for testing; hire consultant for initial certification |
| Caustic soda burns / chemical injury | PPE mandatory; staff training; first aid kit on-site |
| Water quality affecting product | Use filtered/deionised water; test batches before filling |
