# Barbed Wire Manufacturing

**Sub-sector:** Wire and Metal Products
**Sources:** Thankaraju, P. (Startup Manufacturing Business Ideas 200, NestFame 2018)

> Barbed wire demand in Uganda is driven by rapid land titling, farmland enclosure, urban security fencing, and construction site perimeter security. Most barbed wire is currently imported from Kenya, China, and India — a local manufacturer serves the same market at lower transport cost and faster delivery.

## Raw Materials

| Material | Specification | Source |
|---------|--------------|--------|
| Mild steel wire rod (coiled) | 6mm or 8mm diameter, SAE 1006/1008 grade | Uganda Rolling Mill (Tian Tang), Roofings Group, or Kenya imports |
| Zinc (for galvanising) | High-grade zinc ingots or zinc chloride bath | Import via chemical distributors |
| Lubricating oil (wire drawing) | Dry soap / emulsion lubricant | Local industrial suppliers |
| Barb wire (pre-cut or cut from rod) | Cut from same wire stock | In-house |

**Wire rod availability note:** Uganda Rolling Mill (Tian Tang) in Jinja rolls mild steel rod from scrap — a local source that reduces import dependency for one of the primary raw materials.

## Key Machinery

| Machine | Function | Approximate Cost |
|---------|----------|-----------------|
| Wire drawing machine (single or multi-pass) | Reduces rod diameter from 6–8mm to 2–2.8mm (barb wire gauge) | $8,000–$25,000 |
| Annealing furnace | Softens drawn wire (restores ductility after cold drawing) | $5,000–$15,000 |
| Barbing machine (double-twist type) | Twists two wires together; applies barbs at set intervals | $6,000–$18,000 per head |
| Galvanising tank (hot-dip) | Zinc coating for rust protection | $5,000–$20,000 |
| OR: Electro-galvanising line | Lower-cost alternative; thinner coating | $8,000–$20,000 |
| Coiling/spooling machine | Winds finished wire onto reels at specified length | $3,000–$8,000 |
| Bundling and strapping machine | Packages coils for transport | $1,000–$3,000 |

**Note on wire drawing:** Wire drawing is the most capital-intensive and technically demanding step. Multi-pass drawing machines (5–15 dies in sequence) are faster but cost more. A simple single-pass machine with multiple units is a lower-capital alternative.

## Production Process

1. **Wire rod uncoiling** — load rod coil onto payoff stand
2. **Pointing** — point the rod end to feed through first drawing die
3. **Drawing** — pull wire through hardened drawing dies (progressively smaller diameter); apply drawing lubricant to reduce friction and heat
4. **Annealing** — heat drawn wire to 600–800°C in furnace; allow to cool slowly — removes work hardening, restores ductility
5. **Barb cutting** — cut short lengths of wire (2–3cm) for barbs, or feed barb stock wire directly into barbing machine
6. **Barbing** — barbing machine twists two carrier wires together and wraps barb wire at set intervals (typically 75mm or 100mm between barbs)
7. **Galvanising** — run barbed wire through zinc bath (hot-dip) or electro-galvanising line
8. **Cooling and quenching** — quench in water after hot-dip galvanising
9. **Coiling** — wind finished wire onto reels at standard lengths (100m, 200m, 400m)
10. **Bundling** — strap coils; add product label with specification (gauge, length, manufacturer)

**Barb spacing:** Standard double-twist barbed wire — 75mm (3 inches) or 100mm (4 inches) between barbs.
**Wire gauges:** 14 gauge (2.0mm) light duty; 12 gauge (2.5mm) standard; 10 gauge (3.1mm) heavy duty.

## Uganda Regulatory Requirements

- **Business registration:** URA TIN, KCCA/district trading licence
- **NEMA permit:** Required for galvanising operations (zinc bath effluent management; zinc is a water pollutant)
- **Factory licence:** Ministry of Trade (metalworking with annealing furnace)
- **UNBS:** Barbed wire standard — verify applicable Uganda Standard (KS 640 Kenya Standard often referenced for import products)

---

## Market and Distribution

**Key buyer segments:**
- **Hardware shops and distributors:** Primary retail channel; buy by pallet (20–40 rolls); sell to end users
- **Agricultural buyers:** Farmers enclosing land; buy through agro-dealers and hardware shops
- **Construction contractors:** Site perimeter fencing; buy in bulk
- **Security/fencing contractors:** Install barbed wire and chain link; buy from manufacturer direct for margin
- **Institutional buyers:** Prisons, army, police (concertina/razor wire); large volume, competitive tender

**Distribution strategy:** Establish distributor relationships in Kampala hardware district (Nakivubo, Katwe, Kisekka) plus regional town distributors (Mbarara, Gulu, Jinja, Mbale). Barbed wire is a commodity — availability and consistent quality are the main differentiators from imports.

## Key Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Wire rod price volatility | Maintain 30-day stock; monitor rolling mill prices monthly |
| Zinc price volatility | Same as above; consider spot purchases on price dips |
| Wire drawing die wear | Maintain 2 spare die sets per size; replace at first sign of diameter drift |
| Galvanising effluent contamination | NEMA-compliant effluent treatment; zinc recovery from bath |
| Import competition (cheap Chinese product) | Compete on local availability, credit terms to distributors, and reliable spec |
| Wire breaking during drawing | Maintain correct die lubrication; anneal at correct intervals |
