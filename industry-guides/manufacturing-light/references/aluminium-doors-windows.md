# Aluminium Doors and Window Frames Manufacturing

**Sub-sector:** Construction Components
**Sources:** Thankaraju, P. (Startup Manufacturing Business Ideas 200, NestFame 2018)

> Uganda's construction boom — apartments, shopping malls, schools, and offices — creates strong demand for aluminium doors, windows, and shopfronts. Most fabrication shops in Uganda are small workshops competing on price; quality finishing, accurate measurement, and on-time installation are significant differentiators.

## Production Process

1. **Measure and design** — take accurate site measurements from builder or client; draw frame elevation
2. **Material ordering** — order profiles in correct lengths; allow 10% wastage for cutting losses
3. **Profile cutting** — cut frame members at 45° (mitre) on double-head saw; label all pieces
4. **Corner assembly** — insert corner brackets into profile channels; crimp with corner crimping machine
5. **Glazing bead cutting** — cut glazing beads to length; mitre or square cut depending on system
6. **Glass cutting** — cut glass to exact dimensions (allow 3mm clearance all around for thermal expansion)
7. **Rubber seal fitting** — press EPDM glazing seal into channel before glass insertion
8. **Glass insertion** — fit glass into frame; press glazing beads to secure
9. **Hardware fitting** — fit handles, locks, hinges; test operation
10. **Transport and installation** — transport to site; fit into prepared opening; level and plumb; seal with silicon

## Uganda Regulatory Requirements

- **Business registration:** URA TIN, KCCA or district trading licence
- **UNBS:** Aluminium building products — ensure profiles meet minimum wall thickness standards (not under-gauge Chinese profiles)
- **Professional installation warranty:** Increasingly required by architects and quantity surveyors on commercial projects

---

## Market and Distribution

**Key buyer segments:**
- **Developers and contractors:** Largest volume; supply entire apartment block or commercial building; negotiate price per window type
- **Architects and quantity surveyors:** Specify the product; cultivate relationships; being on approved suppliers list is valuable
- **Individual homeowners (extension/renovation):** Higher margin, smaller orders; acquired through referral and Facebook Marketplace
- **Institutions:** Schools, hospitals, clinics; often via tender

**Business development:**
- Maintain a showroom display of standard window and door configurations
- Produce a laminated price list with standard sizes and options
- Respond promptly to site measurement requests — speed closes more business than price
- Build relationships with construction foremen who influence product selection

## Key Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Measurement errors at site | Use standardised measurement form; confirm with client signature before cutting |
| Profile quality (thin-gauge imports) | Buy from reputable profile importers; test gauge with vernier calliper |
| Glass breakage during transport | Use padded vertical transport racks; proper packing |
| Cash flow (large orders, slow payment) | Require 50–70% deposit before cutting; balance on delivery |
| Competition from Chinese pre-made units | Compete on custom sizing and installation speed |
| Glass injury | PPE (leather gloves, safety boots) mandatory when handling glass |
