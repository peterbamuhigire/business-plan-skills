# Wire Nail Manufacturing

**Source:** Crack A Business Kenya (CAB) — Nails Making Guide (2018) | **Sub-sector:** Manufacturing — Light Metal Fabrication
**Conversion for KES figures:** KES ÷ 106 × 1.27 → March 2026 USD | **Equipment prices in USD:** FOB prices from 2018; apply ~15% uplift for 2026 inflation

> Small-to-medium-scale wire nail manufacturing using Chinese or Indian automated nail making machines. Wire is fed into the machine, which cuts and shapes nails to the specified length and diameter. Raw material (wire) is the dominant cost at 85–90% of production cost. Gross margin KES 13–30/kg depending on wire source (finished wire vs. raw wire). Setup scenarios range from US$8,100 to US$17,800 FOB Shanghai for Chinese equipment, or US$10,200–US$15,050 FOB India. Container freight from China to Mombasa approximately US$1,900 ($200,000 KES).

## Profitability Illustration

Using EN-3 equivalent, 3-inch nails, 2.9mm wire, 8hr/day, 30 days:

| Scenario | Wire cost | Gross margin/kg | Daily gross | Monthly gross |
|----------|-----------|----------------|------------|---------------|
| Finished wire (Tarmal KES 95) | KES 1,933,440 | KES 15/kg | KES 10,125 | KES 303,750 |
| Raw wire (KES 80) | KES 1,628,160 | KES 30/kg | KES 20,250 | KES 607,500 |

After monthly expenses (~KES 2,174,440 finished or ~KES 1,869,160 raw), net margins are thin — approximately KES 53,310 (finished) to KES 358,340 (raw wire) before tax, and only if full production is sold. The raw wire model requires a wire drawing machine (add ~US$1,000–$9,600 to setup cost depending on capacity).

## Premises Requirements

- Production area (machines + polisher): allow at least 2× machine footprint per machine for working space
- Store: raw wire + finished nails; allow for at least 4 weeks of raw material stock
- Administrative area, toilets, breakout room
- Minimum practical size: 3,000–5,000 sq ft depending on machine count
- Epoxy flooring recommended; good lighting and ventilation essential (noise and dust)
- Strong floor capable of bearing machine weights (2,000–2,600 kg per Z94-4C/5C)
- Renovation budget: KES 300,000–600,000

## Market and Distribution

**Main buyers:**
- Hardware shops (dominant channel)
- Construction sites and building contractors
- Government/institutional procurement (tenders)
- Wholesale distributors

**Pricing dynamics:** Nails are largely a commodity. Differentiation through consistent quality, reliable supply, and relationship with hardware owners matters more than price variation. A KES 5/kg difference drives purchasing decisions at wholesale.

## East Africa Notes

- **Uganda:** Wire nail manufacturing operates in Kampala's Namanve Industrial Park and Ntinda industrial zone. Uganda Steel Mills, Roofings Uganda, and Kilembe Mines produce steel inputs but availability and pricing for small nail manufacturers varies. Import of Chinese machines follows the same procurement path as Kenya (Mombasa port) but clearing costs differ. Uganda Manufacturers' Association provides buyer connections for manufactured products. KCCA trade licence, URA TIN, UNBS product certification (for construction materials) required. Selling price for nails in Kampala is slightly lower than Kenya due to import competition from Uganda Steel products.
- **Tanzania:** Nail manufacturing exists in Dar es Salaam's industrial areas (Ubungo, Chang'ombe). Tanzania's large construction sector (government housing programmes, private development in Dar, Arusha, Dodoma) creates significant demand. Wire inputs are available from local steel manufacturers (Steel Met Tanzania, ALAF) and import. BRELA registration and TRA TIN required. TBS product certification for construction fasteners is increasingly enforced. Import of machines via Dar es Salaam port; clearing costs similar to Kenya.
- **Rwanda:** Rwanda imports most construction materials including wire nails. Domestic nail manufacturing is very limited. A properly certified small nail manufacturer would face minimal domestic competition. Key inputs (wire) must be imported — via Mombasa/Kampala road or Dar es Salaam. RDB investment promotion can assist with import duty waivers on machinery for new manufacturing investments. RBS certification required for products sold in Rwanda.
- **EA general:** Wire nails are a staple construction input across all EA markets. Demand tracks construction activity. The key competitive risk in all markets is cheap imported nails from China and India at landed prices close to local manufacturing cost per kg. A local manufacturer must compete on quality consistency, delivery speed, and commercial relationships rather than on price alone.
