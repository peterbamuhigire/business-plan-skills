# Incense Sticks (Agarbatti) Manufacturing

**Sub-sector:** Natural Products / Aromatic Products
**Sources:** Thankaraju, P. (Startup Manufacturing Business Ideas 200, NestFame 2018)

> Incense sticks are used across East Africa for religious practice (Hindu temples, mosques, churches), aromatherapy and wellness businesses, and as a natural mosquito deterrent. A small-scale production unit can supply local temples, wellness shops, supermarkets, and export to South Asian diaspora communities. Low entry capital and simple production process make this accessible as a first manufacturing enterprise.

## Raw Materials

| Material | Role | Source |
|---------|------|--------|
| **Bamboo sticks** (pre-made agarbatti sticks) | The core/substrate — sticks onto which paste is rolled | Import from India/China via Kampala importers; or source bamboo locally and split by hand |
| **Jigat powder** (Makko powder / wood powder binder) | Binds aromatic paste to bamboo | Import; available from Indian spice/chemical importers |
| **Charcoal powder** | Adds bulk to paste and enables even burning | Local — charcoal ground to fine powder |
| **Wood powder** (sawdust, fine) | Bulking agent; improves burn quality | Local from sawmills (free or very cheap) |
| **Aromatic/botanical powders** | Fragrance base — sandalwood, frankincense, myrrh, herbs | Import sandalwood; source local botanicals (dried herbs, resins) |
| **Essential oil fragrances** | Primary scent carrier | Import (jasmine, rose, lavender, citronella for mosquito repellent) |
| **DOP / DBP** (plasticiser, optional) | Keeps paste pliable for machine rolling | Chemical distributors |
| **Water** | Makes paste workable | Municipal |
| **Packaging:** small boxes, cellophane wrap, labels | Consumer presentation | Local printers and packaging suppliers |

**Key local sourcing opportunity:** Substitute imported botanical powders with locally dried herbs (lemongrass, rosemary, eucalyptus, neem) for mosquito-repellent formulations — a differentiating EA-market product.

## Production Process

**Step 1 — Prepare paste:**
1. Mix Jigat powder + charcoal/wood powder in dry blender (typical ratio: 60% Jigat, 30% charcoal, 10% aromatic powder)
2. Add water gradually and mix to a consistent, non-sticky, rollable paste (similar to thick dough)
3. Add essential oil fragrance to paste and mix thoroughly
4. Rest paste for 30 minutes before rolling

**Step 2 — Roll sticks:**
5. Feed bamboo stick into machine guide
6. Machine feeds paste onto stick and rolls it to uniform diameter
7. Inspect each stick: uniform diameter, no bare patches, smooth finish

**Step 3 — Dry:**
8. Lay rolled sticks on trays or hang on drying racks
9. Air dry in ventilated shade for 24–48 hours (OR sun dry for 12–24 hours in direct sun)
10. Moisture content must fall below 10% for proper burning

**Step 4 — Quality check and pack:**
11. Test-burn a sample from each batch — check even burning, consistent fragrance release, no cracking or breaking
12. Bundle into standard counts (10, 20, 50 sticks per pack)
13. Pack in boxes or cellophane with label (product name, fragrance, manufacturer, quantity)

## Uganda Regulatory Requirements

- **Business registration:** URA TIN, KCCA/district trading licence
- **UNBS:** No mandatory Uganda Standard for incense sticks as of 2026 — but product must not contain prohibited substances
- **Customs/import:** Bamboo sticks and Jigat powder may attract import duty — check current UBOS tariff schedule
- **Packaging:** Label must include manufacturer name, address, contents, and country of manufacture

## Market and Distribution

**Key customer segments:**
- **Hindu temples and religious communities:** Regular bulk buyers; negotiate monthly supply contracts
- **Mosques and Islamic supply shops:** Oud and rose-scented incense; growing urban market
- **Wellness and spa businesses:** Premium fragrances (lavender, sandalwood, eucalyptus)
- **Supermarkets and general stores:** Branded retail packs
- **Market stalls (wholesale):** Street vendors who sell individual sticks or small packs
- **Hotels:** Aromatherapy in lobbies, spas, and rooms
- **Export:** Indian diaspora communities in UK, Canada, UAE — niche but reachable via social media and international online platforms

**Mosquito-repellent variant (EA-specific):**
Citronella and neem-based incense sticks position as a natural, low-smoke mosquito repellent — premium pricing vs. standard incense, and a distinct market from the religious buyer. This variant has high export appeal.

## Key Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Imported bamboo stick supply disruption | Maintain 30-day stock; explore local bamboo sourcing |
| Paste consistency variation | Standardise water-to-powder ratio per batch; weigh all ingredients |
| Sticks breaking during rolling | Check Jigat ratio; adjust water content |
| Poor burning (uneven, extinguishing mid-stick) | Correct drying time; test burn every batch before packing |
| Competing imports from India/China | Compete on freshness, local fragrance variants, and custom religious formulations |
| Essential oil cost volatility | Source alternatives from local botanicals where possible |
