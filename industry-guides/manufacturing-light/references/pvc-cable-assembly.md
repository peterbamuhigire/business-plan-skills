# PVC Electrical Cable Assembly

**Sub-sector:** Electrical Products
**Sources:** Thankaraju, P. (Startup Manufacturing Business Ideas 200, NestFame 2018)

> Uganda's rural electrification programme (REA) and urban construction boom create large and growing demand for electrical wiring cable. Full cable manufacturing (copper rod drawing + PVC compound production) is capital-intensive heavy industry. PVC cable **assembly** — buying pre-drawn copper conductor and extruding PVC insulation — is a viable light manufacturing operation with significantly lower capital entry.

## Raw Materials

| Material | Specification | Source |
|---------|--------------|--------|
| **Copper conductor** (pre-drawn, annealed) | 1.0mm², 1.5mm², 2.5mm², 4mm², 6mm² coils | Import from Kenya (CMC), India, or China — copper is a globally traded commodity |
| **PVC compound (insulation grade)** | Shore A hardness 80–85; rated for 70°C or 90°C | Import from Kenya/China; specialist PVC compounders |
| **PVC compound (sheath grade)** | Tougher, UV-resistant for outer sheath | Same suppliers as insulation grade |
| **Colour masterbatch** | Produces standard colour coding (red, black, yellow/green, blue, brown) | Plastic additive suppliers |
| **Cable reel / drum** | 100m or 200m wooden/plastic reels | Local reel manufacturers or import |

**IEC standard colour codes (Uganda follows UK wiring practice):**
- Live: Brown (new) or Red (old)
- Neutral: Blue (new) or Black (old)
- Earth: Green/Yellow

## Production Process

### Single-Core Insulated Wire (e.g., 2.5mm² house wire)

1. **Conductor payoff** — load copper conductor reel onto payoff; thread conductor through guide wheels to extruder die
2. **Extruder start-up** — heat extruder barrel to PVC processing temperature (160–180°C); confirm stable melt
3. **Colour change** — add colour masterbatch for required colour per batch
4. **Extrusion** — feed conductor at controlled speed through die; PVC melt coats conductor uniformly
5. **Cooling** — extruded wire passes through water trough; achieves dimensional stability
6. **Online testing** — spark tester applies 1,000–3,000V AC across insulation; any pinhole trips alarm
7. **Online gauging** — diameter gauge confirms OD within tolerance
8. **Haul-off** — caterpillar haul-off controls line speed (determines insulation wall thickness)
9. **Reeling** — cable wound onto 100m or 200m reels; reel counter measures exact length
10. **End-of-reel test** — check insulation resistance (megohm test) per IEC standard
11. **Labelling** — apply reel label: product code, length, conductor size, voltage rating, manufacturer, date, batch

### Twin/Flat Cable (additional steps)
After step 9, feed two insulated cores through bunching machine → second sheathing extrusion line applies outer PVC sheath → cool → spark test → reel

## Uganda Regulatory Requirements

| Requirement | Authority | Notes |
|------------|-----------|-------|
| UNBS product certification | Uganda National Bureau of Standards | US/IEC 60227 (PVC insulated cables); mandatory before commercial sale |
| Factory licence | Ministry of Trade, Industry and Cooperatives | |
| NEMA permit | National Environment Management Authority | PVC processing releases HCl fumes at high temperature; ventilation required |
| URA TIN + VAT | Uganda Revenue Authority | Import duty payable on copper conductor and PVC compound |

**UNBS type testing** involves independent laboratory testing of: conductor resistance, insulation resistance, insulation thickness, spark test, flame resistance, and heat resistance — budget UGX 3,000,000–6,000,000 for initial type approval.

## Market and Distribution

**Key buyer segments:**
- **Electrical wholesalers:** Primary distribution channel; supply hardware shops, contractors
- **Hardware shops:** Sell by the metre and by the reel to electricians and homebuilders
- **Electrical contractors:** Buy in bulk (full reels, pallet quantities) for construction projects
- **Rural Electrification Agency (REA) contractors:** High-volume buyers for electrification projects
- **Property developers:** Specify cable quality and brand for building projects
- **Government procurement:** Public buildings, schools, hospitals — via tender (PPDA)

**Build Uganda Build Uganda (BUBU):** Government policy mandates preference for locally manufactured goods in public procurement — a structural advantage for a Uganda-based cable manufacturer.

## Key Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Copper price volatility (globally traded commodity) | Maintain 15-day stock; price contracts with major buyers at cost-plus |
| Power supply disruption (affects extrusion quality) | Generator backup for extrusion lines; maintain smooth melt |
| UNBS certification costs and timeline | Plan 6–12 months; budget properly; no sales before certification |
| Competition from Kenyan imports (Eland, Nexans Kenya) | Compete on price (lower import duty advantage), delivery, and BUBU policy |
| Insulation pinholes (spark test failures) | Rigorous incoming PVC compound testing; monitor extrusion temperature closely |
| Die wear affecting OD tolerance | Regular die inspection; stock spare die sets per conductor size |
