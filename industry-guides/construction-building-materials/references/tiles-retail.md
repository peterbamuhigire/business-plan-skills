# Tiles Retail Business

**Source:** Crack A Business Kenya (CAB) — Tiles Retail Business Factsheet (2017) | **Sub-sector:** Construction & Building Materials — Tiles Retail
**Conversion:** KES ÷ 106 × 1.27 → March 2026 USD

> A tiles retail shop selling floor and wall tiles to individual home builders and building contractors. Capital KES 1,442,500 ($17,283). Margins range 8–25% depending on tile type and supplier. Daily sales in a good Nairobi location: 20–150 boxes. Key competitive factors: variety of suppliers, product knowledge, winning contractor accounts.

## Tile Types and Classifications

**Main types:**
- Ceramic — widest availability, most affordable, longest market presence
- Porcelain — premium quality, harder, preferred for high-traffic areas
- PVC — budget option, lightweight, easier to lay

**Classified by:**
- Size: 20×30, 30×30, 50×50, 60×60, 100×100 cm
- Texture/material: Ceramic, PVC, Porcelain
- Origin: Local, China, India
- Purpose: Floor tiles, wall tiles, border tiles, bathroom tiles
- Brand: Twyford, SAJ, RAK Ceramics, and others

## Popular Products

- **Chinese tiles** — most popular due to lower price point
- **Floor tiles** — outsell wall tiles because many customers paint walls rather than tiling
- **30×30 Twyford Ceramic** — most boxes have more pieces per box, covering larger area per purchase
- **Ceramic tiles generally** — well-established, affordable, readily available
- Brand popularity varies by location and income level of area

## Customer Profile

- Individual home builders — smaller quantities per transaction
- Building contractors — larger quantities; more valuable accounts; purchase in bulk for multi-storey projects
- Repair and renovation buyers — may purchase a single carton

## Setup Process

1. Identify location — consider construction activity, competition, available premises
2. Approach multiple wholesalers; compare prices, minimum quantities, and terms
3. Renovate premises
4. Obtain business licences
5. Stock with the most popular sizes and a variety of designs
6. Open and begin operations

## Critical Success Factors

- Multiple supplier relationships to provide wide variety
- Deep product knowledge (types, sizes, brands, quality differences) — used to win customers
- Targeting contractors over individual buyers; contractors = higher volumes
- Proactive marketing, not just waiting for walk-in
- Good relationships with wholesalers — access to new products and better pricing
- Quality consciousness — avoid stocking substandard tiles that damage reputation

## Market Trends (2017)

- Increased competition; specialist tile shops proliferating alongside hardware shops
- Growing consumer knowledge about brands and quality
- Price-based competition intensifying
- More large and medium-scale importers entering the market
- Constant introduction of new designs — stock must stay current

## East Africa Notes

- **Uganda:** Tiles retail is concentrated in Kampala's Nakasero, William Street, and Kiswa areas, with the Owino and Nakivubo markets as secondary sources. Most tiles are Chinese imports; Indian decorative tiles have a premium niche. Wholesalers (Simba Cement, Roofings, and independent Chinese-run importers) are the main supply chain. KCCA trade licence and URA TIN required. Uganda's rapid urbanisation and government housing programmes create growing demand. Kampala's construction boom (Muyenga, Kira, Wakiso peri-urban expansion) drives retail tile demand. UNBS does not have specific tiling product standards but general import documentation required.
- **Tanzania:** Dar es Salaam's tile retail cluster is around Kariakoo and Sinza. Chinese tiles dominate. Local manufacturer Saruji does not produce tiles; nearly all tiles are imported. BRELA registration and TRA TIN required. TBS product standards apply to tiles; Chinese tiles technically need TBS import certification. Tanzania's National Housing Corporation and private construction in Dar es Salaam, Arusha, and Dodoma drive demand. Contractor relationships are critical in Tanzania — large contractors procure centrally from a single supplier. Mombasa Road and Mombasa port are the import entry points for EA tiles.
- **Rwanda:** Rwanda's tile retail is concentrated in Kigali's Nyabugogo and Kimironko districts. Strict RBS standards enforcement means all imported tiles must meet certification requirements — factor into purchasing decisions. Rwanda's construction sector (Vision 2050 housing programmes, Kigali residential development) creates steady tile demand. Higher-income consumers and the hotel/MICE sector demand quality and design, making premium tiles a viable segment. RDB registration required. Tiles transported from Mombasa via Uganda road — add logistical premium to landed cost versus Kenya.
- **EA general:** The tiles retail business tracks construction activity across all EA markets. The dominant competitive threat is from large hardware chains and Chinese-run import-retail operations with lower overheads. Success for an independent tile retailer requires either location advantage (near active construction zones), product specialisation (premium/decorative tiles), or contractor relationship networks. Across EA, the shift from floor-only tiling to full wall-and-floor tiling in residential construction is expanding the total market.

## Data Quality Notes

Price data is from 2017 Kenya market surveys. Tile prices fluctuate with ceramic raw material costs, shipping rates, and KES/USD exchange rate movements. The KES 1,300,000 stock figure for 200 cartons should be verified against current wholesale prices before opening. The 8–25% margin range remains structurally valid — verify specific margins with your chosen supplier at current prices.
