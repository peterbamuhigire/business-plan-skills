# Cassava Seed Business — Uganda

## Seed System Overview

Cassava is propagated from stem cuttings (vegetative propagation), not true seed. "Cassava seed" means **stem cuttings** cut from healthy, disease-free parent plants. The quality of planting material is the single most critical determinant of cassava yield and disease resistance in Uganda.

### The Informal vs Formal Seed Market

| Feature | Informal seed system | Formal seed system |
|---------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Prevalence | **96% of Uganda cassava farmers** | ~4% of farmers (but growing) |
| Source | Saved stems; farmer-to-farmer exchange | NARO nurseries; certified CSEs/CBSMs |
| Cost | Near-zero (exchange/gift) | UGX 200–400/cutting (commercial CSEs) |
| Disease status | High CMD/CBSD risk; often infected | Disease-screened; certified clean |
| Variety access | Old/local varieties dominant | NASE 14 and released varieties available |
| Quality control | None | NSCS certification; rouging protocols |

**Key insight:** The 87% informal seed market is the primary disease transmission pathway in Uganda. Breaking this cycle through certified seed access is estimated to be the most cost-effective yield intervention available to smallholder cassava farmers.

## The Formal Seed System — Six Components

The Uganda formal cassava seed system (NARO/MAAIF 2015) has six tiers:

```
[1. NARO Research Stations]
        ↓ Basic seed / pre-basic material
[2. Cassava Basic Seed Multipliers (CBSMs)]
        ↓ Certified cuttings (large batches)
[3. Cassava Seed Entrepreneurs (CSEs)]
        ↓ Commercial clean cuttings (to farmers)
[4. Farmer Groups / Farmer Associations]
        ↓ Bulking and distribution at community level
[5. Individual Smallholder Farmers]
        ↓ On-farm seed saving (certified stock)
[6. Uganda Seed Trade Association (USTA) / NSCS oversight]
        Quality certification and market coordination
```

## Cassava Seed Entrepreneurs (CSEs)

### What is a CSE?

A CSE is a private individual or group who establishes and manages commercial cassava nursery gardens producing clean, disease-screened cuttings for sale. CSEs operate as business enterprises — they source basic material from CBSMs or NARO, multiply it, and sell certified cuttings to smallholder farmers.

### CSE Business Model

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Source material | Basic cuttings from CBSMs or NARO research stations |
| Garden size | Typically 0.5–2 hectares per CSE; expandable |
| Production output | ~10,000–40,000 cuttings per hectare per cropping cycle |
| Selling price | UGX 200–400 per cutting (retail); bulk/wholesale lower |
| Crop cycle | 9–12 months from planting to cutting harvest |
| Revenue potential | 10,000 cuttings × UGX 300 = **UGX 3M per hectare per cycle** |
| Key cost | Land, labour for rouging, NSCS certification fee |
| Certification | Required from NSCS before commercial sale |

### CSE Agronomy Requirements

- Source only from disease-free parent material (NARO or certified CBSM)
- Regular **rouging** (removing) of any CMD or CBSD-infected plants — critical; even one infected plant can spread via whiteflies
- Record all rouging events; keep plant population records
- Maintain isolation distance from non-certified cassava gardens
- Harvest cuttings at correct maturity (not too young/tender; not over-mature/woody)
- Bundle and label cuttings by variety; transport without drying out
- Maintain sales records as required by NSCS

### Income Projections (illustrative)

| Garden size | Cuttings produced | Price/cutting | Gross revenue | Approximate cost | Net income |
|------------|-----------------|--------------|--------------|-----------------|-----------|
| 0.5 ha | 5,000–20,000 | UGX 300 | UGX 1.5–6M | UGX 800K–1.5M | UGX 700K–4.5M |
| 1.0 ha | 10,000–40,000 | UGX 300 | UGX 3–12M | UGX 1.5–3M | UGX 1.5–9M |
| 2.0 ha | 20,000–80,000 | UGX 300 | UGX 6–24M | UGX 3–6M | UGX 3–18M |

*Wide range reflects rouging losses (CMD/CBSD infection rate), management quality, and market timing. Prices higher at planting season peaks.*

## Cassava Basic Seed Multipliers (CBSMs)

CBSMs occupy the tier above CSEs — they receive foundational material directly from NARO and multiply it at larger scale for supply to CSEs and large-scale producers:

- Typically organised as larger enterprises, cooperatives, or NGO-supported groups
- Garden sizes 2–10 hectares
- Supply cuttings to multiple CSEs within a region
- Subject to NSCS inspection and certification at CBSM tier level
- Critical node: if CBSM stock is contaminated, disease spreads across an entire CSE network

## Key Institutional Actors

| Actor | Role |
|-------|------|
| **NARO** (National Agricultural Research Organisation) | Develops and maintains disease-free basic seed stocks; releases improved varieties (NASE series); trains CBSMs |
| **MAAIF** (Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries) | Policy and regulatory oversight of seed system; funding support; extension services |
| **NSCS** (National Seed Certification Service) | Certifies seed/cutting quality; inspects nurseries; issues certification labels |
| **USTA** (Uganda Seed Trade Association) | Industry association; market coordination; advocacy; links CSEs to input suppliers |
| **District Agricultural Officers** | Extension delivery; farmer group coordination at district level |
| **USTA-affiliated agro-dealers** | Retail point for certified cuttings; stocking and sale at farm input shops |

## Seed Quality Management

### Disease Rouging Protocol

Rouging is the systematic removal and destruction of infected or suspect plants from nursery gardens:

1. **Train nursery workers** to identify CMD (mosaic yellow-green leaf patterning) and CBSD (brown stem streaks; necrotic root lesions)
2. **Inspect weekly** — CMD spreads rapidly via whiteflies; early detection is essential
3. **Remove entire plant** including roots; burn or bury removed material; do not compost
4. **Record each rouging event** (date, location in garden, plant count)
5. **NSCS inspector verification** — certification requires documented rouging records and garden inspection
6. **Isolation:** Maintain ≥100 m isolation distance from uncertified cassava plantings if possible

### Cutting Quality Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|----------|------------|
| Maturity | 9–12 months old stem; 2–3 cm diameter preferred |
| Length | 20–30 cm per cutting; minimum 3–5 nodes |
| Health | No visible CMD/CBSD symptoms; bark undamaged |
| Storage | Plant or transport within 3–5 days; keep shaded/moist; do not expose to direct sun |
| Labelling | Variety name; source nursery; NSCS certification number |

## Commercialisation Framework

### Steps to Start a CSE Business

1. **Training** — attend MAAIF/NARO/USTA CSE training programme (3–5 days)
2. **Source material** — obtain basic cuttings from NARO station or certified CBSM; agree supply volume
3. **Land preparation** — prepare nursery garden; fence if possible; apply basal compost/manure
4. **Plant and manage** — plant at recommended density; implement rouging programme; maintain records
5. **Apply for NSCS certification** — submit garden for inspection at 3–4 months; maintain records for inspector
6. **Market development** — register with USTA; link with farmer groups; confirm orders before harvest
7. **Harvest and sell** — cut, bundle, label; deliver promptly; collect payment

### Market Development

- **Seasonal demand peaks:** Coincide with main and secondary rains planting windows; prepare supply in advance
- **Farmer group contracts:** Negotiate pre-orders with farmer groups and cooperatives — reduces sales risk
- **MAAIF/NGO project supply:** Government planting programmes and NGO-supported projects often procure certified cuttings in large quantities (10,000+ cuttings per order)
- **Cross-border demand:** South Sudan, DRC, Rwanda have demand for certified cassava material; requires phytosanitary certificate from MAAIF

### Scaling the Business

| Growth path | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| Volume increase | Expand nursery area; hire additional rouging labour |
| Vertical integration | CSE adds primary processing (chips/flour) using own production cassava |
| Franchise model | Trained CSE supports and certifies satellite smaller nurseries |
| Input dealership | CSE also sells NARO-recommended inputs (NPK fertiliser, tools) to farmers at planting |
| Value-added | Process some root production through HQCF or chips alongside cutting sales |

## Linkages to Production and Processing

The seed business sits at the foundation of the entire cassava value chain:

- **Higher-quality cuttings** → higher-yielding disease-free crops → more fresh roots for processing
- **CMD/CBSD clean supply** → processors can sign reliable off-take contracts knowing consistent yield
- **NASE 14 adoption** (the standard certified variety in Uganda) → better compliance with processor quality requirements (higher dry matter content than local varieties)
- **Mechanised farmers** need certified cuttings at scale (10,000+ per 10 acres) — CSEs that can supply in bulk at planting season are a critical bottleneck in mechanisation programmes
