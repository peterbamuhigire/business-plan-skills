1. FUNDAMENTAL COST & SALES FORMULAS
================================================================================

THE COSTING TRIANGLE (Three Core Formulas)
-------------------------------------------
These three interrelated formulas are the foundation of all restaurant
financial analysis:

  Cost / Sales x 100 = Cost %
  Cost / Cost % (as decimal) = Sales (or Sales Price)
  Sales x Cost % (as decimal) = Cost

Applied to each cost category:

  Food Cost / Food Sales x 100 = Food Cost %
  Beverage Cost / Beverage Sales x 100 = Beverage Cost %
  Labour Cost / Total Sales x 100 = Labour Cost %

PRIME COST
----------
  Prime Cost = Food Cost + Beverage Cost + Labour Cost
             (salaries, wages, and employee benefits)

This represents the largest portion of total costs for virtually all
foodservice operations. Typically 60-70% of total sales.

OVERHEAD COST
-------------
  Overhead = All costs other than prime cost
           (rent, utilities, insurance, depreciation, interest, etc.)

SALES FORMULAS
--------------
  Average Check = Total Sales / Total Number of Covers

  Average Sale per Server = Total Sales for Server / Number of Customers Served

  Total Sales per Seat = Total Sales (annual) / Number of Seats

  Seat Turnover = Number of Customers Served / Number of Seats
  (also called "turns")

  Sales Mix % = Number Sold of Item / Total Number Sold of All Items x 100

  Covers per Hour = Total Covers / Number of Hours of Operation
  Covers per Day = Total Covers / Number of Days of Operation
  Covers per Server = Total Covers / Number of Servers

-------------------------------------------------------
  Cost of food and beverages:                     40%
  Labour cost:                                    20%
  Other controllable/noncontrollable costs:        30%
  Profit before income taxes:                      10%
  TOTAL:                                          100%

  Cost of food and beverages:                     25%
  Labour cost:                                    35%
  Other controllable/noncontrollable costs:        30%
  Profit before income taxes:                      10%
  TOTAL:                                          100%

------------------------------------------------------
  Food cost as % of food sales:                   42.00%
  Beverage cost as % of beverage sales:           32.00%
  Combined food & beverage cost as % of total:    40.50%
  Payroll as % of total sales:                    33.75%
  Overhead as % of total sales:                   23.31%
  Prime cost as % of total sales:                 74.25%
  Profit before taxes as % of total sales:         2.44%

KEY INDUSTRY RANGES
-------------------
  Food cost percent:           25% - 42% (varies by type of operation)
  Beverage cost percent:       18% - 35% (varies by category)
    - Spirits:                 ~18-20%
    - Wine:                    ~35-40%
    - Beer:                    ~25-35%
  Labour cost percent:         15% - 45% (60% in not-for-profit)
  Prime cost percent:          58% - 75%
  Profit margin:               5% - 12% (approx. 10% typical target)
  Most profitable restaurants: ~10% profit margin after all costs

NOTE: "The cost structure for each individual restaurant must be determined
for that restaurant alone, the obvious point being that, as percentages of
sales, costs must always total less than 100.0 percent if the operation is
to be profitable."

4. FOOD COST CALCULATION & CONTROL
================================================================================

MONTHLY FOOD COST DETERMINATION
-------------------------------
Basic Formula:
  Opening Inventory
  + Purchases
  = Total Available
  - Closing Inventory
  = Cost of Food Issued

Full Formula with Adjustments:
  Opening Inventory
  + Purchases
  = Total Available for Sale
  - Closing Inventory
  = Cost of Food Issued
  + Cooking Liquor (alcohol transferred from bar to kitchen)
  + Transfers from Other Units
  - Food to Bar (directs transferred kitchen to bar)
  - Transfers to Other Units
  - Steward Sales (food sold to employees at cost)
  - Gratis to Bar (complimentary hors d'oeuvres for bar)
  - Promotion Expense (food for entertaining potential clients)
  = Cost of Food Consumed
  - Cost of Employee Meals
  = Cost of Food Sold

  Food Cost % = Cost of Food Sold / Food Sales x 100

WORKED EXAMPLE (Grandview Bistro, February):
  Opening inventory:              UGX  3,714,000
  + Food purchases:               UGX  9,721,000
  = Total available:              UGX 13,435,000
  - Closing inventory:            UGX  3,857,000
  = Cost of food issued:          UGX  9,578,000
  + Cooking liquor:               UGX     82,000
  = Subtotal:                     UGX  9,660,000
  - Food to bar (directs):        UGX     70,000
  - Steward sales:                UGX     33,000
  - Gratis to bar:                UGX    120,000
  - Promotion expense:            UGX    103,000
  = Cost of food consumed:        UGX  9,334,000
  - Employee meals:               UGX    433,000
  = Cost of food sold:            UGX  8,901,000

  Food Cost % = UGX 8,901,000 / UGX 26,963,000 = 33.02%

EMPLOYEE MEAL COST METHODS
--------------------------
1. Cost of separate issues (separate requisitions for employee food)
2. Prescribed amount per meal (UGX 2,000 breakfast, UGX 3,500 lunch, UGX 5,000 dinner)
3. Prescribed amount per period (flat daily/weekly amount)
4. Sales value x food cost % (employees record selections, multiply
   total menu price by average food cost percent)

5. BEVERAGE COST CALCULATION & CONTROL
================================================================================

MONTHLY BEVERAGE COST DETERMINATION
------------------------------------
  Opening Beverage Inventory
  + Beverage Purchases
  = Total Available for Sale
  - Closing Inventory
  = Value of Beverages Issued to Bar
  +/- Bar Inventory Differential*
  = Cost of Beverages Consumed

  * Bar Inventory Differential:
    Bar inventory at beginning of month
    - Bar inventory at end of month
    = Differential (positive = add; negative = subtract)

  Beverage Cost % = Cost of Beverages Consumed / Beverage Sales x 100

FULL ADJUSTMENT FORMULA:
  Cost of Liquor Consumed
  + Food to bar (directs: oranges, lemons, cream)
  + Storeroom issues (olives, cherries, onions)
  + Mixers
  = Subtotal
  - Cooking liquor (transferred to kitchen)
  - Officers' drinks
  - Special promotions
  = Cost of Beverages Sold (adjusted)

  Adjusted Beverage Cost % = Adjusted Cost / Net Beverage Sales

BEVERAGE COST BY CATEGORY (Grandview Bistro, February):
  Spirits:    18.63%   (UGX 520,200 / UGX 2,792,200)
  Wine:       39.14%   (UGX 621,300 / UGX 1,587,200)
  Beer:       34.62%   (UGX 119,800 / UGX 346,100)
  Overall:    26.69%   (UGX 1,261,300 / UGX 4,725,500)

THREE APPROACHES TO MONITORING BEVERAGE OPERATIONS:
1. Cost Approach - comparing cost of beverages sold with actual/standard cost
2. Liquid Measure Approach - comparing ounces sold vs ounces consumed
3. Sales Value Approach - comparing potential sales value with actual revenue

STANDARD COST METHOD FOR BEVERAGES:
  For each drink type:
    Number Sold x Standard Cost per Drink = Total Standard Cost
  Sum all drink types = Total Standard Beverage Cost for Period
  Compare standard cost with actual cost to identify variances

DAILY BEVERAGE COST TRACKING:
  Same cumulative approach as food:
  Beverage Cost to Date / Beverage Sales to Date x 100 = Cost % to Date

6. LABOUR COST CALCULATION & CONTROL
================================================================================

LABOUR COST RANGES
------------------
  General range:           15% to 45% of sales
  Well-managed full-service: ~24.5% (including benefits)
  Not-for-profit:          up to 60%

LABOUR COST FORMULA
-------------------
  Labour Cost % = (Salaries + Wages + Employee Benefits) / Total Sales x 100

EMPLOYEE CATEGORIES FOR CONTROL
-------------------------------
Variable-Cost Personnel (linked to business volume):
  - Servers, bussers, dishwashers, prep cooks
  - Number varies with volume; hours staggered to match demand

Fixed-Cost Personnel (unrelated to business volume):
  - Managers, bookkeepers, chefs, stewards, cashiers
  - Number remains relatively constant regardless of volume

STANDARD WORK HOURS
-------------------
  Standard Work Hours = Number of Employees x Hours Worked

  Example: 8 servers x 3-hour lunch = 24 standard work hours for 500 covers

  Covers per Standard Work Hour = Covers Served / Standard Work Hours
  Example: 500 / 24 = 20.83 covers per work hour

STANDARD LABOUR COST
--------------------
  Standard Labour Cost = Standard Work Hours x Hourly Wage Rate

  Example: 24 hours x UGX 3,000/hour = UGX 72,000 standard cost for servers

STANDARD STAFFING TABLE EXAMPLE:
  Covers     Servers  Warewashers  Prep Personnel
  200-299       5         2             3
  300-399       6         2             3
  400-499       7         3             4
  500-599       8         4             4

STANDARD WORK-HOUR REQUIREMENTS TABLE:
  Covers     Service  Warewashing  Preparation
  200-299      15         6            9
  300-399      18         6            9
  400-499      21         9           12
  500-599      24        12           12

SCHEDULING METHODOLOGY
----------------------
1. Maintain daily and hourly records of covers served
2. Calculate median volume (not average - unaffected by extremes)
3. Develop staffing tables based on test period observations
4. Create hourly schedules matching staff to anticipated demand
5. Use split shifts and part-time staff to cover peak periods
6. Track maximum, minimum, and median hourly volumes

COVERS PER SERVER GUIDELINE:
  Approximately 20 covers per server per hour (varies by service type)
