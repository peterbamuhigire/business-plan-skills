# Reselling and Trading Side Hustles

**Audience:** EA professionals with sourcing, market, or logistics connections
**Sources:** Guillebeau (2017), Simon (2022)

> Reselling is the oldest trade in Africa. The modern version uses WhatsApp, Facebook Marketplace, and Jumia to reach buyers, while sourcing from China (Alibaba/DHgate), local wholesalers, or farm-gate producers. A professional who understands supply chains from their day job is better positioned than most to identify gaps and arbitrage opportunities.

## 1. WhatsApp Fashion Reselling

**The model:** Source clothing (new or second-hand) from wholesale markets; sell at retail prices to your WhatsApp network.

**Product sources:**

| Source | Product Type | Cost Advantage |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Owino Market (Kampala) / Gikomba (Nairobi) | Mitumba (second-hand clothes) | Low cost; high variety; negotiate on bulk |
| Kikuubo (Kampala) | New clothing wholesale | Cheaper than retail; negotiate on orders of 10+ pieces |
| Alibaba / DHgate (China) | New clothing, footwear, accessories | Cheapest but requires import logistics; minimum order quantities |
| Local boutiques (clearance stock) | End-of-season stock at discount | Buy at 30–50% of retail; sell at full retail |

**WhatsApp reselling mechanics:**
1. Source 10–20 items
2. Photograph on a clean background or on yourself/a model
3. Add prices and size information
4. Post on WhatsApp Status, Facebook, Instagram
5. Accept orders via WhatsApp; delivery via boda or Uber courier
6. Reinvest profits into next stock purchase

**Income scenario:**
- Buy 20 tops at UGX 15,000 each (Owino wholesale) = UGX 300,000 investment
- Sell 18 of 20 at UGX 35,000–50,000 each = UGX 630,000–900,000
- Gross profit after unsold stock: UGX 330,000–600,000 on one stock rotation (1–2 weeks)

**Building a following:** Post consistently (daily WhatsApp Status); create "limited stock" urgency; offer free delivery within your area on orders above UGX 100,000; feature customer photos.

## 2. Chinese Imports (Alibaba / DHgate)

**The model:** Import small consumer goods from China via air cargo or cargo companies; sell at 3–10× purchase price locally.

**High-margin categories for EA markets:**

| Category | China Cost | EA Retail Price | Markup |
|----------|-----------|----------------|--------|
| Phone cases and accessories | $0.50–$2 | UGX 10,000–25,000 ($3–$7) | 3–5× |
| Wigs and hair extensions | $5–$20 | UGX 50,000–200,000 ($14–$54) | 3–10× |
| Jewellery and accessories | $0.50–$3 | UGX 10,000–40,000 | 4–8× |
| Stationery and school supplies | $0.20–$1 | UGX 5,000–20,000 | 5–10× |
| Household items (kitchen gadgets) | $2–$8 | UGX 25,000–80,000 | 3–5× |
| Sports and gym equipment (small items) | $5–$20 | UGX 60,000–200,000 | 3–6× |
| Cosmetics (non-regulated) | $1–$5 | UGX 20,000–60,000 | 4–8× |

**Import logistics for Uganda:**

| Method | Lead time | Minimum | Cost/kg |
|--------|-----------|---------|---------|
| Air cargo via freight forwarder (Guangzhou) | 7–14 days | 1 kg | $6–$12/kg |
| Sea cargo (container) | 45–60 days | 1 CBM | $1–$3/kg (much cheaper) |
| Buying from agents in Dubai/Nairobi with Ugandan redistribution | Variable | Variable | Varies |
| Sending via a personal contact travelling | Occasional | Small quantities | Cost of excess baggage |

**Starting safely:**
1. Order a small test shipment (UGX 500,000–1,000,000 in goods) via an established Kampala freight forwarder
2. Use Alibaba Trade Assurance or DHgate buyer protection for first orders
3. Never pay 100% upfront to a new supplier
4. Factor in: product cost + shipping + import duty (EAC rates vary by product) + clearing fees
5. Test product quality before large orders

**Duty consideration (Uganda URA):** Import duties apply on most consumer goods entering Uganda. Use a licensed clearing agent to calculate accurate landed cost before pricing. Duties can range from 0% (some machinery) to 25% (most consumer goods) + 18% VAT.

## 3. Fresh Produce Reselling

**The model:** Source vegetables, fruits, or specific commodities from farm-gate or wholesale markets; redistribute to urban professionals who pay a premium for convenience and quality.

**EA professional market demand:** Working professionals in Kampala/Nairobi apartments will pay a 30–50% premium over market price for:
- Weekly vegetable boxes delivered to their door
- Washed and portioned salad greens
- Organic/natural produce from known sources
- Specific items not always available locally (cherry tomatoes, avocado, fresh herbs)

**Operations:**
1. Identify a reliable farmer or wholesale market supplier (Nakawa, Kalerwe, or farm partnerships)
2. Build a WhatsApp order list: announce availability weekly, take orders, deliver on a fixed day
3. Transport: personal vehicle for medium volumes; hire a pickup truck monthly for larger volumes

**Income scenario (weekly vegetable box delivery):**
- Source vegetables from Nakawa: UGX 15,000/box (mixed vegetables for a family)
- Sell to urban professional client: UGX 25,000/box delivered
- 20 clients × UGX 10,000 margin = UGX 200,000/week = UGX 800,000/month

**Higher-value model — speciality produce:** Supply specific items to restaurants and hotels (cherry tomatoes, baby spinach, rocket, herbs). A supply contract with one Kampala hotel = UGX 200,000–500,000/week of steady income.

## 4. Digital Products (Templates, Files)

**The model:** Create a digital product once; sell it infinitely for near-zero marginal cost.

**Highest-demand digital products in EA:**

| Product | Platform | Price | EA-specific demand |
|---------|---------|-------|--------------------|
| Professional CV template | Jiji, WhatsApp, Gumroad | UGX 5,000–20,000 | Very high — job market extremely competitive |
| Business plan template | WhatsApp, direct | UGX 50,000–200,000 | High — banks and investors require |
| Company profile / proposal template | WhatsApp, direct | UGX 30,000–100,000 | High — SMEs need professional presentation |
| Excel financial model template | WhatsApp, direct | UGX 50,000–200,000 | Medium — accountants and business owners |
| Canva social media template pack | Gumroad, WhatsApp | UGX 20,000–80,000 | High — businesses need graphics |
| Church/wedding programme templates | WhatsApp | UGX 15,000–40,000 | Very high — events happen weekly |
| School academic calendar and timetable | WhatsApp, Facebook | UGX 10,000–30,000 | High — schools and parents |

**Creating digital products:**
- Use Canva, Google Docs, Excel, or PowerPoint — tools already familiar to corporate professionals

**Revenue example:** 200 CV template sales/year at UGX 15,000 = UGX 3,000,000 from one product created in one day.

## 5. Agency / Drop-Shipping Model

**The model:** Sell products from an established supplier without holding inventory. You take the order and commission; the supplier fulfils.

**How it works:**
1. Identify a manufacturer or wholesaler who will give you 10–20% commission on sales you refer
2. You market the products via WhatsApp, Facebook, Instagram
3. Customer places order with you → you forward to supplier → supplier delivers → you earn commission

**EA-relevant agencies:**
- Cosmetics and beauty product distributors (e.g., wholesale cosmetics companies)
- Insurance agents (commission-based, no capital required — licensed separately)
- Cooking gas resellers (become an agent for EBK, Total, or Shell GasPlus)
- Airtime and data bundle resellers (formal or informal MTN/Airtel sub-dealerships)
- Agricultural inputs (seeds, fertiliser) referral agents for agro-dealers

**Commission rates:** 10–25% per sale depending on product and supplier relationship.

## Building a Trading Brand on WhatsApp

**The platform (Amitabh, 2022 / Guillebeau, 2017):**

1. **WhatsApp Business account** — Use instead of personal WhatsApp; allows business hours, catalogue, quick replies, labels
2. **Product catalogue** — Upload photos, descriptions, and prices in WhatsApp Business catalogue (free)
3. **Broadcast lists** — Send price updates to segmented lists (existing buyers; interested but not yet bought; bulk buyers)
4. **WhatsApp Status** — 24-hour product posts; consistently high engagement from warm contacts
5. **Facebook Marketplace** — List products with price and photos; local discovery without ads

**Trust building (critical for resellers):**
- Post customer testimonials and photos with permission
- Share "behind the scenes" (sourcing trip to Owino; products arriving from China)
- Never misrepresent product quality — one disappointed customer posts publicly; 10 satisfied customers stay quiet
- Offer returns on defective items; absorb the loss and learn from the supplier feedback

## Risk and Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Slow-moving stock (can't sell what you bought) | Start small; sell test quantities before committing to bulk |
| Counterfeit or poor-quality Chinese imports | Order samples first; use verified Alibaba Gold Suppliers with 4+ star ratings |
| Customs seizure or unexpected duties | Use a licensed clearing agent; declare goods accurately |
| Currency exchange risk (USD buying power fluctuates) | Factor in ±10% exchange rate buffer in your pricing |
| Competition (others copy your product/price) | Build brand loyalty; compete on service and consistency, not just price |
| WhatsApp banning your number | Maintain a backup number; never spam; keep a separate Excel/Sheets customer list |
