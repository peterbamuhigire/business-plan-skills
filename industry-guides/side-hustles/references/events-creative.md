# Events and Creative Side Hustles

**Audience:** EA professionals with photography, performance, or organisational skills
**Sources:** Guillebeau (2017), Simon (2022)

> Events are the backbone of East African social and professional life. Weddings, graduations, church services, corporate functions, fundraisers, and community celebrations happen every weekend. Creative professionals who build a reputation in this space earn premium fees for weekend work that does not interfere with weekday employment.

## 1. Photography

**Sub-types and entry requirements:**

| Type | Camera Needed | Skill Level | Starting Rate |
|------|--------------|-------------|---------------|
| Smartphone photography (events, content) | Phone (Tecno Camon, Samsung A-series) | Basic | UGX 50,000–150,000/event |
| Entry-level DSLR (graduations, corporate) | Canon 1300D / Nikon D3500 | Intermediate | UGX 150,000–400,000/event |
| Full-frame DSLR/Mirrorless (weddings) | Canon 6D, Sony A7 series | Advanced | UGX 800,000–2,500,000/event |

**The progressive equipment strategy:**
1. **Phase 1 (0–6 months):** Use your phone. Offer to photograph a friend's event for free or UGX 50,000 to build your portfolio
2. **Phase 2 (6–12 months):** Rent a DSLR per event (UGX 50,000–100,000/day rental at photography shops in Kampala's William Street) — include rental in your pricing
3. **Phase 3 (12–24 months):** Purchase your own camera from first year's profits

**Building a portfolio:**
- Photograph 5–10 events for free or near-free for friends/family
- Post consistently on Instagram and Facebook with location tags
- Create a Google Photos or Canva portfolio link to share on WhatsApp

**Pricing structure (weddings):**
- Basic package (4 hours + 200 edited photos): UGX 800,000
- Standard package (8 hours + 400 edited photos + USB drive): UGX 1,500,000
- Premium package (2 shooters, full day, album): UGX 2,500,000

**Editing tools:** Lightroom Mobile (free on phone); Adobe Lightroom (PC); Canva for collages and photo books

**Key income opportunity — corporate photography:** Companies need product photography, headshots, events, and office documentation. A single corporate client retainer (2 events/month) at UGX 300,000/event = UGX 600,000/month with minimal marketing effort.

## 2. Videography

**Why videography commands premium rates:** Editing is time-intensive and skill-dependent. A well-edited wedding video at UGX 2,000,000 for 2–3 weeks of weekend shooting + editing work earns more per hour than most corporate salaries.

**Entry point:** An iPhone 13+ or Samsung S-series shoots broadcast-quality video. Start with phone video; invest in a gimbal stabiliser (UGX 150,000–400,000) for smooth footage.

**Software:**
- CapCut (free, phone-based — excellent for reels and short clips)
- DaVinci Resolve (free, PC-based — professional colour grading)
- Premiere Pro (paid, subscription)

**Income acceleration:** YouTube content creation for businesses is an emerging service. A monthly retainer to produce 4 short videos for a company's YouTube/TikTok page at UGX 400,000/month = reliable recurring income.

## 3. Event MC / Emcee

**What it is:** Hosting and facilitating events — weddings, corporate functions, product launches, NGO events, church fundraisers, gala dinners.

**Requirements:** Confident English and/or local language speaker; professional appearance; ability to keep an audience engaged and an event on schedule.

**How to price:**
- Introductory rate (to build portfolio): UGX 100,000–200,000
- Established MC (weddings, corporate): UGX 300,000–600,000
- Premium MC (high-profile corporate, international events): UGX 800,000–2,000,000

**Building a reputation:**
1. MC one free event for a well-connected family or organisation
2. Ask for a video testimonial and permission to post it
3. Build a showreel (3–5 minute highlight video across multiple events)
4. List on event planning WhatsApp groups; partner with event planners and caterers who can refer you

**Competitive edge:** Bilingual MCs (English + Luganda, or English + Swahili/Kinyarwanda) command premium rates and are in shorter supply.

## 4. Wedding and Event Decor

**What it is:** Designing and setting up event venue decorations — chair covers, table centrepieces, flower arrangements, draping, lighting, and themed setups.

**Capital structure:**

| Approach | Startup Cost | How |
|----------|-------------|-----|
| Hire-in model | UGX 0–200,000 | Rent all decor items from a decor supplier; mark up 30–50% and manage setup |
| Own basic inventory | UGX 500,000–2,000,000 | Buy chair covers, organza, fairy lights; rent speciality items |
| Full inventory | UGX 3,000,000–10,000,000 | Own all items; maximum margin but requires storage |

**Recommended entry:** Start with the hire-in model. Learn client preferences and popular styles before investing in your own inventory.

**Income example:** Decor for 100-person wedding:
- Cost (hiring in materials): UGX 400,000
- Charged to client: UGX 800,000–1,200,000
- Gross profit: UGX 400,000–800,000 for 1–2 days work

**Complementary services to package with decor:** Photography, catering, cake baking — bundle as a one-stop event package and increase total invoice value.

## 5. Event Planning and Coordination

**What it is:** Managing the full logistics of an event — vendor sourcing, timeline management, budget tracking, day-of coordination.

**Who hires:** Busy professionals and families who can afford events but lack time to plan them; corporations; NGOs for conferences and launches.

**Fee structures:**
- Flat fee per event: UGX 500,000–2,000,000 depending on event size
- Percentage of event budget: 10–15% (common for large weddings/corporate events)
- Day-of coordination only: UGX 200,000–500,000

**Key competitive advantage:** Corporate event planning experience from your day job transfers directly. A finance manager who understands budget control, a project manager who can run a Gantt chart, or a marketing professional who understands brand presentation — all of these skills are directly applicable.

## Marketing Your Creative Services

**Primary channels (EA):**
1. **WhatsApp Status** — Post event photos/videos consistently; every viewer is a potential client
2. **Facebook personal profile + business page** — Tag venues, families (with permission); use location tags
3. **Instagram** — Visual portfolio; hashtag strategy (e.g., #KampalaWeddings, #NairobiEvents)
4. **WhatsApp vendor groups** — Join wedding planner, events, and corporate procurement groups
5. **Word of mouth** — Every event is an audition; collect phone numbers; follow up 6 months later ("Do you know anyone with an event coming up?")

**Pricing mistake to avoid:** Underpricing to win clients destroys the market for everyone and signals low quality. Research what comparable services cost in your city and price at or above the midpoint.

## Risk and Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Equipment failure on event day | Always carry backup battery; backup lens; test equipment night before |
| Client cancellation (no refund policy) | Non-refundable 30% deposit in writing at booking |
| Event day conflicts with day job | Never book events during work hours; be honest about availability |
| Scope creep ("can you also do video?") | Written agreement specifying exactly what is included |
| Editing backlog | Deliver within agreed timeline; communicate delays in advance |
