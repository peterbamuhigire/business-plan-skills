# Food Commerce Side Hustles

**Audience:** EA professionals with cooking skills or food sourcing connections
**Sources:** Guillebeau (2017), Simon (2022)

> Food is the most universal side hustle in East Africa. Every office has someone selling cakes, samosas, or homemade meals to colleagues. Done with consistency and quality, a food side hustle can scale from a UGX 200,000/month colleague trade to a UGX 5,000,000+/month WhatsApp business in 12–18 months.

## 1. WhatsApp Home Baking

**The model:** Bake from your home kitchen; take orders via WhatsApp; deliver or arrange collection.

**Products with highest demand in Kampala/Nairobi:**
- Birthday and celebration cakes (custom)
- Mandazi, samosas, chapati (bulk office orders)
- Doughnuts and pastries
- Cupcakes (event and corporate orders)
- Banana bread and loaves

**Starting with zero capital:**
1. Bake one product you make well; offer to colleagues or WhatsApp contacts at cost price
2. Get feedback; improve; photograph every batch professionally (phone + good natural light)
3. Set prices at cost × 2.5–3 (food businesses target 60–70% gross margin)
4. Start posting on WhatsApp Status 3x/week: new products, testimonials, order confirmations
5. Create a simple price list as a graphic (Canva) to share in groups

**Custom cake pricing (Kampala, 2026):**

| Cake Type | Size | Market Price (UGX) |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| Basic round (sponge, simple icing) | 1 kg | 60,000–90,000 |
| Fondant/themed cake | 2 kg | 150,000–250,000 |
| Tiered wedding cake (3 tiers) | — | 400,000–1,200,000 |
| Cupcakes | Per dozen | 60,000–120,000 |
| Corporate branded cakes | 2–3 kg | 200,000–500,000 |

**Key investment:** Oven (electric or gas) — UGX 400,000–800,000 for a domestic oven; or use charcoal oven (sufuria method) to start for UGX 0.

## 2. Office and Colleague Sales

**The most accessible food side hustle:** Sell to colleagues at work.

**High-performing products for office sales:**
- Samosas and spring rolls (prep evening before; sell at break time)
- Homemade juice/smoothies in sealed bottles
- Chapati rolls with egg or beans
- Mandazi and tea biscuits
- Health snacks (granola bars, dried fruit) for health-conscious offices

**Pricing:** Mark up 100–150% on raw material cost. A samosa costing UGX 1,000 to make sells for UGX 2,000–3,000.

**Volume:** 20 colleagues buying UGX 3,000/day = UGX 60,000/day = UGX 1,320,000/month

**Scaling:** Once colleagues trust your food, start accepting WhatsApp orders from their family/friends. Your office is your marketing team.

**Regulatory note (Uganda):** Selling food in an office is not regulated if done informally. When you exceed ~UGX 500,000/month consistently, consider registering as a sole trader and obtaining a food handler's certificate from your district health office (low cost, annual).

## 3. Meal Prep and Lunch Delivery

**The model:** Prepare daily or weekly meals; deliver to busy professionals who want home-cooked food without cooking.

**Target customers:** Young professionals in apartments (no time to cook); expats wanting local food; fitness-conscious workers wanting portioned healthy meals; office workers bored of restaurant food.

**Pricing structures:**

| Model | Price | Revenue |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Daily lunch delivery (1 meal) | UGX 8,000–15,000/meal | 20 clients × UGX 10,000 = UGX 200,000/day |
| Weekly meal prep (5 lunches) | UGX 40,000–70,000/week | 10 clients = UGX 400,000–700,000/week |
| Meal plan subscription (monthly) | UGX 150,000–250,000/month | 15 clients = UGX 2,250,000–3,750,000/month |

**Operations (minimum viable for a side hustle):**
- Cook in the morning before work (4–6am) or evening before
- Partner with a boda boda driver for delivery (pay per delivery or monthly retainer)
- Use insulated food boxes (UGX 15,000–40,000 each)
- Accept orders by WhatsApp the evening before for next-day delivery

**Niche angle — healthy/diet meals:** The demand for calorie-counted, protein-focused, or diabetic-friendly meal plans is growing in Nairobi, Kigali, and Kampala's professional districts. A professionally packaged healthy meal plan at UGX 200,000/week commands a premium and attracts long-term subscribers.

## 4. Event and Weekend Catering

**The model:** Provide food for social events — office parties, birthday parties, church gatherings, homecoming celebrations — on weekends when you are not working.

**Entry package:** Offer one speciality (e.g., buffet for 50 people, rolex/street food for corporate events, pork ribs and chips for birthday parties) and do it exceptionally well before adding more options.

**Minimum viable catering package (50 people):**

| Item | Cost (UGX) | Charged (UGX) |
|------|-----------|--------------|
| Food ingredients | 250,000 | — |
| Cooking gas/fuel | 20,000 | — |
| Disposable plates and cutlery | 30,000 | — |
| Transport | 20,000 | — |
| **Total cost** | **320,000** | — |
| **Price charged (50 people)** | — | **650,000–900,000** |
| **Gross profit** | — | **330,000–580,000** |

**Scaling:** Hire a helper (UGX 30,000–50,000/event) to assist with prep and serving. As volume grows, invest in chafing dishes, serving equipment, and a branded gazebo.

## 5. Speciality and Niche Food Products

**The model:** Produce a differentiated food product; sell via WhatsApp, supermarket placement, or market stalls.

**High-potential speciality products for EA urban markets:**

| Product | Unique Angle | Target Customer |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| Granola / muesli (local grains) | "Made in Uganda, no additives" | Health-conscious professionals |
| Flavoured nut butters (peanut, sesame) | Local ingredients, zero preservatives | Expats, health segment |
| Herbal teas (lemongrass, ginger, moringa) | Medicinal positioning | Wellness market |
| Homemade hot sauce / chilli paste | Small-batch, authentic flavour | Food enthusiasts |
| Pickled vegetables / kimchi | Fermented health food trend | Urban millennials |
| Dairy-free milk (oat, soy, nut) | Growing vegan/lactose-intolerant market | Nairobi/Kampala health market |
| Artisanal chocolate | Uganda grows premium cocoa | Premium gifting, export-minded |
| Honey (repackaged/branded) | Local sourcing story, quality packaging | Retail, gifting, export |

**Getting into retail:** Once your product is consistent and packaged professionally, approach:
1. Specialty shops (Village Supermarket in Nairobi; Nakumatt/Carrefour equivalents; organic food shops)
2. Nakawa/Kalerwe market stalls (Kampala)
3. Farmers markets (Nairobi, Kigali)
4. Corporate gift hamper orders (Christmas season is extremely lucrative)

**UNBS registration (Uganda):** Required for any packaged food product sold commercially. Budget UGX 500,000–2,000,000 and 3–6 months for initial certification. Consider delay to get the business profitable first, then certify.

## Food Business Foundations

**The key metrics to track (Guillebeau, 2017):**
1. **Cost of goods** per unit — ingredient cost only, not your time yet
2. **Gross margin** — should be 50–70% for food products
3. **Orders per week** — track growth month-on-month
4. **Average order value** — are clients buying more over time?

**Food safety basics:**
- Store ingredients properly; FIFO rotation (first in, first out)
- Hand washing and clean preparation surfaces are non-negotiable
- Communicate allergens clearly (peanuts, gluten, dairy)
- Never sell food that is more than 24 hours old

## Risk and Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Food safety complaint | Maintain strict hygiene; use fresh ingredients only; keep batch records |
| Inconsistent quality | Standardise recipes with exact measurements; test every batch |
| Day job timing conflict (morning prep) | Evening prep is more manageable; consider twice-weekly delivery instead of daily |
| Seasonal ingredient price increases | Build 10% price buffer into your pricing; communicate price adjustments in advance |
| Running out of capacity (orders exceed your time) | Hire a part-time helper; increase minimum order size; raise prices strategically |
| Employer policy conflict | Food businesses are generally not a conflict of interest; confirm in your employment contract |
