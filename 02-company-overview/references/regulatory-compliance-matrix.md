# Uganda Regulatory Compliance Matrix

**Source:** Self-Employment and Small Business Guide for Uganda (NRC / CR. Amanya Advocates / Thomson Reuters Foundation, May 2025); Trade Licensing Act Cap 101; Uganda Companies Act 2012.

---

## 1. Introduction

This matrix is a sector-specific compliance lookup tool for Uganda business plans. It answers the question: *"If my business is in [sector], what licences and registrations do I need, and from which authority?"*

**How to use this matrix:**

1. Complete universal compliance (Section 2) first — these apply to every business regardless of sector.
2. Identify your sector in Section 3 and collect the listed sector-specific requirements.
3. Use Section 4 to determine your trading licence grade, then look up your fee in Section 5.
4. Use Section 6 for authority contact details and web portals.

All fees are in Uganda Shillings (UGX). Fees are annual unless otherwise stated. Confirm current fees with the issuing authority, as schedules are periodically revised.

## 2. Universal Compliance Requirements

The following apply to **all** Uganda businesses, regardless of sector or size.

### 2.1 National Identification (NIN) — prerequisite for everything

Individual directors and proprietors must hold a valid **National Identification Number (NIN)** issued by the **National Identification and Registration Authority (NIRA)** before any business registration or TIN application can proceed. As of 2025 amendments, TIN is assigned on the basis of NIN (for individuals) or BRN (for companies).

### 2.2 Business Registration — URSB

Register the legal structure of the business with the **Uganda Registration Services Bureau (URSB)** at [https://obrs.ursb.go.ug/](https://obrs.ursb.go.ug/).

| Step | Action | Fee (UGX) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Name reservation | 35,000 |
| 2a | Business name registration (sole trader) | 55,000 |
| 2b | Partnership (Limited Liability) | 55,000 |
| 2c | Company formation — share capital UGX 1M–5M | 105,000 |
| 2d | Company formation — share capital > UGX 5M | 1.5% of share capital |
| 2e | Stamp duty (companies, share capital > UGX 5M) | 0.5% of share capital |
| 3 | Annual returns (private company with share capital) | 55,000 / year |
| — | Registration of mortgage or charge | 55,000 |

The output is a **Business Registration Number (BRN)** — required before applying for a TIN as a company.

### 2.3 Tax Identification — URA

Register with the **Uganda Revenue Authority (URA)** at [https://ura.go.ug](https://ura.go.ug) to obtain a **Tax Identification Number (TIN)**.

- Required for all trading, invoicing, and bank account opening.
- **VAT registration** is mandatory once annual turnover exceeds UGX 150,000,000 (UGX 150M).
- File income tax returns annually; VAT returns monthly or quarterly depending on classification.
- Employers must register as a withholding tax agent and file PAYE for all employees monthly.

### 2.4 Trading Licence — KCCA or District Council

All businesses operating from a fixed or mobile location require an annual **trading licence** issued by:

- **KCCA** — for businesses within Kampala City boundaries.
- **District/municipal council** — for businesses outside Kampala.
- **Ministry of Trade, Industry and Cooperatives** portal: [https://www.businesslicences.go.ug/](https://www.businesslicences.go.ug/)

See Section 4 for grade determination and Section 5 for fee schedule.

### 2.5 NSSF Registration — National Social Security Fund

Mandatory for employers with **five or more employees** (in practice, advisable for any employee count to avoid future penalty).

- Register at [https://www.nssfug.org/](https://www.nssfug.org/)
- **Employer contribution:** 10% of each employee's gross monthly wage.
- **Employee deduction:** 5% of gross monthly wage (deducted at source).
- **Total contribution:** 15% per employee per month.
- **Due date:** 15th of the following month.
- **Penalty for non-compliance:** fine of up to UGX 10,000,000 (500 currency points × UGX 20,000 each) and/or up to 6 months' imprisonment.

### 2.6 Health and Safety — Local Authority

All business premises must pass a health and sanitation inspection by the local authority (KCCA Public Health Department or district health office) before a trading licence is issued or renewed. Food-handling businesses face more rigorous inspection.

## 4. Trading Licence Grade Determination Guide

Trading licences are graded **I to IV**, where Grade I applies to the largest operations and Grade IV to the smallest. Local authority officials assess grade at application, but the following indicators are typical:

| Grade | Typical Business Profile |
|---|---|
| **Grade I** | Large premises; high turnover; multiple employees; prime commercial location (e.g., Kampala CBD supermarkets, large garages, 3-star+ hotels) |
| **Grade II** | Medium premises; moderate turnover; established neighbourhood business (e.g., town-centre restaurant, pharmacy in a trading centre) |
| **Grade III** | Small premises; lower turnover; typically outside main CBD (e.g., local kiosk with small stock, rural eating house) |
| **Grade IV** | Micro-enterprise; minimal premises or mobile (e.g., roadside hawker, one-person mobile money agent, village kiosk) |

**Practical guidance:**

- When in doubt, declare your estimated annual turnover and number of employees to the licensing officer — these are the primary grade determinants.
- Kampala businesses are graded by **KCCA revenue officers** who may inspect premises.
- District businesses are graded by **district commercial officers**.
- Grade disputes can be appealed to the relevant council.
