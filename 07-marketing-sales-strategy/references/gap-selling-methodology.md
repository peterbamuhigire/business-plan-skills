# Gap Selling Methodology

> Reference: *Gap Selling: Getting the Customer to Yes* by Keenan (A Sales Guy Inc., 2019)

> Adapted to Ugandan business plan context. The methodology is universal.

---

## 1. The Core Principle

Gap Selling is problem-centric, not product-centric. The sale exists in the gap between the customer's **current state** (where they are now, with all its problems) and their **desired future state** (where they want to be). The bigger the gap, the more urgency to buy and the more the customer will pay.

```
CURRENT STATE ──────── THE GAP ──────── FUTURE STATE
(problems, costs,       (your sale        (desired outcomes,
 inefficiencies,         lives here)       improvements,
 missed opportunities)                     new capabilities)
```

**Business plan implication:** Every product/service description should articulate the gap it closes. Investors fund solutions to big gaps, not clever products.

---

## 2. Nine Truth Bombs of Selling

These principles underpin every sales interaction:

| # | Truth | Implication for Business Plans |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **No problem, no sale** | Always lead with the customer's problem, not your product |
| 2 | **In every sale there is a gap** | Quantify the gap between current and future state |
| 3 | **All sales are about change** | Your product is a change agent — show the change clearly |
| 4 | **Customers don't like change** | Address switching costs, risk, and implementation burden |
| 5 | **Sales are emotional** | Connect to emotional impact of problems (frustration, fear, ambition) |
| 6 | **Customers accept change when worth the cost** | Demonstrate ROI that exceeds total cost of change |
| 7 | **Asking "Why?" gets customers to "Yes"** | Discovery process must uncover root causes, not just symptoms |
| 8 | **Sales happen when future state is better** | Paint a vivid, credible picture of life after adoption |
| 9 | **No one cares about you** | Customers care about their problems, not your features |

## 3. Current State Analysis Framework

Before any sales conversation, map the customer's current state across five dimensions:

### The Current State Map

| Dimension | What to Discover | Example (Ugandan Agribusiness) |
|---|---|---|
| **Facts and data** | Measurable situation metrics | 30% post-harvest loss, 200 farmers, UGX 15M monthly revenue |
| **Problems** | What is going wrong | Inconsistent grading, no cold chain, middleman dependency |
| **Impact of problems** | Business consequences | UGX 50M annual revenue lost, inability to access export markets |
| **Root cause** | Why the problem exists | No quality certification, manual processes, poor market information |
| **Emotion** | How it makes them feel | Frustrated, anxious about business viability, embarrassed by waste |

### Problem Identification Checklist

For each customer segment in the business plan:

- [ ] Literal problem identified (what is observably wrong)
- [ ] Business impact quantified (revenue lost, time wasted, cost incurred)
- [ ] Root cause understood (why it happens, not just what happens)
- [ ] Emotional impact articulated (how the problem makes them feel)
- [ ] Current workaround documented (what they do today instead)
- [ ] Cost of current workaround calculated

## 4. Future State Framework

The future state is what the customer's world looks like after adopting your solution:

### Future State Map

| Dimension | What to Define | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **New facts and data** | Measurable improvements | Post-harvest loss reduced to 5%, 500 farmers connected, UGX 45M monthly revenue |
| **Problems solved** | Which current problems disappear | Automated grading, cold chain access, direct buyer relationships |
| **Positive business impact** | Gains realised | UGX 150M additional annual revenue, export market access |
| **New capabilities** | What they can now do | Real-time price discovery, quality certification, bulk aggregation |
| **Emotional outcome** | How they will feel | Confident, in control, proud of quality |

### The Gap Equation

```
Size of Gap = (Value of Future State) - (Cost of Current State + Cost of Change)

If Gap > 0 → Sale is possible
If Gap is large → Sale is urgent and price-insensitive
If Gap ≤ 0 → No sale — problem is not big enough or change cost is too high
```

**Business plan application:** Quantify the gap for each customer segment. Show the gap in monetary terms where possible.

## 5. Discovery Process

Gap Selling discovery is structured to uncover the full current state before presenting any solution.

### Discovery Question Framework

Move through these layers in order:

| Layer | Purpose | Question Types |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Situation** | Establish facts | "How many [units/customers/staff] do you currently have?" |
| **2. Problem** | Surface issues | "What challenges do you face with [process/area]?" |
| **3. Impact** | Quantify consequences | "What does that cost you in terms of [revenue/time/reputation]?" |
| **4. Root cause** | Find the "why" | "Why do you think that happens?" / "What have you tried?" |
| **5. Emotion** | Connect personally | "How does that affect you/your team/your goals?" |

### Discovery Rules

1. **Never pitch before discovery is complete** — selling without understanding the gap is guessing
2. **Ask "why" at least 3 times** — first answers are symptoms, not root causes
3. **Quantify everything** — "a lot of waste" becomes "UGX 50M annual loss"
4. **Let silence work** — pause after questions; customers fill silence with truth
5. **Take notes visibly** — shows respect and captures details for the proposal
6. **Confirm understanding** — repeat back what you heard; let them correct you

### Discovery Output Template

After discovery, document:

```
Customer: [Name/Company]
Current State:
  Facts: [measurable situation data]
  Problems: [specific issues identified]
  Impact: [quantified business consequences]
  Root Cause: [underlying reasons]
  Emotion: [how it affects them personally]

Future State:
  Desired Outcomes: [what success looks like]
  New Capabilities: [what they want to be able to do]
  Success Metrics: [how they will measure improvement]

The Gap:
  Size: [quantified difference between current and future]
  Urgency: [timeline pressure, competitive pressure, regulatory pressure]
  Decision Criteria: [what matters most to them]
  Decision Process: [who decides, how, when]
```

## 6. Gap Selling vs. Traditional Selling

| Aspect | Traditional Selling | Gap Selling |
|---|---|---|
| **Starts with** | Product features | Customer's problems |
| **Discovery focus** | Budget, authority, need, timeline (BANT) | Current state, impact, root cause, emotion |
| **Presentation** | Feature/benefit slides | Gap analysis showing current → future state |
| **Objection handling** | Overcome resistance | Revisit discovery — objections mean gap is unclear |
| **Closing** | Techniques and pressure | Natural conclusion when gap is clear and compelling |
| **Competition** | Other vendors | The status quo (doing nothing / current workaround) |
| **Price sensitivity** | High — customers compare prices | Low — customers compare gap value to price |

**Key insight:** Your real competition is almost never another vendor. It is the **status quo** — what the customer already does. Most deals are lost to "no decision," not to competitors.

---

## 7. Objection Reframing

In Gap Selling, objections signal incomplete discovery, not customer resistance:

| Objection | What It Really Means | Response |
|---|---|---|
| "Too expensive" | Gap is not big enough to justify the price | Return to impact — quantify the cost of not changing |
| "We need to think about it" | Future state is not compelling enough | Return to future state — clarify desired outcomes |
| "We're happy with current solution" | Current state problems are not acknowledged | Return to problem identification — dig deeper |
| "Not the right time" | Urgency is missing | Return to impact — what happens if they wait 6 months? |
| "Send me information" | Trust or relevance not established | Ask what specific information would be useful and why |

## 8. Gap Prospecting

Outbound prospecting uses the gap framework to write compelling outreach:

### Prospecting Message Structure

```
1. Identify a likely problem (based on your ICP research)
2. Reference the impact of that problem (quantified if possible)
3. Hint at a future state (what could be different)
4. Ask for a conversation (not a pitch — a discovery call)
```

### Prospecting Message Template

```
Subject: [Specific problem statement]

Hi [Name],

I noticed that [observation about their situation — shows you did research].

Many [role/industry] leaders in [region] tell us that [specific problem]
costs them [quantified impact] every [time period].

We've helped [similar company/segment] move from [current state summary]
to [future state summary] in [timeframe].

Would it make sense to spend 15 minutes exploring whether
a similar gap exists in your operation?

[Your name]
```

**Uganda adaptation:** Reference local market conditions, regulatory changes, or industry shifts that create urgency. For example: "With UNBS tightening quality standards in Q2..." or "As mobile money adoption exceeds 70% in your district..."

## 9. Applying Gap Selling in Business Plans

### Sales Strategy Section

When writing the sales process for a business plan:

1. **Define the gap for each customer segment** — current state, future state, quantified difference
2. **Map discovery questions** — what salespeople will ask to uncover the gap
3. **Design sales materials around the gap** — not features, but before/after transformation
4. **Set pricing relative to gap size** — price should be a fraction of the gap value
5. **Train salespeople on discovery first** — not product knowledge first
6. **Track gap metrics** — average gap size per deal, correlation between gap size and close rate

### Sales Forecast Validation

Use gap analysis to validate revenue projections:

| Validation Check | Question | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem severity** | Is the problem painful enough to drive action? | "Nice to have" problems with small gaps |
| **Gap size vs. price** | Is the gap at least 3-5x the price? | Price exceeds 50% of gap value |
| **Status quo strength** | How entrenched is the current workaround? | Prospect has invested heavily in current solution |
| **Decision urgency** | Is there a forcing function (deadline, regulation, growth)? | No timeline pressure |
| **Buyer authority** | Can the person you're selling to actually decide? | Talking to influencers, not decision-makers |
