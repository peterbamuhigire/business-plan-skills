## 1. Guerrilla Marketing Principles

### Core Philosophy

Guerrilla marketing invests time, energy, and imagination instead of money. It is built for small businesses that cannot outspend competitors but can out-think and out-hustle them.

### Key Differences from Traditional Marketing

| Traditional Marketing | Guerrilla Marketing |
|---|---|
| Requires large budget | Requires imagination and effort |
| Measures by sales | Measures by profit |
| Based on experience and guesswork | Based on psychology and testing |
| Grows linearly | Grows geometrically (via referrals and partnerships) |
| Targets groups | Targets individuals and small segments |
| Counts new customers | Counts relationships and referrals |
| Uses single weapons | Uses combinations of weapons |

### The Guerrilla Mindset

1. **Commitment** — Stick with a strategy long enough for it to work (minimum 90 days)
2. **Investment** — Treat marketing as an investment, not an expense
3. **Consistency** — Consistent mediocre marketing beats sporadic brilliant marketing
4. **Confidence** — Customers buy from businesses that project certainty
5. **Patience** — Results compound over time; most businesses quit too early
6. **Assortment** — Use a combination of tactics, never rely on one channel
7. **Consent** — Build permission-based relationships, not interruption campaigns

## 2. The 7-Step Guerrilla Marketing Plan

Every business plan should include a one-page marketing plan covering:

```
1. PURPOSE: What specific action should marketing generate?
   Example: "Drive 50 qualified leads per month to our sales team"

2. COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE: Why should customers choose us?
   Example: "Only provider offering same-day installation with a 5-year guarantee"

3. TARGET AUDIENCE: Who specifically are we targeting?
   Example: "Property managers of 20-100 unit residential complexes in Nairobi"

4. MARKETING WEAPONS: Which specific tactics will we use?
   Example: "Content marketing, referral programme, strategic partnerships, email"

5. NICHE: What narrow focus defines our expertise?
   Example: "Affordable smart home systems for mid-market residential properties"

6. BUDGET: What percentage of projected revenue?
   Example: "12% of projected gross revenue, allocated as: content 40%, partnerships 25%, referral 20%, email 15%"

7. IDENTITY: What personality does our brand project?
   Example: "Reliable, innovative, locally expert, professionally friendly"
```

## 3. Guerrilla Marketing Weapons Arsenal

### Free or Nearly Free Weapons

| Weapon | Description | Implementation |
|---|---|---|
| **Business cards** | Every interaction is a marketing opportunity | Include compelling offer, not just contact details |
| **Testimonials** | Social proof from satisfied customers | Collect systematically after every successful delivery |
| **Referral system** | Structured incentive for customer referrals | Offer value to both referrer and referred |
| **Online reviews** | Google, industry-specific review platforms | Ask satisfied customers at peak satisfaction moments |
| **Public speaking** | Position as expert in target industry | Offer free talks at industry events, chambers of commerce |

## 4. Education-Based Marketing

### The Principle

Instead of selling, teach. Customers who are educated about their problem and the available solutions buy more, pay more, and stay longer.

### Implementation Framework

```
Step 1: Identify the knowledge gap
  What does the customer not know that, if they knew it, would make them buy?

Step 2: Create educational content
  Blog posts, guides, videos, webinars, workshops that fill the gap

Step 3: Distribute through relevant channels
  Where does the target customer go to learn?

Step 4: Include a soft call to action
  "If you'd like help implementing this, here's how we can help"

Step 5: Nurture with more education
  Follow-up content that deepens understanding and builds trust
```

### Education-Based Content Types

| Content | Purpose | Conversion Stage |
|---|---|---|
| "How to" guides | Solve immediate problems | Awareness |
| Industry reports | Establish authority | Consideration |
| Comparison guides | Help decision-making | Evaluation |
| Case studies | Prove results | Decision |
| Calculator tools | Quantify the problem | Evaluation/Decision |
| FAQ / Objection content | Remove purchase barriers | Decision |

## 5. Guarantee Marketing

### The Power of Guarantees

A strong guarantee transfers risk from the buyer to the seller, dramatically reducing purchase friction.

### Guarantee Types

| Type | Example | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Money-back** | "Full refund within 30 days" | Products, subscriptions |
| **Performance** | "We'll increase your output by 20% or work free until we do" | Services, consulting |
| **Time** | "Delivered in 48 hours or it's free" | Logistics, services |
| **Satisfaction** | "If you're not satisfied, we'll redo it at no cost" | Custom services |
| **Result** | "If you don't see results in 90 days, full refund" | Training, coaching |

### Guarantee Design Rules

1. Make it specific and measurable (not vague "satisfaction guaranteed")
2. Make the timeframe generous (reduces perceived risk more)
3. Make the claim-process simple (complex processes destroy trust)
4. Track claim rates — if < 5%, the guarantee is working and profitable
5. Feature the guarantee prominently in all marketing materials

## 6. Fusion Marketing Partnerships

### How Fusion Marketing Works

Two or more non-competing businesses share marketing resources to reach each other's customers at a fraction of the individual cost.

### Partnership Selection Criteria

```
Ideal fusion partner:
- Serves the same target customer
- Does NOT compete with your offering
- Has a similar quality/price positioning
- Has an audience you want to reach
- Is willing to reciprocate equally
```

### Fusion Marketing Tactics

| Tactic | Description | Cost Sharing |
|---|---|---|
| **Joint content** | Co-authored blog, webinar, or guide | Each contributes expertise |
| **Cross-promotion** | Feature each other in email, social media | Mutual audience access |
| **Bundled offers** | Combined package deal | Shared revenue |
| **Shared events** | Co-hosted workshop, meetup, or conference | Split costs 50/50 |
| **Referral exchange** | Structured mutual referral system | Commission or reciprocal |
| **Shared space** | Pop-up within partner's location | Reduced rent |

### Measuring Fusion Marketing Success

```
Track per partnership:
- Leads generated from partner's audience
- Conversion rate of referred leads
- Revenue attributed to partnership
- Cost saving vs. acquiring these customers independently
- Partner satisfaction and renewal intent
```

## 7. Follow-Up and Retention Systems

### The Follow-Up Framework

Most businesses fail at follow-up — this is the biggest guerrilla opportunity:

```
After first purchase:
  Day 0: Thank-you message (email or handwritten)
  Day 3: "How's it going?" check-in
  Day 14: Useful tip or resource related to purchase
  Day 30: Request for review/testimonial
  Day 60: Cross-sell or upsell relevant offer
  Day 90: Referral request with incentive

Ongoing:
  Monthly: Newsletter with valuable content
  Quarterly: Exclusive offer for existing customers
  Annually: Anniversary acknowledgement
```

### Retention Economics

```
Cost of retaining a customer = 1/5 to 1/7 the cost of acquiring a new one

A 5% increase in retention = 25-95% increase in profit (Farris)

Revenue from existing customers should eventually exceed revenue from new customers
```

### Reactivation Campaigns

For lapsed customers:

```
Identify: Customers who haven't purchased in > [2x normal purchase cycle]
Segment: By last purchase date, lifetime value, reason for leaving
Outreach: Personal email/call, special "we miss you" offer
Track: Reactivation rate, reactivated customer CLV
```

## 8. The Irresistible Offer Framework (Joyner)

### Core Concept

The Irresistible Offer is an identity-building offer central to a product, service, or company where the believable return on investment is communicated so clearly that it is immediately apparent you would have to be a fool to pass it up. It is the business's permanent identity, not a temporary promotion.

### The 3-Second Rule

You have exactly 3 seconds to capture a prospect's mind. The Irresistible Offer cuts through noise by answering the buyer's four unspoken questions simultaneously.

### The Big Four Questions (Buyer's Unspoken Inner Dialogue)

Every buyer unconsciously asks:

| # | Question | What It Addresses |
|---|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | "What are you trying to sell me?" | Nature of the offer |
| 2 | "How much?" | Cost/investment |
| 3 | "Why should I believe you?" | Credibility and trust |
| 4 | "What's in it for me?" | Benefits and personal return |

Questions 1-3 address logic. Question 4 addresses emotion. The Irresistible Offer addresses all four simultaneously.

### HTB Framework (Three Elements)

1. **High ROI Offer** — Customer must perceive they are getting something greater in value than what they invest. Do not simply lower price — find ways to add value without detracting from margins.

2. **Touchstone** — The core statement that communicates the offer. Must be clear, simple, brief, and immediate.
   - Great touchstones: "Pizza hot and fresh in 30 minutes or it's free" (Domino's), "When it absolutely, positively has to be there overnight" (FedEx), "10 CDs for 1 Cent" (Columbia House)

3. **Believability** — Without believability, even the most attractive offer fails.
   - **Proof:** Social proof (testimonials), technical proof (test results), factual proof (research)
   - **Credibility:** Endorsements, high-profile customers, qualifications, awards, logical explanation of how you can make such a great offer

### The Great Formula (3-Step Business Success)

```
1. Create The Irresistible Offer
2. Present it to a Thirsty Crowd (people with genuine hunger for your product)
3. Sell Them a Second Glass (upsell, cross-sell, follow-up, continuity)
```

Step 3 is where most profit is made. Cost of selling to existing customers is far less than acquiring new ones.

### Offer Intensifiers

| Intensifier | Description | Application |
|---|---|---|
| **Urgency** | Expiring offers eliminate procrastination | Time-limited, but always honour promises |
| **Added value** | Stack unexpected value onto the offer | Surprise bonuses after the sale |
| **Risk reversal** | Remove buyer's fear of risk | Money-back guarantees, pay-for-results, free trials |
| **Scarcity** | Limited editions, numbered items | Fear of loss stimulates passive prospects |
| **Ease** | Make ordering dead simple | Every added step reduces response |
| **Uniqueness** | Be perceived as the only option | Perception matters more than reality |
| **Recommendations** | Word of mouth from trusted sources | Incentivise referrals — the most powerful intensifier |

### Offer Continuum (Evaluation Checklist)

Rate each 1-10 to evaluate any offer:

| Question | Low Score Action | High Score Action |
|---|---|---|
| How obvious is the need? | Craft touchstone around problems solved | State what the product IS immediately |
| How genuine is the need? | Create desire through education | Fulfil the existing need directly |
| How common is a solution? | Differentiate with touchstone | Leverage perceived uniqueness |
| Can you demonstrate ROI? | Intensify selling and proof | Use facts, research, graphs |
| How emotional is the offer? | Tie to emotional need | Balance emotion with substance |
| How timely is the offer? | Create urgency artificially | Capitalise on natural urgency |
| How do you stack up vs. competition? | Outmarket competitors | Market superiority aggressively |
| How do you compare on price? | Use value framing and contrast | Highlight the deal clearly |

### Key Rules

- The Irresistible Offer IS your identity, not a temporary campaign
- Selling sizzle without steak is a scam — always deliver genuine value
- Negative information spreads faster than positive — delight, do not just satisfy
- Higher price can generate more sales than lower price (perceived value)
- Prices ending in 7 or 9 outperform other endings ($97 outsold $99 by 2x)
