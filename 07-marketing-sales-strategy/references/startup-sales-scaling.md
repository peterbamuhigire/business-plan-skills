# Startup Sales Playbook and Scaling

> References: *Founding Sales: The Startup Handbook* by Kazanjy (2020); *The Zero to 100 Million Sales Blueprint* by Goliger (2023)

> Adapted to Ugandan business plan context. Frameworks are universal.

---

## 1. Founder-Led Selling Stages

Every startup passes through predictable sales stages. Trying to skip stages causes failure (Kazanjy):

| Stage | Team | Focus | Exit Criteria |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Founder only** | Founder selling alone | Prove the product can be sold | 20-30 customers acquired and getting value |
| **2. Founder + SDR + 2 AEs** | First hires alongside founder | Prove someone other than the founder can sell | AEs closing at founder's rate |
| **3. Sales manager + team** | Dedicated sales leader | Prove sales can be managed, not just done | Repeatable hiring, onboarding, and quota attainment |
| **4. VP Sales + scaled team** | Executive leadership | Scale to multiple teams, segments, geographies | Predictable revenue engine |

### Anti-Patterns at Each Stage

| Stage | Common Mistake | Why It Fails |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Hiring a "sales guy" to do founder selling | No one understands the product and market like the founder |
| 2 | Hiring too many reps too fast | Founder cannot coach 5+ reps effectively |
| 3 | Promoting top rep to manager | Selling and managing are different skills |
| 4 | Hiring VP Sales before Stage 3 is proven | VP Sales needs a repeatable process to scale — cannot create one from scratch |

**Business plan implication:** The staffing plan should show which stage the business is in and the planned progression. Investors reject plans that jump from "founder" to "VP Sales + 10 reps" without the middle stages.

## 2. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### ICP Definition Framework (Goliger)

| Dimension | What to Define | Example (Ugandan SaaS) |
|---|---|---|
| **Industry/Vertical** | Which sectors | Hospitality, retail, healthcare |
| **Company size** | Revenue or employee count | UGX 100M-1B annual revenue, 10-100 employees |
| **Geography** | Where they operate | Kampala metro, secondary cities (Gulu, Mbarara, Jinja) |
| **Pain points** | Specific problems they have | Manual inventory, no real-time reporting, cash leakage |
| **Technology readiness** | Current tech adoption | Using smartphones, basic POS, no integrated system |
| **Decision maker** | Who buys | Owner-operator or general manager |
| **Budget range** | What they can spend | UGX 200,000-500,000/month for SaaS subscription |

### Buyer Persona Psychographics (Goliger)

Beyond demographics, understand what drives each buyer:

| Driver | Description | Sales Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Certainty/Security** | Needs assurance, low risk tolerance | Emphasise guarantees, case studies, proven results |
| **Variety/Growth** | Attracted to new approaches | Lead with innovation, market trends, competitive edge |
| **Significance** | Wants to feel important, recognised | Position them as visionary, show how they'll be seen |
| **Connection** | Values relationships, trust | Build rapport first, introduce to community of users |
| **Contribution** | Wants to make a difference | Show social impact, job creation, community benefit |

## 3. Sales Models

### One-to-One vs. One-to-Many (Goliger)

| Model | Description | Best For | CAC | Scalability |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Direct (field sales)** | In-person meetings, demos | High-value B2B (>UGX 5M/deal) | High | Low |
| **Inside sales** | Phone/video selling | Mid-value B2B (UGX 500K-5M/deal) | Medium | Medium |
| **Channel/partner** | Sell through resellers | Geographic reach, commodity products | Low (but margin shared) | High |
| **Self-serve / PLG** | Customer buys online without rep | Low-value, high-volume SaaS | Very low | Very high |
| **Hybrid** | Combination of above | Products with varied deal sizes | Varies | High |

### Sales Model Selection Criteria

| Factor | Favours Direct/Inside Sales | Favours Self-Serve/Channel |
|---|---|---|
| **Deal size** | Large (>UGX 5M) | Small (<UGX 500K) |
| **Complexity** | High — needs explanation | Low — self-explanatory |
| **Customer sophistication** | Variable — needs guidance | High — can evaluate independently |
| **Sales cycle** | Long (>30 days) | Short (<7 days) |
| **Customisation** | Required | Standard product |

## 4. Sales Narrative and Materials

### Sales Narrative Structure (Kazanjy)

Build a compelling story that follows this arc:

```
1. THE PROBLEM (Current State)
   → Industry-level problem statement
   → Specific pain points your prospects face
   → Quantified cost/impact of the problem

2. EXISTING SOLUTIONS AND THEIR FAILURES
   → What prospects currently do (status quo)
   → Why current approaches fall short
   → Cost of continuing with broken solutions

3. YOUR SOLUTION (Future State)
   → How your product works (keep it simple)
   → "How it works" diagram for complex products
   → Key differentiators vs. existing approaches

4. PROOF
   → Quantitative: metrics, case study results, ROI data
   → Qualitative: testimonials, customer quotes, logos
   → "Why now?" — urgency drivers (market shift, regulation, competition)

5. THE ASK
   → Clear next step (demo, pilot, trial)
   → Make it easy and low-risk to say yes
```

### Essential Sales Materials

| Material | Purpose | When Created | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| **One-pager / Sell sheet** | Leave-behind, email attachment | Before first sales call | Must-have |
| **Pitch deck (8-12 slides)** | Structured presentation | Before first demo | Must-have |
| **Demo script** | Consistent, value-focused product walkthrough | After 5-10 demos refined | Must-have |
| **Case study** | Social proof with quantified results | After first 3-5 successful customers | High |
| **ROI calculator** | Quantify the gap for each prospect | After pricing is validated | High |
| **Objection handling guide** | Consistent responses to common pushback | After 20+ sales conversations | Medium |
| **Proposal template** | Standardised, professional proposal | After sales process is repeatable | Medium |

## 5. Prospecting for Startups

### Outbound Prospecting Sequence (Goliger)

A multi-touch sequence combining channels:

| Day | Action | Channel | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Personalised intro referencing their business | Email | Open and read |
| 3 | Follow-up with value content (article, insight) | Email | Build credibility |
| 5 | Connection request with personal note | LinkedIn/Social | Establish relationship |
| 7 | Phone call referencing emails sent | Phone | Live conversation |
| 10 | Share relevant case study or data point | Email | Demonstrate proof |
| 14 | Engage with their social media content | Social | Stay visible |
| 21 | Final outreach with specific value proposition | Email + Phone | Get decision |

### Uganda-Specific Prospecting Channels

| Channel | Effectiveness | Best For | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **WhatsApp Business** | Very high | SMEs, informal sector | Free-Low |
| **Industry association events** | High | B2B, professional services | UGX 200K-1M per event |
| **Referral from existing customers** | Very high | All segments | Free (incentive cost only) |
| **Radio mentions** | Medium-High | Mass market, rural | UGX 500K-2M per spot |
| **Agent/reseller network** | High | Geographic reach, rural | Commission-based |
| **Church/community group presentations** | Medium | Consumer, community-based | Free |
| **Market visits (door-to-door)** | High | Retail, traders | Transport + time cost |

### Inbound Lead Response Protocol (Kazanjy)

| Response Time | Impact on Conversion |
|---|---|
| < 5 minutes | Optimal — 391% higher conversion than 1-hour response |
| < 1 hour | Good — still captures attention |
| Same day | Acceptable — but losing edge |
| Next day | Poor — prospect has likely contacted competitors |
| > 24 hours | Critical — most inbound leads are lost |

**Rule:** Respond to every inbound lead within 5 minutes during business hours. Set up mobile notifications for all lead capture forms.

## 6. Demo and Presentation Best Practices

### Demo Structure (Kazanjy)

```
1. RECAP DISCOVERY (2 min)
   → "Based on our conversation, you're dealing with [problem]
      which costs you [impact]. Is that still accurate?"

2. SOLUTION OVERVIEW (3 min)
   → High-level: "Here's how we solve that"
   → Map each problem to a specific capability

3. LIVE DEMONSTRATION (15-20 min)
   → Show the workflow that solves their #1 problem first
   → Use their data/scenario if possible
   → Pause after each key feature: "How would this change things for you?"

4. PROOF POINTS (3 min)
   → "Company X had the same problem. After implementing, they saw [result]"

5. PRICING AND NEXT STEPS (5 min)
   → Present pricing in context of the gap value
   → Propose specific next step with date
```

### Demo Anti-Patterns

- **Feature dumping** — showing every feature instead of solving their problem
- **No discovery recap** — jumping into demo without confirming understanding
- **Talking more than listening** — demo should be 60% showing, 40% discussing
- **No next step** — ending with "Let me know what you think" instead of a specific action

## 7. Sales Metrics for Business Plans

### Core Sales KPIs (Goliger)

| Metric | Formula | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| **Lead-to-Customer Rate** | Customers / Total Leads x 100 | 2-5% (cold), 15-25% (warm) |
| **Sales Cycle Length** | Average days from first contact to close | Varies by deal size |
| **Average Deal Value** | Total revenue / Number of deals | Track trend over time |
| **Customer Acquisition Cost** | Total sales + marketing spend / New customers | Must be < 1/3 of CLV |
| **Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)** | Sum of all monthly subscriptions | Track month-over-month growth |
| **Pipeline Coverage** | Total pipeline value / Quota | 3-4x coverage minimum |
| **Win Rate** | Deals won / Total deals in pipeline | 20-30% (new business) |
| **Average Lead Response Time** | Time from inbound inquiry to first response | Target < 5 minutes |
| **Customer Retention Rate** | (Customers at end - New) / Customers at start x 100 | Target > 85% annually |
| **Revenue per Sales Rep** | Total revenue / Number of sales reps | Track for scaling decisions |
| **Quota Attainment** | Actual revenue / Quota x 100 | Target 70-80% of reps at quota |
| **Sales Velocity** | (Opportunities x Win Rate x Deal Size) / Sales Cycle | Higher = healthier pipeline |

### Sales Velocity Formula

```
Sales Velocity = (Number of Opportunities x Win Rate x Average Deal Size)
                 ÷ Average Sales Cycle Length (days)

Example:
  50 opportunities x 25% win rate x UGX 5M avg deal ÷ 30 days
  = UGX 2.08M per day in expected revenue
```

## 8. Building the First Sales Team

### Hiring Sequence (Kazanjy)

| Hire # | Role | When to Hire | What They Do |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **SDR (Sales Dev Rep)** | After founder has closed 20-30 deals | Prospecting, qualifying, booking meetings for founder |
| 2-3 | **Account Executives (AEs)** | After SDR is generating consistent meetings | Running demos, closing deals |
| 4 | **Customer Success** | After 30+ active customers | Onboarding, retention, expansion |
| 5 | **Sales Manager** | After 3+ AEs are consistently hitting quota | Coaching, pipeline management, hiring |

### Sales Compensation Structure (Goliger)

| Role | Base : Variable Split | OTE (Uganda Context) |
|---|---|---|
| **SDR** | 70:30 | UGX 1.5-3M/month |
| **Account Executive** | 60:40 | UGX 2.5-5M/month |
| **Sales Manager** | 70:30 | UGX 4-8M/month |
| **VP Sales** | 60:40 | UGX 8-15M/month |

**OTE** = On-Target Earnings (base + commission when hitting 100% quota)

### Commission Structures

| Structure | How It Works | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Straight commission** | % of revenue only, no base | Experienced reps, high deal values |
| **Base + commission** | Fixed salary + % of revenue | Standard for most roles |
| **Tiered commission** | Higher % above quota threshold | Motivating overperformance |
| **Draw against commission** | Advance on future commissions | New reps ramping up |
| **Team bonus** | Shared bonus for team targets | Collaborative selling environments |

## 9. Sales Scaling Framework

### The 100M Blueprint Scaling Stages (Goliger)

| Revenue Stage | Focus | Key Activities |
|---|---|---|
| **UGX 0-50M** | Product-market fit | Founder selling, ICP validation, first 20 customers |
| **UGX 50-200M** | Repeatable sales process | Document process, hire first reps, prove they can sell |
| **UGX 200M-1B** | Scalable team | Sales manager, training programme, CRM discipline |
| **UGX 1-5B** | Multi-channel | Inside sales + field sales + channel partners |
| **UGX 5B+** | Enterprise + expansion | Enterprise sales team, international expansion, acquisitions |

### Sales Process Documentation (for scaling)

Before hiring beyond the founder, document:

```
1. ICP Definition — exactly who to target and who to disqualify
2. Prospecting Playbook — channels, sequences, messaging templates
3. Discovery Script — questions to ask, what to listen for
4. Demo Flow — step-by-step walkthrough, key talking points
5. Objection Responses — top 10 objections with proven answers
6. Proposal Template — standardised format with pricing options
7. Closing Process — decision criteria checklist, next steps framework
8. Handoff to Onboarding — what customer success needs to know
```

### Sales Technology Stack

| Category | Purpose | Options (Budget-Friendly) |
|---|---|---|
| **CRM** | Pipeline and contact management | HubSpot Free, Zoho, Google Sheets (early stage) |
| **Email outreach** | Automated sequences | Mailchimp, Sendinblue, Gmail + templates |
| **Meeting scheduling** | Reduce back-and-forth | Calendly (free tier), Google Calendar |
| **Video calls** | Remote demos | Google Meet, Zoom (free tier) |
| **Document signing** | Close deals faster | DocuSign, HelloSign |
| **Analytics** | Track performance | Google Analytics, CRM dashboards |
| **Communication** | Team coordination | WhatsApp Business, Slack (free tier) |

## 10. Customer Success and Retention

### Customer Success as a Sales Function (Kazanjy)

Customer success is not just support — it is the foundation of expansion revenue:

| Activity | Goal | Impact on Revenue |
|---|---|---|
| **Onboarding** | Get customer to first value quickly | Reduces churn in first 90 days |
| **Health monitoring** | Track usage, satisfaction, outcomes | Early warning for at-risk accounts |
| **Quarterly reviews** | Assess value delivered, plan next steps | Identifies upsell/cross-sell opportunities |
| **Expansion selling** | Upgrade, add users, new modules | 5-25x cheaper than new customer acquisition |
| **Referral generation** | Turn happy customers into advocates | Highest-quality, lowest-cost leads |

### Customer Health Score

| Factor | Weight | Green | Yellow | Red |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Product usage** | 30% | Weekly active | Monthly active | Inactive 30+ days |
| **Support tickets** | 20% | < 2/month | 3-5/month | > 5/month or escalation |
| **Payment** | 20% | On time | 1-7 days late | > 7 days late |
| **Engagement** | 15% | Attends reviews, responds | Slow to respond | Unresponsive |
| **NPS / Satisfaction** | 15% | Promoter (9-10) | Passive (7-8) | Detractor (0-6) |

## 11. Sales Outsourcing Decision (Goliger)

### When to Outsource vs. Build In-House

| Factor | Build In-House | Outsource |
|---|---|---|
| **Product complexity** | High — needs deep knowledge | Low — straightforward pitch |
| **Sales cycle** | Long, relationship-based | Short, transactional |
| **Budget** | Can afford fixed salaries | Need variable-cost model |
| **Market knowledge** | You know your market best | Outsourcer has established local networks |
| **Speed to market** | Have time to build team | Need revenue immediately |
| **Control** | Need full control of brand experience | Can accept partner representing you |

### Outsourcing Options

| Option | Description | Cost Model |
|---|---|---|
| **Freelance sales reps** | Independent reps on commission | Commission only (10-30%) |
| **Sales agency** | Outsourced team managed by agency | Retainer + commission |
| **Channel partners / Resellers** | Partners who sell alongside their products | Margin sharing (20-40%) |
| **Referral partners** | Individuals/businesses who refer leads | Per-lead or per-close fee |
