## 1. AARRR Funnel Deep-Dive

### Acquisition

**Goal:** Drive qualified traffic to the business.

| Tactic | Effort | Cost | Time to Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| SEO + Blog content | High | Low | 6-12 months |
| Google/Bing ads | Low | High | Immediate |
| Social media ads | Low | Medium | Immediate |
| Referral programme | Medium | Low | 2-4 months |
| Partnerships | Medium | Low | 2-6 months |
| Community building | High | Low | 3-12 months |
| PR / Media outreach | Medium | Low | Variable |
| Events / Conferences | Medium | Medium | Immediate |

**Key metric:** CAC per channel. Track which channels bring the highest-quality (lowest-churn) customers, not just the cheapest.

### Activation

**Goal:** Deliver the "aha moment" — the first experience of value.

Define the activation event for the business:
- SaaS: Completed onboarding + first core action
- E-commerce: First purchase delivered successfully
- Service: First consultation or deliverable completed
- Marketplace: First transaction between buyer and seller

**Optimisation levers:**
- Simplify onboarding (fewer steps = higher completion)
- Provide quick wins early
- Use tooltips, guides, or concierge onboarding
- Send activation nudges via email/SMS for incomplete onboarders

### Retention

**Goal:** Keep customers coming back.

```
Retention cohort analysis:
Day 1 retention = % of new users who return on day 1
Day 7 retention = % who return within 7 days
Day 30 retention = % who return within 30 days
```

**Retention strategies:**
- Habit loops (trigger → action → reward → investment)
- Regular value delivery (new content, features, updates)
- Re-engagement campaigns (email, push, SMS)
- Customer success programmes (proactive outreach)
- Loyalty rewards (points, tiers, exclusive access)

### Referral

**Goal:** Turn customers into advocates.

```
Viral coefficient (K) = Invitations per user x Conversion rate of invitations

If K > 1: Viral growth (each customer brings more than one new customer)
If K = 0.5: Each customer brings half a new customer (still valuable)
```

**Referral programme design:**
- Reward both referrer and referred (two-sided incentive)
- Make sharing frictionless (pre-written messages, one-click sharing)
- Track referral source for attribution
- Time referral request to peak satisfaction moments

### Revenue

**Goal:** Maximise customer monetisation.

**Revenue expansion tactics:**
- Upselling (higher tier, more features)
- Cross-selling (complementary products)
- Annual billing discounts (improve cash flow, reduce churn)
- Usage-based pricing (grows with customer success)
- Add-on services (training, support, consulting)

## 2. Growth Loops

### What Growth Loops Replace

Traditional funnels are linear (top → bottom). Growth loops are circular — the output of one cycle becomes the input of the next:

```
User joins → Uses product → Creates content/data → Attracts new users → Repeat
```

### Common Growth Loop Types

**Content loop:**
```
Create content → Content ranks in search → Attracts visitors → Visitors sign up → Users create content → Repeat
```

**Referral loop:**
```
Customer buys → Has great experience → Refers friend → Friend buys → Repeat
```

**Data loop:**
```
Users provide data → Product improves with data → Better product attracts users → More data → Repeat
```

**Marketplace loop:**
```
More sellers → More selection → More buyers → More revenue for sellers → More sellers → Repeat
```

### Identifying Your Growth Loop

For the business plan, identify:
1. What is the primary growth loop?
2. What is the input (what starts the cycle)?
3. What is the output (what completes the cycle and feeds back)?
4. What is the cycle time (how long for one loop)?
5. What could break the loop?

## 3. Digital Channel Playbooks

### SEO Playbook

```
Month 1-3: Foundation
- Keyword research (target 50-100 relevant keywords)
- Technical SEO audit and fixes
- Create 10-15 cornerstone content pieces

Month 4-6: Scale
- Publish 4-8 pieces per month
- Build backlinks through outreach and partnerships
- Optimise existing content based on performance

Month 7-12: Compound
- Content library drives organic traffic growth
- Update and expand top-performing content
- Target featured snippets and long-tail keywords
```

### Paid Advertising Playbook

```
Phase 1: Test (Budget: 20% of ad budget)
- Test 3-5 audience segments
- Test 3-5 ad creatives per segment
- Test 2-3 landing pages
- Run for 2-4 weeks

Phase 2: Optimise (Budget: 30%)
- Kill underperforming segments and creatives
- Scale winning combinations
- Refine targeting based on data
- Run for 4-8 weeks

Phase 3: Scale (Budget: 50%)
- Increase budget on proven winners
- Expand to adjacent audiences
- Add retargeting campaigns
- Ongoing with weekly optimisation
```

| Instagram | Visual content, stories, reels | 4-7x/week | B2C, lifestyle brands |

## 4. Content Strategy Framework

### Content Pillar Model

Organise content around 3-5 core topics (pillars):

```
Pillar topic: [Core subject area]
├── Cornerstone article (2000+ words, comprehensive)
├── Supporting blog posts (800-1500 words each)
├── Social media content derived from above
├── Email newsletter content
└── Video/podcast episodes
```

### Content-to-Revenue Pipeline

```
Awareness content → Email capture → Nurture sequence → Sales content → Conversion

Metrics per stage:
  Awareness: Traffic, social shares, time on page
  Capture: Lead magnet conversion rate, subscriber growth
  Nurture: Email open rate, click rate, engagement score
  Conversion: Sales page visits, demo requests, purchases
```

### Content Calendar Structure

| Week | Blog | Social | Email | Video |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pillar article | Daily posts | Newsletter | Tutorial |
| 2 | Supporting post | Daily posts | Nurture email | — |
| 3 | Guest post / PR | Daily posts | Case study | Behind-scenes |
| 4 | Supporting post | Daily posts | Newsletter | — |

## 5. Immersive and Phygital Marketing (Kotler 6.0)

### When to Include Immersive Marketing

Include phygital/immersive elements if:
- Target market includes Gen Z or Gen Alpha
- Industry is retail, entertainment, education, or real estate
- Competitors are investing in AR/VR experiences
- Product benefits from "try before buy" experiences

### Immersive Marketing Tactics

| Tactic | Cost | Impact | Maturity |
|---|---|---|---|
| AR product try-on | Medium | High (conversion lift) | Mainstream |
| Virtual showroom/tour | Medium | Medium | Growing |
| Gamified loyalty | Low-Medium | Medium | Mainstream |
| NFT/digital collectibles | Low | Low-Medium | Declining |
| Metaverse presence | High | Low (currently) | Experimental |
| AI-powered personalisation | Medium | High | Mainstream |

### Phygital Integration Points

For businesses with physical and digital presence:
- **Discovery:** Online ads → in-store experience
- **Trial:** AR try-on online → physical purchase
- **Purchase:** Browse in-store → buy online (or reverse)
- **Support:** AI chatbot → human escalation
- **Loyalty:** Digital rewards → physical experiences
