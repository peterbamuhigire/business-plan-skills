# Marketing & Sales Strategy Skill

Generate a comprehensive go-to-market strategy that shows exactly how the business will attract, convert, and retain customers.

## What to Generate

### Required Elements

1. **Marketing objectives** — Specific, measurable goals (SMART format)
2. **Positioning statement** — How the brand is perceived relative to competitors
3. **Marketing mix (4Ps / 7Ps)**
   - **Product** — Features, packaging, branding (reference section 03)
   - **Price** — Pricing strategy, model, and justification
   - **Place** — Distribution channels, sales locations, delivery methods
   - **Promotion** — Advertising, PR, content marketing, social media, partnerships
   - **People** — Customer-facing staff and training (for services)
   - **Process** — Service delivery process (for services)
   - **Physical evidence** — Tangible proof of quality (for services)
4. **Sales strategy and process** — How leads become customers
5. **Customer acquisition funnel** — Awareness → Interest → Decision → Action
6. **Channel strategy** — Online, offline, direct, indirect, partnerships
7. **Marketing budget** — Allocation by channel with expected ROI
8. **Customer retention strategy** — How to keep customers (loyalty, support, upsell)
9. **Key metrics** — CAC, conversion rate, churn rate, marketing ROI

### Pricing Strategy Options

- **Cost-plus** — Cost + margin
- **Value-based** — Price based on perceived value to customer
- **Competitive** — Priced relative to competitors
- **Penetration** — Low initial price to gain market share
- **Skimming** — High initial price, lower over time
- **Freemium** — Free base tier, paid premium features
- **Subscription** — Recurring revenue model

### Sales Funnel Framework

```
AWARENESS  → How do prospects discover us?
INTEREST   → What makes them engage further?
EVALUATION → How do they compare us to alternatives?
DECISION   → What triggers the purchase?
RETENTION  → How do we keep them and generate referrals?
```

## Generation Process

1. Ask for: business model (B2B/B2C/D2C), price point, primary channels, budget range
2. Define positioning statement
3. Build complete marketing mix
4. Design the sales funnel with specific tactics at each stage
5. Allocate marketing budget by channel
6. Define retention and upsell strategy
7. Set measurable KPIs for each channel

## Quality Criteria

- Strategy is specific to this business, not generic marketing advice
- Budget is realistic and allocated with ROI expectations
- Sales process is defined step-by-step
- Online and offline channels are both considered
- Metrics are defined for measuring success
