# Marketing & Sales Strategy Skill

Generate a comprehensive go-to-market strategy that shows exactly how the business will attract, convert, and retain customers.

## Required Elements

1. **Marketing objectives** — Specific, measurable goals (SMART format)
2. **Marketing evolution stage** — Where the business sits on Kotler's 1.0-6.0 spectrum
3. **Positioning statement** — How the brand is perceived relative to competitors
4. **Marketing mix (4Ps / 7Ps)**
   - **Product** — Features, packaging, branding (reference section 03)
   - **Price** — Strategy, model, and justification with competitive benchmarking
   - **Place** — Distribution channels, sales locations, delivery methods
   - **Promotion** — Channel mix with budget allocation and expected ROI
   - **People** — Customer-facing staff and training (for services)
   - **Process** — Service delivery process (for services)
   - **Physical evidence** — Tangible proof of quality (for services)
5. **Sales strategy and process** — How leads become customers, step by step
6. **Customer acquisition funnel (AARRR)** — Metrics at each stage
7. **Digital channel strategy** — Online presence, social media, content, SEO, paid
8. **Guerrilla marketing tactics** — Low-cost, high-impact approaches for budget-constrained businesses
9. **Marketing budget** — Allocation by channel with expected ROI
10. **Customer retention strategy** — Loyalty, support, upsell, referral programmes
11. **Key metrics and KPIs** — Dashboard of marketing performance indicators

Position the business's marketing approach:

|---|---|---|---|
| 1.0 | Product-centric | "Build it, they'll come" | Commodity markets |
| 2.0 | Customer-centric | Segmentation, targeting | Traditional service |

| 4.0 | Digital transition | Multi-channel digital | E-commerce, digital-first |
| 5.0 | Technology for humanity | AI, IoT, big data | Tech-enabled services |

State current stage and target stage (where the business aims to be in 3 years).

## Pricing Strategy Options

| Strategy | Description | Best For | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cost-plus** | Cost + fixed margin | Simple products, low competition | Ignores customer value |
| **Value-based** | Price by perceived value | Differentiated products | Requires strong positioning |
| **Competitive** | Match or undercut rivals | Commodity markets | Margin pressure |
| **Penetration** | Low initial price | Market share capture | Hard to raise later |
| **Skimming** | High initial, lower over time | Innovation, early adopters | Invites competition |
| **Freemium** | Free base, paid premium | SaaS, digital products | Conversion rate risk |
| **Subscription** | Recurring revenue | Predictable cash flow | Churn management |
| **Tiered** | Multiple price points | Diverse customer segments | Complexity |

## Customer Acquisition Funnel (AARRR)

```
ACQUISITION  → How do prospects discover us?
               Channels, CAC per channel, traffic volume
ACTIVATION   → Do they have a great first experience?
               Onboarding completion, time to first value
RETENTION    → Do they come back?
               Day 1/7/30 retention, churn rate
REFERRAL     → Do they tell others?
               NPS, referral rate, viral coefficient
REVENUE      → Can we monetise them?
               ARPU, CLV, conversion rate, expansion revenue
```

For each stage, specify: tactic, metric, target, and budget allocation.

## Guerrilla Marketing Tactics (Levinson)

For businesses with limited budgets, prioritise high-impact, low-cost tactics:

### The 7-Step Guerrilla Marketing Plan

1. Define the purpose of marketing (what action should customers take?)
2. State the competitive advantage (why choose us?)
3. Define the target audience (who specifically?)
4. Choose marketing weapons (which channels/tactics?)
5. Define the niche (what narrow focus?)
6. Set the budget as percentage of projected revenue
7. Commit to consistency (guerrilla marketing works through repetition, not volume)

### Top Guerrilla Weapons for Small Business

- **Fusion marketing** — Partner with non-competing businesses to share marketing costs
- **Guarantee marketing** — Bold guarantees that reduce purchase risk
- **Education-based marketing** — Teach prospects to position as the expert
- **Follow-up systems** — Systematic post-purchase communication
- **Referral programmes** — Structured incentives for customer-to-customer marketing
- **Community building** — Create a tribe around the brand
- **Testimonial collection** — Systematically gather and deploy social proof

## Small Business Marketing Tactics (Jarvis)

For small businesses, incorporate Jarvis's three growth levers and practical marketing methods:

### Three Ways to Grow Any Business

Every marketing plan must address all three levers simultaneously:
1. **Get new customers** — Alliances, networking, PR, advertising, events, direct sales
2. **Increase spend per customer** — Upselling, cross-selling, packaging, customer reviews
3. **Increase purchase frequency** — Communication calendars, newsletters, loyalty, special offers

### Alliance and Joint Venture Strategy

- Identify non-competing businesses that serve the same target customer
- Offer a free experience of your service so alliance partners can evaluate quality
- Formalise the arrangement with clear expectations and reciprocal commitments
- Co-host events, create joint packages, cross-promote to each other's customer bases

### PR and Expert Positioning

- Write articles for industry publications using "How to" titles and tip lists
- Submit press releases for announcements, milestones, customer stories, and events
- Speak at conferences and networking events to position as the expert in a niche
- Build relationships with editors and journalists before pitching stories

### Customer Retention System

- Build a 12-month communication calendar with planned touches each month
- Conduct annual customer needs reviews to uncover upsell opportunities
- Handle complaints as gifts: listen, apologise, exceed expectations, learn, and prevent recurrence
- Keep detailed CRM records and update at least annually

### Marketing Evaluation Discipline

- Test and measure every marketing activity (ask enquirers where they heard about you)
- Rate each channel 1-5 for success and stop expensive activities with poor returns
- Track cost per lead and cost per acquisition per channel
- Hold monthly marketing review meetings with a structured agenda

## Sales Process

### B2C Sales Funnel

```
Awareness → Interest → Consideration → Purchase → Loyalty → Advocacy
```

### B2B Sales Process (Schiffman Four-Step Model)

```
Step 1: OPENING (Prospecting, cold calls, initial contact)
   → Objective: Get agreement to move into a meaningful interview

Step 2: INTERVIEWING (Discovery and information gathering — ~75% of effort)
   → Objective: Gather enough information to build a tailored presentation
   → Use do-based questions: what they do, how, when, where, with whom, why
   → Your real competition is the STATUS QUO — what the prospect already does

Step 3: PRESENTATION (Proposal delivery)
   → Objective: Present a plan that makes sense based on verified information
   → Insert VERIFICATION step before formal presentation (preliminary proposal)
   → Never present until: right person confirmed, budget discussed, timetable set

Step 4: CLOSING (Decision)
   → "It makes sense to me — what do you think?"
   → If "no": take responsibility, ask where you went wrong, listen for "The thing is..."
```

For each stage, define: responsible person, tools used, conversion rate target, and average time in stage.

### PIPA First Meeting Framework (Schiffman)

Structure every initial prospect meeting:

1. **Present** — Brief 2-3 sentence company introduction ("Would it help if I told you a little about us?")
2. **Interview** — Immediately transition to do-based questions (past/present/future of what the prospect does)
3. **Present a next step** — Propose a specific next action with date and time ("Why don't I come back Tuesday at 2?")
4. **Agreement** — Confirm the prospect agrees ("Does this make sense?")

Key rules: Never product dump. Listen more than talk (70/30 ratio). Take detailed notes. The prospect who corrects you is giving you gold. Use parable selling (reference past client success stories) to position next steps.

### Sales Pipeline Ratios (Schiffman)

Track and improve continuously:

| Ratio | What to Track |
|---|---|
| **Dials to discussions** | How many calls to reach one live person? |
| **Discussions to appointments** | How many conversations to get one meeting? |
| **First to second appointments** | How many first meetings advance? |
| **Visits to sales** | How many total meetings per closed deal? |
| **Average deal value** | What is each sale worth? |

Pipeline attrition reality: When you close 1 sale from 20 prospects at a 1-in-5 ratio, you lose 5 prospects (not 1). You cannot ease up on prospecting after making a sale. Prospect every day without exception.

### Prospect Probability Tiers (Schiffman)

| Prospect Type | Close Probability | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Existing customer (upsell/cross-sell) | ~50% (1 in 2) | Highest |
| Past customer (inactive) | ~25% (1 in 4) | High — maintain regular contact |
| New prospect (cold) | ~5% (1 in 20) | Volume-dependent — daily prospecting |
| Fallback prospect (previously rejected) | 5-10% on revisit | Revisit every ~3 months |

### Relationship Progression Levels (Schiffman)

| Level | Role | Vulnerability | How to Advance |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Seller | Peddler (price-only) | Extreme — any competitor beats you | Build repeat business |
| 2. Supplier | Regular vendor | High — still largely transactional | Deepen information about their business |
| 3. Vendor | Trusted provider | Moderate — advance warning of changes | Deliver consistently, build trust |
| 4. Partner | Strategic ally | Low — embedded in operations | Participate in planning, provide strategic input |

Progress every significant account toward partner level over time.

### Small Business Sales Tactics (Jarvis)

For service businesses and small B2B, use Jarvis's practical sales approach:

```
1. Warm introduction (letter/email before phone call)
2. Research call (qualify prospect, find decision maker)
3. Appointment setting (offer free consultation or assessment as entry point)
4. Build rapport (find common ground, match tone, show genuine interest)
5. Discovery (listen deeply, uncover the prospect's "pain" with targeted questions)
6. Tailored proposition (use their language, connect each need to a specific solution)
7. Winning proposal (2-3 pages: situation, objectives, solution, value, price, plan)
8. Follow-up (increases response 20-50%, maintain discipline until conclusion)
```

**Key principle:** Following up sales letters or direct mail increases response by 20-50%. Most businesses quit follow-up too early.

### Persuasion-Based Sales Process (Wes Lee 10-Step)

A 3-phase, 10-step process for face-to-face, phone, or high-touch sales:

```
PHASE 1 — INVOLVEMENT
  Step 1: Preparation (know client, competitors, objections)
  Step 2: Peak emotional state (transfer positive emotions)
  Step 3: Contact clients (law of averages, ask for referrals)
  Step 4: Build deep relationship (trust, mirroring, rapport)
  Step 5: Captivate them (6-step benefit formula, social proof)

PHASE 2 — QUALIFY AND MAGNIFY
  Step 6: Qualify (WPNID: Wants, Pain, Needs, Income, Decision)
           + Identify client thought patterns (11 patterns)
  Step 7: Cultivate belief and test close (12 compelling reasons, 3 test close types)

PHASE 3 — URGENCY
  Step 8: Make tangible (Direct/Exclaim/Visualise, sensory experience, assumptive posture)
  Step 9: Transform objections into commitments (5-step: Ignore → Listen → Repeat → Dig → Commit)
  Step 10: Create lasting relationship (prevent buyer's remorse, referral system, make buying simple)
```

**Core principle**: People buy on emotion and justify logically. Emotional and logical reasons to buy must overwhelm significant reasons not to buy. ## Key Marketing Metrics (Farris)

### Performance Dashboard

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|---|---|---|
| **CAC** | Total marketing spend / New customers | < 1/3 of CLV |
| **CLV:CAC ratio** | Customer lifetime value / CAC | > 3:1 |
| **Marketing ROI** | (Revenue from marketing - Marketing cost) / Marketing cost | > 5:1 |
| **Conversion rate** | Customers / Leads x 100 | Industry benchmark |
| **Churn rate** | Lost customers / Total customers x 100 | < 5% monthly |
| **NPS** | % Promoters - % Detractors | > 50 |
| **Share of voice** | Brand ad spend / Category ad spend x 100 | > Market share |
| **CAC payback** | CAC / Monthly margin per customer | < 12 months |

## Generation Process

1. Ask for: business model (B2B/B2C/D2C), price point, primary channels, budget range
2. Position on Kotler's marketing evolution spectrum
3. Define positioning statement, USP, and strap-line
4. Build complete marketing mix (4Ps or 7Ps)
5. Map the AARRR funnel with specific tactics and metrics at each stage
6. Design digital channel strategy with channel selection rationale
7. Include guerrilla tactics for budget-constrained scenarios
8. For small businesses: apply Jarvis's three growth levers, alliance strategy, PR plan, and communication calendar
9. Allocate marketing budget by channel with ROI expectations
10. Define retention and upsell strategy including customer review process and complaint handling
11. Design the sales process appropriate to business type (B2C funnel, B2B Schiffman four-step process, Jarvis service selling, or Wes Lee persuasion)
12. For B2B: define the PIPA first meeting structure, pipeline ratios, prospecting cadence, and relationship progression targets
13. Set measurable KPIs for each channel and funnel stage
14. Include marketing evaluation system with test-and-measure discipline

## Quality Criteria

- Strategy is specific to this business, not generic marketing advice
- Marketing evolution stage is identified with trajectory
- USP is specific, measurable, and differentiated from competitors
- Budget is realistic and allocated with ROI expectations per channel
- Sales process is defined step-by-step with conversion targets and pipeline ratios
- B2B sales includes PIPA first meeting framework, prospecting cadence, and relationship level targets
- AARRR funnel has specific tactics and metrics at each stage
- Online and offline channels are both considered
- Guerrilla tactics are included for budget-constrained scenarios
- All three growth levers are addressed (new customers, spend per customer, purchase frequency)
- Customer retention includes communication calendar, review process, and complaint handling
- Alliance and referral strategies are included for small businesses
- PR and expert positioning plan is defined for profile building
- Marketing evaluation system includes test-and-measure processes
- Metrics dashboard covers acquisition, retention, and revenue
- All cost assumptions are stated and traceable
