# Budget Narrative Templates for Uganda Grant Proposals

Reference file for `11b-grant-proposal` skill. Provides Uganda-specific budget narrative guidance,
cost benchmarks, standard justification language, and compliance requirements for donor-funded
project budgets.

---

## 1. What Is a Budget Narrative?

A budget narrative (also called a budget justification) is a written explanation accompanying a
grant budget spreadsheet. It explains **why** each cost is necessary, **how** each figure was
calculated, and **how** each cost relates to project activities.

Donors evaluate budget narratives against three core tests:

- **Cost reasonableness:** Is the unit cost consistent with what a prudent person would pay in the
  local market? Costs must not exceed current market rates for similar goods or services.
- **Cost allowability:** Is the expenditure type permitted under the donor's rules? Some costs are
  categorically prohibited (see section 5 below).
- **Cost allocability:** Is the cost genuinely attributable to the project? Costs shared between
  multiple projects must be split on a documented, rational basis.

A strong budget narrative removes uncertainty for the donor's grants officer and signals that the
organisation understands financial accountability norms.

---

## 2. Standard Budget Line-Item Format

For each budget line, provide the following four elements:

```
[Line item name]: [Unit cost] x [Number of units] x [Duration/frequency] = [Total cost]
Justification: [One to three sentences explaining why this cost is necessary, how the unit
rate was determined, and any relevant procurement approach.]
```

## 3. Uganda Cost Benchmarks by Category

The following rates reflect Q1 2026 Kampala and peri-urban Uganda market norms. Adjust downward
by 15-25% for upcountry locations. Adjust upward by 10-15% for Kampala CBD.

### 3.1 Personnel (Staff)

Personnel costs must be justified by position, time commitment (% FTE), and salary grade.
Where the organisation has an approved salary scale, reference it and attach as an annex.

| Position | Monthly Salary Range (UGX) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Project Director / CEO | 4,500,000 - 8,000,000 | Typically charged at 20-50% FTE to the grant |
| Project Coordinator / Manager | 2,500,000 - 4,000,000 | Full-time on project |
| M&E Officer | 2,000,000 - 3,500,000 | Full-time or 50-75% FTE |
| Field Officer / Extension Worker | 1,200,000 - 1,800,000 | Per officer; may have multiple |
| Accountant / Finance Officer | 1,500,000 - 2,500,000 | Often shared across projects |
| Administrative Assistant | 800,000 - 1,400,000 | Support role |
| Driver | 700,000 - 1,100,000 | Include fuel separately |
| Guards / Watchmen | 400,000 - 700,000 | Per guard |

**Standard justification language:**

> "Project Coordinator (1.0 FTE x 36 months x UGX 3,200,000/month = UGX 115,200,000).
> The Project Coordinator will provide full-time management of all project activities, including
> field supervision, partner coordination, and monthly reporting to the donor. The salary rate
> reflects the organisation's approved salary scale (Grade 4, attached as Annex C) and is
> consistent with market rates for equivalent roles in Kampala-based NGOs."

### 3.2 Consultants and Technical Assistance

Short-term consultants must be justified by the specific expertise required, number of days,
and why this expertise cannot be met by existing staff.

| Consultant Type | Daily Rate Range (UGX) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| National consultant (senior) | 600,000 - 1,500,000 | e.g., agronomy, finance, legal |
| National consultant (mid-level) | 300,000 - 600,000 | e.g., training design, data analysis |
| International consultant equivalent | 800,000 - 1,500,000 | If a nationally-hired international expert |
| Auditor (annual audit) | 3,500,000 - 8,000,000 | Lump sum per audit; competitive tender |

**Standard justification language:**

> "National Financial Systems Consultant (1 person x 10 days x UGX 500,000/day = UGX 5,000,000).
> A short-term consultant will develop the project's financial management manual and chart of
> accounts in Month 2. This expertise is not available in-house; the rate is consistent with
> UNDP Uganda roster rates for national consultants at this level."

### 3.3 Training and Capacity Building

Training budgets are scrutinised closely. Provide a per-participant cost breakdown.

| Item | Unit Cost Range (UGX) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Venue hire (full day) | 150,000 - 400,000 | Kampala; lower upcountry |
| Meals and refreshments | 15,000 - 35,000 | Per person per day; itemise AM/PM tea + lunch |
| Accommodation (upcountry) | 80,000 - 200,000 | Per person per night; budget hotel standard |
| Printing and stationery | 5,000 - 15,000 | Per participant per workshop day |
| Facilitator fee (per day) | 200,000 - 600,000 | National facilitator |
| Participant transport refund | 20,000 - 80,000 | Per person; distance-based |

**Standard justification language:**

> "Training Workshop - Farmer GAP Training (4 workshops x 50 participants x 2 days):
> Venue: 4 x 2 days x UGX 250,000/day = UGX 2,000,000
> Meals/refreshments: 4 x 50 x 2 x UGX 20,000 = UGX 8,000,000
> Printing: 4 x 50 x UGX 8,000 = UGX 1,600,000
> Total training cost: UGX 11,600,000 (UGX 29,000 per participant-day)
> Cost is consistent with MAAIF extension training benchmarks for equivalent programmes."

### 3.4 Transport and Logistics

| Item | Unit Cost Range (UGX) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Field vehicle hire (with driver) | 250,000 - 400,000 | Per day; include fuel separately |
| Fuel allowance | 200 per km | Mid-2026 estimate |
| Motorcycle hire (boda boda) | 60,000 - 100,000 | Per day; upcountry field work |
| Air ticket (Kampala-Arua/Gulu) | 400,000 - 700,000 | Economy; one-way |
| Bus/taxi transport | 30,000 - 120,000 | Per trip; intercity |

**Standard justification language:**

> "Vehicle hire for field supervision (Project Coordinator, 36 months x 4 field days/month
> x UGX 300,000/day = UGX 43,200,000). Monthly field visits to 4 project sub-counties require
> transport not covered by the organisation's owned vehicle (which is allocated to another
> programme). Rate is based on three competitive quotations received in January 2026."

### 3.5 Office and Operating Costs

| Item | Unit Cost Range (UGX) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Office rent (Kampala) | 800,000 - 2,500,000 | Per month; shared office space lower |
| Office rent (upcountry town) | 300,000 - 800,000 | Per month |
| Communications (internet + phone) | 50,000 - 150,000 | Per staff member per month |
| Utilities (electricity, water) | 80,000 - 200,000 | Per month; office |
| Office consumables (stationery, etc.) | 50,000 - 150,000 | Per month |

### 3.6 Equipment and Capital Items

Capital items (unit cost above UGX 500,000 and useful life above 1 year) must list the item,
quantity, unit price, and procurement method (direct purchase, local shopping, or competitive
tender).

**Standard justification language:**

> "Laptop computers (3 units x UGX 2,400,000 = UGX 7,200,000). Required for Project Coordinator,
> M&E Officer, and Accountant for data management, report writing, and financial accounting.
> The organisation does not have spare equipment available. Unit price based on Kampala market
> survey (January 2026); procurement will follow the organisation's procurement policy requiring
> three quotations for items above UGX 1,000,000."

## 4. Indirect Costs / Overhead Rate

Most donors allow implementers to charge indirect costs (organisational overheads not directly
attributable to project activities) as a percentage of direct costs.

**Typical acceptable range: 7-15% of direct costs.**

- UN agencies (UNDP, UNICEF): typically accept 7-10%
- USAID: uses Negotiated Indirect Cost Rate Agreement (NICRA); can range from 5-30%
- EU: accepts up to 7% of total eligible direct costs without justification; higher rates require
  a documented indirect cost rate study
- GIZ/bilateral donors: typically 10-15% with supporting documentation

**What indirect costs cover:**
- Headquarters management and governance
- Central finance, HR, and IT systems
- Executive oversight of field programmes
- Organisational insurance and legal costs
- Shared office costs not allocated to specific projects

**Standard justification language:**

> "Indirect costs (UGX 48,000,000 = 10% of total direct costs of UGX 480,000,000).
> The organisation's indirect cost rate of 10% has been calculated based on a cost allocation
> study conducted in FY2024/25 and is consistent with the organisation's approved overhead rate.
> Indirect costs cover headquarters management, shared finance and HR functions, and
> organisational governance. Supporting documentation is available on request."

SECTION C: TRAINING AND WORKSHOPS

C1. [Training event name]
Venue: [number] events x [days] days x UGX [rate]/day = UGX [subtotal]

Printing: [participants] x UGX [rate] = UGX [subtotal]

Justification: [Purpose; participant count; geographic location; cost basis]

SECTION D: TRAVEL AND TRANSPORT

D1. Vehicle hire for field supervision
[months] x [days/month] field days x UGX [rate]/day = UGX [total]

SECTION E: EQUIPMENT

[quantity] x UGX [unit price] = UGX [total]

[months] x UGX [rate]/month = UGX [total]

## 6. Disallowable Costs

The following costs are **categorically disallowable** under most donor frameworks and must
never appear in a grant budget:

- Alcohol or beverages with alcohol content
- Entertainment (meals not related to project activities, gifts, social events)
- Fines, penalties, and interest charges
- Costs incurred before the grant agreement is signed (unless specifically pre-approved)
- Costs without supporting documentation (receipts, contracts, payment records)
- Personal expenses of staff (personal phone bills, personal travel)
- Contingency reserves exceeding 5% (some donors prohibit contingency entirely)
- Contributions to political parties or campaigns
- First-class or business-class travel (unless prior approval obtained for health or disability)
- Bonuses not specified in employment contracts at project start

## 7. Multi-Year Budgets: Inflation Escalation

For projects spanning two or more financial years, build in annual cost escalation to reflect
Uganda's inflation environment.

**UBOS CPI headline inflation:** approximately 3-4% per annum (2024-2026 average).
**Recommended planning escalation rate: 5% per annum** — conservative buffer above CPI,
accounting for fuel price and food cost volatility.

**How to apply escalation:**

```
Personnel costs example:
  Year 1 monthly salary: UGX 2,500,000
  Year 2 monthly salary: UGX 2,500,000 x 1.05 = UGX 2,625,000
  Year 3 monthly salary: UGX 2,500,000 x 1.1025 = UGX 2,756,250

Escalate: salaries, venue hire, accommodation, fuel/transport, consumables
Do NOT escalate: fixed-price contracts already signed, equipment already procured
```

**Standard justification language:**

> "Personnel costs in Year 2 and Year 3 have been escalated at 5% per annum to reflect
> expected salary adjustments consistent with Uganda CPI trends (UBOS, 2025). The 5% rate
> represents a conservative estimate above recent headline inflation of approximately 3.5%,
> providing a buffer for fuel and food price volatility that affect staff cost-of-living."
