# Competitive Analysis Skill

Generate a rigorous competitive landscape analysis that honestly assesses the competition and demonstrates a defensible competitive advantage.

## What to Generate

### Required Elements

1. **Direct competitors** — Companies offering the same solution to the same market
2. **Indirect competitors** — Alternative solutions to the same problem
3. **Substitute threats** — Different approaches customers might take (including "do nothing")
4. **Competitor profiles** (3-5 key competitors) — Detailed analysis of each
5. **Competitive matrix** — Feature/capability comparison table
6. **SWOT analysis** — Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats
7. **Competitive advantage / moat** — What makes the business defensible
8. **Barriers to entry** — What stops new competitors from entering
9. **Market positioning** — Where the business sits relative to competitors

### Competitive Matrix Format

| Capability | Our Company | Competitor A | Competitor B | Competitor C |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Feature 1 | Strong | Medium | Weak | Strong |
| Pricing | $X | $Y | $Z | $W |
| Market presence | ... | ... | ... | ... |

### Types of Competitive Moats

- **Cost advantage** — Lower cost structure enables lower prices
- **Network effects** — Value increases with more users
- **Switching costs** — Expensive or difficult for customers to leave
- **Brand/reputation** — Trust built over time
- **IP/patents** — Legal protection of innovations
- **Data advantage** — Proprietary data that improves the product
- **Regulatory advantage** — Licences or approvals competitors cannot easily obtain

## Generation Process

1. Ask for: industry, key competitors (if known), primary differentiator
2. Research direct, indirect, and substitute competitors
3. Build competitor profiles with honest assessment
4. Create competitive comparison matrix
5. Conduct SWOT analysis
6. Identify and articulate the competitive moat
7. Assess sustainability of the advantage over 3-5 years

## Quality Criteria

- Analysis includes indirect competitors and substitutes (not just direct)
- Competitor strengths are acknowledged honestly — investors spot bias
- SWOT weaknesses are real, not disguised strengths
- Competitive advantage is specific and defensible, not "better customer service"
- Positioning is distinct — clear blue water between this business and alternatives
