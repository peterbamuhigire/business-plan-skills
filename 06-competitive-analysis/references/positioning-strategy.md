## 1. Positioning Statement Framework

### Standard Positioning Statement

```
For [target segment]
who [need/problem statement],
[brand name] is a [category description]
that [key benefit/differentiator].
Unlike [primary competitor],
our product/service [unique advantage].
```

### Positioning Validation Checklist

The positioning must be:
- **Relevant** — addresses a real customer need
- **Distinct** — clearly different from competitors
- **Credible** — believable given the company's capabilities
- **Sustainable** — defensible over 3-5 years
- **Communicable** — explainable in one sentence

## 2. Guerrilla Positioning Tactics (Levinson)

### Core Principles

Guerrilla marketing prioritises time, energy, and imagination over large budgets:

- **Invest time before money** — Research deeply, then spend surgically
- **Focus on profit, not revenue** — Small businesses need margins, not vanity metrics
- **Measure everything** — Track every tactic to its revenue impact
- **Create consent-based relationships** — Permission marketing over interruption marketing
- **Leverage partnerships** — Fusion marketing with complementary businesses

### Guerrilla Positioning Against Larger Competitors

| Tactic | Description | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Niche dominance** | Own a category too small for big players | Market leader ignores the segment |
| **Expert positioning** | Become the go-to authority through content | Customers need education before buying |
| **Speed positioning** | "We deliver/implement faster" | Incumbent is known for slow delivery |
| **Personal positioning** | Founder/team as the brand differentiator | B2B services, consulting, creative |
| **Community positioning** | Build a movement, not just a brand | Values-driven customers (Gen Z/Alpha) |
| **Local positioning** | "We know this market" | Multinational competitors lack local depth |

### Guerrilla Marketing Weapons (Low-Cost, High-Impact)

Prioritise these channels for small businesses:

1. **Content marketing** — Blog posts, guides, videos (cost: time)
2. **Strategic partnerships** — Joint marketing with complementary businesses (cost: mutual effort)
3. **Referral programmes** — Incentivise word-of-mouth (cost: referral reward)
4. **Community building** — Online groups, events, workshops (cost: time + modest venue)
5. **PR and earned media** — Press releases, expert commentary (cost: time)
6. **Email marketing** — Permission-based nurture sequences (cost: platform fee)
7. **Social proof** — Customer reviews, testimonials, case studies (cost: time to collect)

### Fusion Marketing

Combine efforts with non-competing businesses that share the same target market:

```
Step 1: Identify 3-5 businesses that serve your customers but don't compete
Step 2: Propose mutual value (co-marketing, bundled offers, shared events)
Step 3: Split costs and share customer access
Step 4: Track cross-referral revenue to validate the partnership
```

## 3. Value-Based Differentiation

### Differentiation Dimensions

| Dimension | Examples | Sustainability |
|---|---|---|
| **Product** | Features, quality, design, customisation | Medium — can be copied |
| **Service** | Speed, support, training, onboarding | Medium — requires culture |
| **Channel** | Unique distribution, exclusive access | Medium — first-mover advantage |
| **People** | Expertise, relationships, personal touch | High — hard to replicate |
| **Image** | Brand perception, emotional connection | High — built over years |
| **Price** | Cost leadership, value pricing | Low — easily undercut |

### Value Proposition Canvas (Simplified)

```
Customer segment: [Who]
Jobs to be done: [What they're trying to accomplish]
Pains: [What frustrates them about current solutions]
Gains: [What would delight them beyond expectations]

Our offer:
Pain relievers: [How we eliminate or reduce each pain]
Gain creators: [How we deliver each desired gain]
Differentiator: [What makes our pain relief / gain creation unique]
```

### Avoiding False Differentiation

Investors reject these as genuine differentiators:
- "Better customer service" (everyone claims this)
- "Higher quality" (without measurable proof)
- "More innovative" (without specific innovation)
- "Lower price" (not sustainable without cost advantage)
- "First mover" (this is timing, not a moat)

Replace with specific, measurable claims:
- "24-hour response SLA with 99.2% achievement rate"
- "ISO 9001 certified with <0.5% defect rate"
- "AI-powered feature X that reduces processing time by 60%"
- "20% lower cost due to automated manufacturing"

## 4. Perceptual Mapping

### Two-Axis Positioning Map

Choose the two attributes most important to the target customer:

```
High [Attribute A]
        |
  Quadrant II  |  Quadrant I
  (e.g. High   |  (e.g. High quality,
   quality,    |   High price)
   Low price)  |
--------|--------
  Quadrant III |  Quadrant IV
  (e.g. Low    |  (e.g. Low quality,
   quality,    |   High price)
   Low price)  |
        |
Low [Attribute A]
   Low [Attribute B] ----------- High [Attribute B]
```

**Usage:** Plot all competitors and the business on the map. Identify:
- Where the cluster of competitors sits (the "red ocean")
- Where open space exists (potential "blue ocean")
- Whether the business occupies a distinct position

### Common Axis Pairs

| Industry Type | Axis 1 | Axis 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Consumer goods | Price | Quality/Premium |
| Technology | Ease of use | Feature richness |
| Services | Speed | Personalisation |
| Food/Beverage | Health | Indulgence |
| B2B SaaS | Simplicity | Enterprise capability |

## 5. Blue Ocean Positioning

### Eliminate-Reduce-Raise-Create Grid

| Action | What | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Eliminate** | Factors the industry takes for granted but add no value | Reduce cost structure |
| **Reduce** | Factors that are over-designed beyond what customers need | Simplify offering |
| **Raise** | Factors that should be raised above industry standard | Exceed expectations |
| **Create** | Factors the industry has never offered | New value creation |

### Blue Ocean Strategy Test

A valid blue ocean position must pass all three:
1. **Divergence** — The value curve looks different from competitors
2. **Focus** — The business excels on 2-3 dimensions, not all
3. **Compelling tagline** — The positioning can be communicated in one clear sentence
