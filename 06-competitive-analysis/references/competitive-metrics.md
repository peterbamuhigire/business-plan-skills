## 1. Market Share Metrics

### Unit Market Share

```
Unit Market Share (%) = Unit sales of brand / Total unit sales in market x 100
```

Best for: comparing brands within a product category with similar unit definitions.

### Revenue Market Share

```
Revenue Market Share (%) = Revenue of brand / Total market revenue x 100
```

Best for: all businesses, especially when unit definitions vary.

### Revenue vs. Unit Share Divergence

| Pattern | Meaning | Strategic Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue share > Unit share | Brand commands premium pricing | Price leader — protect premium |
| Revenue share = Unit share | Price at market average | Differentiate or risk commoditisation |
| Revenue share < Unit share | Brand competes on volume/price | Volume player — watch margin pressure |

### Relative Market Share

```
Relative Market Share = Brand's market share / Largest competitor's share
```

| Value | Position | BCG Implication |
|---|---|---|
| > 1.0 | Market leader | Star or Cash cow |
| 0.5 - 1.0 | Strong challenger | Potential Star |
| 0.1 - 0.5 | Minor player | Question mark |
| < 0.1 | Niche or negligible | Dog or niche specialist |

### Market Share Trend

```
Share change = Current period share - Prior period share

Share growth rate = (Current share - Prior share) / Prior share x 100
```

Positive share trend + growing market = ideal position.
Positive share trend + declining market = may be last-man-standing effect — investigate.

## 2. Industry Concentration Metrics

### Concentration Ratio (CR)

```
CR4 = Combined market share of top 4 firms
CR8 = Combined market share of top 8 firms
```

| CR4 Value | Structure | Entry Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| 0-40% | Competitive | Many niches available |
| 40-60% | Moderate | Differentiated entry viable |
| 60-80% | Oligopolistic | Must disrupt or find underserved niche |
| 80-100% | Highly concentrated | Very high barriers, regulatory or disruptive entry only |

### Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI)

```
HHI = Sum of (market share of each firm in %)^2

Example: 3 firms with 40%, 30%, 30% shares
HHI = 40^2 + 30^2 + 30^2 = 1600 + 900 + 900 = 3400
```

| HHI Range | Competition Level | New Entrant Implication |
|---|---|---|
| < 1,500 | Competitive | Room for new players |
| 1,500 - 2,500 | Moderately concentrated | Niche entry possible |
| > 2,500 | Highly concentrated | Significant barriers |

### Why Concentration Matters for Business Plans

- **High concentration** = large players dominate, but may leave niches underserved
- **Low concentration** = fragmented market, easier entry but more competition
- **Increasing concentration** = consolidation trend, potential M&A opportunity or barrier
- **Decreasing concentration** = market fragmenting, new entrants gaining ground

## 3. Brand Strength Indicators

### Brand Development Index (BDI)

```
BDI = (% of brand sales in segment / % of population in segment) x 100
```

- BDI > 100 = brand over-indexes in this segment (strength)
- BDI < 100 = brand under-indexes (opportunity or weakness)

### Category Development Index (CDI)

```
CDI = (% of category sales in segment / % of population in segment) x 100
```

### BDI/CDI Strategic Matrix

| Scenario | BDI | CDI | Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| High potential | Low | High | Category demand exists, brand does not — invest heavily |
| Strong position | High | High | Brand and category strong — defend and grow |
| Niche strength | High | Low | Brand strong in weak category — maintain, don't over-invest |
| Low priority | Low | Low | Weak brand in weak category — deprioritise |

### Share of Voice (SOV)

```
SOV = Brand's advertising spend / Total category advertising spend x 100
```

**SOV vs. SOM rule:** When SOV exceeds SOM, market share tends to grow. When SOM exceeds SOV, share tends to decline. New entrants should aim for SOV > SOM.

## 4. Competitive Benchmarking

### Competitive Response Matrix

Anticipate how competitors will respond to market entry:

| Competitor | Likely Response | Speed | Severity | Our Counter |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Market leader | Price reduction | Slow | Medium | Differentiate on value, not price |
| Direct rival | Feature matching | Fast | High | Stay ahead on innovation speed |
| Indirect rival | Pivot toward our space | Medium | Medium | Build switching costs early |

### Competitive Advantage Durability Test

For each claimed advantage, assess:

```
Advantage: [Name]
Can competitors replicate it? [Yes / No / Partially]
Time to replicate: [Months / Years / Never]
Cost to replicate: [Low / Medium / High / Prohibitive]
Evidence of durability: [Patent / Data moat / Network effect / Brand / Regulatory]
Durability rating: [High / Medium / Low]
```

### Win/Loss Analysis Framework

Track competitive outcomes:

```
Total opportunities: X
Wins vs. Competitor A: Y (Z%)
Losses vs. Competitor A: W (V%)

Top 3 reasons for winning:
1. [Reason] — cited in X% of wins
2. [Reason] — cited in Y% of wins
3. [Reason] — cited in Z% of wins

Top 3 reasons for losing:
1. [Reason] — cited in X% of losses
2. [Reason] — cited in Y% of losses
3. [Reason] — cited in Z% of losses
```
