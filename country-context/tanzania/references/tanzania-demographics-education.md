## 15. Key Development Implications for Business Plans

**Labour cost advantage:**
- 92% informal workforce → large, accessible labour pool
- Low formal wage base in most sectors
- EAC nationals work permit-free — regional talent accessible

**Consumer market dynamics:**
- 59% of households cannot afford nutritious diet — business plans must assess effective demand carefully
- Income inequality rising — premium and mass market segments diverge
- Food and beverage market: USD 2.27B (2023) → USD 3.1B (2026); CAGR 8.24%
- Workforce nutrition loss: ~1.55% of GDP (~USD 1 billion) — health sector opportunity

**Education sector opportunity:**
- SDG4 requires additional +4.2% of GDP in education spending
- 335,000+ tertiary students; 5,000+ teachers needed per year
- 4,500 classrooms/year construction requirement
- Private sector participation target: 46% (from current ~32%)

**Skills constraint:**
- 61% of graduates lack job-ready skills
- STEM graduates only 13.7% of tertiary total
- Business/management dominant — service economy orientation
- Technical/vocational skills gap is the most acute constraint
