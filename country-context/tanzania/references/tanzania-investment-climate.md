## 2. Tanzania Investment Act 2022 — Key Provisions

**Act No. 10 of 2022; Regulations 2023**

### Investment Thresholds
| Investor Type | Minimum Capital |
|--------------|----------------|
| Foreign investor (FDI) | USD 50 million |
| Local investor (Domestic) | USD 20 million (revised down to encourage local investment) |
| Strategic investor | Determined by capital and national priorities |

### Certificate of Incentives
- Issued through OSFC at TIC
- Grants access to: tax incentives, land acquisition, work permits for expatriates, investment protection

### One Stop Facilitation Centre (OSFC)
Houses 14 government ministries and agencies (MDAs). Services provided within one centre:
1. Certificates of Incentives (TIC)
2. Company Registration (BRELA)
3. Business and Industrial Licences (MITI)
4. Residence and Work Permits (Immigration)
5. Land Acquisition and Derivative Rights (Ministry of Lands)
6. Environment Certificates (NEMC)
7. Standard Product Certificates (TBS)
8. Food and Drug Licences (TFDA/TMDA)
9. Occupation, Health & Safety Compliance (OSHA)
10. Tax Identification Number — TIN (TRA)
11. Tax Exemptions (TRA)
12. Banking (Azania Bank available on-site)

**Q3 2024/25 OSFC throughput:** 15 companies registered; 18 TIC projects registered; 15 Azania Bank accounts opened; 63 investors served through PISC (Pre-Investment Service Centre)

### Land Policy (2023 — enacted February 2025)
- **Foreign investors can now acquire land** for investment purposes
- Land allocated based on: capital amount + national priority alignment
- Real estate sector: fully open to private investment
- Previous restriction (leasehold only, max 99 years) remains for non-investment land

### Annual Compliance
- Investors must submit annual progress reports to TIC
- Physical Verification Visits (PVVs) conducted by TIC
- Non-compliance may result in loss of Certificate of Incentives

### e-Regulations Platform
- Online portal for investment procedures
- Tracks all required steps, costs, and timelines

## 4. Trade Agreements

| Agreement | Status | Key Benefit |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **EAC Customs Union** | Active | Free trade among 8 members; no tariffs on qualifying goods; common external tariff |
| **EAC Common Market** | Active | Free movement of goods, services, capital, labour (EAC nationals no work permit) |
| **SADC FTA** | Active | 16 member states; preferential trade |
| **AfCFTA** | Active | Tanzania exports to Nigeria, Ghana, South Africa; gold, soap, soda ash |
| **AGOA** | Historical | US tariff-free access — check current status (US tariff policy changes 2025) |

**Dar es Salaam Port:** 80 million tonnes cargo (2023); target 100M by 2025 (AfDB). Gateway to EAC hinterland (Uganda, Rwanda, Burundi, DRC, Zambia).

**Intra-African Trade 2024:**
- Value: USD 5.6 billion (21.3% of total trade, up from 18.6% in 2023)
- Top destination: Nigeria 51.3% (gold dominant)
- Other: Uganda 9.8%, DRC 9.4%, Rwanda 6.8%, Kenya 6.0%
- Key intra-Africa imports from: South Africa 47.6% (iron/steel, vehicles, fertilisers)

## 5. Financial Sector Detail

### Banking Sector (2024)
- Licensed banks: **44** (commercial + community banks)
- Microfinance providers: **50,000+**
- Insurance + reinsurance companies: **40**

**Six largest banks (60% of sector assets):**
1. CRDB Bank
2. NMB Bank
3. National Bank of Commerce
4. Exim Bank Tanzania
5. Stanbic Bank Tanzania
6. People's Bank of Zanzibar

**Sector metrics (2024):**
- Total assets: TZS 62.2 trillion (~USD 25.2B); +14.6% YoY
- Capital adequacy ratio: 19.3% (minimum: 12%)
- Liquidity ratio: 29% (minimum: 20%)
- NPL ratio: 3.3% (down from 4.3% in 2023)
- Domestic credit to private sector: 16.4% of GDP (lowest in EAC region)
- Credit growth Q1 2025: 16.1%

**EAC Credit Comparison (IMF 2023):**
| Country | Credit/GDP |
|---------|-----------|
| Tanzania | 16.4% |
| Uganda | 14.8% |
| Rwanda | 22.7% |
| Kenya | 31.6% |
| SSA average | 33.4% (2022) |

### Mobile Money (June 2024)
- Active mobile money accounts: **54.6 million**
- Total transaction value: TZS 117.1 trillion
- Platforms: M-Pesa (Vodacom), Tigo Pesa, Airtel Money
- Note: Mobile money accounts far exceed total population — reflects multi-SIM use

### Financial Inclusion
- Adults using formal financial services 2023: **76.5%** (up from 65% in 2017)
- National Financial Inclusion Framework: 2023–2028 (Third Edition)

### Capital Markets
- **DSE** (Dar es Salaam Stock Exchange) — single national exchange
- Bank of Tanzania (BoT): central bank and primary regulator
- **CMSA**: Capital Markets and Securities Authority

### Interest Rates (Q1 2025 — Budget Execution Report)
| Rate | Q1 2025 | Q1 2024 |
|------|---------|---------|
| Lending rate (average) | 15.14% | 15.36% |
| Time deposit rate | 8.64% | 8.11% |
| Negotiated deposit rate | 10.92% | 9.99% |

### Foreign Exchange Reserves
- Sept 2025: USD 6,664.2 million = **5.1 months** import cover
- Q1 2024: USD 5,413.6 million
- EAC convergence criteria: ≥ 4.5 months; Tanzania exceeds target

| Firms relying on own funds for fixed assets | 84% | — | — |
| Access to finance constraint ranking | **Highest** | — | — |
| Transport as obstacle 2023 | 9.4% | — | — |
| Transport as obstacle 2013 | 38.2% | — | — |

**Key observations:**
- Tanzania better than SSA average on power outages and corruption
- Tanzania worse on regulatory burden (tax meeting frequency)

- Transport improved dramatically (38% → 9% citing as obstacle)

## 9. Key Investment Opportunities (TIC 2025)

**Sectors prioritised in 2025:**
1. **Manufacturing** — largest sector by project count and capital; textile SEZ active
2. **Agriculture** — value chain development; TIC actively placing investors
3. **Energy** — renewable energy; government SEZ support
4. **Commercial real estate** — new land policy (2025) opens doors
5. **Economic infrastructure** — SGR, port expansion, logistics
6. **Tourism** — +35.6% visitor growth Q1 2025; service exports USD 1.78B/quarter
7. **Mining** — gold 42.5% of exports; sector growing 19% (Q1 2025)
8. **ICT** — 11.1% sector growth; COSTECH/HEET driving digital skills

**Honey processing** (example project): Commercial beehives + processing; export markets for honey along rural value chains.
**Cage fish farming** (example project): Lake Nyasa; cage aquaculture; export processed fish + ornamental species.
