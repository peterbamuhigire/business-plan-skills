# Kenya Credit Pricing 2025 — CBK Risk-Based Credit Pricing Model

## 1. What the Model Is and How It Works

The Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) published its **Revised Risk-Based Credit Pricing Model (RBCPM)** in August 2025. The model applies to all **variable-rate loans** (excluding foreign-currency loans and fixed-rate loans) issued by commercial banks. It came into effect for new loans on **1 September 2025**; existing variable-rate loans transition by **28 February 2026**.

### Core formula

```
Total Lending Rate = KESONIA (compounded in arrears) + Premium ("K")
Total Cost of Credit = KESONIA + Premium ("K") + Fees & Charges
```

**KESONIA** — Kenya Shilling Overnight Interbank Average — is a volume-weighted average of actual overnight interbank transactions, published daily by CBK at 9:00 a.m. It replaced the old overnight interbank average rate in name only; the methodology is unchanged. CBR (Central Bank Rate) serves as the fallback where KESONIA is unavailable.

### Premium ("K") components

Each bank defines its own "K", which must cover:

| Component | What it includes |
|---|---|
| Operating costs | Salaries, director remuneration, repairs, depreciation, occupancy, contract services, other overheads related to lending |
| Return to shareholders | Expected equity return from the lending book |
| Borrower risk premium | Customer-specific credit risk, based on a detailed qualitative and quantitative credit-scoring model |

Fees and charges (origination, arrangement, commitment, default, late payment) are disclosed separately within the **Annual Percentage Rate (APR)** and published on the Total Cost of Credit (TCC) website.

### KESONIA compounding mechanics

- Interest is compounded daily over the loan's interest period (compounding in arrears).
- A **5-day lookback period** allows banks time to calculate and advise the final interest amount before payment is due.
- CBK publishes both the daily KESONIA rate and a **KESONIA Compounded Index** (base = 100.00000000 on start date), enabling borrowers to independently verify interest.

**Worked example (January 2025, KES 10 million, 30-day tenor):**

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Principal | KES 10,000,000 |
| KESONIA 30-day compounded rate | 11.3953% |
| Accrued interest (30 days) | KES 93,660.24 |
| Principal + Interest | KES 10,093,660.24 |

## 2. What Changed in August 2025 vs the April 2025 Proposal

| Dimension | April 2025 Proposal | August 2025 Revised Model |
|---|---|---|
| Reference rate | CBR (policy rate) | KESONIA (overnight interbank average, market-based) |
| Rate name | Unnamed | KESONIA — aligned with SONIA (UK) and SOFR (US) |
| Compounding method | Not specified | Compounded in arrears (daily) |
| Lookback period | Not specified | 5 business days |
| Bank "K" submission | Submit to CBK for review/noting before rollout | Banks develop internally; CBK reviews post-implementation as surveillance |
| Transition for existing loans | 3 months | 6 months (28 February 2026) |
| Transparency | CBK website + two national newspapers | TCC website + monthly CBK submission |
| Excluded facilities | Not listed | Foreign-currency loans, fixed-rate loans |

**Key reason for shift to KESONIA:** Stakeholders (banks, IMF, EBRD) argued that the actual cost of bank funds is driven by deposit costs, not the policy rate — and that market-based overnight rates better reflect real funding costs and align with global benchmark reform (post-LIBOR transition).

### Rate trend summary

- Market average lending rate fell **150 bps** (16.90% → 15.44%) over Dec 2024 – May 2025, tracking CBR reductions.
- Cheapest lenders in May 2025: Citibank (10.36%), Stanbic (12.84%), Standard Chartered (13.35%) — typically serving large corporates.
- SME-accessible banks cluster at **15.44%–16.90%** (KCB, Co-op, NCBA, Family Bank, Equity).
- Highest-rate banks (19–20%): Credit Bank, Middle East Bank, Access Bank — these serve higher-risk or niche segments.
- Average deposit rate: **8.70%** in May 2025 — giving banks a gross spread of approximately **6.7 percentage points** over the market average.

## 4. Implications for Business Plans Seeking Kenyan Bank Finance

### Understanding the rate you will be quoted

Under RBCPM, your loan rate = **KESONIA + K (your bank's spread)**. As of early 2025, KESONIA tracks close to the CBR. The premium "K" is not disclosed individually per borrower, but must be published by your bank on the TCC website at product level.

| Factor that raises your risk premium | Effect |
|---|---|
| Short operating history (<2 years) | Higher K — lender has limited cash-flow track record |
| Informal/unaudited financials | Higher K or refusal — credit-scoring model penalises data gaps |
| Unsecured or thin collateral | Higher K — compensates for recovery risk |
| Sector concentration risk | Higher K — single-sector exposure |
| Existing NPL or CRB listing | Likely decline |
| Strong audited accounts, 12-month statements, title deed security | Lower K — closer to prime rate |

### Practical benchmarks for projections

| Borrower type | Expected rate range (2025) |
|---|---|
| Top-tier corporate, well-secured | 10–13% |
| Mid-market SME, audited accounts, good collateral | 13–16% |
| Growth SME, partial collateral | 16–18% |
| Micro/emerging business, unsecured | 18–20%+ |

**Use 16–17% as the base assumption for a Kenyan SME business plan** unless the promoter has a strong existing relationship and security to support a lower rate. Apply a stress scenario at 18–19% (see `meta-financial-stress-test`).

### Debt Service Cover Ratio (DSCR) discipline

Regardless of the rate quoted, lenders require DSCR ≥ 1.25× (see `11-funding-request/SKILL.md`). At 16% over 5 years, a KES 5 million loan costs approximately KES 121,000/month. Ensure projected monthly net operating income covers this with a 25% margin.

### Total Cost of Credit (TCC) website

The CBK/KBA TCC portal (www.costofcredit.co.ke) allows borrowers to compare loan costs across banks. All banks are required to publish their weighted average lending rates, weighted average K, fees, and APR monthly. Verify figures there before selecting a lender.
