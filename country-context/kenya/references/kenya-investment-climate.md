# Kenya Investment Climate — Detailed Reference

## 1. Foreign Investment Framework

### Openness to FDI
- Foreign investors receive **same treatment as domestic investors** under Investment Promotion Act 2004
- **KenInvest** (Kenya Investment Authority) — official one-stop shop for investment facilitation; helps navigate regulations and licensing
- National Investment Promotion Strategic Plan 2023–2027: target to grow FDI from ~USD 500M (2022) to **USD 10 billion by 2027**
- World Bank MIGA member — insures FDI against non-commercial risk

### Investment Threshold for Incentives
- Minimum: **USD 100,000** qualifying investment for investment certificate benefits (including expatriate entry permits)

### Sector-Specific Ownership Restrictions

| Sector | Restriction |
|--------|-------------|
| Mineral dealership | 60% Kenyan citizens minimum |
| Private security firms | 25% Kenyan minimum (Private Security Regulations Act 2016) |
| Construction (foreign contractors) | 30% subcontracted to local firms; local skills transfer required |
| Insurance companies | Foreign capital max: two-thirds; no single person >25% |
| ICT companies | 30% domestic equity requirement removed (August 2023) |
| Agricultural land | Foreign ownership prohibited; leasehold ≤99 years (requires Presidential exemption) |
| Mining companies | Must be registered/established in Kenya |

## 2. Tax Incentives and Special Zones

### Export Processing Zones (EPZs)
- 101 EPZs as of end 2022; 170 companies; 75,598 workers
- EPZ exports contribute ~KES 111.4 billion (~USD 863M) to economy
- **Benefits:**
  - 10-year corporate tax holiday; 25% rate for next 10 years
  - 10-year withholding tax holiday
  - Stamp duty exemption
  - 100% tax deduction on initial investment over 20 years
  - VAT and customs duty exemption on industrial inputs
- 53% of EPZ products exported to US under AGOA; 80% of Kenya's textiles/apparel from EPZs
- 39% of EPZ companies fully foreign-owned (mainly Sri Lanka, India)

### Special Economic Zones (SEZs)
- SEZs in 14 counties: Dongo Kundu/Mombasa (3,000 acres), Lamu (4,136 acres), Kisumu (10,000 acres), Naivasha (1,000 acres), Konza Technopolis (5,000 acres) + private designations
- **Benefits:**
  - VAT exemption on all SEZ-produced goods and services
  - Corporate tax: 10% (first 10 years from licence date), 15% (next 10 years) — reduced from 30%
  - Exemption from customs/excise, Income Tax Act provisions, stamp duty
  - Exemption from county-level advertisement and licence fees
  - Customs duty exemption on goods/services within SEZ (2024 amendment)
- **Key change (2024):** SEZ incentive duration capped at 10 years from licence issuance (Business Laws Amendment Act, December 2024)

### Nairobi International Financial Centre (NIFC)
- Preferential capital gains tax: **5%** (reduced from 15%) for NIFC-certified firms investing ≥KES 3 billion for ≥5 years
- VAT exemption on continuing business operation sales
- Finance Bill 2025: new tax benefits for NIFC-certified entities (lower corporate rates, WHT exemptions on dividends)

## 3. Detailed Tax Framework

### Corporate Income Tax
- Standard rate: **30%**
- Manufacturing companies (outside SEZ/EPZ): 30%

### VAT
- Standard rate: **16%**
- VAT registration threshold: KES 5 million/year
- VAT refunds: must be submitted within 12 months of purchase; historically delayed but targeted for reform
- Manufacturing equipment: VAT exemption under EAC Common External Tariff Section 84
- Tourist hotel construction: zero-rated VAT on qualifying materials (NB: National Treasury approval required)

### Capital Gains Tax
- Standard: **15%**
- SEZ/NIFC-certified: **5%** (from 2024)
- Property in SEZ: exempt (Finance Act 2023)

### Digital Economy Tax
- Previous: 1.5% digital services tax
- Current: **3% significant economic presence tax** on non-residents deriving income from digital marketplace services in Kenya (Tax Laws Amendment Act, December 2024)

### Minimum Top-Up Tax
- Multinational subsidiaries with consolidated group turnover ≥USD 810M (€750M): minimum top-up tax to ensure effective rate ≥15% (OECD global minimum tax alignment)

### PAYE (Pay-As-You-Earn)
*Per KRA guidelines — subject to annual Finance Act amendments*

| Monthly income (KES) | Rate |
|---------------------|------|
| 0 – 24,000 | 10% |
| 24,001 – 32,333 | 25% |
| 32,334 – 500,000 | 30% |
| 500,001 – 800,000 | 32.5% |
| Above 800,000 | 35% |
| Personal relief | KES 2,400/month |

### Withholding Tax (standard rates)

| Category | Resident | Non-resident |
|----------|---------|--------------|
| Dividends | 5% | 15% |
| Management/professional fees | 5% | 20% |
| Royalties | 5% | 20% |
| Rent (commercial) | 10% | 30% |
| Rent (residential) | 7.5% | 30% |
| Interest (banks) | 0-5% | 15% |
| Consultancy fees | 5% | 20% |

### Railway Development Levy
- Applied to most imports; exceptions for official aid-funded projects, diplomatic missions, Privileges and Immunities Act listed organisations

## 4. Business Registration Process

**eCitizen portal** (ecitizen.go.ke) — primary access point for all government services

**Steps to register a business:**
1. Check name availability and reserve on eCitizen
2. Register company through Business Registration Service (BRS) on eCitizen
3. Obtain Tax PIN / TIN from KRA (iTax)
4. Register with Social Health Authority (SHA) — Taifa Care (replaced NHIF Oct 2024)
5. Register with NSSF (National Social Security Fund)
6. Obtain business permit from County Government (e.g., Nairobi City County)
7. Sector-specific licences (KEBS for food; KEPHIS for agriculture; CBK for financial services; etc.)

**Key reform:** Companies (Amendment) Act 2017 eliminated requirements for small enterprises to hire company secretaries, have lawyers register firms, or hold AGMs.

**KenTrade InfoTrade portal** (kentrade.go.ke) — documents import/export requirements by product.

## 5. Trade Agreements and Market Access

| Agreement | Status | Key benefit |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| **EAC Customs Union** | Active | Preferential trade among 8 members; free movement of goods, services, capital |
| **AfCFTA** | Active trading | Kenya among first to ratify; exports tea, soaps, soda ash, honey to Ghana, Nigeria, South Africa |
| **EU-Kenya EPA** | In force (July 2024) | EU Kenya's top export destination (14%+ of exports); EUR 3.4B bilateral trade (2023); +13% in 2024 |
| **US-Kenya STIP** | Negotiating (8th round Sep 2024) | First-of-kind bilateral US-sub-Saharan Africa trade partnership; covers agriculture, customs, environment, labour |
| **AGOA** | Expires Sep 2025 | USD 567M exports to US (2024; textiles dominant); Trump 10% tariff imposed April 2025 erodes benefits |
| **UAE-Kenya CEPA** | Active (signed Jan 2025) | USD 3.1B bilateral non-oil trade (9 months 2024, +29.1%); Comprehensive Economic Partnership |
| **UK-Kenya EPA** | Ratified March 2021 | Post-Brexit bilateral trade continuity |
| **COMESA FTA** | Active | 21 member states; COMESA Competition Commission enforcement applies |
| **PAPSS** | Active | Pan African Payments and Settlement System — real-time cross-border African transactions in local currencies |
| **US bilateral investment** | No BIT with US | Investment protection through MIGA; ICSID dispute resolution |

**Mombasa Port** — gateway to EAC; handles ~500 million consumer market across East and Central Africa.

## 6. Financial Sector Detail

### Banking Sector (2024)
- 39 commercial banks; 14 microfinance banks; 10 foreign bank representative offices; 74 forex bureaus
- 37 banks privately owned; 2 with majority government ownership
- Banking sector total net assets: **KES 7.6 trillion** (~USD 59 billion, December 2024)
- Gross NPL ratio: **16.4%** (elevated — banks cautious on SME lending)
- Average lending rate: **16.89%** (loans and advances maximum 2024)
- CBR: **11.25%** (December 2024)

### Capital Markets
- NSE (Nairobi Securities Exchange) — best performing in Africa in 2024 (MSCI Emerging and Frontier Markets Index)
- NSE derivatives market launched July 2019
- Market cap: KES 1.94 trillion (2024)
- Venture capital: USD 638M raised 2024; Kenya #1 in Africa; attracted 67% of total Africa energy/environment finance (USD 537M)
- CDSC (Central Depository and Settlement Corporation) for electronic settlement

### Mobile Money
- M-Pesa (Safaricom): processes more transactions in Kenya annually than Western Union globally
- Financial inclusion: ~80% of adults have access to financial services through conventional or mobile banking
- Regional interoperability: Kenyan diaspora can transact from abroad

### Movable Property as Collateral
- Movable Property Security Rights Act 2017: enables use of intellectual property rights as collateral for financing

## 7. Intellectual Property

| Right | Registering body |
|-------|----------------|
| Trade marks | KIPI (Kenya Industrial Property Institute) |
| Patents | KIPI |
| Copyright | KECOBO (Kenya Copyright Board) |
| Anti-counterfeit enforcement | ACA (Anti-Counterfeit Authority) |
| Regional filing | ARIPO (Harare Protocol on Patents and Industrial Designs) |

**Kenya IP Authority Bill 2025:** Proposes to merge KIPI, KECOBO, and ACA into single Intellectual Property Office of Kenya. Cabinet approved merger January 2025.

**TRIPS Agreement:** Kenya is a signatory; minimum IP protection standards enforced.

**AI Strategy:** Draft National AI Strategy (January 2025) proposes Kenya as regional hub for AI research, model development, scalable innovation; covers agriculture, healthcare, security, education, public service.

## 8. Labour Law Summary

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Primary law | Employment Act 2007 (Cap. 226) |
| Annual leave | 21 working days minimum |
| Maternity leave | 3 months (paid) |
| Paternity leave | 2 weeks |
| Notice period | 28 days minimum |
| Redundancy/severance | 15 days pay per year of service |
| Working hours | 52 hours/week maximum (including overtime) |
| Employment contract | Required for all employees (written, 3 months+) |
| Discrimination | Prohibited on various grounds (2010 Constitution) |
| EAC nationals | No work permit required (EAC Common Market) |

## 9. Key Business Risks Summary

| Risk | Level | Detail |
|------|-------|--------|
| Public debt burden | High | USD 81B; 60% of tax revenue to debt service; limited development spending |
| Political risk | Medium-High | June–Aug 2024 protests forced Finance Bill withdrawal; election cycles (2027) |
| Corruption | Medium | TI CPI rank 121/180 (2024); below regional average; judiciary delays |
| NPL ratio | Medium | 16.4% gross NPL (2024); banks cautious; SME lending constraints |
| FATF grey list | Medium | Added Feb 2024; new AML law June 2025; compliance burden on financial transactions |
| Tax policy volatility | Medium | Annual Finance Acts; reversals under political pressure; AGOA uncertainty |
| SEZ incentive horizon | Low-Medium | Now capped at 10 years (2024 amendment) |
| Land rights | Medium | 70% untitled; leasehold complications; county interference in renewals |
| Exchange rate | Low-Medium | KES gained 21% 2024; but 2023 saw 25% depreciation — high volatility possible |
| Infrastructure | Low | 90% renewable electricity; reliable grid; good road network; JKIA aviation hub |
| Digital services tax | New risk | 3% significant economic presence tax on non-resident digital services providers (2024) |
