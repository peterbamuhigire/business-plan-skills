# Kenya Bank Loan Standards — Application Requirements

Reference forms: Family Bank Business Loan Application Form; NCBA Business Banking Loan Application Form. Both are regulated by the Central Bank of Kenya.

---

## 1. Standard Business Loan Application Sections

Kenyan banks apply a consistent structure across business loan applications. The sections below are common to both Family Bank and NCBA:

| Section | What it covers |
|---|---|
| Business Details | Legal identity, registration, ownership structure, location, operating history |
| Loan Request | Facility type, amount, tenure, purpose, existing borrowings |
| Shareholders / Directors | Ownership, ID, designation, sector experience |
| Key Management | Education, experience, tenure with business |
| Financial Information | Balance sheet, P&L, cash-flow projection |
| Banking Relationship | Account history, services used, other bank relationships |
| Credit History | All existing facilities — business and personal |
| Security Offered | Type, description, registered owner, estimated value |
| References | Character, supplier, customer, landlord references |
| Documentation Checklist | Specific documents to attach |

| Business premises status | Yes (owned / rented / leased) | Yes (own / rented) |

| Borrowing powers confirmation | Yes | — |

|---|---|

| Education level | Highest qualification |

## 4. Financial Information Required

### Statements and periods

| Document | Family Bank | NCBA |
|---|---|---|
| Audited accounts (balance sheet + P&L) | 3 most recent years | 3 consecutive accounting periods |
| Bank statements | 6 months (if banking elsewhere) | 6 months (other banks) |
| Cash-flow projections | Yes | Yes (with underlying assumptions) |
| Latest management accounts | — | Yes (if applicable) |
| Aged debtors schedule | Yes (list + ageing) | Yes (if applicable) |
| Aged creditors schedule | Yes (list + ageing) | Yes (if applicable) |
| Current stock schedule | Yes (current value) | Implied |

### Financial statement fields (NCBA form — as minimum standard)

**Balance Sheet:**
- Assets: Cash, Stocks held, Trade Debtors outstanding, Fixed assets, Other
- Liabilities: Bank overdraft, Trade Creditors outstanding, Other Loans, Paid-up Capital
- Surplus / Deficit

**Profit and Loss Account:**
- Sales, Cost of Sales, Gross Profit
- Operating Expenses, Other Costs
- Net Profit before Tax, Tax Paid, Dividend Paid

**Cash-Flow Projection:**
- Projected Sales, Projected Expenses, Projected Operating Profit
- Period to be stated; assumptions to be documented

## 5. Collateral and Security Documentation Standards

### Types of security accepted

| Security type | Documentation required |
|---|---|
| Freehold / leasehold property | Nature (shop, house, factory, farm), freehold or leasehold, lease terms and rent, owner-occupied or let, price paid on acquisition, current market value, amount and date of existing charge (if any), estimated valuation date and valuer details |
| Other securities (vehicles, plant, equipment) | Description, price paid and acquisition date, estimated market value, amount and date of charge (if held) |
| Motor vehicle (logbook) | Copy of logbook — NCBA checklist |
| Standby Letter of Credit (SBLC) | — NCBA checklist |
| Cash cover | — NCBA checklist |

**Valuation:** A current independent valuation report is required or must be arranged. Both forms ask for "estimated market value"; a professional valuation is expected for property security.

### Property security fields (Family Bank detail)

- Address of property
- Freehold or leasehold designation; if leasehold: terms and rent
- Whether third-party security: yes/no; if yes, address of third party
- Price paid on acquisition and acquisition date
- Current market value
- Amount and date of any existing charge

## 6. Banking Relationship and Credit History

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Account number with applying bank | Required |
| Date account opened | Required |
| Existing facilities with applying bank | Account number, approved limit, balance (debit and credit), duration |
| Other bank accounts | Account number, account name, bank, branch |
| Existing loans (all lenders) | Name of lender, date granted, initial amount, current balance, monthly instalment |
| NCBA format for credit history | Lender name, type of loan, credit limit (KES '000), outstanding balance, monthly repayment, maturity date, interest rate %, type and value of security pledged |

## 7. References Required

Family Bank requires up to six references of specified types:

| Reference type | Who provides it |
|---|---|
| Relative | A family member who knows the applicant |
| Supplier | Business supplier confirming trading relationship |
| Customer | Business customer confirming trading relationship |
| Landlord | Property landlord confirming tenancy |
| Peer | Business peer or community leader |
| Other | Any additional reference |

Each reference: full name, address, telephone number.

## 8. Practical Documentation Checklist — Business Plan Appendix

Use this checklist when preparing the loan documentation appendix of a Kenyan business plan. Tick items before submission.

### A. Business legal documents

- [ ] Certificate of Incorporation or Business Name Registration Certificate
- [ ] Memorandum and Articles of Association (limited companies) or Partnership Deed
- [ ] Company PIN Certificates (KRA)
- [ ] CR12 / Latest Annual Returns (limited companies)
- [ ] Confirmation that borrowing is within the company's and directors' powers

### B. Financial documents

- [ ] Audited financial statements — 3 consecutive years (balance sheet + P&L)
- [ ] Latest management accounts (if audited accounts are more than 6 months old)
- [ ] Cash-flow projection with documented assumptions
- [ ] Bank statements — last 6 months (from all banks, if not banking with the applying bank)
- [ ] Aged debtors schedule with ageing periods
- [ ] Aged creditors schedule with ageing periods
- [ ] Current stock valuation schedule

### C. Collateral and security documents

- [ ] Copy of title deed(s) for property security
- [ ] Independent valuation report on property (current — within 12 months)
- [ ] Copy of logbook(s) for vehicle security
- [ ] Any existing charge documentation on security assets

### D. Director / shareholder / owner documents

- [ ] National ID copies for all directors, shareholders, and guarantors
- [ ] Valid passport and work permit (foreign nationals)
- [ ] Personal details for each director: occupation, address, education, dependants

### E. Business profile documents

- [ ] Detailed business profile: history, current activities, future plans
- [ ] CV / profile of directors and key management (experience and qualifications)
- [ ] SWOT analysis (recommended by NCBA; expected by most lenders)
- [ ] Products/services list, major markets, suppliers, customers, competitors

### F. Loan-specific documents

- [ ] Completed, signed loan application form
- [ ] Sketch map of business premises location
- [ ] Copies of active business contracts (if loan is tied to contracts)
- [ ] Letters of offer from other lenders (if refinancing)

### G. Insurance confirmation

- [ ] Stock insurance certificate and insured amount
- [ ] Machinery / equipment insurance certificate
- [ ] Business premises insurance certificate
- [ ] Professional indemnity insurance (if applicable — lawyers, doctors, stockbrokers)

### H. Project finance (construction) — additional items

- [ ] Project team details (main contractor, architects, QS, engineers)
- [ ] Approved architectural plans and structural drawings
- [ ] Bill of Quantities (BQ); secondary BQ if project is ongoing
- [ ] Project feasibility report
- [ ] Project timetable and works schedule
- [ ] County government approvals
- [ ] NEMA environmental approvals
- [ ] National Construction Authority (NCA) approvals
- [ ] Client contribution details and proof of source

## 9. Excluded / Restricted Business Activities

Both Family Bank and NCBA apply an exclusion list (aligned with IFC environmental and social standards). Businesses in the following categories are ineligible for standard bank finance:

- Production or trade in items illegal under Kenyan law or international conventions
- Weapons and munitions
- Alcoholic beverages (non-beer/wine), tobacco (where the activity is a primary business)
- Gambling and casinos
- Radioactive materials (outside regulated medical/measurement use)
- Unbonded asbestos fibres
- Drift-net marine fishing (nets exceeding 2.5 km)
- Forced labour or harmful child labour
- Commercial logging in primary tropical moist forest
- Non-sustainably managed forestry products
- Large-scale transport or storage of hazardous chemicals
- Activities affecting Indigenous Peoples' lands without documented consent
- Pornography or prostitution
- Racist or anti-democratic media
