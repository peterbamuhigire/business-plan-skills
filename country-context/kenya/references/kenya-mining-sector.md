# Kenya Mining Sector Investment Guide

**Source:** Kenya Mining Investment Handbook, Ministry of Mining, Blue Economy and Maritime Affairs (2024–25)

**Currency Context:** KES 135/$1 (2025); Current tax regime: 30% corporate income tax, 16% VAT, 10%+ withholding taxes on dividends/interest

---

## 1. SECTOR OVERVIEW & ECONOMIC CONTRIBUTION

### Current Contribution & Growth Targets

- **GDP Contribution (2019):** 0.7% (mining and quarrying combined)
- **Vision 2030 Target:** Increase to 4–10% by 2030
- **Strategic Importance:** Focus area for economic development and regional industrialisation

### Employment & Workforce

- **Formal Sector Workers:** 15,900+ (2019)
- **Small-Scale & Artisanal Mining:** 100,000+ directly employed
- **Indirect Employment:** Thousands of supply-chain jobs (e.g., Base Titanium sourced USD 100M+ annually from 380+ Kenyan suppliers)
- **Skill Development:** Higher education institutions now offering undergraduate and postgraduate mining engineering, mineral processing, and petroleum engineering programmes

### Export Performance

- **Key Export Minerals:** Iron and steel, ilmenite, rutile, zircon, soda ash, copper, gold
- **Copper Exports (Growth Trajectory):**
  - 2021: 2,270 tonnes (USD 1.13M; USD 500/tonne)
  - 2022: 7,250 tonnes (USD 1.76M; USD 970/tonne)
  - 2023: 8,120 tonnes (USD 2.33M; USD 290/tonne implied)
- **Gold (Artisanal & Small-Scale):** ~5 metric tonnes annually; estimated value USD 250 million
- **Titanium Sands (Base Titanium, decade of operation):** 5 million tonnes (rutile, ilmenite, zircon); total value ~USD 2 billion

### Infrastructure Impact

Mining projects stimulate infrastructure development in underserved regions:
- Roads and access networks
- Power generation and transmission
- Water and port facilities
- Training and skills hubs

## 2. KEY MINERALS & DEPOSITS

### A. INDUSTRIAL & NON-METALLIC MINERALS

#### Soda Ash
- **Status:** Major global producer
- **Source:** Lake Magadi
- **Use:** Chemical manufacturing, glass production
- **Export Profile:** Significant contributor to national mineral exports

#### Titanium (Ilmenite, Rutile, Zircon)
- **Status:** Largest and most successful recent mining project (Base Titanium, 2011–2024)
- **Locations:** Kwale Mineral Sands Project (coastal); wider deposits in Kilifi-Kwale sedimentary basins
- **Production (Base Titanium, Kwale Operation):**
  - Initial Investment: USD 350 million
  - Peak Employment: 1,600 (99% Kenyan; 70%+ from Kwale County)
  - Total 10-Year Output: 5 million tonnes
  - Estimated Value: USD 2 billion
  - Export: First shipment February 2014 via Likoni Port
- **Sustainability Record:** Internationally recognised for safety, transparency, environmental management
  - 500+ hectares rehabilitated by closure
  - 1.3 million indigenous trees planted (largest East Africa tree nursery by species)
- **Economic Impact:**
  - Built lasting infrastructure: 132 kV power line (16 km), 8.6 million m³ dam, 8 km access road, port facility, training centre
  - Community Development Agreement (CDA) initiatives:
    - Kwale PAVI Farmers' Cooperative: 3,000 farmers trained in financial literacy and agribusiness
    - 4,000 students supported via scholarships and bursaries
    - SS Assad FC (national premier league) mentorship programme
    - Elders Welfare Programme (medical support, social inclusion via Red Cross Kenya/Equity Bank partnership)
  - Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) programme: 180 employees gained formal technical certifications

#### Limestone
- **Status:** Critical for Kenya's vibrant cement manufacturing industry
- **Distribution:** Multiple locations across country
- **Use:** Cement production, construction materials

#### Fluorspar (Fluorite)
- **Status:** Major mine suspended operations in recent years; revival potential exists
- **Geography:** Western Kenya locations
- **Use:** Steel manufacturing, chemical industry, refrigerants

#### Other Industrial Minerals
- **Diatomite, Gypsum, Salt, Silica Sand, Talc, Dolomite, Vermiculite, Baryte, Bentonite, Graphite**
- **Graphite (Emerging Opportunity):**
  - Locations: Chawia (Taita-Taveta), Samburu, Kitui, Turkana, Kamstoi, Tsavo
  - Geology: Disseminated flakes in meta-sedimentary gneisses (Mozambique Belt, Nyanzian, Kavirondian rocks)
  - Potential: Viable open-pit mining; crystalline graphite for batteries and lubricants
  - 2023 Nationwide Airborne Survey: High mineralization potential flagged; Turkana and Taita-Taveta recommended for ground truthing
  - Development Lag: Limited processing capacity and investment; resources remain underexplored

### B. METALLIC MINERALS

#### Gold

**Archean-Hosted (Western Kenya Greenstone Belts)**
- **Geography:** Migori, Kakamega, Vihiga, Siaya (Nyanzian Craton)
- **Geological Analog:** Similar settings to Canada's Abitibi and Tanzania's Lake Victoria Goldfields
- **Migori Gold Belt:**
  - Area: ~245 km² of Migori Greenstone Belt
  - Resources: Estimated JORC-compliant >1.2 million ounces
  - Status: Under exploration and feasibility studies in preparation for development
  - Activity: Exploratory drilling at high grades 8–12 g/t in some targets; strong commercial potential
- **Shanta Gold's West Kenya Project:**
  - Among Africa's highest-grading deposits
  - Resources: 14.05 million tonnes @ 1.56 g/t gold (705,000 ounces)
  - Status: Advanced exploration/feasibility reports available
- **ASGM Output:** ~5 metric tonnes annually; estimated value USD 250 million

**Orogenic-Hosted (Turkana & East Kenya)**
- **Geography:** Turkana, Marsabit, Isiolo, West Pokot (Mozambique Mobile Belt, Precambrian terranes)
- **Setting:** Ancient tectonic collisions and metamorphism (ideal for gold mineralization)
- **Activity:** ASGM documented in Lochoremoit, Namoruputh, Lokiriama, Ng'akoriyiek, Naduat
- **Recent Discoveries:** Marsabit, Isiolo, West Pokot counties

**Government Priority:**
- Central to Vision 2030 objective: Boost sector GDP contribution from <1% to 10% by 2030

#### Niobium (Columbium)

**Mrima Hills (Kwale County) — Flagship Project**
- **Geology:** Cretaceous age alkaline carbonatite intrusion; high prospective setting
- **Resource Estimate (Jul 2013, JORC-Compliant):**
  - **>1.0% Nb₂O₅ Cut-Off:**
    - Total Measured + Indicated: 5.8 million tonnes @ 1.41% Nb₂O₅ (181 million pounds)
    - Total Inferred: 17.5 million tonnes @ 1.41% (547 million pounds)
  - **>0.2% Nb₂O₅ Cut-Off:**
    - Total Measured + Indicated: 47.8 million tonnes @ 0.66% Nb₂O₅ (699 million pounds)
    - Total Inferred: 94.4 million tonnes @ 0.73% (1,525 million pounds)
- **Strategic Value:** Estimated total value KES 5.4–8.18 trillion (approximately USD 40–60 billion at 2025 exchange rates)
- **Associated Minerals:** Apatite, pyrochlore, barite, florencite, monazite, rutile, sphalerite, galena, goyazite, hematite, magnetite, anatase, ilmenite, pandaïte
- **Government Role:** NAMICO holds licensing interest; strategic opportunity for government to harness significant deposits

**Other REE/Niobium Prospects:**
- **Homa Mountain** (Western Kenya): Carbonatites with fluorite and light REEs; Rift Valley volcanism origin
- **Rangwa** (Homa Bay): Carbonatites with niobium and REE; Miocene-Pliocene rifting
- **Sukulu** (Eastern Rift): Phosphate and niobium; less explored
- **Unexplored:** Buru (Kericho), Ruri, Homa, Kuge (Homa Bay), Tharaka Nithi

#### Rare Earth Elements (REEs)

**Mrima Hills (Kwale County) — Primary Resource**
- **Resource Estimate (Jul 2013, JORC-Compliant):**
  - **>5.8% TREO Cut-Off:**
    - Total Measured + Indicated: 11.2 million tonnes @ 6.93% TREO; 0.78% CREO
    - Total Inferred: 15.6 million tonnes @ 7.12% TREO; 1.11% CREO
  - **>1.0% TREO Cut-Off:**
    - Total Measured + Indicated: 48.7 million tonnes @ 4.4% TREO; 0.91% CREO
    - Total Inferred: 110.7 million tonnes @ 3.61% TREO; 0.8% CREO
- **Strategic Importance:** Critical for battery, renewable energy, and advanced technology industries
- **Estimated Total Value:** KES 5.4–8.18 trillion (approximately USD 40–60 billion)

#### Copper

- **Status:** Early-stage exploration; no large-scale commercial mining yet
- **Locations:** Meru, Embu, Tharaka Nithi, Kitui, Kajiado, Samburu, Turkana, Isiolo
- **Geology:** Rift-related intrusions and metamorphic copper belts
- **Surface Grades:** Up to ~8% (highly promising but requires deeper exploration)
- **Activity:** Artisanal activity hints at significant potential
- **Market Drivers:** Global demand for copper in wiring and renewable energy (solar, wind, EV infrastructure)
- **Investment Path:** NAMICO holds Special Prospecting License; aiming for mining lease conversion as feasibility and reserve data emerge
- **Export Trajectory (2021–2023):**
  - Growing volume and expanding price range reflect rising early-stage market entry
  - Growth in export volumes suggest accelerating commercial exploration

**Investor Opportunity:**
- Partnerships and feasibility studies to tap untapped resources
- Early-mover advantage in emerging commodity

#### Iron Ore

**Geographic Distribution & Characteristics:**

| Location | County | Fe₂O₃ Content | Key Feature | Grade |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Kishushe** | Taita-Taveta | Magnetite >60% | 60 km from Mombasa-Nairobi Road; viable for steel production with beneficiation | High |
| **Marimanti** | Tharaka-Nithi | 41–57% | Ilmenite-rich ore; suitable for upgrading to industrial standards | Medium-High |
| **Samia** | Siaya | 18–25% | Lower-grade; requires beneficiation to reduce silica/alumina gangue | Lower |
| **Laikipia County** | Laikipia | Haematite, Laterite | Recent surveys (2020) identified significant deposits; transformative for industrialisation | Unquantified |
| **Mbaru** | West Pokot | >60% | Magnetite deposits; ore depths to 80m; significant potential | High |

**Global Competitiveness:**
- Deposits with grades 62–64% Fe comparable to international standards
- Potential to fuel local steel industries if infrastructure and investment grow

#### Other Metallic Minerals
- **Manganese, Chromite, Lead, Silver, Zinc, Cobalt:** Confirmed occurrences across various regions
- **Nickel-Chromite (Emerging):** 15 counties flagged by 2023 Nationwide Airborne Geophysical Survey for ground truthing verification
  - Locations: West Pokot, Samburu, Marsabit, Kitui, Turkana, Taita-Taveta
  - Geology: Nickel often occurs with copper and cobalt in mafic-ultramafic rocks; possibly linked to Proterozoic Mozambique Belt and EARS intrusions
  - Chromite noted in small deposits in ultramafic zones
  - Application: Battery technologies and renewable energy infrastructure

## 3. GEOLOGICAL SETTING & EXPLORATION POTENTIAL

### Tectonic Framework: East African Rift System (EARS)

**Geographic Context:**
- Kenya sits along EARS, a divergent boundary where the Somali and Nubian tectonic plates are pulling apart
- Rifting active for approximately 30 million years
- Spans from Red Sea to Mozambique; Kenya hosts the eastern branch

**Mineral Concentration Mechanism:**
- Intense volcanic activity and faulting driven by mantle upwelling
- Forms alkaline igneous rocks and carbonatites (REE/niobium sources)
- Metamorphic belts (e.g., Mozambique Mobile Belt, 600–500 million years old) concentrate base and precious metals

**Archean Greenstone Belts:**
- Host gold deposits (Western Kenya; e.g., Migori belt)
- Similar geological setting to world-class goldfields (Canada's Abitibi, Tanzania's Lake Victoria)

### Ongoing Geological Processes

- **Active Rifting & Weathering:** Expose and enrich mineral deposits, making them accessible for both small-scale and large-scale mining
- **Strategic Location:** Proximity to Mombasa and Lamu ports boosts economic viability of mining operations

### 4. GEOLOGICAL DATA COVERAGE & MAPPING

#### High-Resolution Airborne Geophysical Data

**Historical Surveys:**
- 1977: Terra Survey Ltd airborne geophysics
- 1982: Geosurvey International airborne geophysics
- **2018–2023: Kenya National Airborne Geophysics Survey (KNAGS) — High-Resolution Initiative**
  - Quality-driven; informed by complex geology
  - Data subjected to rigorous QC and processing procedures
  - Geophysical products suitable for interpretation at scale 1:20,000 (1 cm = 200 m on ground)

**KNAGS Parameter Specification:**
- Nominal Survey Height: 80 m
- Survey Line Spacing: 200 m
- Tie Line Spacing: 2,000 m
- Survey Speed: 260 km/h
- Data Sampling Rates: Magnetics 25 Hz; Radiometrics 2 Hz; GPS 10 Hz
- Figure of Merit (FOM): 0.4–1.2 nT

**Key Findings (KNAGS 2018–2023):**
- Identified **970 mineral anomalies** across Kenya
- Oil, gas, and coal deposits (Mui Basin) added to mineral prospects
- Vast unexplored potential confirmed; transformation in Kenya's exploration landscape

#### Geospatial Coverage

- **Landmass Mapped:** 80% of Kenya's total landmass has been geologically mapped
- **Available Data Products:**
  - Geological maps (catalogue at: https://eprocedures.investkenya.go.ke/)
  - Drill logs and reports
  - Borehole completion reports and groundwater data
  - Publicized termly reports from exploration and mining companies
  - Memoirs, bulletins, and other government publications

## 5. INVESTMENT LICENSING & PROCEDURES

### Mining License Types

**Large-Scale Operations (Commercial Mining):**
1. **Reconnaissance License** — Early-stage exploration
2. **Prospecting License** — Detailed mineral exploration
3. **Retention License** — Holding claims during feasibility/financing
4. **Mining License** — Full extraction rights (duration up to 25 years)

**Small-Scale Operations:**
1. **Prospecting Permits** — Limited-scope exploration
2. **Mining Permits** — Small-scale extraction

**Artisanal Mining:**
- **Artisanal Mining Permits**

### Application Requirements for Mining License

#### 1. Identity & Tax Compliance

**Documentation Required:**
- Registration Certificate (not more than 1 year old) for incorporated companies; or
- List of Partners for Business Name; or
- List of Members for Community-Based Organisations (CBOs); or
- List of Elected Officials for Cooperative Societies (with current membership roll)
- **Valid Tax Compliance Certificate** (mandatory)

#### 2. Technical & Financial Capacity

**Technical Ability Proof:**
- Curriculum vitae (CV) of key personnel
- List of owned equipment
- List of equipment to be hired/leased
- Operational experience and track record

**Financial Ability Proof:**
- Audited financial report (preferred); or
- 6-month bank statement; or
- Letter of bank guarantee; or
- Pre-arranged credit facility from financial institution

#### 3. Operational & Environmental Plans

**Mining Programme:**
- Detailed proposed mining programme showing all activities with corresponding budget estimates
- Feasibility study report prepared by competent expert
- Mine closure plan with cost estimation
- Environmental License (obtained separately from NEMA)

**Employment & Procurement:**
- Proposal for employment and training of Kenyan citizens detailing various cadres and annual budget
- Proposal for procurement of local goods and services with detailed descriptions and annual budget estimates

#### 4. Community & Land Engagement

**Land Requirements:**
- Land owner consent in prescribed form (required for all private/community land)
- For community land: Community Development Agreement (CDA) detailing:
  - Socially responsible investment proposals
  - Local employment and skills development
  - Revenue-sharing mechanisms

**Proposed License Term:**
- Duration not exceeding 25 years
- Renewable subject to performance and regulatory compliance

### Processing Timeline & Costs

- **Duration:** Processing timeline not explicitly stated in handbook; varies by license type
- **Fees:** Not specified in provided document; consult KenInvest or Mining Cadastre Office for current fee schedule

### Royalty Payment Framework

**Mineral Royalties:**
- Prescribed by Mining Act, 2016
- Calculated on gross value of sales (mineral-specific rates)
- **Distribution:**
  - 70% to National Government
  - 20% to County Government
  - 10% to Local Community (where mining occurs)

## 6. TAX INCENTIVES & INVESTMENT BENEFITS

### A. GENERAL MINING TAX INCENTIVES

#### Capital Allowances
- **100% capital deduction** over 4 years on machinery, buildings, and equipment
- **Accelerated depreciation** supports capital-intensive mining projects
- Applies to exploration and development phase expenditure

#### Investment Deduction Allowance (IDA)
- **Up to 150% deduction** on qualifying capital expenditure
- **Higher deductions** for projects in underdeveloped areas (regional development incentive)
- Supports initial phase investment absorption

#### Withholding Tax Reductions
- **Interest payments to foreign lenders:** Reduced rates (aligned with Double Taxation Treaties)
- **Specialized service fees:** Reduced withholding tax
- Enhances attractiveness for foreign investors and project financiers

#### Loss Carry-Forward
- **Tax losses carried forward for up to 10 years** to offset future profits
- Supports projects with long payback periods or cyclical commodity exposure

#### Import Duty & VAT Exemptions
- **Duty exemptions** on specialized mining equipment
- **VAT exemptions** on inputs for exploration and development
- Lowers capex for equipment-intensive operations

### B. EXPORT PROCESSING ZONES (EPZ) & SPECIAL ECONOMIC ZONES (SEZ) INCENTIVES

**Note:** Mining companies may qualify for EPZ/SEZ status if oriented toward export-oriented production.

#### Comparative Tax Regime Table

| **Tax Element** | **EPZ Regime** | **SEZ Regime** | **Standard Kenya Corporate** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **VAT (16%)** | Exemption on raw materials, machinery, inputs; zero-rated supply TO zone | Same exemption; zero-rated supply TO zone | Standard 16% VAT applies; no exemptions for mining |
| **Import Duty** | None on basic raw materials; VAT on machinery | None on basic raw materials, machinery | Standard duty applied (varies by HS code) |
| **Corporate Income Tax** | Exempt first 10 years from first sale; 25% next 10 years; 30% thereafter | 10% first 10 years; 15% next 10 years; 30% thereafter | Standard 30% corporate income tax |
| **Withholding Tax** | 10-year holiday on payments to non-residents; thereafter: Interest 15%, Dividend 10%, Management Fees 20%, Rent (immovable) 30%, Rent (movable) 15%, Royalties 20% | No withholding holiday; Interest 5%, Dividend 15%, Mgmt Fees 5%, Rent (immovable) 10%, Rent (movable) 10%, Royalties 5% | Standard rates: Interest ~15%, Dividend 15%, Mgmt Fees 20%, Royalties 20% |
| **Investment Deduction Allowance** | 50% year 1 of capex + 25% residual per year (max 100% on buildings/machinery) | 50% year 1 + 25% residual per year | Standard 50% + 25% residual |
| **Import Declaration Fee** | 3.5% normally; **Exempt for EPZ** | **Exempt for SEZ** | Standard 3.5% applies |
| **Stamp Duty** | Normally 1–4%; **Exempt for EPZ** | **Exempt for SEZ** | Standard rates apply |
| **Railway Development Levy** | 2.5% normally; **Applicable** | **Applicable** | Standard 2.5% |

#### Non-Fiscal Incentives (EPZ & SEZ)

1. **Single Unified License** — Full operation under one license; streamlined compliance
2. **Fast-Track Approval** — Project approval and licensing within 30 days
3. **One-Stop Shop Service** — Facilitation and after-care services in single location
4. **Ready-Made Infrastructure:**
   - Factory buildings
   - Water, sewerage, electricity supply
   - Landscaping and waste management
5. **Security & Compliance:**
   - 24-hour security
   - Illuminated perimeter fences
   - On-site customs documentation (streamlined border procedures)
6. **Work Permit Facilitation** — Up to 20% of full-time employees (for SEZ)

**Strategic Advantage:** SEZ regime more favorable for mining due to lower withholding taxes on dividends (15% vs. 10% EPZ), interest (5% vs. 15% EPZ), and royalties (5% vs. 20% EPZ).

## 7. INVESTOR PROTECTIONS & DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### Constitutional & Legislative Protections

#### Foreign Investments Protection Act (FIPA)
- **Guarantees against expropriation** of investment by government
- Protects investments by foreign nationals and entities
- Provides legal recourse in case of unlawful seizure

#### Explicit Constitutional Protection
- Kenya Constitution 2010 explicitly protects **private property rights**
- Enforced through independent judiciary

### International Investment Guarantees

#### ICSID Membership
- Kenya is a member of **International Centre for Settlement of Investment Disputes (ICSID)**
- Signatory rights for investment dispute arbitration
- Neutral forum for investor-state disputes

#### MIGA (Multilateral Investments Guarantee Agency)
- Kenya is a member of **MIGA**
- Provides **political risk insurance** guarantees to:
  - Private sector investors
  - Foreign lenders
  - Member country enterprises
- Covers risks such as currency inconvertibility, expropriation, political violence, breach of contract

#### ATIA (Africa Trade Insurance Agency)
- Kenya is a member of **ATIA**
- Provides:
  - Export credit insurance
  - Political risk insurance
  - Investment insurance
- Lowers risk and cost of doing business across Africa

### Foreign Exchange & Profit Repatriation

#### No Foreign Exchange Controls
- **Full repatriation of profits** permitted (upon tax compliance)
- **Repatriation of capital** and interests allowed
- No restrictions on currency conversion for legitimate business transactions

#### Tax Compliance Requirement
- Repatriation requires compliance with Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) tax regulations
- Withholding taxes apply per income type (dividend, interest, royalties, management fees)
- Compliance certified by licensed tax agent or KRA endorsement

### Land Ownership & Access

#### Non-Citizen Land Tenure
- **Leasehold only** for non-citizens (no freehold ownership)
- **Lease term:** Maximum 99 years
- **Registration:** Ministry of Lands responsible for registration of interests

#### Land Transfer Procedures
- Prescribed procedures for leasehold transfers
- Registration requirements, fees, and timelines available at: https://lands.go.ke/

#### Community & Private Land Access
- **Private land:** Owner consent required (prescribed form)
- **Community land:** Community consent via Land Act 2012 and Community Land Act 2016
- **Public land:** National/County government approval via National Land Commission

## 8. ENVIRONMENTAL, SOCIAL & GOVERNANCE (ESG) FRAMEWORK

### Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) Requirement

**Mandatory Process:**
- EIA must be concluded per Environment (Impact Assessment and Audit) Regulations, 2003
- No project implementation permitted without completed EIA
- Regulated by **National Environment Management Authority (NEMA)**
- Reference: http://www.nema.go.ke/

**Mine Closure Plan:**
- Cost estimation and environmental rehabilitation plan required for all mining licenses
- Long-term liability accounting (e.g., Base Titanium rehabilitated 500+ hectares and planted 1.3M trees)

### Occupational Health & Safety

**Regulatory Basis:**
- Occupational Safety and Health Act, 2007
- Mining Act, 2016 (occupational compliance provisions)
- Compliance with international best practices expected

### Community Engagement & Social Responsibility

#### Community Development Agreements (CDAs)
- **Mandatory requirement** for all mining licenses (especially large-scale)
- Elements typically include:
  - **Local employment:** Preference for local workforce and skills development
  - **Skills transfer:** Training programmes and career pathways
  - **Community development:** Infrastructure, health, education, livelihood support
  - **Revenue sharing:** Direct community benefit from royalties
  - **Grievance mechanisms:** Community complaint and resolution procedures

#### Base Titanium Example (Kwale Operations, 2011–2024)
- **Employment:** 1,600 peak (99% Kenyan; 70%+ from Kwale County)
- **Skills Development:** 180 employees gained formal technical certifications (RPL programme)
- **Farmer Cooperative:** Kwale PAVI Farmers' Cooperative trained 3,000 farmers in financial literacy and agribusiness
- **Education:** 4,000 students benefited from scholarships and bursaries
- **Youth & Community:** SS Assad FC mentorship (national premier league); Elders Welfare Programme (Red Cross/Equity Bank partnership)
- **Environmental:** 500+ hectares rehabilitated; 1.3 million indigenous trees planted

#### Women in Mining: AWEIK Programme
- **Association for Women in Energy & Extractives in Kenya (AWEIK)**
- Established 2016; operates across 9 counties
- **Initiatives:**
  - **Mine-to-Market:** Gems Africa and Moyo Gems Market Days (direct buyer access for artisanal miners; ethical sourcing and traceability)
  - **Financial Inclusion:** 44+ Village Savings & Loans Associations (strengthening local economies, supply chain resilience)
  - **Value Addition:** Gemstone processing, branding, marketing, export readiness training (for artisanal/small-scale miners)
  - **Capacity Building:** 5,000+ artisanal miners trained in responsible mining, occupational health, gender-based violence (GBV) prevention
  - **Mentorship:** AWEIK Mentorship Programme connecting women across value chain with seasoned professionals

   - Migori belt: 1.2M+ ounces JORC resources; exploration-ready projects
   - Shanta Gold WK Project: 14.05M tonnes @ 1.56 g/t (705K oz); advanced feasibility
   - Government Target: Boost mining sector GDP from <1% to 10% by 2030 (gold-led)
   - Market Momentum: ASGM producing USD 250M/year; large-scale potential substantial

3. **Titanium/Heavy Mineral Sands (Kwale)**
   - Proven large-scale mining capability (Base Titanium model)
   - Supply chain expertise and local content ecosystem established
   - Export infrastructure (Likoni Port) operational
   - Additional deposits in Kilifi-Kwale untapped

#### Tier 2: Emerging Opportunities (5–10 Year Horizon)

4. **Copper (Meru, Embu, Tharaka Nithi, Eastern Rift)**
   - Surface grades to 8%; rift-related intrusions
   - NAMICO Special Prospecting License; mining lease conversion pathway
   - Global demand driver: EV batteries, renewable infrastructure
   - Investment Stage: Partnerships, feasibility studies

5. **Iron Ore (Multi-Region)**
   - Seven major deposits identified; grades 18–>60% Fe
   - Local steel industry demand growing
   - Potential to support industrialisation (Vision 2030)
   - Investment Stage: Detailed feasibility; infrastructure development

6. **Graphite (Taita-Taveta, Turkana)**
   - Battery technology surge (EV, energy storage)
   - 2023 KNAGS flagged high mineralization; ground-truthing underway
   - Development lag due to limited processing capacity; investment opportunity
   - Potential: Crystalline graphite for lithium batteries (global shortage)

#### Tier 3: Strategic Reserves (Long-Term Optionality)

7. **Lithium-Beryllium (Tharaka-Nithi, Marsabit, Samburu)**
   - Battery and renewable energy infrastructure critical materials
   - Identified deposits; limited exploration depth
   - Potential as battery cathode source if global supply tightens

8. **Rare Earth Elements beyond Mrima (Homa Mountain, Rangwa, Sukulu)**
   - Emerging deposits; less explored than Mrima
   - Lower risk of concentration risk (diversified sources)
   - Long-term strategic reserve value

## 12. BUSINESS PLANNING CONSIDERATIONS FOR MINING INVESTORS IN KENYA

### Market Entry & Due Diligence Checklist

**Phase 1: Regulatory Compliance (0–3 months)**
- Obtain geological data and historical reports (KenInvest/geophysical archives)
- Engage licensed tax agent and legal counsel (mining-experienced firms)
- Register company (eCitizen, KRA PIN, bank account setup)
- Preliminary EIA scoping (NEMA pre-submission consultation)

**Phase 2: Prospecting License Application (3–6 months)**
- Technical feasibility assessment by qualified engineer
- Financial capability proof (bank guarantee or pre-arranged credit)
- Land owner/community consent negotiations
- Prospecting permit or reconnaissance license application (via Mining Cadastre Office)

**Phase 3: Exploration & Feasibility (6–18 months)**
- Airborne geophysical surveys (leverage KNAGS data; supplement with targeted surveys)
- Drilling programs and resource estimation (JORC-compliant reports)
- Environmental and social baseline studies
- Preliminary feasibility study (PFS)

**Phase 4: Mining License Application (18–24 months)**
- Complete all 12 application requirement categories (see Section 5)
- Detailed Feasibility Study (DFS) by competent expert
- EIA approval from NEMA
- Community Development Agreement (CDA) finalization
- Mining license application to Mining Cadastre Office

**Phase 5: Project Finance & Development (24+ months)**
- Debt/equity financing (DFI interest: IFC, AfDB, Kenya government support)
- Infrastructure development (power, water, access roads)
- Supply chain engagement (leverage local supplier base)
- Workforce recruitment and training programs

### Financial Planning Considerations

#### Capital Expenditure (CapEx) Assumptions
- Exploration CapEx: 5–15% of total project cost (varies by deposit maturity)
- Mine development CapEx: 30–50% of total project cost (large-scale operations)
- Infrastructure & facilities: 15–25% (power, water, access roads, port facilities)
- Working capital: 10–20% (inventory, receivables, operational buffer)

**Tax Impact on CapEx:**
- 100% capital deduction over 4 years (mining equipment, buildings)
- 150% investment deduction allowance (certain capex)
- Accelerated depreciation (machinery)
- **Result:** Effective CapEx tax shield 25–35% of eligible expenditure

#### Operating Expenditure (OpEx) Assumptions
- Mining OpEx: 30–50% of revenue (varies by deposit, processing complexity, labor costs)
- Kenya labor cost advantage: Lower than South Africa, comparable to Tanzania
- VAT (16%) recovery possible on imported inputs (for licensed operations)
- Royalty payment: 0–5% of gross sales (mineral-dependent; distributed as 70% central, 20% county, 10% community)

**Wage Cost Benchmarks (Mining Sector, Kenya 2025):**
- Entry-level/artisanal: KES 200K–500K/month
- Semi-skilled operators: KES 500K–1.2M/month
- Technicians: KES 1.2M–2.5M/month
- Engineers/supervisors: KES 2.5M–5M+/month
- Expatriate specialists: KES 5M–10M+/month (subject to work permit and withholding taxes)

#### Revenue & Valuation Framework

**Valuation Multiples (Mining Sector, Kenya Context):**
- **Revenue Multiples:** 1–3× (depends on mine life, commodity price cycle, country risk)
- **EBITDA Multiples:** 4–8× (mature operations; 2–4× exploration-stage)
- **Discounted Cash Flow (DCF):** WACC 8–12% (Kenya country risk premium 4–6%, base rate 4–6%, leverage adjustment)

**Commodity Price Sensitivity:**
- Gold: High sensitivity (5–15% revenue change per 1% price move)
- REEs/Niobium: Very high sensitivity (strategic scarcity premium; 10–20% margin expansion potential)
- Base metals (copper, iron): Medium-high sensitivity (6–12% revenue change per 1% price move)
- Industrial minerals (soda ash, limestone): Medium sensitivity (3–8% revenue change per 1% price move)

**Tax Impact on Cash Flow:**
- Corporate income tax: 30% (standard); 10–15% in SEZ regime (first 10–20 years)
- Withholding taxes: 10–20% on dividends, interest, royalties (varies by recipient and treaty)
- Capital gains tax: Typically not applicable to mineral extraction (check current law)
- VAT recovery: 90–100% on export-oriented mining operations

## 14. RISKS & MITIGATION

### Regulatory & Political Risks

**Risk Factor:** Licensing delays, policy changes, community opposition
- **Mitigation:** Early engagement with KenInvest, KCM, and county governments; structured CDA process; transparent ESG reporting

**Risk Factor:** Foreign exchange controls on profit repatriation
- **Mitigation:** Kenya Constitution guarantees no FX controls; diversify currency exposure; use hedging instruments; maintain compliance with KRA

### Geological & Commodity Price Risks

**Risk Factor:** Reserve estimation uncertainty; commodity price volatility
- **Mitigation:** JORC-compliant resource estimates; staged development (reconnaissance → prospecting → mining); commodity hedging; diversify mineral portfolio

**Risk Factor:** Mining technology and cost inflation
- **Mitigation:** Leverage KNAGS geophysical data (reduces exploration risk); partner with experienced operators; establish supply chain stability agreements

### Environmental & Social Risks

**Risk Factor:** Environmental impact during mining; community relocation; cultural heritage disputes
- **Mitigation:** Independent ESIA (Environmental & Social Impact Assessment); early community engagement; CDA with grievance mechanisms; environmental bonding/rehabilitation fund

**Risk Factor:** Artisanal mining incursions on mining license areas
- **Mitigation:** Community benefit-sharing; formalisation support via AWEIK model; government security cooperation; local employment priority

### Country Risk

**Risk Factor:** Political instability, security concerns, infrastructure gaps
- **Mitigation:** MIGA political risk insurance; ICSID arbitration clause in contracts; diversified investor base; strong government relationships (KenInvest facilitation)
