# Lender Criteria — Uganda Banks, DFIs, and Microfinance Institutions

*Formal credit assessment criteria mapped to Ugandan lenders. Use during bankability scoring to verify that the business plan meets the specific standards of the target lender. Sources: RSM/Baker Tilly (Doing Business in Uganda, 2025), Uganda Development Bank lending guidelines, Bank of Uganda CBR December 2025, and credit-assessment-frameworks.md.*

---

## 1. Uganda Commercial Bank Landscape — Tier Summary

| Tier | Institutions | Minimum Loan | Typical Rate | Collateral Required |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — Large international** | Stanbic, ABSA, Standard Chartered, Citibank | UGX 50M+ | 19–22% p.a. | Land title / commercial property |
| **Tier 2 — Mid-size local** | dfcu, Centenary, Equity Bank, KCB, I&M | UGX 10M–50M | 20–25% p.a. | Land title, logbook, or combined |
| **Tier 3 — Small local / Islamic** | Finance Trust, Cairo International, Tropical | UGX 2M–20M | 22–28% p.a. | Logbook, guarantors, or group |
| **Tier 4 — MDIs (Micro-deposit-taking)** | Pride Microfinance, FINCA, UGAFODE | UGX 200K–5M | 28–48% p.a. (flat or effective) | Group guarantee or salary |
| **DFIs** | Uganda Development Bank (UDB), ACF | UGX 50M+ | 12–17% p.a. | Land title or movable assets |
| **SACCO / Cooperative** | Variety | UGX 100K–10M | 18–30% p.a. | Share savings or group |

## 3. Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratios by Collateral Type

| Collateral Type | LTV — Tier 1–2 Banks | LTV — Tier 3–4 | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Kampala land with title | 50–60% | 40–50% | Forced-sale value discount applied |
| Upcountry land with title | 40–50% | 30–40% | Lower liquidity = lower LTV |
| Customary land (non-titled) | Not accepted | 0–20% (rare) | Most banks reject; some rural lenders accept with LC1 letter |
| Motor vehicle (logbook, ≤5 yrs old) | 60–70% | 50–60% | Comprehensive insurance mandatory |
| Commercial building (certificate of title) | 50% | 35–40% | Must be rentable to third party |
| Plant and machinery | 30–40% | 20–30% | Must be readily realisable; valuation required |
| Stocks/inventory | 20–30% | 10–20% | Rarely accepted alone; must be easily saleable |
| Personal guarantee (director) | Up to 30% supplement | Up to 20% | Only with net worth statement |
| UCGS guarantee | Up to 80% of loan | Up to 80% | Uganda Credit Guarantee Scheme — applies to qualifying SMEs |

**Collateral coverage minimum:** Most Ugandan banks require collateral valued at ≥125% of loan value. Some Tier 1 banks require 150%.

| Documentation | Stanbic / ABSA (Tier 1) | Centenary / Equity (Tier 2) | Microfinance (Tier 4) |
|---|---|---|---|

| Business registration | URSB certificate + TIN | URSB certificate + TIN | URSB certificate preferred |

| Director CVs | Required (all directors) | Required | Not required |

## 5. Minimum Ratio Requirements by Lender Tier

| Ratio | Tier 1–2 Banks | Tier 3 / MDI | UDB / DFI | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **DSCR** (EBITDA ÷ Annual Debt Service) | ≥ 1.25x | ≥ 1.10x | ≥ 1.30x | Critical — plan is declined below threshold |
| **Current Ratio** | ≥ 1.5x preferred | ≥ 1.0x | ≥ 1.5x | |
| **Debt-to-Equity** | ≤ 2.0x | ≤ 3.0x | ≤ 1.5x | DFI stricter due to longer terms |
| **Gross Margin** | Sector-specific | No minimum | No minimum stated | Banks compare to sector norms |
| **Owner Equity Contribution** | ≥ 20% of project cost | ≥ 10% | ≥ 20–30% | DFI requires higher equity from promoter |

## 6. Uganda Development Bank (UDB) — DFI Criteria

UDB provides long-term capital at subsidised rates. Criteria differ significantly from commercial banks:

**Eligible sectors (2025):**
- Agribusiness and food processing
- Manufacturing (import substitution)
- Infrastructure (energy, ICT, transport)
- Tourism (gazetted areas)
- Healthcare
- Education

**UDB Minimum Requirements:**
- Minimum loan: UGX 500 million (some windows: UGX 100M for SMEs)
- Promoter equity: ≥ 30% of total project cost
- DSCR: ≥ 1.30x (stricter than commercial banks)
- Environmental and Social Management Plan (ESMP) required for projects >UGX 1 billion
- Board of directors required for companies borrowing >UGX 500M (minimum 3 directors, one independent)
- Gender action plan: percentage of women employees and beneficiaries
- Local content plan: proportion of local inputs and staff

**UDB security requirements:**
- First charge over project assets
- Personal guarantees from all directors
- Land/property as security where available
- Escrow account for project cash flows

## 7. Agricultural Credit Facility (ACF) — Subsidised Rate

Managed by Bank of Uganda, disbursed through participating commercial banks at 12% p.a.:

**Eligible activities:**
- Agricultural production (crops, livestock, fisheries)
- Agricultural value addition (processing, storage)
- Agricultural equipment and machinery

**ACF process:**
1. Apply to a participating bank (Centenary, Equity, dfcu, Stanbic)
2. Bank appraises and approves
3. Bank submits to BoU for ACF refinancing at 6%
4. Bank on-lends at 12% maximum

**Key requirement:** Must be for primary agriculture or direct value addition — not for trade or services.

## 8. Uganda Credit Guarantee Scheme (UCGS)

The UCGS (managed by PSFU/BoU) enables SMEs without sufficient collateral to access bank loans:

- **Guarantee coverage:** Up to 80% of loan value
- **Eligible loan range:** UGX 5M to UGX 500M
- **Eligible borrowers:** Registered SMEs with 12-month trading history
- **Application:** Through participating bank — bank applies to UCGS on behalf of client
- **UCGS fee:** Typically 1–2% p.a. on guaranteed portion (adds to borrower's effective rate)
- **Impact on DSCR calculation:** Include UCGS fee in annual debt service

**When to recommend UCGS:**
- Collateral available is ≤ 80% of required coverage (125% of loan)
- Business is creditworthy but asset-light (e.g., service businesses, youth startups)

## 9. CAMPARI Pre-Scoring Quick Reference

When running the CAMPARI bankability assessment, use these Uganda-specific benchmarks:

### Character Benchmarks
- NIN: Required for all directors (2025 law — no NIN, no TIN, no licence)
- CRB check: Banks run Uganda Credit Reference Bureau (CRB) check — any NPL in the last 5 years is a red flag
- URSB registration: Must be current (renewable annually for business names; once for companies)
- Trading licence: Must be from relevant authority (KCCA for Kampala; district for upcountry)

### Means Benchmarks
- **DSCR calculation (Uganda context):**
  - EBITDA = Net profit + depreciation + interest (before tax adjustments)
  - Annual debt service = 12 × monthly instalment (principal + interest)
  - Target: ≥ 1.25x in all years of loan repayment

- **Cash flow adequacy:**
  - Positive operating cash flow within 12 months of loan disbursement
  - Minimum 1 month of operating expenses held as cash reserve

### Insurance Benchmarks
- Land in Kampala: Current market value (Kampala Land Board or registered valuer) ÷ 1.25 = maximum loan against that collateral
- Vehicle: Forced sale value = 60% of current market value × LTV rate
- Comprehensive insurance: Required on all pledged vehicles and equipment

## 10. Sector-Specific Lending Norms

| Sector | Typical Loan Term | Collateral Preference | DSCR Concern | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Agriculture (smallholder) | 1–3 years | Group guarantee / crop receipts | Seasonal cash flow | ACF rate applicable |
| Agriculture (large farm/processing) | 3–7 years | Land + equipment | DSCR must cover off-season | UDB preferred lender |
| Manufacturing | 5–10 years | Equipment + building | Break-even month critical | Import dependency = forex risk |
| Retail / wholesale trade | 1–2 years (revolving) | Stock + receivables | Working capital cycle | Short terms only; banks cautious on pure trade |
| Hospitality (hotel/lodge) | 5–10 years | Property | Occupancy rate assumption | Tourist-dependent = volatile |
| Construction (contractor) | 12–18 months | Contract receipts + equipment | Project-based cash flow | Advance payment guarantee required |
| Health (clinic/pharmacy) | 3–7 years | Equipment + building | Licence risk | MUST have MOH operating licence |
| Education (school) | 5–10 years | Buildings | Enrolment risk | Ministry of Education registration required |
| ICT / digital services | 1–3 years | Equipment | Revenue concentration | Banks often require IP valuation for software |

## 11. Women Entrepreneurs — Supplementary Lending Pathways

Based on World Bank 2022 data: women-owned SMEs in Uganda face structural barriers (30% revenue gap). Lenders should consider — and plans should include:

- **UWEP (Uganda Women Entrepreneurship Programme):** Revolving fund UGX 0–30M, applied through SACCO groups of ≥ 30 women; 0% interest for qualifying groups
- **WEP SACCOs:** Women's SACCOs under Ministry of Gender can access blended finance at 10–15% p.a.
- **Equity Bank She Leads / dfcu Women in Business:** Reduced documentation requirements, mentorship, priority processing
- **Mastercard Foundation / Tony Elumelu:** Grant + loan blends for women-led businesses; equity-free
- **USAID She Trades, UN Women Impact:** Grant funding for women export-ready businesses

See `11-funding-request/references/women-financing-uganda.md` for full programme details.

## 12. Credit Scoring Estimate — Quick Map

| Business Characteristic | Positive Signal | Negative Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Years in operation | ≥ 3 years | < 1 year |
| Financial records | Audited or formal | Reconstructed or verbal |
| Existing bank relationship | Same bank, no bounced cheques | First-time applicant |
| Collateral | Title deed in applicant's name | No collateral or third-party title |
| DSCR | ≥ 1.5x | < 1.25x |
| Sector | Essential goods, food, health | Hospitality, events, seasonal |
| Owner equity | ≥ 30% of project | < 10% of project |
| Tax compliance | URA TCC held | No TCC or outstanding arrears |
| Prior loan history | Repaid on time | Rescheduled or written off |
| Gender of owner | No formal advantage at most banks | None — some banks offer women-friendly terms |

## 13. CAMPARI Score — Lender Tier Eligibility Map

Use this table when scoring a business plan to identify which lender tier it is likely to qualify
for. Each CAMPARI dimension is scored 1-10; the table maps average scores to lender eligibility.

| CAMPARI Average Score | Likely Eligible Lender | Next Step |
|---|---|---|
| **9-10** | Uganda Development Bank (UDB), IFC, AfDB lending windows | Prepare full ESMP; board resolution; UDB application form |
| **8-9** | Tier 1 commercial banks (Stanbic, ABSA, Standard Chartered) | Audited accounts required; title deed collateral |
| **7-8** | Tier 2 banks (Centenary, Equity, dfcu, KCB) | 12-month bank statements; formal business plan |
| **6-7** | Tier 3 banks (Finance Trust, Tropical, Cairo) + UCGS-backed loan | Consider UCGS to bridge collateral gap |
| **5-6** | MDIs (Pride, FINCA, UGAFODE) | Group guarantee or salary deduction |
| **4-5** | SACCOs and cooperative lenders | Build savings history; member share contribution |
| **Below 4** | Informal lenders only (not recommended for bankable plan) | Return to planning phase; address fundamental gaps |

**Score dimension weightings for this map:**
The Character and Means (DSCR) dimensions are gating factors. A score below 5 on either
Character or Means drops the overall eligibility down one full tier regardless of other scores.

**Example:**
- Character: 8, Ability: 7, Means (DSCR 1.32x): 8, Purpose: 9, Amount: 8, Repayment: 7, Insurance: 6
- Average: 7.6 → Tier 2 bank eligible; but collateral (Insurance: 6) may require UCGS to bridge.

## 14. Documentation Checklist Before Loan Application

Use this checklist to confirm the business plan package is complete before submitting to any lender:

**Legal and registration:**
- [ ] URSB certificate of incorporation or business name registration
- [ ] Company/partnership deed (where applicable)
- [ ] Trading licence (KCCA or district council)
- [ ] TIN certificate (URA)
- [ ] URA Tax Compliance Certificate (TCC) — valid within 12 months
- [ ] NIN for all directors and guarantors

**Financial records:**
- [ ] 2 years audited financial statements (Tier 1-2) or 12 months bank statements (Tier 3-4)
- [ ] 12-month cash flow projection with stated assumptions
- [ ] 36-month income statement and balance sheet projection
- [ ] Existing debt schedule (all outstanding loans listed)

**Business plan:**
- [ ] Executive summary (2 pages maximum)
- [ ] Market analysis with demand justification
- [ ] Operations plan
- [ ] Management team CVs (all directors)
- [ ] Financial projections with DSCR calculated
- [ ] Use of funds itemised table

**Collateral:**
- [ ] Land title (certified copy; original presented at signing)
- [ ] Valuation report from approved valuer (dated within 12 months)
- [ ] Vehicle logbook (for vehicle collateral) + motor insurance certificate
- [ ] UCGS pre-qualification letter (if UCGS route)

**Sector-specific:**
- [ ] NEMA permit (if environmental impact applicable)
- [ ] UNBS licence (if manufacturing)
- [ ] MOH operating licence (if health sector)
- [ ] MAAIF registration (if agribusiness)
