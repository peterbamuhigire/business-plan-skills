# Quarterly Strategy Map

Use this reference to make quarterly execution plans strategic rather than activity-heavy.

## 1. Choose the quarter's bottleneck

A quarter should attack the constraint that most limits the annual plan.

Typical constraints:

- sales conversion
- fulfilment capacity
- working capital discipline
- management bandwidth
- system adoption

## 2. Link the quarter to the scorecard

Each quarter should advance one dominant perspective:

- Financial: margin, cash, DSCR, collection discipline
- Customer: acquisition, retention, service quality
- Internal Process: cycle time, defect reduction, throughput
- Learning / Capability: hiring, training, systems, management routines

## 3. Show causality

Do not stop at tasks. Show the causal chain:

- capability built
- process improved
- customer result achieved
- financial effect created

## 4. Review both leading and lagging signals

Lagging:

- revenue
- gross margin
- cash
- completed milestones

Leading:

- pipeline quality
- proposal conversion
- production uptime
- staffing readiness
- process-compliance rates

## 5. Red flags

- too many objectives
- tasks without owners
- no monthly correction point
- no scorecard linkage
- action volume mistaken for progress
