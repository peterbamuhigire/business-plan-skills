# Empathy-Based Validation Tools

> Based on Alam, M.M. *Transforming an Idea Into a Business with Design Thinking*. Springer.

## Transform3+1 Framework Overview

An 18-step process across 4 stages designed to be completed in 6-12 weeks:

- **Stage 1: Understand Problem** (Desirability) — Steps 1-5
- **Stage 2: Devise Solution** (Feasibility) — Steps 6-12
- **Stage 3: Craft Business** (Viability) — Steps 13-16
- **Stage 4: Manage Risk** (Uncertainty) — Steps 17-18

The Desirability-Feasibility-Viability triad provides three fundamental lenses for validation.

## Stage 1: Understanding the Problem (Steps 1-5)

### Step 1: Stakeholder Map

Three concentric rings for identifying who matters:

- **Target stakeholders** (1-3 primary beneficiaries/users) — who directly experiences the problem
- **Connected stakeholders** (directly influencing/influenced) — may be payers, implementers, or gatekeepers
- **Influenced stakeholders** (indirectly affected) — community, regulators, adjacent businesses

### Step 2: User Research / Empathy Methodology

**8 engagement guidelines for interviews:**
1. Ask "why?" repeatedly — dig beneath surface answers
2. Encourage stories — "Tell me about the last time you..."
3. Look for inconsistencies between what people say and do
4. Listen to non-verbal cues — body language, hesitation, enthusiasm
5. Embrace silence — don't fill pauses; let interviewees think
6. Ask neutral questions — avoid leading ("Don't you think...?")
7. Be genuinely curious — adopt a beginner's mindset
8. Don't suggest solutions during the interview

**8-category interview guide:**
1. **Introduction** — build rapport, explain purpose
2. **Jobs to Be Done** — what are you trying to accomplish?
3. **Customers** — who else is involved in this process?
4. **Challenges** — what makes this difficult?
5. **Aspirations** — what would the ideal look like?
6. **Stories** — tell me about a specific recent experience
7. **Emotions** — how did that make you feel?
8. **Conclusion** — anything else? Who else should I talk to?

### Step 3: Empathy Map Template

Three-column synthesis tool:

| Observations | Interpretations | Insights |
|---|---|---|
| What they're doing | What they might be thinking | What they need |
| What they're saying | What they might be feeling | What challenges they face |
| What they're seeing | Hidden motivations | Unmet needs |
| Their environment | Unstated assumptions | Opportunities |

Colour-code by interviewee, then synthesise patterns across all interviews.

### Step 5: Current Journey Map

Map the customer's current experience step by step:

| Row | Content |
|---|---|
| Steps | Sequential actions the customer takes |
| Touch Points | Systems, resources, people involved at each step |
| Emotions | How the customer feels (draw emotional line) |
| Pain Points | Red dots marking frustration/failure moments |
| Moments of Truth | Green dots marking emotional vulnerability/opportunity |

## Ideation Methodology (Stage 2, Step 6)

**Five brainstorming rules:**
1. Quantity over quality
2. Defer judgement
3. Encourage wild ideas
4. Stay focused on the problem
5. Build on others' ideas

**Four prompt categories for structured ideation:**

- **Value Proposition Prompts:** Better? Faster? Cheaper? Easier?
- **External Prompts:** How would [favourite brand / another industry / biggest competitor] solve this?
- **Constraint Prompts:** What if we had quarter the funds? Lost a team member? Didn't have access to X?
- **Contrarian Prompts:** What if we flipped conventional wisdom? (Airbnb: strangers in your home; Uber: strangers in your car)

## BFCE Use Case Evaluation Framework (Step 11)

Evaluate each product/service capability against four advantage types:

| Advantage | Question | Metric |
|---|---|---|
| **Better** (Quality) | Does it deliver superior outcomes? | Quality indicators, satisfaction scores |
| **Faster** (Efficiency) | Does it save time? | Time-to-completion, throughput |
| **Cheaper** (Cost) | Does it reduce total cost? | Cost comparison, TCO analysis |
| **Easier** (Experience) | Does it reduce effort/complexity? | Steps eliminated, learning curve |

Link each capability to the customer's Job → Goal → Vision hierarchy.

## User Testing Do's and Don'ts

### Session Lead — 13 Do's
1. Prepare a test plan with specific questions to answer
2. Set the context without revealing what you want to hear
3. Give tasks, not instructions ("Find a way to..." not "Click the...")
4. Observe behaviour before asking questions
5. Ask follow-up questions: "What did you expect?" "Why did you do that?"
6. Be neutral — don't react positively or negatively
7. Keep sessions to 30-45 minutes
8. Test with 5-8 people per round
9. Record sessions (with consent)
10. Debrief immediately after each session
11. Look for patterns across sessions, not individual opinions
12. Prioritise findings by impact
13. Plan next iteration based on findings

### Session Lead — 10 Don'ts
1. Don't explain how the product works
2. Don't defend design decisions
3. Don't ask leading questions
4. Don't help users when they struggle (observe the struggle)
5. Don't test with friends/family/colleagues
6. Don't combine testing with selling
7. Don't test too many things at once
8. Don't skip note-taking
9. Don't assume one person's opinion represents all users
10. Don't delay acting on critical findings

## Assumptions Tracking Template

| # | Assumption | Impact | Assignee | Due Date | Step | Status | Comments |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | "Customers will pay UGX X for Y" | Critical | Founder | Week 3 | 7 | New | Needs pricing test |
| 2 | "Market size is Z units/year" | Major | Analyst | Week 2 | 13 | In Progress | Checking URA data |
| 3 | "Supplier can deliver at cost Q" | Minor | Ops | Week 4 | 12 | Validated | Confirmed by 2 suppliers |

**Impact classifications:**
- **Minor** — if wrong, can adjust without major consequence
- **Major** — if wrong, requires significant business model changes
- **Critical** — if wrong, the entire venture may not be viable

**Status values:** New → In Progress → Validated / Disproved

**Hourglass metaphor:** Unvalidated assumptions flow from top; validated facts collect at bottom. The goal is to empty the top.

## Risk Score Calculation

Quantify overall venture risk from unvalidated assumptions:

```
Risk Score = (Minor count × 1) + (Major count × 5) + (Critical count × 25)
```

**Target:** Keep Risk Score below 100.

Can be broken down by stage:
- Stage 1 (Desirability) risk score
- Stage 2 (Feasibility) risk score
- Stage 3 (Viability) risk score

**Usage in business plans:** Present the Risk Score trajectory — showing how it decreased as assumptions were validated through Customer Development activities. Investors want to see risk being systematically retired.

## Business Context Map (Step 13)

Six-sector environmental scanning tool, each split into near-term (<3 years) and long-term (>3 years):

1. **Technology trends** — emerging tech affecting the industry
2. **Business model trends** — new ways of delivering/capturing value
3. **Competition** — anything that replaces the need for your solution
4. **Industry trends** — structural shifts in the sector
5. **Macroeconomic trends** — GDP, inflation, demographics, urbanisation
6. **Regulatory/Other** — government policy, licensing, standards

### Long Version (adds investor-facing elements)

Our unique advantage is [USP]. Our team brings [relevant experience]. We have [traction/prototype evidence]. We are seeking [funding amount] for [equity stake] to [use of funds].
