# Rapid Validation Methods

> Based on Kagan, N. (2024). *Million Dollar Weekend*. Portfolio/Penguin.

## The Golden Rule of Validation

Find **three customers in forty-eight hours** who will give you money for your idea.

Three non-negotiable rules:
1. **Only 48 hours allowed** — urgency forces action over analysis
2. **Get three paying customers** — not three interested people, three who pay
3. **Collect money up front** — a promise of payment is not validation

## Vitamin vs. Painkiller Test

Before investing time in validation, classify the idea:
- **Vitamin** — nice-to-have; customers might buy if convenient
- **Painkiller** — must-have; customers actively seeking a solution

Painkillers are far more viable businesses. If your idea is a vitamin, either reframe it as a painkiller or move on.

## Three Methods to Validate ANY Business Idea

### 1. Direct Preselling
Contact real people (start with your Dream Ten), tell them what you're selling, ask for money. Use the LOT framework below.

### 2. Marketplaces
Post on Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, Gumtree, Reddit, or niche forums to test demand. See if people respond. Track interest vs. actual purchase intent.

### 3. Landing Pages
Simple web page with a price and a buy/pre-order button. Tools: Instapage, Unbounce, ClickFunnels, Carrd. Track clicks on the buy button as a proxy for willingness to pay.

## Dream Ten List

Before any outreach, build a spreadsheet of 10 ideal validation prospects:

| Name | Company/Context | Phone | Email | Date Contacted | Follow-up Date | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

**Selection criteria:** people who most likely have the problem you're solving, within your existing network or one connection away.

Inspired by Chet Holmes' "Dream 100" methodology from *The Ultimate Sales Machine*.

## LOT Validation Framework

Three-part conversation framework for preselling:

### 1. Listen
Get customers talking about their problem with three questions:
- What is most frustrating about [problem area]?
- How would [solving X] make your life/work better?
- What should [solution X] cost?

### 2. Options
Suggest solutions and pricing. Watch for:
- **Excitement** (leaning in, asking questions) = signal to proceed
- **Eye rolls** (polite disinterest, "interesting...") = signal to pivot

### 3. Transition
Close the sale using the Price + Benefit + Time formula (below).

## Price + Benefit + Time Offer Formula

Structure every offer as: **"For [price], I will [benefit] in [time]."**

Examples:
- "For UGX 100,000, I will teach you how to save 2 hours a day on bookkeeping, in just one training session."
- "For UGX 500,000/month, I will manage your social media and grow your following by 50% in 90 days."
- "For UGX 2,000,000, I will build you a professional website that generates leads, delivered in 2 weeks."

The formula works because it makes the value concrete: what they pay, what they get, how soon.

## Four-Question Rejection Script

When someone says no during validation, extract maximum learning:

1. **"Why not?"** — understand the real objection
2. **"Who is one person you know who would really like this?"** — get a warm referral
3. **"What would make this a no-brainer for you?"** — discover the missing value
4. **"What would you pay for that?"** — calibrate pricing

Every rejection should produce at least one referral or one insight.

## Million-Dollar Opportunity Assessment

Two quick checks before deep validation:

### 1. Is the Market Growing?
Use Google Trends to check 5-year search trends for your product/service category. Flat or declining = warning sign.

### 2. Is This a Million-Dollar Opportunity?
Use Facebook Ads audience tool (or equivalent) to estimate addressable audience size. Multiply by your price point:

**Audience size × Price per unit ≥ target revenue?**

If yes, the market is large enough. If no, adjust the Six Revenue Dials (below).

## One-Minute Business Model

Quick feasibility calculation:

```
Revenue per unit - Cost per unit = Profit per unit
Target annual revenue ÷ Profit per unit = Units needed per year
Units needed ÷ 12 = Units needed per month
```

If the monthly target feels achievable, proceed. If not, adjust the dials.

## Six Revenue Dials

When the One-Minute Business Model doesn't work, adjust these levers:

1. **Average order value** — increase purchase amount (bundles, premium versions)
2. **Frequency** — increase how often someone buys (subscriptions, consumables)
3. **Price point** — increase or decrease price (test willingness to pay)
4. **Customer type** — target wealthier or higher-value segments
5. **Product line** — add complementary products
6. **Add-on services** — bundle services with products (installation, training, support)

## Customer First Approach: Three Ws

Work backward from the customer, not the product:
- **Who** are you selling to? (specific person, not "everyone")
- **What** problem are you solving? (their words, not yours)
- **Where** are they? (physical and digital locations)

## Zone of Influence

Start validation within your existing networks and communities. Seasoned entrepreneurs find opportunities within who they already know:
- Your professional network
- Your social media following (even small)
- WhatsApp/Telegram groups you belong to
- Your local community (church, school, market)
- Industry events you attend

## Content Circle Framework for Audience Building

Three concentric circles for building an audience post-validation:

1. **Core Circle** — very narrow niche within a niche (e.g., Ugandan poultry feed formulation tips)
2. **Medium Circle** — broader overlapping topic (e.g., poultry farming in East Africa)
3. **Large Circle** — largest related audience (e.g., small-scale agriculture, agribusiness)

Start with core, expand outward as audience grows.

## Five-Question Marketing Plan

Once validated, answer these five questions:
1. What is your one goal for this year?
2. Who exactly is your customer and where can you find them?
3. What is one marketing activity you can double down on?
4. How can you delight your first 100 customers?
5. If you HAD to double your business with no money in 30 days, what would you do?

## Experiment-Based Marketing

Create a spreadsheet of marketing experiments:

| Channel | Expected Sales | Actual Sales | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| WhatsApp broadcast | 10 | ? | Free | Test this week |
| Facebook ad | 5 | ? | UGX 200K | Run for 30 days |
| Market stall sampling | 3 | ? | UGX 100K | Saturday market |

Run each experiment for 30 days. Then: **double down on what works, kill what doesn't.**

## The Law of 100

Commit to 100 repetitions of any activity before evaluating whether it works:
- 100 customer conversations
- 100 social media posts
- 100 cold emails
- 100 market visits

This combats premature abandonment during "the dip" (Godin).
